## How AI Coaching Works

Every lesson in the Academy includes an AI coach that goes far beyond a generic chatbot. The coaching engine combines three sources of context:

1. **Your business profile** — Industry, stage, target customer, challenges
2. **Lesson content** — The specific concepts and frameworks being taught
3. **Curriculum knowledge** — The full Academy content indexed for retrieval (RAG)

This means when you ask "How do I apply this to my business?", the coach actually knows your business.

## In-Lesson Coach

Each lesson has a coach chat interface at the bottom. Use it to:

- **Clarify concepts** — "Can you explain ICP scoring in simpler terms?"
- **Get examples** — "Give me an example cold email for my specific market"
- **Apply frameworks** — "Walk me through the SPIN framework for my product"
- **Get feedback** — "Here's my positioning statement — what would you change?"
- **Go deeper** — "What resources or next steps would you recommend?"

The coach maintains conversation context within a lesson, so you can have multi-turn discussions.

<Banner type="note">
  AI coaching responses draw from the full curriculum library using RAG
  (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). This means the coach can reference concepts
  from other lessons when relevant to your question.
</Banner>

## Roleplay Practice

The Roleplay feature lets you practice real sales scenarios with AI personas. Available personas include:

### Alex the Skeptic

A cautious buyer who pushes back on everything. Great for practicing objection handling and building confidence under pressure.

### Jordan the Builder

A technical evaluator who cares about implementation details. Helps you practice positioning for technical buyers.

### Morgan the Perfectionist

A detail-oriented decision maker who needs every question answered. Builds your patience and thoroughness in sales conversations.

### Sam the Mentor

A friendly, experienced buyer who gives constructive feedback. Useful for refining your pitch in a supportive environment.

Each persona has distinct personality traits, communication styles, and buying criteria. They'll challenge you in different ways, building well-rounded sales skills.

## Getting the Most from AI Coaching

### Be Specific

Instead of: "How do I get more customers?"
Try: "I sell project management software to 5-person marketing agencies. What's the best channel to reach them?"

### Share Your Work

Paste your actual copy, emails, or scripts into the coach and ask for feedback. The coach gives much better advice when reviewing concrete work.

### Use Iterative Refinement

1. Share your first draft
2. Get feedback
3. Revise based on feedback
4. Share the revision
5. Repeat until strong

### Reference Your Situation

Remind the coach of relevant context: "Given that I'm pre-revenue and targeting enterprise..." This helps the coach tailor advice to your stage.

## AI Assessment Feedback

When you complete assessments (open-ended exercises), the AI provides structured feedback:

- **Score** — Numerical rating on a rubric specific to the exercise
- **Strengths** — What you did well
- **Areas for Improvement** — Specific, actionable suggestions
- **Example** — A model response for reference

You can always revise and resubmit assessments to improve your score.

## Privacy & AI Providers

Solo GTM OS uses multiple AI providers for coaching:

- **OpenAI** (GPT models)
- **Anthropic** (Claude models)
- **OpenRouter** (for flexibility)

Your business context is sent to these providers to generate personalized responses. These providers do not store your data for training. You can see which provider is active in Settings.
