## LESSON 1: When Pipeline Load Justifies a VA (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Delegation Threshold** — You need help when: >40-50 active opportunities in your pipeline, OR >5 hours/week on pure admin (data entry, CRM updates, scheduling, research). Below this, automation (Course 42) is enough.
2. **The Time Audit** — Track your hours for 1 week. Categorize: Revenue-generating (calls, proposals, relationship building), Revenue-supporting (research, CRM updates, follow-up admin), Revenue-neutral (scheduling, data entry, inbox management). Delegate revenue-neutral first, then revenue-supporting.
3. **The $50/hour Test** — If a task can be done by someone at $5-15/hour, and you value your time at $50-150/hour, delegation has a 5-10x ROI. Even if the VA is 70% as good as you, the math works.
4. **What NOT to Delegate (Yet)** — Discovery calls, pricing conversations, relationship-building emails to top prospects, strategy decisions, creative direction. These require your voice, judgment, and context.
5. **The Delegation Ladder** — Level 1: Data entry and scheduling. Level 2: Research and CRM updates. Level 3: Inbox triage and first-touch communication. Level 4: Prospect outreach with templates. Each level requires more training and trust.
6. **Signs You're Overdue** — Leads going un-followed-up for 3+ days. CRM data is weeks out of date. You cancel calls because you're drowning in admin. You work nights doing tasks a VA could handle.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Solo founders spend 40-60% of time on non-revenue tasks | Founder time studies | Delegation recovers 15-25 hours/month |
| At 40+ active opportunities, manual CRM management breaks down | Sales operations data | This is the inflection point |
| VA cost: $3-8/hr for Filipino VAs (full-time equivalent) | OnlineJobs.ph market rates | 10-20x cheaper than founder time |
| Tasks delegated to VAs see 60-80% quality of founder-performed | VA management studies | Good enough for admin tasks |
| Founders who delegate admin report 30% more time for sales activities | Productivity research | Delegation directly increases revenue capacity |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The Delegation Threshold Calculator:**
  - Active opportunities > 40-50? → Ready for VA
  - Admin hours/week > 5? → Ready for VA
  - Revenue-neutral tasks > 10 hrs/week? → Overdue for VA
  - If none of the above: maximize Course 42 automations first

- **The Time Audit Categories:**
  - Revenue-Generating: Discovery calls, demos, proposals, closing conversations, relationship emails to top 20%
  - Revenue-Supporting: Prospect research, CRM updates, follow-up scheduling, pipeline reporting
  - Revenue-Neutral: Data entry, calendar management, inbox sorting, admin tasks
  - Revenue-Negative: Unnecessary meetings, social media browsing, tool tinkering

### Artifact Component

## LESSON 2: VA vs Part-Time SDR: Which First? (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **VA: Administrative Support** — Handles CRM updates, research, scheduling, inbox triage, data entry. Does NOT make sales calls or write original outreach. Cost: $500-1,500/month for 20-40 hrs/week.
2. **Part-Time SDR: Sales Support** — Handles prospect outreach (with templates), follow-up sequences, qualification calls (with scripts), meeting booking. Makes sales-oriented decisions. Cost: $1,500-3,000/month for 20 hrs/week.
3. **VA First (Most Solo Founders)** — If your bottleneck is admin drowning your selling time, hire a VA first. Free up your time for revenue-generating activities. The VA amplifies YOUR selling capacity.
4. **SDR First (Exceptions)** — If you have a proven, documented outreach process AND you need more outbound volume AND you can't increase your own outreach hours, consider an SDR first. But this is rare before $20-30K MRR.
5. **The Hybrid Approach** — Start with a VA for 3 months. Document everything. Then decide: does the VA's role naturally grow into SDR territory? Or do you need a separate SDR? Many VAs can evolve into SDR support.
6. **Budget Reality Check** — VA: $500-1,500/month. Part-time SDR: $1,500-3,000/month. Full-time SDR: $3,500-5,500/month. Budget must come from revenue, not savings.

## LESSON 3: SOP 1: Inbox Triage (Lead/Customer/Admin/Noise) (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Inbox Triage Framework** — Every incoming email gets categorized: Lead (potential customer), Customer (existing customer), Admin (internal/vendor), Noise (spam/irrelevant). VA reads, tags, and routes. Founder handles Leads and Customers.
2. **Triage Decision Rules** — Lead indicators: mentions product/service, asks about pricing, requests a call, comes from target company. Customer indicators: from known customer domain, references existing project/subscription. Admin: invoices, team updates, vendor emails. Noise: everything else.
3. **Routing Rules** — Lead (high score): Flag urgent → Founder responds within 4 hours. Lead (low score): Tag in CRM → Add to nurture sequence. Customer: Log in CRM → Route to appropriate response. Admin: File → Create task if action needed. Noise: Archive or delete.
4. **VA Response Templates** — For standard replies the VA can handle: "Thanks for reaching out, [Founder Name] will be in touch shortly." Meeting scheduling: VA handles Calendly link sharing. Information requests: VA sends pre-approved collateral.
5. **Escalation Protocol** — When the VA is unsure: tag as "Unsure" → Founder reviews within 24 hours. Track "Unsure" frequency to identify training gaps.
6. **SOP Documentation Format** — Each SOP follows: Purpose → Trigger → Steps → Decision rules → Templates → Escalation → QA checklist.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Average founder receives 50-150 emails/day | Email management studies | VA can triage 80% of these |
| 30-40% of emails are noise/spam even after filters | Email analytics | VA eliminates the mental load of sorting |
| Response time to leads improves 3-5x with VA triage | VA management data | VA flags leads immediately |
| Inbox triage SOP reduces founder email time by 60-70% | Productivity studies | From 90 min/day to 30 min/day |

### Frameworks & Models

- **Inbox Triage Decision Tree:**
  1. Is the sender a known customer? → YES → Category: Customer. Log in CRM. Route to founder.
  2. Is the sender asking about product/service/pricing? → YES → Category: Lead. Create CRM contact. Flag for founder.
  3. Is the sender from a target company (ICP match)? → YES → Category: Lead. Research → CRM. Flag for founder.
  4. Is it an internal/vendor/admin email? → YES → Category: Admin. File. Create task if action needed.
  5. None of the above → Category: Noise. Archive.

- **SOP Template Format:**
  - **Purpose**: What this SOP accomplishes
  - **Trigger**: When to execute (e.g., "Every incoming email")
  - **Steps**: Numbered steps with screenshots
  - **Decision Rules**: If/then logic for each category
  - **Templates**: Pre-written responses for each category
  - **Escalation**: When to flag for founder
  - **QA**: Checklist to verify accuracy

### Artifact Component

**Inbox Triage SOP** — Complete SOP document with decision tree, routing rules, response templates, escalation protocol, and QA checklist.

### Interactive Element

**Guided Build:** Student provides their email patterns (lead sources, customer domains, common admin senders). AI generates a customized inbox triage SOP with decision rules and templates.

## LESSON 4: SOP 2: CRM Updates (Stage, Amount, Next Steps) (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The CRM Update SOP** — VA reviews CRM daily (15-20 min). Updates deal stages based on email/call outcomes. Verifies every deal has a next action with a date. Logs meeting notes from calendar events.
2. **Stage Update Rules** — VA checks for: emails sent (Contacted), replies received (Engaged), meetings scheduled (Meeting), proposals sent (Proposal). VA updates stages when evidence is clear. Ambiguous cases: flag for founder.
3. **Amount and Close Date Updates** — VA updates deal amount when pricing is shared in emails. VA updates expected close date based on timeline mentioned in correspondence. VA does NOT estimate amounts or dates — only uses explicit information.
4. **Next Steps Verification** — Every deal must have a "next action" with a date. VA checks daily: any deals without next steps? Flag them. Any next steps overdue? Flag them.
5. **Meeting Note Entry** — After founder's meetings: VA enters structured notes from a template (Attendees, Topics Discussed, Decisions Made, Next Steps, Follow-Up Owner). Founder provides raw notes; VA formats and enters.
6. **Data Quality Checks** — VA runs weekly: duplicate contacts? Deals in wrong stages? Missing required fields? Contacts without company? This maintains the AI-ready schema from Course 40.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 30-40% reduction in CRM maintenance time with VA support | BCG / Operations data | Saves founder 5-10 hours/month |
| 91% of CRM data goes stale within 12 months without maintenance | ZoomInfo | VA keeps data fresh |
| Deals without next actions die 2x faster | CSO Insights | VA verifies every deal has a next step |
| Structured CRM notes improve AI agent accuracy by 40-60% | Practical AI testing | VA maintains AI-ready schema quality |

### Frameworks & Models

- **CRM Update Daily Checklist:**
  1. Check inbox for deal-related emails. Update stages for any deals with new activity. (5 min)
  2. Verify every active deal has a next action with a date. Flag any missing. (3 min)
  3. Update deal amounts and close dates based on explicit correspondence. (3 min)
  4. Enter meeting notes from completed meetings (using template). (5 min)
  5. Run data quality spot-check: 5 random contacts for completeness. (4 min)

### Artifact Component

## LESSON 5: SOP 3: Prospect Research Tasks (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Prospect Research SOP** — VA researches prospects before founder outreach. Produces a 1-page "Prospect Brief" for each target. Covers: company overview, key person details, recent news/triggers, ICP fit assessment, suggested talking points.
2. **Research Sources** — LinkedIn (company page, personal profile, recent posts), company website (about page, blog, press), Crunchbase (funding, headcount), Google News (recent mentions), Product Hunt (for SaaS targets), social media (Twitter, podcasts they've been on).
3. **The Prospect Brief Template** — Name, Title, Company, LinkedIn URL, Company Size, Industry, Recent Trigger (job change, funding, product launch), ICP Fit Score (VA's assessment 1-10), Suggested Talking Point, Potential Objections.
4. **Research Quality Standards** — Brief must be completed in <15 minutes per prospect. All facts must have a source link. "Suggested talking point" must reference something specific to this person, not generic. ICP score must reference the scoring model from Course 40.
5. **Batch Research Workflow** — Founder provides list of 10-20 prospects on Monday. VA researches and delivers briefs by Wednesday. Founder reviews briefs and personalizes outreach Thursday-Friday.
6. **AI-Assisted Research** — VA can use ChatGPT/Perplexity to summarize company info and suggest talking points. VA must verify AI output against primary sources. AI speeds up research from 15 min to 8-10 min per prospect.

## LESSON 6: Hiring, Onboarding, and Managing a VA (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **Writing the Job Post** — Title: "Virtual Assistant — Sales Operations Support." Description: Specific tasks (inbox triage, CRM updates, prospect research), tools used (HubSpot, Google Sheets, Slack), hours (part-time 10-20 hrs/week or full-time), timezone requirements.
2. **Screening Process** — (1) Review applications for English proficiency and relevant experience. (2) Send a paid test task ($10-20): triage 10 sample emails and research 3 sample prospects. (3) 15-minute video interview: assess communication, ask about tool experience, give a scenario.
3. **Onboarding Protocol** — Week 1: VA completes SOPs 1-3 with oversight. Founder reviews every output. Daily 15-minute check-in. Week 2: VA works independently with spot-checks. 3x/week check-in. Week 3-4: VA is autonomous with weekly check-in and QA reviews.
4. **Tool Access** — CRM: view + edit access (not admin). Email: delegated access or shared inbox. Slack: dedicated channel. Google Drive: shared SOP folder. Password manager: use LastPass or 1Password for shared credentials (never share passwords in chat).
5. **Management Cadence** — Weekly: 30-minute 1:1 (what went well, what was confusing, what needs updating). Monthly: SOP review and update. Quarterly: performance review and rate adjustment.
6. **Common Mistakes** — (1) Not documenting SOPs before hiring. (2) Giving too much responsibility too fast. (3) Not providing feedback. (4) Micromanaging instead of spot-checking. (5) Not having clear escalation rules.

### Data Points & Statistics

|-----------|--------|---------|
| Average onboarding time for VA: 2-3 weeks to full productivity | VA management data | Invest upfront, save long-term |
| Paid test tasks filter 60-70% of unqualified candidates | Hiring best practices | Never hire without a test task |
| VA turnover in first 3 months: 30-40% | OnlineJobs.ph data | Good onboarding reduces this to 15-20% |
| VAs with weekly 1:1s are 2x more likely to stay 12+ months | Remote work studies | Consistency builds retention |
| Filipino VAs on OnlineJobs.ph: average tenure 2-4 years with good management | OnlineJobs.ph | Longer than most US contractors |

## LESSON 7: Tools for VA Collaboration (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The VA Collaboration Stack** — Slack (communication), Notion (SOPs and documentation), Loom (async video training), CRM (shared workspace), Google Drive (file sharing). Total cost: $0-30/month.
2. **Loom for Training** — Record screen walkthroughs of every SOP. VA can rewatch at their own pace. Update recordings when processes change. Free tier: 25 videos, 5 minutes max. Creator: $15/mo for unlimited.
3. **Notion for SOPs** — Create a "VA Hub" workspace: SOPs, templates, FAQ, escalation contacts, tool logins reference. VA can update SOPs as they learn. Version history tracks changes.
4. **Slack Communication Rules** — Dedicated #va-channel. VA posts daily start-of-shift summary and end-of-shift update. Founder responds within 2 hours during business hours. Urgent escalations: @mention in channel.
5. **QA Process** — Weekly spot-check: review 5-10 random outputs (triaged emails, CRM updates, prospect briefs). Score on accuracy (correct categorization) and completeness (all fields filled). Target: 90%+ accuracy by Week 4.
6. **Tango and Scribe for SOP Creation** — Tango auto-captures your screen clicks and generates step-by-step guides. Scribe does similar with AI-generated instructions. Use these to create SOPs by simply doing the task yourself.

## LESSON 8: Your Delegation Playbook (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Complete Delegation Review** — Walk through all 7 lessons' artifacts and verify completeness
2. **The Delegation Scorecard** — One-page document listing all delegated tasks, who owns them, quality standards, and review cadence. This is the management dashboard for your VA relationship.
3. **The 7-Day Delegation Sprint** — Day 1: Finalize SOPs and record Loom videos. Day 2: Post job listing and begin screening. Day 3-4: Review applications and send test tasks. Day 5: Interview top 2 candidates. Day 6: Make offer and share onboarding materials. Day 7: Begin Week 1 onboarding.
4. **Scaling Delegation** — VA handles Level 1-2 tasks well? Consider: adding Level 3-4 tasks (inbox response, outreach with templates), hiring a second VA for different timezone coverage, transitioning VA to part-time SDR role.
5. **When Delegation Fails** — Signs: QA scores consistently below 80%, VA misses deadlines, communication breaks down, you're spending more time managing than the tasks would take. Solutions: retrain (first), restructure tasks, or part ways.
6. **Handoff to Course 45** — When you need more than a VA — a real sales hire — Course 45 covers hiring, compensation, and management. Your SOPs and playbooks from this course become the onboarding materials for that hire.

### Frameworks & Models

**7-Day Delegation Sprint:**

| Day | Activity | Output |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 1 | Finalize 3 SOPs + record Loom videos | SOP library ready |
| 2 | Post job listing on OnlineJobs.ph/Upwork | Applications incoming |
| 3-4 | Review applications, send test tasks ($10-20) | Shortlist of 3-5 candidates |
| 5 | Video interviews with top 2 candidates | Final candidate selected |
| 6 | Make offer, share Notion hub + Loom library | VA accepted and onboarding |
| 7 | Begin Week 1: daily oversight, first real tasks | VA producing output |
