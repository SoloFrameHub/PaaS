## LESSON 1: Why Advocacy Beats Advertising for Solo Founders (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Trust Hierarchy** — People trust: (1) recommendations from people they know, (2) online reviews and testimonials, (3) editorial content, (4) branded advertising. Solo founders should invest in the top of the hierarchy, not the bottom.
2. **Advocacy vs Advertising ROI** — A single case study or referral can generate more pipeline than $5,000 in ads, with zero marginal cost. For bootstrapped founders, advocacy is the highest-ROI acquisition channel.
3. **The Advocacy Flywheel** — Great product → Customer success → Testimonial/case study → New customer acquired via social proof → Great product (repeat). Each revolution compounds.
4. **Why Most Founders Fail at Advocacy** — They don't ask. 83% of satisfied customers are willing to refer, but only 29% actually do — because nobody asked them.
5. **The Solo Founder Advocacy Advantage** — You have a personal relationship with every customer. This makes asking for testimonials and referrals feel natural, not corporate.
6. **Advocacy as Part of the Customer Journey** — Advocacy is not an afterthought bolted onto the end. It's a designed stage: Onboard → Retain → Expand → Advocate.

## LESSON 2: Testimonial Collection System (2-3 Question Form) (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Simple Ask** — Most founders over-complicate testimonials. The best system: send a 2-3 question form at the right moment. That's it.
2. **The 3-Question Testimonial Form** — (1) "What was your biggest challenge before working with us?" (2) "What specific result have you achieved?" (3) "What would you say to someone considering [product/service]?"
3. **Why 3 Questions, Not 10** — Completion rate drops 50% for every additional question beyond 3. A 3-question form gets 40-60% completion; a 10-question form gets 5-10%.
4. **The Written → Permission → Publish Flow** — Customer fills out form → You edit into a polished testimonial → You send it back for approval → They approve → You publish. The approval step prevents surprises.
5. **Editing Testimonials Ethically** — Fixing grammar and tightening language is fine. Changing meaning, adding claims they didn't make, or inflating results is not. When in doubt, ask.
6. **The Testimonial Request Email** — Short, personal, specific: "Hey [name], I'd love to share your story with others who are in the same situation you were in. Would you mind answering 3 quick questions? It takes about 2 minutes."

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| 3-question forms have 40-60% completion rate vs 5-10% for 10+ question forms | SurveyMonkey / Typeform benchmark data |
| 72% of customers will leave a review or testimonial if simply asked | BrightLocal consumer survey |
| Testimonials with specific numbers ("increased revenue by 40%") are 2x more persuasive than generic praise ("great product!") | CXL / conversion optimization research |
| The best time to ask for a testimonial: within 48 hours of a measurable success milestone | CS best practices / timing research |
| Edited testimonials (with customer approval) perform 30% better than raw, unedited responses | Copywriting research |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The 3-Question Testimonial Form**:
  1. "What was your biggest challenge before working with us / using [product]?"
  2. "What specific result or outcome have you achieved since starting?"
  3. "What would you say to someone who's considering [product/service] right now?"

- **The Testimonial Collection Workflow**:
  | Step | Action | Timing | Tool |
  |------|--------|--------|------|
  | 1 | Trigger: customer achieves success milestone | Day 30-60 | Zapier/Manual |
  | 2 | Send testimonial request email | Within 48 hours of trigger | ESP / personal email |
  | 3 | Customer completes 3-question form | 2-5 days to respond | Tally / Typeform / Google Form |
  | 4 | Edit response into polished testimonial | Same day | Google Docs |
  | 5 | Send edited version for approval | Same day as edit | Email |
  | 6 | Customer approves (or suggests edits) | 1-3 days | Email |
  | 7 | Publish to website, social proof library, sales materials | Same day as approval | Website CMS |

- **Testimonial Request Email Template**:
  - Subject: "Quick favor? (2 min)"
  - Body: "Hey [name], Congratulations on [specific milestone/result]! I'd love to share your story with others who are facing the same challenge you were. Would you mind answering 3 quick questions? It takes about 2 minutes: [link to form]. No pressure at all — and I'll send you the edited version for your approval before publishing anything. Thanks for being an amazing [customer/client]!"

## LESSON 3: Mini Case Studies: Challenge → Solution → Results (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Mini Case Study Format** — Not a 2,000-word PDF. A 200-400 word story with 3 sections: Challenge (what they struggled with), Solution (what you did), Results (measurable outcomes). Takes 1-2 hours to write.
2. **Challenge Section** — Describe the customer's situation before your product/service. Use their words from the testimonial form question #1. Make it relatable to your ICP.
3. **Solution Section** — Describe what you did (or what they did with your product). Focus on the approach, not the features. "We implemented a 90-day onboarding system" not "They used Feature X."
4. **Results Section** — Quantifiable outcomes: revenue increase, time saved, churn reduced, users acquired. Specific numbers are 3x more persuasive than vague claims.
5. **The Customer Approval Process** — Same as testimonials: write the draft, send for approval, publish after sign-off. Some customers prefer anonymized ("a B2B SaaS founder") — respect that.
6. **Deploying Case Studies** — On your website, in sales emails, in proposals, on LinkedIn posts, in webinar presentations. One case study has 5-10 deployment surfaces.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Case studies are the #2 most influential content for B2B buyers (behind whitepapers) | DemandGen Report |
| Case studies with specific metrics convert 3x better than those without | CXL conversion research |
| Mini case studies (200-400 words) are read 5x more than full case studies (1,500+ words) | Content engagement benchmarks |
| 65% of B2B buyers want to see case studies from similar companies before purchasing | Demand Gen / TrustRadius |
| A single case study can be repurposed into 5-10 content pieces (social posts, email, proposal insert, etc.) | Content repurposing research |
| Customers featured in case studies have 28% higher retention (they feel invested in your success) | CS engagement data |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The Mini Case Study Template (200-400 words)**:

  **[Customer Name or Anonymized Description]**

  **Challenge:**
  [2-3 sentences describing their situation before your product/service. Use their words where possible. Make it relatable to your ICP.]

  **Solution:**
  [2-3 sentences describing what you did or what they did with your product. Focus on approach and methodology, not feature names.]

  **Results:**
  [2-3 sentences with specific metrics. e.g., "Within 90 days, [customer] reduced churn from 8% to 3% monthly, saving $4,500/month in lost revenue."]

  **Quote:**
  [One sentence direct quote from the customer, pulled from testimonial form question #3.]

- **Case Study Deployment Map**:
  | Surface | Format | When to Use |
  |---------|--------|------------|
  | Website /case-studies page | Full mini case study | Always live |
  | Sales email sequence | 2-sentence summary + link | During nurture sequence |
  | Proposal insert | Full mini case study | In every proposal |
  | LinkedIn post | Challenge + Result as story format | 1-2x per month |
  | Webinar / presentation | Slide version with key metrics | During pitch section |
  | Landing page | Quote + Result as social proof block | On key conversion pages |

### Artifact Component

**Case-Study Outlines** — 3 blank mini case study templates pre-filled with the student's product/service context, plus a deployment checklist for each surface.

### Interactive Element

## LESSON 4: Video Testimonials on a Budget (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **Why Video Testimonials Convert** — Video testimonials are 2x more persuasive than written because viewers see real emotion, body language, and authenticity. They're harder to fake.
2. **The Budget Video Stack** — Loom (free, customer records themselves), VideoAsk ($30/mo, interactive video collection), Zoom recording (free, you interview them), iPhone/smartphone (free, self-recorded)
3. **The 3-Question Video Interview** — Same 3 questions as written, but on a 5-10 minute Zoom call. Record with permission. You ask the questions; they answer naturally.
4. **Remote Collection (No Production Team)** — Customer records themselves using Loom or their phone. Send clear instructions: "Find a quiet spot, good lighting, face a window. Record yourself answering these 3 questions. Don't overthink it — authentic > polished."
5. **Editing on a Budget** — Descript ($24/mo) for text-based video editing, or CapCut (free). Cut out ums and pauses, add subtitles, add your logo. 30-60 second highlight reel is ideal.
6. **Permission and Usage Rights** — Always get written permission (even a simple email confirmation: "Do I have your permission to use this video on our website and marketing materials?"). Keep it on file.

|-----------|--------|

- **Video Testimonial Instructions for Customers (Loom/Self-Record)**:

## LESSON 5: Referral Loop Design ("Know 1-2 People?") (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Simple Referral Ask** — "Do you know 1-2 people who might benefit from what we've built?" This single question, asked at the right time, is the most cost-effective acquisition channel available.
2. **When to Ask** — The ask window: after a measurable success milestone, after a positive NPS response (9-10), after a successful expansion/upsell, during a quarterly review. NEVER ask an unhappy customer for a referral.
3. **The Referral Incentive Debate** — Simple approach for solo founders: no incentive needed for the first 5-10 referrals (ask is enough). After that, consider: mutual discount (10-15% for both), gift card ($25-50), or feature credit.
4. **Formal Referral Programs** — Rewardful, FirstPromoter, ReferralCandy: platforms that automate tracking and payouts. Worth it when you have 50+ customers and consistent referral flow.
5. **The Personal Intro Request** — More effective than a referral link: "Would you be willing to make a quick intro via email? I'll write a draft you can send." Intro conversion rate: 30-50% vs referral link: 5-10%.
6. **Referral Tracking** — Even without a formal program, track referrals in your CRM: source = referral, referred by = [customer name]. Measure: referrals requested, intros made, meetings booked, customers acquired.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| 92% trust recommendations from people they know | Nielsen |
| Referred customers have 16% higher LTV | Wharton School |
| Referred customers have 37% higher retention rate | Deloitte |
| Personal intro referrals convert at 30-50% vs 5-10% for referral links | Referral marketing benchmarks |
| Companies with referral programs grow 86% faster | Referral Saasquatch |
| 83% of satisfied customers are willing to refer but only 29% do (because they're not asked) | Texas Tech University |
| Double-sided incentives (reward for both referrer and referred) perform 25% better than single-sided | ReferralCandy data |
| The optimal ask: "Do you know 1-2 people?" converts 40% better than "Tell all your friends" | Referral psychology research |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The Referral Ask Framework**:
  | Context | Ask Version | Expected Conversion |
  |---------|------------|-------------------|
  | After NPS 9-10 | "I'm glad you're getting great results. Do you know 1-2 people who might benefit similarly?" | 25-35% make an intro |
  | After expansion/upsell | "Since you've found so much value that you upgraded, do you know a colleague who'd benefit too?" | 20-30% |
  | During quarterly review | "As we look at your results, I'd love to help more people like you. Anyone come to mind?" | 15-25% |
  | In testimonial follow-up | "Thanks for the amazing testimonial! While we're at it — know anyone who's in the situation you were in before?" | 30-40% |

- **The Personal Intro Email Draft**:
  Provide the referrer with a pre-written email they can forward:
  - Subject: "Quick intro — [Your Name] from [Product]"
  - Body: "Hey [referred name], I've been using [product] for [X months] and it's helped me [specific result]. I thought you might find it useful for [their situation]. I'm connecting you with [founder name] — they can answer any questions. No pressure, just thought it might be a good fit."

- **Referral Program Tiers (By Stage)**:
  | Stage | Approach | Tool | Cost |
  |-------|----------|------|------|
  | 0-50 customers | Manual ask + CRM tracking | Google Sheets / CRM | Free |
  | 50-200 customers | Simple incentive (mutual 10% discount or $25 gift card) | CRM + manual fulfillment | $25-50 per referral |
  | 200+ customers | Formal program with automated tracking + payouts | Rewardful / FirstPromoter | $49-59/mo + incentive costs |

## LESSON 6: Timing Advocacy Asks to Success Milestones (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Advocacy Window** — There's a 48-72 hour window after a customer achieves a measurable success when they're most willing to advocate. After that, the emotional high fades and response rates drop 50%.
2. **Mapping Milestones to Advocacy Types** — Not every milestone warrants every ask. Small wins → testimonial. Big wins → case study. Expansion/renewal → referral.
3. **The Advocacy Calendar** — A timeline of advocacy opportunities mapped to the customer journey: Day 30 (first win → testimonial), Day 60 (habit formed → review), Day 90 (results achieved → case study + referral), Renewal (referral).
4. **Automated Trigger → Manual Ask** — Automate the trigger detection (customer hit milestone); keep the ask personal (human-written email, not automated template). The "personal ask from the founder" converts 3x better.
5. **The "Stack" Strategy** — Don't ask for everything at once. Space advocacy asks across the journey. One ask per milestone, maximum one ask per month.
6. **Handling "Not Now"** — Some customers aren't ready. Respect it. Flag in CRM for re-ask in 60-90 days after the next milestone.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Advocacy requests within 48 hours of a success milestone have 60-70% response rates vs 15-25% for random timing | CS timing research |
| Personal founder emails for advocacy get 3x the response of automated templates | Email engagement benchmarks |
| Customers who provide one form of advocacy (testimonial) are 2x more likely to provide another (referral) later | Advocacy funnel data |
| Over-asking (more than 1 advocacy request per month) reduces response rates by 40% | Customer experience surveys |
| The optimal advocacy journey: testimonial first (lowest friction), then review, then case study, then referral | Advocacy program design data |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The Advocacy Calendar**:
  | Customer Journey Stage | Milestone | Advocacy Ask | Friction Level |
  |-----------------------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
  | Day 30 | First value achieved | Written testimonial (3-question form) | Low |
  | Day 60 | Habit formed, regular usage | Online review (G2, Capterra, or Google) | Low |
  | Day 90 | Measurable results achieved | Mini case study participation | Medium |
  | Day 90+ | Strong results + relationship | Video testimonial | Medium |
  | Renewal / Expansion | Renewed or upgraded | Referral ask | Low |
  | Ongoing (quarterly) | Continued success | Referral re-ask + new success story | Low |

- **Milestone-to-Ask Automation**:
  | Trigger | Automation Action | Human Action |
  |---------|------------------|-------------|
  | Customer achieves first value (Day 30) | Zapier: flag in CRM + Slack alert | Send personal testimonial request email |
  | NPS 9-10 submitted | Zapier: flag as promoter + Slack alert | Send personal referral ask within 48 hours |
  | Customer renews/expands | Stripe webhook → Zapier → CRM flag | Send renewal thank-you + referral ask |
  | Case study candidate identified | Manual flag in CRM | Schedule 15-min case study interview |

### Artifact Component

**Advocacy Trigger Map** — Complete timeline mapping customer milestones to advocacy asks, with automation triggers and personal outreach templates for each.

### Interactive Element

**Guided Build:** AI maps the student's customer journey milestones and generates a personalized advocacy calendar with trigger logic and outreach templates for each stage.

## LESSON 7: Building a Social Proof Library (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Social Proof Library Concept** — A centralized, organized collection of all testimonials, case studies, reviews, screenshots, metrics, and video clips. Your "proof arsenal" that you draw from for every marketing and sales activity.
2. **Library Organization** — Organize by: (1) Type (testimonial, case study, review, video, metric), (2) Industry/niche, (3) Use case/problem solved, (4) Strength/metric type ("grew revenue," "saved time," "reduced churn")
3. **Deployment Surfaces** — Website homepage, landing pages, sales emails, proposals, LinkedIn posts, webinar decks, email signatures, ad creatives, onboarding materials
4. **The "Social Proof Insertion Rule"** — Every piece of customer-facing content should include at least one proof element: a quote, a metric, a logo, a case study reference, or a screenshot.
5. **Maintaining the Library** — Add new proof quarterly. Remove outdated proof (older than 18 months unless the customer is still active). Update metrics as customers achieve new milestones.
6. **Proof for Different Buying Stages** — Awareness: logos and headline metrics. Consideration: case studies and video testimonials. Decision: detailed results and referral conversations.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Social proof on landing pages increases conversion by 15-20% | CXL / ConversionXL |
| Pages with 3+ testimonials convert 34% better than pages with 0-1 | A/B testing benchmarks |
| 88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations | BrightLocal survey |
| Logo bars ("trusted by") increase credibility perception by 20-30% | Conversion research |
| Sales proposals with embedded case studies close 15-25% more deals | Proposal analytics / PandaDoc |
| Proof elements in email sequences increase click-through by 10-15% | Email marketing benchmarks |

### Frameworks & Models

- **Social Proof Library Structure**:
  | Category | Contents | Format | Deployment |
  |----------|----------|--------|-----------|
  | Written testimonials | 10-20 customer quotes | Text (2-3 sentences each) | Website, emails, proposals |
  | Mini case studies | 3-5 Challenge→Solution→Results stories | 200-400 words each | Website, proposals, LinkedIn |
  | Video testimonials | 3-5 video clips | 30-90 second clips | Website, social media, webinars |
  | Online reviews | Screenshots from G2, Capterra, etc. | Image / link | Website, ads |
  | Metrics / data points | Key customer results | Numbers + context | Everywhere |
  | Customer logos | Permission-granted company logos | Image files | Homepage, proposals |

- **Social Proof Deployment Checklist**:
  | Surface | Proof Type | Minimum |
  |---------|-----------|---------|
  | Homepage | Logo bar + 2-3 quotes + 1 metric | Required |
  | Landing page | 2-3 testimonials + 1 case study summary | Required |
  | Sales email sequence | 1 proof element per email | Recommended |
  | Proposal | 1-2 relevant case studies | Required |
  | LinkedIn profile | 1 featured testimonial | Recommended |

- **Library Maintenance Cadence**:
  | Frequency | Action |

## LESSON 8: Your Advocacy Playbook (45 min)

### Key Topics

1. **Assembling the Complete Advocacy System** — Testimonial collection + case studies + video testimonials + referral loop + advocacy timing + social proof library = one cohesive system
2. **The One-Page Advocacy Summary** — Advocacy types, triggers, ask scripts, tracking metrics, and deployment surfaces on a single reference page
3. **Measuring Advocacy Success** — 4 metrics: testimonials collected per quarter, referrals requested vs received, referral-sourced customers, social proof deployment coverage (% of surfaces with proof)
4. **The 7-Day Implementation Sprint** — Build and launch the advocacy system
5. **Connecting Back to Acquisition** — Advocacy feeds directly back into the acquisition engine (Tracks 1-4): social proof on landing pages, case studies in outreach emails, referrals as a channel
6. **The Compounding Effect** — Each customer who advocates makes acquiring the next customer easier. Over 12-24 months, advocacy can become your #1 acquisition channel, reducing dependence on paid/outbound.

### Frameworks & Models

- **The 7-Day Implementation Sprint**:
  | Day | Activity | Output |
  |-----|----------|--------|
  | 1 | Audit existing social proof (testimonials, reviews, case studies) | Inventory complete |
