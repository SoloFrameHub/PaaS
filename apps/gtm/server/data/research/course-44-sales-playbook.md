## LESSON 1: The 5 Core Questions Every Playbook Answers (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **Question 1: WHO am I selling to?** — Your ICP from Course 1. Specific name, title, company size, industry, pain point. If you can't name 5 real people who fit your ICP, it's not specific enough.
2. **Question 2: HOW am I reaching them?** — Your acquisition channels from Course 3. Primary channel (where 70% of effort goes) and secondary channel (30%). No more than 2 channels until primary is working.
3. **Question 3: WHAT message am I using?** — Your positioning from Course 2, cold email framework from Course 8, LinkedIn strategy from Course 7. Message must be specific to ICP and channel.
4. **Question 4: HOW am I measuring success?** — Your analytics from Course 41. Leading metrics (weekly): outreach volume, reply rate, meetings booked. Lagging metrics (monthly): deals won, revenue, CAC.
5. **Question 5: WHAT commitment am I making?** — Your 90-day commitment contract. Specific daily/weekly activities. Minimum viable effort below which you can't expect results. The difference between "trying sales" and "doing sales."
6. **The Playbook-First Principle** — "If it doesn't fit on one page, you lack clarity." Your playbook is a forcing function for simplicity. It should be taped to your wall, not filed in a drawer.

## LESSON 2: Playbook: Starting from Zero (0 Customers) (55 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Zero-to-Ten Mindset** — First 10 customers don't come from systems. They come from hustle, conversations, and manual effort. The playbook for zero is different from the playbook for scaling.
2. **WHO: Start with Your Network** — Existing contacts who match your ICP. Former colleagues. Friends of friends. LinkedIn connections in target industries. The "Dream 25" list: 25 people you'd love as customers.
3. **HOW: Manual Outreach + Communities** — (1) Direct outreach to Dream 25 (personal emails, not templates). (2) Community participation (Reddit, Slack, Discord from Course 9). (3) LinkedIn content and engagement (Course 7). No automation. No sequences. All manual.
4. **WHAT: The Conversation Starter** — Not a pitch. "I'm building [X] to solve [Y]. I've been talking to people like you to understand the problem better. Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation?" Focus on learning, not selling.
5. **MEASURED: Conversations, Not Revenue** — Leading metric: conversations per week (target: 5-10). Lagging metric: people who say "I'd pay for that." Revenue is too lagging at zero. Measure learning velocity.
6. **COMMITMENT: 90 Days of Daily Outreach** — Send 3-5 personal messages every day. No days off except weekends. 90 days = 180-300 conversations initiated. At a 20-30% response rate = 40-90 conversations. At 10% conversion = 4-9 first customers.

## LESSON 3: Playbook: B2B SaaS Founder (55 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The B2B SaaS Founder Context** — You have a product (at least MVP). You've validated with 5-20 customers. Now you need to build a repeatable acquisition engine. Budget: $100-300/month for tools.
2. **WHO: ICP-Filtered Pipeline** — Use Course 1 ICP with firmographic filters: company size, industry, role, tech stack. Build target account lists using Apollo/Sales Navigator (Course 23). 100-200 new prospects/month.
3. **HOW: Outbound Email + LinkedIn** — Primary: Cold email sequences (Course 8 + Course 24). 100-200 emails/week. Secondary: LinkedIn content + DM (Course 7 + Course 25). 3 posts/week + 10-15 DMs/week.
4. **WHAT: Problem-First Messaging** — PAS framework (Course 8). Lead with the pain your product solves. Include 1 proof point (customer result, metric, testimonial). CTA: 15-minute call, not a demo.
5. **MEASURED: Pipeline Math** — 200 emails/week × 5-10% reply rate = 10-20 replies. 10-20 replies × 50% meeting rate = 5-10 meetings. 5-10 meetings × 25% close rate = 1-3 deals/month. Track all conversion rates weekly.
6. **COMMITMENT: The B2B Weekly Rhythm** — Monday: prospect research + list building (90 min). Tuesday: email sequence creation + launch (60 min). Wednesday: follow-ups + sequence monitoring (45 min). Thursday: discovery calls + LinkedIn engagement (90 min). Friday: metrics review + CRM cleanup (45 min). Total: ~5.5 hours/week.

## LESSON 4: Playbook: Coach/Consultant (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Coach/Consultant Context** — You sell expertise, not software. High-touch, relationship-driven sales. Typical deal: $2,000-10,000+ (coaching packages, retainers). Longer consideration cycle but higher close rates with warm leads.
2. **WHO: Transformation-Seeking Buyers** — People stuck at a specific plateau (revenue ceiling, career stall, health plateau). They've tried DIY and failed. They have budget (not hobbyists). Pain is urgent, not academic.
3. **HOW: Content + Community + Referrals** — Primary: LinkedIn content demonstrating expertise (Course 7) + lead magnet → email nurture → enrollment call. Secondary: Referrals from existing clients. Tertiary: Community participation (Course 9).
4. **WHAT: Transformation Messaging** — Lead with the outcome they want (not the process). "Go from [current state] to [desired state] in [timeframe]." Use case studies as proof. CTA: application form, not "book a call."
5. **MEASURED: Applications and Enrollment** — Leading: content engagement, lead magnet downloads, applications submitted. Lagging: enrollment calls held, clients signed, revenue. Target: 5-10 applications/month → 3-5 calls → 1-2 new clients.
6. **COMMITMENT: Content + Call Cadence** — Monday: create 1 long-form LinkedIn post (60 min). Tuesday-Thursday: engage in communities + respond to DMs (30 min/day). Friday: enrollment calls (60-90 min each, 2-3 per week). Weekly total: 5-6 hours.

## LESSON 5: Playbook: Creator with Audience (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Creator Context** — You have an existing audience (500-50,000+). You produce content regularly. Revenue comes from converting followers to buyers. Challenge: most of your audience will never buy — the playbook identifies and converts the 1-5% who will.
2. **WHO: The Buyer Within Your Audience** — Not all followers are buyers. Buyer signals: replies to emails, DMs you, asks specific questions, consumes premium content, has the problem your paid offer solves AND the budget to pay.
3. **HOW: Content Funnel + DM Outreach** — Primary: Regular content (posts, newsletters, videos) → lead magnet → email nurture → offer. Secondary: DM conversations triggered by engagement signals (replies, comments, questions). Tertiary: Webinars/challenges (Course 30).
4. **WHAT: Bridge Content** — Content that bridges from "free follower" to "paid customer." Not pure value content (that keeps them as free consumers) and not pure pitch (that alienates). Bridge content: "Here's the framework. My [product/program] helps you implement it."
5. **MEASURED: Subscriber-to-Buyer Rate** — Leading: email list growth, content engagement, DM conversations. Lagging: sales from audience (vs external), subscriber-to-buyer conversion rate (target: 1-5%), revenue per subscriber.
6. **COMMITMENT: The Creator Weekly Rhythm** — Monday: long-form content creation (90 min). Tuesday-Wednesday: repurpose to social + engage (60 min). Thursday: email newsletter with bridge content (60 min). Friday: DM outreach to engaged followers (45 min). Total: ~5 hours/week.

## LESSON 7: The One-Page Personal Acquisition System (55 min)

### Key Topics

1. **"If It Doesn't Fit on One Page, You Lack Clarity"** — The one-page system forces you to eliminate noise and focus on what matters. It's not a simplification — it's a distillation. Every word must earn its place.
2. **The One-Page Layout** — Top: WHO (ICP in 1 sentence). Left: HOW (primary + secondary channel with tools). Center: WHAT (core message in 2 sentences). Right: MEASURED (3 leading + 2 lagging metrics). Bottom: COMMITMENT (daily/weekly activities with specific numbers).
3. **Filling In the Page** — Use artifacts from Courses 1-3 (ICP, positioning, channel), Course 41 (metrics), and Lessons 1-6 of this course (situation-specific playbook). The one-page is the executive summary of everything.
4. **The Lamination Test** — Would you laminate this page and put it on your wall? If any section makes you wince, it's not right yet. Revise until every section feels true and specific.
5. **Versioning Your System** — v1.0: initial (today). v1.1: after 30 days (data-informed adjustments). v2.0: after 90 days (major pivot or scale shift). Track changes in a version log with rationale.
6. **Sharing Your System** — Share with: accountability partner, mentor, community peers. The act of sharing creates commitment. Feedback from others catches blind spots.

## LESSON 8: The 90-Day Commitment Contract (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **Why 90 Days** — Less than 60 days: not enough data to evaluate a channel or strategy. More than 120 days: too long without checkpoints. 90 days = 3 monthly review cycles, enough data to know if something works.
2. **The Commitment Contract Structure** — (1) What I will do (specific daily/weekly activities). (2) What I will NOT do (distractions I'm cutting). (3) How I will measure (specific metrics and targets). (4) My review dates (Day 30, Day 60, Day 90). (5) My accountability mechanism (who I'm reporting to).
3. **Activity Commitments** — Be ruthlessly specific. Not "do more outreach." Instead: "Send 15 personalized cold emails every Monday and Thursday. Post 3 LinkedIn posts per week. Hold 2 discovery calls per week minimum."
4. **The "Will NOT Do" List** — Equally important: what you're committing to STOP doing for 90 days. Examples: stop building new features, stop redesigning the website, stop exploring new tools, stop attending networking events that don't convert.
5. **Review Checkpoints** — Day 30: "Am I doing the activities?" (input check). Day 60: "Are the activities producing results?" (output check). Day 90: "Is this working well enough to continue, or do I need to pivot?" (strategy check).
6. **The Signing Ritual** — Print the contract. Sign it. Share with accountability partner. Post it where you see it daily. This sounds trivial but behavioral psychology shows that physical signatures increase commitment by 50%+.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| It takes 60-90 days to determine if a sales channel works | Marketing channel maturity data | Don't pivot before 60 days |
| Written commitments increase follow-through by 42% | Dominican University | Physical signing adds another boost |
| Accountability partners increase goal achievement by 65% | ASTD / accountability research | Share your contract with someone |
| 30-day checkpoints prevent 60% of "I gave up at Week 3" failures | Behavioral science | Short feedback loops maintain motivation |
| Founders who cut distractions see 20-40% more selling time | Productivity research | The "will NOT do" list is as important as the "will do" |

## LESSON 9: When to Pivot vs Persist (60-90 Day Rule) (50 min)

### Key Topics

1. **The Premature Pivot Trap** — Most founders pivot too early. They try outbound for 3 weeks, get low response, and switch to content. They try content for 4 weeks, don't see traffic, and switch to paid ads. After 6 months, they've tried everything and mastered nothing.
2. **The 60-Day Minimum** — Give any strategy a minimum of 60 days of consistent execution before evaluating. "Consistent" means hitting the activity commitments from your contract, not "I tried it when I felt like it."
3. **Persist Signals** — (1) Activity metrics are being hit (you're doing the work). (2) Leading indicators show improvement (reply rates increasing, meetings growing). (3) You've received at least some positive signals (replies, interest, meetings booked). (4) The effort-to-result ratio is improving week over week.
4. **Pivot Signals** — (1) 60+ days of consistent activity. (2) Zero or near-zero positive signals. (3) Leading indicators are flat or declining despite adjustments. (4) Market feedback suggests fundamental ICP or channel mismatch. (5) You dread the activity and can't sustain it.
5. **Pivot Options** — (a) Pivot the message (keep ICP and channel, change the angle). (b) Pivot the channel (keep ICP and message, switch delivery method). (c) Pivot the ICP (keep message framework, target different market). Never change more than one variable at a time.
6. **The Decision Log** — At Day 60 and Day 90: document what happened, what you learned, and what you're changing (or not). This log becomes invaluable for future strategy decisions.

### Data Points & Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 70% of founders who pivot do so prematurely (before 60 days) | Startup coaching data | Persistence is the default, not pivoting |
| Channels typically take 60-90 days to show meaningful results | Marketing maturity data | Patience required |
| Founders who change only one variable at a time are 3x more likely to find what works | A/B testing principles | Isolate the variable |
| Cold email typically produces results in 2-4 weeks | Outreach data | Faster feedback loop than content |
| Content/SEO typically produces results in 3-6 months | Content marketing data | Requires longer commitment |
| Founders with decision logs make 40% better strategic choices over 12 months | Decision journal research | Write down your reasoning |

### Frameworks & Models

- **The Pivot/Persist Decision Framework:**

| Question | Persist If... | Pivot If... |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Have I done the work? | Yes, consistent activity for 60+ days | If no, persist — you haven't really tried |
| Are leading indicators improving? | Even slowly, yes → persist | Flat or declining after 60+ days → pivot |
| Am I getting any positive signals? | Some replies, some meetings, some interest | Zero positive signals after 60 days |
| Is market feedback directional? | Feedback suggests refinement needed → persist + adjust | Feedback suggests fundamental mismatch → pivot |
| Can I sustain this for 30 more days? | Yes, energy and motivation allow → persist | Burnout or dread → pivot channel or approach |

- **The One-Variable Pivot Rule:**
  - Pivot A: Change MESSAGE only (new angle, new CTA, new proof point)
  - Pivot B: Change CHANNEL only (email→LinkedIn, content→community)
  - Pivot C: Change ICP only (different segment, different role, different industry)
  - Never change 2+ variables simultaneously. You'll learn nothing.
