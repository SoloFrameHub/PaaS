## Week 4 Review: Document Results and Reflect

You ran a 30-day sprint. That's not nothing. Most people who set that goal didn't do it.

Now you close it out properly. Week 4 has two jobs: **keep executing** (don't coast in the last week) and **start documenting** (your sprint evidence is your certification portfolio).

This review session is longer than the others — it covers your complete sprint summary, ROI analysis, lessons learned, and your transition plan to a sustainable weekly rhythm.

<InsightCard icon="🏆" title="The Week 4 Mindset">
Week 4 is not a wind-down. Deals in your pipeline need active closing work. Your outreach should continue at the same volume as Week 3 — the leads you generate in Week 4 will close in the month after the sprint, building the pipeline that sustains you. The sprint ends, but the system continues.
</InsightCard>

## Part 5: Transition to Sustainable Rhythm

The sprint was a burst. You cannot maintain 30-day sprint intensity indefinitely. Now you define the steady-state system.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="What Stays (Permanent Habits)">

From your sprint, some activities should become permanent weekly habits:

- **Outreach volume:** What weekly volume can you maintain sustainably at your current quality level?
- **Pipeline review:** When weekly — what day, how long, what questions do you answer?
- **CRM hygiene:** 15-minute weekly sweep stays. Daily data entry does not.
- **Referral asks:** At defined customer milestones — this becomes part of the onboarding sequence.

</Slide>
<Slide title="What Changes (Post-Sprint Normal)">

Some sprint activities scale back:

- **Daily sprint tracking:** Weekly tracking is sufficient post-sprint
- **Weekly review sessions:** You won't have Lessons 7-10 anymore — schedule a 30-minute solo weekly review instead
- **A/B testing intensity:** Once you've found your winning variants, you test quarterly, not weekly

</Slide>
<Slide title="The 5-7 Hour Weekly Rhythm">

From Course 21, the sustainable acquisition rhythm is 5-7 hours/week:

- Monday (90 min): Prospect review + sequence loading + outreach batch 1
- Tuesday (60 min): Outreach batch 2 + reply inbox management
- Wednesday (60 min): Discovery calls (if scheduled) + proposal writing (if needed)
- Thursday (60 min): Client check-ins + pipeline follow-ups
- Friday (30 min): Weekly pipeline review — update CRM, capture numbers, set next week's priorities

**Total: 5 hours/week.** The remaining 2 hours are buffer for unexpected deal activity.

</Slide>
