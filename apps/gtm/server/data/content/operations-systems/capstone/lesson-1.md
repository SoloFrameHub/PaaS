## The Three Connected Systems

Your complete acquisition machine has three systems that feed each other in a loop:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="System 1: Acquisition">

**What it does:** Finds the right people and starts conversations.

**Components you've built:**
- ICP Document (Course 1) — who you're targeting
- Positioning Statement (Course 2) — why they should care
- Channel Strategy (Course 3) — where you find them
- Prospecting SOP (Course 4) — how you build lists
- AI Research Agent (Course 23/27) — automation layer
- Outreach sequences (Courses 8, 24, 25) — what you send
- CRM pipeline (Courses 20, 40) — where it all lives

**The output:** Qualified prospects in active conversations.

</Slide>
<Slide title="System 2: Conversion">

**What it does:** Turns conversations into signed contracts.

**Components you've built:**
- Discovery framework (Course 14) — BANT/MEDDIC questions
- DISC adaptation (Course 13) — tailored communication by personality
- Demo script (Course 16) — how you show value
- Objection database (Course 17) — tested responses to every pushback
- Proposal template (Course 18) — how you package the offer
- Closing system (Course 19) — from verbal yes to signed contract
- Contract templates (Course 46) — legal protection
- Invoicing SOP (Course 47) — automated billing

**The output:** Signed contracts and received payments.

</Slide>
<Slide title="System 3: Retention">

**What it does:** Turns customers into repeat buyers and referral sources.

**Components you've built:**
- Onboarding playbook (Course 36) — first 30-90 days
- Health scoring (Course 37) — predict and prevent churn
- Expansion triggers (Course 38) — identify upsell moments
- Advocacy system (Course 39) — referrals and testimonials
- AI CS/Retention Agent (Course 27) — monitors and alerts

**The output:** Customers who stay, grow, and bring new customers.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## What the Capstone Looks Like

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Phase 1: System Assembly (Lessons 2-5)">

Four workshops where you review, update, and integrate every artifact from Tracks 1-7. You are not re-learning the material — you are connecting the pieces and updating with real data from your actual experience.

- **Lesson 2:** ICP + Positioning + Channel
- **Lesson 3:** Outreach + Sequences + CRM
- **Lesson 4:** Discovery + Proposals + Closing
- **Lesson 5:** Retention + Expansion + Advocacy

Output: Your assembled acquisition system, ready to sprint.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Phase 2: Sprint Planning (Lesson 6)">

You design a 30-day execution sprint. Not "try harder" — a structured plan with specific daily targets, weekly milestones, ICE-scored priorities, and a tracking system.

The sprint is where theory becomes evidence.

Output: Your 30-day sprint plan.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Phase 3: Execution (Lessons 7-10)">

Four weekly review sessions during your sprint. Each week you execute, then review results with guided reflection:

- Week 1: Launch and calibrate
- Week 2: Optimize and iterate
- Week 3: Scale what works
- Week 4: Document results

Output: Execution evidence, sprint scorecard, lessons learned.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Phase 4: Certification (Lessons 11-12)">

- **Lesson 11:** Document the complete system (architecture, SOPs, agent specs, tool inventory, 90-day roadmap)
- **Lesson 12:** Present your system for AI review and earn the Customer Acquisition Architect certification

Output: Your certification badge + 90-day roadmap.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Certification: What You're Working Toward

The Customer Acquisition Architect certification is not handed out for showing up. You earn it by demonstrating a working system with execution evidence.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Rubric Overview">

| Criteria | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Artifact completeness | 25% |
| System integration | 25% |
| Sprint execution | 30% |
| Documentation quality | 10% |
| Roadmap viability | 10% |

**Pass threshold: 80%**

The highest-weighted criterion is sprint execution — because evidence of doing beats evidence of planning.

</Slide>
<Slide title="What Counts as Evidence">

The AI reviewer will ask you to demonstrate:

- How a lead moves from first touch to closed deal (tools at each step)
- How your CRM tracks the journey end-to-end
- What your sprint numbers were (emails sent, reply rates, conversations held)
- What you learned and what changed because of it
- What you plan to do in the next 90 days

Screenshots, metrics dashboards, conversation summaries, and filled TemplateBuilders from this course all count as evidence.

</Slide>
<Slide title="The Badge">

When you pass, your certification shows specific stats:

*"Customer Acquisition Architect — executed 30-day sprint: [X] outreach, [Y] conversations, [Z] closed deals"*

This is shareable on LinkedIn. It demonstrates real execution, not just course completion. It is meaningfully different from a completion certificate.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>
