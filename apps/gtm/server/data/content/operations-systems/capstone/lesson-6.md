## From Assembly to Execution

You've spent four lessons reviewing, updating, and connecting your system. You know exactly what's working, what's broken, and where the highest-leverage improvements are.

Now the real work begins.

The 30-day sprint is not about doing everything. It's about executing the 2-3 highest-priority actions with discipline, measuring the results, and adjusting based on data. Thirty days from now, you will have execution evidence — real numbers from real activity — that goes into your certification portfolio.

<InsightCard icon="⏱️" title="Why 30 Days, Not 90">
90-day plans are easy to postpone. 30-day sprints create urgency. When the window is short, you execute today instead of planning for tomorrow. Research shows that structured 30-day sprints produce 3-5x more measurable results than vague quarterly goals. The constraint is the feature.
</InsightCard>

## Step 1: Identify Your Sprint Priorities

Your system assembly revealed gaps, bottlenecks, and opportunities. Before you can set targets, you need to decide what to focus on.

Use the ICE framework to evaluate each possible sprint goal:
- **Impact** (1-10): How much will this move the needle on revenue or pipeline?
- **Confidence** (1-10): How confident are you that this action will produce the expected result?
- **Ease** (1-10): How easy is this to execute given your current tools and capacity?

**ICE Score = Impact × Confidence × Ease**

<ExampleCard label="ICE Scoring in Practice">
Keisha had five possible sprint goals after system assembly:

1. Scale cold email to 200/week: I=9, C=7, E=6 → Score: **378**
2. Fix LinkedIn DM logging in CRM: I=5, C=9, E=8 → Score: **360**
3. Build referral ask email sequence: I=8, C=8, E=7 → Score: **448**
4. Record discovery calls for analysis: I=6, C=8, E=5 → Score: **240**
5. Improve proposal ROI section: I=8, C=7, E=8 → Score: **448**

She picked Goals 3 and 5 as her primary sprint focus. High impact, high confidence, executable immediately.

Note what she did NOT pick: Goal 1 (cold email scaling) got a high impact score but lower ease score because it required new sending infrastructure. She set it as a stretch goal after the primary goals were in motion.
</ExampleCard>

## Step 2: Set SMART Sprint Targets

A sprint goal is not "do more outreach." A sprint goal is measurable, time-bound, and specific enough that on Day 31 you can say definitively whether you hit it.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="SMART Target Framework">

Each sprint goal needs:
- **Specific:** Exact action and quantity
- **Measurable:** A number you can track
- **Achievable:** Based on your actual capacity (5-7 hours/week)
- **Relevant:** Directly impacts revenue or pipeline
- **Time-bound:** 30 days or broken into weekly milestones

**Bad target:** "Send more cold emails"

**Good target:** "Send 120 personalized cold emails per week (targeting VP Sales at 50-200 employee SaaS companies) to achieve a 4%+ reply rate and book 5+ discovery calls by Day 30"

</Slide>
<Slide title="Capacity Reality Check">

Before setting targets, calculate your real capacity:

- Available hours per week for acquisition: ___
- Hours per week already committed to existing clients: ___
- Hours per week for admin, meetings, etc.: ___
- Net hours available for sprint activities: ___

At 5-7 hours/week for acquisition:
- 150 emails/week = ~2 hours (if using AI personalization)
- 3 discovery calls = ~3 hours (including prep + follow-up)
- 1 proposal = ~2 hours
- 2 client check-ins = ~1 hour

Overloading your sprint = failing the sprint. Be conservative in Week 1.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Target Examples by Founder Type">

**B2B SaaS (early stage, 0-5 customers):**
- Goal 1: Send 100 outreach/week, book 4 discovery calls by Day 30
- Goal 2: Close 1 pilot deal at reduced rate to build case study

**Consultant (project-based):**
- Goal 1: Make 30 LinkedIn connection requests/week + follow-up DM sequence, book 3 paid engagements discovery calls
- Goal 2: Send referral ask to all 8 past clients, aim for 2 referral conversations

**Creator (course/coaching):**
- Goal 1: Run 1 webinar with 50 registrations, run 15 enrollment calls
- Goal 2: Close 5 enrollments at $2,000+ each

**B2B (established, 10+ customers):**
- Goal 1: Identify 3 expansion opportunities in existing accounts, have 3 expansion conversations
- Goal 2: Send 80 targeted outreach/week in new vertical, book 3 discovery calls

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="Your Sprint Starts Now">
The most common mistake in sprint planning is spending too long planning and not enough time executing. You have your plan. The rest will be figured out in the doing.

Lessons 7-10 are your weekly review sessions. Come to each one with your actual numbers — what you sent, what replied, what booked, what closed. The review sessions are where the learning compounds.

One more thing: imperfect execution beats perfect planning. Your Week 1 sprint will not go exactly as planned. Something will be misconfigured, a call will run long, an automation will break. That's what Week 2 is for.

**Start.**
</InsightCard>
