## Part 2: The Certification Rubric

Understand exactly how you will be evaluated. The AI reviewer uses this rubric. You should self-assess against it before presenting.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Criterion 1: Artifact Completeness (25 points)">

| Element | Points |
|---------|--------|
| All track artifacts present | 5 |
| Artifacts customized to your specific business | 5 |
| Artifacts updated with real data (not just original course defaults) | 5 |
| Artifacts organized and accessible in portfolio | 5 |
| Quality and depth of content | 5 |

**What earns full marks:** Every artifact is present, customized, and updated with real information from your sales activity. The objection database has real objections from real conversations. The ICP has real customer data, not just your original hypothesis.

**What loses marks:** Artifacts left in template form with placeholder text. Missing artifacts without explanation. Artifacts not updated since the course was originally completed.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Criterion 2: System Integration (25 points)">

| Element | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Acquisition → Conversion flow is documented end-to-end | 5 |
| Conversion → Retention flow is documented | 5 |
| Retention → Advocacy → Acquisition loop is closed | 5 |
| AI agents are configured and connected | 5 |
| CRM is the single source of truth (verified) | 5 |

**What earns full marks:** You can describe exactly how a prospect moves from first outreach through closed deal through onboarding through renewal — naming the tool at every step and the automation that connects them.

**What loses marks:** Gaps in the flow ("and then I manually handle it"), systems that exist in theory but haven't been tested, CRM with stale data or missing stages.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Criterion 3: Sprint Execution (30 points)">

| Element | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Sprint plan was specific and measurable | 5 |
| Evidence of daily/weekly execution | 10 |
| Results documented with real metrics | 10 |
| Lessons learned are captured and specific | 5 |

**What earns full marks:** Sprint tracker with real numbers across 20+ working days. Screenshots of sent emails, CRM pipeline at various points, closed deals. Specific lessons learned ("trigger-event first lines outperformed compliment-based by 75%").

**What loses marks:** Sprint plan without execution evidence, vague lessons learned ("I learned cold email is hard"), missing sprint tracker data.

This criterion is 30% of your score and is the most differentiating. Evidence of execution is what makes the certification meaningful.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Criteria 4 & 5: Documentation + Roadmap (20 points)">

**Documentation Quality (10 points):**

| Element | Points |
|---------|--------|
| System architecture is clear and complete | 5 |
| SOPs are actionable by someone else | 5 |

**Roadmap Viability (10 points):**

| Element | Points |
|---------|--------|
| 90-day goals are specific and achievable | 5 |
| Initiatives are ICE-scored and prioritized | 5 |

**What earns full marks:** Someone unfamiliar with your business could follow your SOPs and produce the expected output. Your 90-day roadmap has specific monthly goals and ICE-scored initiatives — not "grow revenue."

</Slide>

## Part 5: AI Reviewer Likely Questions

The AI reviewer will probe specific areas. Be ready for these:

<ProgressiveReveal title="Anticipated Reviewer Questions" persistKey="capstone-L12-qa">
<RevealSection title="On Your System">

**Q: How does a lead move from first email to signed contract in your system?**

Prepare: walk through every step, naming the tool at each point and the automation or human action that moves the lead forward.

**Q: What happens when a customer goes yellow in your health score?**

Prepare: describe exactly what the signal looks like, how quickly you respond, and what your intervention looks like.

**Q: Show me how your AI research agent works.**

Prepare: be ready to describe the input, the process, the output, and the human gate between agent output and sequence loading.

</RevealSection>
<RevealSection title="On Your Sprint">

**Q: Walk me through a specific deal that closed during the sprint.**

Prepare: tell the story from first email to signed contract — every touchpoint, timeline, and what the prospect said.

**Q: What was your worst week and why?**

Prepare: this tests honesty and self-awareness. A perfect sprint is suspicious. Describe what went wrong and what you changed.

**Q: If you ran this sprint again, what would you do differently on Day 1?**

Prepare: your answer should show genuine learning from the experience.

</RevealSection>
<RevealSection title="On Your Roadmap">

**Q: Your 90-day roadmap targets $15K MRR. How many customers does that require?**

Prepare: know your math. Target MRR ÷ AOV = customers needed. Sprint close rate suggests how many outreach contacts are needed to get there.

**Q: What's the single biggest risk to your 90-day roadmap?**

Prepare: show strategic thinking. The answer is not "I might not work hard enough." The answer is a specific external or internal factor that could prevent the goal.

</RevealSection>
</ProgressiveReveal>
