## Assembly Workshop 3: The Conversion Engine

You have conversations coming in from your outreach system. Now build the machine that converts those conversations into signed contracts.

This is where the academy pays off most visibly — the moment a prospect says "yes" and a contract is signed. But most founders get to discovery calls and then improvise. The conversion engine removes improvisation and replaces it with a tested, documented process.

Have these ready:
- Your discovery framework and question bank (Course 14)
- Your DISC adaptation cheat sheets (Course 13)
- Your objection database (Course 17)
- Your proposal template (Course 18)
- Your closing checklist (Course 19)
- Any real sales call notes or recordings

<InsightCard icon="💰" title="The Conversion Gap">
Solo founders who document their discovery, proposal, and closing processes close 28% more deals than those who wing it. This is not because the document is magic — it's because building the document forces you to think through every step, identify failures, and pre-solve objections.
</InsightCard>

## Part 2: DISC Adaptation Review

Course 13 taught you to adapt your communication style to buyer personality type. Are you actually doing this on discovery calls?

<FlipCard front="D-Style Buyer (Dominant)" back="Direct, fast, results-focused. Skip the rapport-building, get to outcomes immediately. 'Here's what we'll achieve and by when.' They hate vague proposals — give them a one-page executive summary with specific ROI numbers." />

<FlipCard front="I-Style Buyer (Influential)" back="Relationship-first, big picture, visionary. Spend more time on vision and story. They buy on excitement and relationship trust. Reference other innovative companies using similar approaches. Proposals should be visually engaging, not just text." />

<FlipCard front="S-Style Buyer (Steady)" back="Risk-averse, process-oriented, needs security. Address 'what happens if this doesn't work' proactively. Give them case studies of companies like them who succeeded. Proposals should have clear timelines, checkpoints, and exit options." />

<FlipCard front="C-Style Buyer (Conscientious)" back="Analytical, detail-oriented, skeptical. Provide data, methodology, and detailed rationale. They need to understand exactly how you work. Proposals should include methodology sections, metrics definitions, and references to source data." />

## Part 4: Proposal System Review

<ExampleCard label="The 80% Proposal">
Julia's proposals were beautiful — 15 pages, full branding, detailed methodology, case studies, testimonials.

Her proposal-to-close rate: 18%.

She cut the proposal to 3 pages and added one element: a "Your ROI" section that calculated their specific revenue outcome based on numbers from the discovery call.

Proposal-to-close rate 90 days later: 34%.

The lesson: proposals don't close deals with volume of information. They close deals with clarity of value and specificity of outcome.
</ExampleCard>
