## Week 3 Review: Scale What's Working

Week 3 is the push. The system is calibrated. You know your reply rate. You know which message variant works. You know your funnel conversion at each stage.

Now you scale.

This is also the week when most sprints produce their first closed deal — or the week you realize a close will land in Week 4. Either way, you should have active pipeline with expected close dates.

The Week 3 mindset: **more of what works, less of everything else.**

<InsightCard icon="🚀" title="What Scaling Actually Means">
Scaling is not spraying messages everywhere with lower quality. Scaling is sending more messages at the same quality that's been producing results. The Draft + Human Gate model matters here: AI generates, you approve. More volume with maintained quality equals better results. More volume with degraded quality equals worse results and damaged deliverability.
</InsightCard>

## Part 4: Discovery Call Quality Review

By Week 3, you've held at least 2-3 discovery calls. Let's analyze them.

<ProgressiveReveal title="Discovery Call Analysis Framework" persistKey="capstone-L9-calls-guide">
<RevealSection title="What a Strong Discovery Call Looks Like">

After each call, score these elements (1-5):

1. **BANT/MEDDIC coverage:** Did you uncover Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline?
2. **Pain depth:** Did they articulate pain in their own words, or were you telling them what their pain is?
3. **Decision process clarity:** Do you know who else is involved and what the approval process looks like?
4. **Next step agreement:** Did you close the call with a specific next step both parties committed to?
5. **Energy and momentum:** Did they seem genuinely interested, or were you dragging the conversation?

**Score of 20-25:** Strong call, proposal warranted.
**Score of 12-19:** Acceptable, but you may have missed something. Consider a second call to fill gaps.
**Score of below 12:** Poor call. Either wrong prospect or your discovery process broke down.

</RevealSection>
<RevealSection title="Common Week 3 Discovery Patterns">

By Week 3, most sprint runners notice patterns:

- The calls that convert to proposals share a common characteristic (specific pain, specific timeline, real budget)
- The calls that don't convert share a different characteristic (vague interest, no urgency, not the decision maker)
- The DISC types that move fast vs the ones that take time to decide are becoming visible

Use these patterns to refine your qualification questions for Week 4.

</RevealSection>
</ProgressiveReveal>
