## The $20,000 CRM Mistake

Sarah spent six months in HubSpot Pro ($450/month) before realizing she was using exactly three features: contact storage, a pipeline, and email tracking. Everything else — workflows, custom reports, the marketing hub — sat untouched while she manually updated deals in a spreadsheet "because it was faster."

Meanwhile, her friend Marcus chose Attio Plus ($29/month) and built a relationship-first system that auto-enriched every contact from LinkedIn, synced emails without thinking about it, and surfaced exactly who to follow up with each morning.

Same business stage. Same revenue ($8K MRR). $421/month difference in CRM spend.

The gap wasn't features. It was **fit**.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Real Question">
You don't need the "best" CRM. You need the CRM that matches how you actually sell — and doesn't charge you for features you'll never use.
</InsightCard>

Today you're going to compare HubSpot and Attio head-to-head, not as enterprise tools, but as **solo founder systems**. By the end, you'll know which one fits your sales motion, budget, and AI plans.

## HubSpot Free: The Generous Giant

HubSpot's free tier is shockingly complete for a $0 product. Here's what you actually get:

- **Unlimited contacts** (no artificial caps)
- **1 deal pipeline** with customizable stages
- **Email tracking** (200 open/click notifications per month)
- **Meeting scheduler** (embeddable booking links)
- **Live chat widget** for your website
- **Basic reporting** (pipeline value, deal stage distribution)
- **Mobile app** (iOS/Android)
- **1,600+ integrations** in the marketplace

<FlipCard 
  front="What's the catch with HubSpot Free?" 
  back="1 pipeline only, no automation workflows, HubSpot branding on emails/forms, limited email templates (5 max), and you can't remove 'Powered by HubSpot' footers." 
/>

### What HubSpot Free Does Best

**Ecosystem depth.** If you need to connect your CRM to Calendly, Stripe, Typeform, Slack, Zapier, and 1,594 other tools, HubSpot has native integrations. This matters when you're stitching together a solo founder tech stack on a budget.

**Familiarity.** HubSpot's UI is the industry standard. If you hire a VA or bring on a co-founder later, they've probably used HubSpot before. Zero onboarding friction.

**Upgrade path.** When you outgrow free, HubSpot Starter ($20/seat/month, billed annually) removes branding, adds simple automation, gives you 2 pipelines, and unlocks 1,000 custom properties. It's a smooth ramp.

<ExampleCard label="Real Usage: Technical Founder">
Alex runs a dev tools SaaS at $12K MRR. He uses HubSpot Free to track 400 contacts, log every support conversation (via Slack integration), and run a 6-stage pipeline. His only complaint: he manually tags contacts with "high-intent" because he can't automate it on the free tier. Cost: $0/month.
</ExampleCard>

### HubSpot Free Limitations (The Stuff That Hurts)

<InteractiveChecklist 
  title="HubSpot Free Dealbreakers" 
  persistKey="crm-setup-L2-hubspot-limits"
  items={[
    "Only 1 pipeline (can't separate inbound vs outbound)",

The **1 pipeline** limit is the real constraint. If you run both inbound (content leads) and outbound (cold prospecting), you'll need to mix them in one pipeline or upgrade.

The **no automation** hurts more over time. You can't auto-create tasks when a deal sits idle for 7 days. You can't auto-send a follow-up email when someone opens your proposal. Everything is manual.

## Attio: The AI-Native Challenger

Attio launched in 2019 with a single thesis: **CRMs should work like modern software, not 2005 enterprise tools**. It shows.

### Attio Free Tier (Up to 3 Users)

- **Limited records** (not unlimited like HubSpot)
- **Basic email sync**
- **1 workspace**
- **Mobile app**
- **Attio branding** on shared views

The free tier is a trial, not a long-term solution. Most solo founders hit the record limit within weeks.

### Attio Plus ($29/user/month)

This is where Attio shines:

- **Unlimited records**
- **Automatic contact enrichment** from public data (LinkedIn, company websites, social profiles)
- **Full email sync** (bidirectional Gmail/Outlook)
- **Custom data model** (build any object structure you want, not just "contacts" and "deals")
- **Automations** (trigger actions based on field changes, new records, time delays)
- **AI features** (summarization, field suggestions, smart search)
- **Zapier/Make integrations**
- **No Attio branding**

<FlipCard 
  front="What does 'custom data model' mean?" 
  back="You can create objects beyond contacts/deals — like 'Content Pieces,' 'Partnerships,' 'Investors' — and relate them however you want. HubSpot locks you into their schema." 
/>

### What Attio Does Best

**Zero manual data entry.** When you add a contact, Attio auto-fills their company, role, LinkedIn URL, location, and recent activity from public sources. HubSpot makes you type or import this.

**Modern UX.** Attio feels like Notion or Airtable — clean, fast, keyboard-driven. HubSpot feels like... well, HubSpot.

**AI-native design.** Attio's AI features (summarization, field auto-population, smart search) are baked in, not bolted on. This matters for Course 27 when you build custom agents.

<ExampleCard label="Real Usage: Coach/Consultant">
Maria runs a leadership coaching practice. She uses Attio Plus to track 150 clients and prospects. Every new contact auto-enriches with their LinkedIn bio, current company, and recent posts. She built a custom "Engagement Score" object that tracks email replies, meeting attendance, and referrals. Her pipeline has 4 stages: Inquiry → Discovery → Proposal → Enrolled. Cost: $29/month.
</ExampleCard>

### Attio Limitations (The Stuff That Hurts)

<InteractiveChecklist 
  title="Attio Dealbreakers" 
  persistKey="crm-setup-L2-attio-limits"
  items={[
    "Smaller integration ecosystem (no 1,600 apps like HubSpot)",
    "Free tier is too limited for real use",
    "Less familiar to VAs/hires (steeper onboarding)",
    "No built-in calling or SMS (HubSpot has this in paid tiers)",
    "Newer product = fewer community resources/tutorials"
  ]}
/>

The **integration gap** is real. If you need a native Stripe sync or a pre-built Calendly integration, HubSpot has it. Attio might require Zapier glue.

The **familiarity gap** matters if you plan to hire. A VA who's used HubSpot can jump in immediately. Attio requires training.

## The Sales Motion Test

Your CRM choice depends on **how you actually sell**. Let's map it:

<SlideNavigation>

**Best fit:** HubSpot Starter ($20/mo) + Instantly.ai for sequences  
**Why:** HubSpot's integration ecosystem lets you connect outreach tools seamlessly. The 2-pipeline limit works (Inbound + Outbound). Automation handles follow-up tasks.

**Attio alternative:** Attio Plus ($29/mo) works but requires Zapier for sequence tools. The auto-enrichment saves research time, but you lose native integrations.

**Best fit:** Attio Plus ($29/mo)  
**Why:** Auto-enrichment means you never manually research a contact. Custom data model lets you track "Champion," "Economic Buyer," "Blocker" as separate objects. Modern UX makes daily use pleasant.

**HubSpot alternative:** HubSpot Free works if you're okay with manual enrichment. Upgrade to Starter for automation when deal count grows.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Creator/Coach Enrollment">

**Profile:** You nurture an audience, run application funnels, and book enrollment calls. Sales is relationship-first, not volume-first.

**Best fit:** Attio Plus ($29/mo) or HubSpot Free  
**Why Attio:** Auto-enrichment from social profiles. Custom objects for "Content Engagement" or "Community Activity." Modern feel matches your brand.  
**Why HubSpot:** Free tier handles 100-500 contacts easily. Meeting scheduler is built-in. Integrates with Calendly, ConvertKit, Circle.

</Slide>

## The AI-Readiness Factor

If you're planning to build AI agents in Course 27, your CRM choice matters **now**.

### What Makes a CRM "AI-Ready"?

1. **Structured data model** — Fields are typed (text, number, date, dropdown), not free-text blobs
2. **API access** — Agents can read/write CRM data programmatically
3. **Webhook support** — CRM can trigger external actions when data changes
4. **Custom objects** — You can model relationships beyond "contact → deal"
5. **Event logging** — Every interaction (email, call, meeting) is timestamped and categorized

### HubSpot's AI-Readiness Score: 7/10

**Strengths:**
- Full API access (even on free tier)
- Webhook support (paid tiers)
- Structured fields (properties system)
- Event timeline (emails, calls, meetings logged)

**Weaknesses:**
- Fixed schema (can't create custom objects on Starter)
- Free tier has no workflow automation (limits agent triggers)
- AI features are add-ons, not core

### Attio's AI-Readiness Score: 9/10

**Strengths:**
- Fully flexible data model (custom objects, relationships)
- Native API + webhooks
- Auto-enrichment = less manual data entry = cleaner data
- AI features baked in (summarization, smart search)
- Modern architecture designed for programmatic access

**Weaknesses:**
- Smaller ecosystem = fewer pre-built AI integrations (for now)

<InsightCard icon="🤖" title="For AI Builders">
If you're serious about Course 27 (custom AI agents), Attio's flexible schema and native AI features give you a 6-month head start. HubSpot works but requires more custom code.
</InsightCard>
