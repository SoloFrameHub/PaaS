## The Fork in the Road

You've decided you need help. Now comes the first real decision: do you hire a Virtual Assistant or a part-time SDR?

These two roles sound related but they solve completely different problems. Hiring the wrong one will not just waste money — it will introduce new management complexity without solving the underlying bottleneck.

This lesson gives you a clear framework for making this decision based on your specific situation: your revenue stage, your pipeline volume, how well your outreach process is documented, and your budget.

<InsightCard icon="🗺️" title="The Key Distinction">
A VA solves an admin capacity problem. An SDR solves an outbound volume problem. If you are drowning in admin and can't focus on selling, hire a VA. If your admin is under control but you need more outreach volume than you can generate alone, consider an SDR — but only if you have a proven, documented process for them to follow.
</InsightCard>

## What Each Role Actually Does

Before you can choose, you need to be clear about what you're buying:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="The Virtual Assistant">

**Primary function:** Administrative and operational support.

A VA handles the work around the selling — not the selling itself. Their job is to keep your pipeline organized, your inbox sorted, your CRM current, and your research done so that YOUR selling time is as high-leverage as possible.

**What a VA can do from week one:**
- Inbox triage: read, categorize, route, and flag emails
- CRM updates: log activity, update stages, verify next steps
- Prospect research: fill the Prospect Brief template for your targets
- Calendar management: schedule calls, send Calendly links, handle rescheduling
- Data entry and list hygiene

**What a VA cannot do:**
- Make judgment calls about your sales strategy
- Represent your voice in relationship-building emails
- Conduct discovery calls or qualification conversations
- Decide who to prioritize — that's your job with clean pipeline data

**Budget reality:** $500-1,300/month for a full-time Filipino VA from OnlineJobs.ph. $1,500-2,500/month for a part-time US-based VA from Belay or Time Etc.

</Slide>

<Slide title="The Part-Time SDR">

**Primary function:** Outbound sales development.

An SDR handles prospect-facing outreach with templates and scripts you've built. Their job is to generate more qualified conversations than you could create alone — which means they make sales-oriented decisions based on your documented playbook.

**What a part-time SDR can do:**
- Send outbound emails and LinkedIn messages from your templates
- Run multi-touch follow-up sequences
- Conduct basic qualification calls (with a defined script)
- Book discovery calls onto your calendar
- Report on outreach activity and reply rates

**What a part-time SDR cannot do without your guidance:**
- Handle complex objections outside the script
- Write original outreach that sounds like you
- Make pricing or scope decisions
- Be effective without a proven, documented process

**Budget reality:** $1,500-3,000/month for 20 hours/week. Plus significant management overhead — SDRs require more active coaching than VAs.

</Slide>

<Slide title="The Critical Difference">

The distinction comes down to one question: **Is your bottleneck admin or volume?**

**Admin bottleneck:** You have a good outreach process and conversion rate. But admin overhead is stealing your selling time. You're spending 8+ hours/week on CRM updates, inbox triage, and research instead of calls and emails. A VA solves this by handling the admin so you can sell more.

**Volume bottleneck:** Your admin is under control (or minimal). Your outreach process is documented and converting. But you physically cannot send enough volume at your quality level. An SDR running your proven process multiplies your outbound.

Most solo founders have an admin bottleneck first. Volume becomes the bottleneck only after you've maximized your own selling time — which requires clearing the admin first.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## The Hybrid Path: VA First, SDR Later

For most founders, the journey looks like this:

<ProgressiveReveal title="The Natural Delegation Progression" persistKey="outsourcing-L2-progression">

<RevealSection title="Month 1-3: VA Handles Admin">

Your VA takes over inbox triage, CRM updates, and prospect research. You get 6-10 hours per week back. You use those hours for more personal outreach and better quality selling conversations.

Your pipeline gets cleaner. Your follow-up speed improves. Your CRM becomes reliable. This is the foundation.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Month 3-6: Document Your Process">

With admin cleared, you now have bandwidth to document your outreach process properly. You track what's working: which messages get replies, which triggers lead to meetings, which qualification questions predict close.

You build the playbook that would eventually allow an SDR to operate.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Month 6-12: Evaluate Volume Need">

By now you have two pieces of data: the capacity your VA freed up, and whether personal outreach has hit a ceiling. If you've maxed out outreach quality and volume and pipeline is not keeping up with your closing capacity, the SDR conversation makes sense.

Importantly, your SOPs from Lessons 3-5 become the SDR's training materials. You're not starting from zero.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Month 12+: Some VAs Grow Into SDR Support">

Approximately 40% of VAs can grow into light SDR support — sending templated outreach, running sequences, handling follow-up logistics. This is not guaranteed, but it's a path worth exploring before making a separate SDR hire.

If your VA shows good judgment, understands your ICP, and communicates clearly, consider expanding their role incrementally before hiring a dedicated SDR.

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>
