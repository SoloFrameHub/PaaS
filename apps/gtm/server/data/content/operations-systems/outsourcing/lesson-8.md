## Everything You've Built, Now Assembled

Over seven lessons you've built the full infrastructure for delegating your sales operations without losing control of quality. You've defined when you need help, chosen the right type of help, written three operational SOPs, learned how to hire and onboard, and set up the tools for sustainable collaboration.

This final lesson does two things. First, it assembles everything into a single Delegation Playbook — a living document that defines your delegation system and serves as onboarding material for any current or future VA. Second, it gives you the 7-Day Delegation Sprint: a structured action plan to go from "I have the SOPs" to "I have a VA hired and producing output."

<InsightCard icon="📋" title="What You're Building Today">
The Delegation Playbook is a single document (in Notion or Google Docs) that contains every artifact from this course: your threshold assessment, your hiring decision, your three SOPs, your onboarding protocol, your QA scorecard, and your delegation scorecard. If your VA leaves tomorrow, their replacement can be onboarded from this document alone.
</InsightCard>

## The 7-Day Delegation Sprint

If you're ready to hire now, here is your day-by-day action plan. This is not a suggestion — it's a structured sprint that takes you from completed SOPs to a VA producing output in one week.

<ProgressiveReveal title="7-Day Delegation Sprint" persistKey="outsourcing-L8-sprint">

<RevealSection title="Day 1: SOP Finalization and Loom Recording">

**Morning (2-3 hours):**
- Review all three SOPs and verify they are complete, clear, and ready to hand to a stranger
- Add any missing examples to the decision trees (use real email scenarios you've encountered)
- Record Loom walkthroughs for all three SOPs (10-15 min each)
- Build your Notion VA Hub with all SOPs, templates, and reference pages

**Output:** Complete SOP library and Notion VA Hub ready to share on Day 6.

**Don't skip this step.** Posting a job before your SOPs are complete leads to a frustrated VA and a degraded onboarding experience.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Day 2: Job Post and Applications">

**Morning (1 hour):**
- Finalize your job post using the template from Lesson 6
- Post on OnlineJobs.ph (if budget-focused) or Upwork (if flexible/specialized)
- For Belay or Time Etc: submit your request and begin their matching process

**What to expect:** OnlineJobs.ph applications often arrive within 24-48 hours. Upwork is slightly faster. Filter immediately using your application instructions (the filter phrase you included in the post).

**Output:** Live job posting. Applications incoming.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Day 3-4: Review and Test Tasks">

**Day 3 morning (1 hour):**
- Review all applications received so far
- Apply your initial filter (followed instructions? Relevant experience? Good English?)
- Shortlist 3-5 candidates who passed your filter

**Day 3 afternoon:**
- Send your paid test task to all shortlisted candidates with a 48-hour deadline
- Include payment details upfront ($15-20 via PayPal, Wise, or direct bank transfer)

**Day 4:**
- Continue reviewing new applications as they arrive
- Answer any clarifying questions from test task candidates within 2 hours (this signals professionalism to them)

**Output:** 3-5 candidates in your test task pipeline. Results due Day 5.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Day 5: Evaluate and Interview">

**Morning (2 hours):**
- Review all test task submissions
- Score each against your evaluation criteria (triage accuracy, research quality, note formatting, communication)
- Identify your top 1-2 candidates

**Afternoon (30-45 min):**
- Schedule and conduct 15-minute video interviews with top candidates
- Ask the specific questions from Lesson 6: how they handled ambiguity in the test task, how they approach questions when a process doesn't cover a situation

**Output:** Final candidate selected by end of Day 5.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Day 6: Offer and Onboarding Materials">

**Morning (1 hour):**
- Send offer message via Slack or email: confirm rate, hours, start date, and trial period expectations
- Share access to: Notion VA Hub, Loom video library, Slack workspace invitation
- Set up 1Password shared vault with all tool credentials
- Send them the 4-week onboarding schedule so they know what to expect

**Don't skip the trial period framing:** "We'll start with a 2-week trial at full pay. At the end of week 2, we'll evaluate fit on both sides and confirm ongoing." This sets the right tone and gives both parties an exit if it's not working.

**Output:** VA accepted, access granted, onboarding materials shared.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Day 7: First Day">

**Day 7 routine:**
- VA starts their shift and posts their first start-of-shift message in #va-channel
- VA completes first inbox triage and CRM update with you reviewing every output
- 15-minute check-in call or Slack conversation: walk through any questions from Day 1 tasks
- End of Day 7: VA posts first end-of-shift summary. You confirm what they got right and flag any corrections with SOP context.

**Your goal for Day 7:** Build confidence in the system, not perfection in the output. Expect errors. Treat each as a training moment. Update the SOP. Do not criticize — explain.

**Output:** VA is producing output. Week 1 oversight mode begins.

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## When to End a VA Relationship

Not every VA relationship works out. Knowing when and how to part ways is part of managing well.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Signs the Relationship Isn't Working">

**QA scores:** Consistently below 80% after 6 weeks and multiple SOP clarifications. If the errors are different each week (new situations), the SOP needs work. If the errors are the same each week after SOP updates, the relationship may not fit.

**Communication breakdown:** Missed check-ins, slow Slack responses, end-of-shift updates that stop appearing, questions asked repeatedly despite being answered in Notion.

**Management overhead exceeding delegation benefit:** If you're spending more than 1 hour per day managing your VA, the delegation is costing you time rather than saving it. Retraining should fix this within 1-2 weeks. If it doesn't, the fit is wrong.

</Slide>

<Slide title="How to Part Ways Professionally">

Give a minimum of 1-2 weeks notice. Pay for all hours worked. Be specific about what wasn't working so the VA has useful feedback for their next role.

Message template: "I want to let you know that after [X weeks], I've decided to end our working relationship. You've handled [specific tasks] well, and I appreciate your effort. The area where we weren't a fit is [specific, factual description]. I'll pay you through [end date] and provide a Upwork/OnlineJobs.ph review that's honest and fair."

Then restart the hiring process using what you learned about what didn't work. Adjust your test task to screen for the gap more effectively.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Protecting Yourself Before Ending">

Before telling your VA: change all shared passwords in your 1Password vault. Remove their access from your CRM, email, and Notion. Then have the conversation.

This is not adversarial — it's standard security practice. Most VAs will understand. But access should be removed proactively rather than as a reaction to a problem.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>
