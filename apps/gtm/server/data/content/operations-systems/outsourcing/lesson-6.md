## The Three Mistakes That Kill VA Relationships in the First Month

Most VA relationships that fail don't fail because the VA was bad. They fail because the founder didn't know how to hire, onboard, or manage.

**Mistake 1: Hiring before the SOPs exist.** If you don't have documented procedures, you're not hiring a VA — you're hiring a confused person and then wondering why they make mistakes.

**Mistake 2: Too much autonomy too fast.** You hire someone, hand them a folder, and expect professional output in week one. When quality is inconsistent, you either micromanage or give up.

**Mistake 3: No feedback mechanism.** VA works, founder checks occasionally, small errors accumulate. By week six, the quality issues feel systemic but no one knows where they started.

This lesson fixes all three. By the end, you'll know exactly how to write a job post that attracts the right candidates, design a test task that filters unqualified applicants, and run a 4-week onboarding that builds quality output without babysitting.

<InsightCard icon="✅" title="Prerequisites for Hiring">
Before you post a job listing, you should have: (1) All three SOPs from Lessons 3-5 written and documented. (2) A clear sense of weekly hours you need (10 hours/week vs 40 hours/week changes your platform choice). (3) A budget that fits within 15-20% of your MRR. If any of these are missing, finish them before hiring.
</InsightCard>

## The Paid Test Task: Your Most Powerful Filter

Never hire a VA based on interviews alone. Always run a paid test task. This does two things: it filters applicants who can't do the actual work, and it signals to good applicants that you're serious and professional.

<ExampleCard label="Example: A Paid Test Task for Sales Operations VA">
**Test task design (for $15-20 payment, takes 2-3 hours for candidate):**

Task 1 — Inbox Triage: Here are 10 sample emails [attach anonymized examples]. Categorize each as Lead, Customer, Admin, or Noise. For any Lead, describe what CRM action you'd take.

Task 2 — Prospect Research: Research these three companies and complete our Prospect Brief template for the key decision-maker at each: [Company A, Company B, Company C]. Use LinkedIn, company website, and Crunchbase. Source all factual claims.

Task 3 — Meeting Notes: Here are raw founder notes from a sales call [provide a realistic example]. Format them using our meeting note template and note any information that's missing.

**What you're evaluating:**
- Triage accuracy (did they get the categories right?)
- Research quality (are the briefs specific and sourced, or generic and guessed?)
- Note formatting (did they follow the template or improvise?)
- Communication (how did they ask questions? Did they flag ambiguity professionally?)
</ExampleCard>

## Screening: How to Evaluate Applicants

<ProgressiveReveal title="The 3-Stage Screening Process" persistKey="outsourcing-L6-screening">

<RevealSection title="Stage 1: Application Review (10 min per applicant)">

For a well-written job post on OnlineJobs.ph, expect 20-50 applications. Your first filter is ruthless but fair:

**Immediate disqualify:**
- Did not follow application instructions (no filter phrase, no CRM task description)
- Generic cover letter that could apply to any job
- No example of relevant work (CRM, research, inbox management)
- English proficiency clearly insufficient for client-facing email templates

**Keep for Stage 2:**
- Followed instructions exactly
- Described relevant experience with specifics (not "I managed a CRM" but "I updated HubSpot deal stages daily for a B2B software company")
- English is clear and professional
- Mentions tools you actually use

Aim to pass 5-10% of applicants to Stage 2. If you pass 30%, your job post was too vague.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Stage 2: Paid Test Task (3-5 candidates)">

Send your paid test task to the top 3-5 candidates. Pay $15-20 regardless of outcome — this is non-negotiable. Candidates who do good work deserve payment even if you don't hire them.

**Evaluation criteria:**

Inbox triage accuracy: Did they categorize correctly? Did they miss any lead signals? Did they flag ambiguous cases rather than guessing?

Research quality: Are Prospect Briefs specific and sourced, or generic and unverified? Is the ICP scoring reasoned, not just a number?

Note formatting: Did they follow the template precisely? Did they handle missing information correctly (flagging it vs. inventing it)?

Professionalism: How did they communicate during the task? Did they ask good clarifying questions? Were they on time?

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Stage 3: 15-Minute Video Interview">

For your top 1-2 candidates after the test task. Keep it focused:

**Questions that matter:**
- "Walk me through how you completed the prospect research section of the test task."
- "What did you find confusing or unclear in the instructions, and how did you handle that?"
- "Describe a time a process you were following didn't cover a situation you encountered. What did you do?"
- "What tools do you use daily, and what are you most comfortable with?"

**What you're evaluating:**
- Can they explain their reasoning clearly? (Tests communication quality)
- Do they ask good questions, or do they guess? (Tests judgment)
- Are they honest about uncertainty? (Tests trustworthiness)
- Do they match the written communication quality you saw in the test task?

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## The 4-Week Onboarding Protocol

This is the most important section in this lesson. Good onboarding reduces VA turnover by 50% and gets you to quality output in 4 weeks rather than 3 months.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Week 1: Full Oversight">

**Authority level:** VA executes with founder reviewing every output.

**Daily routine:**
- VA completes SOP tasks (inbox triage, CRM updates, any research assigned)
- Founder reviews every categorized email and CRM update before EOD
- 15-minute check-in via Slack or Zoom each day

**What you're doing in reviews:**
- Catching errors before they matter
- Explaining the reasoning behind correct decisions
- Adding examples to the SOP for edge cases you encounter

**What you're NOT doing:** Criticizing general performance. Every error is a gap in the SOP, not a failure by the VA. If they categorized an email wrong, the SOP didn't give them clear enough rules.

**Target by end of Week 1:** VA knows the tools, can navigate the SOP, and is asking the right questions.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Week 2: Spot-Check Mode">

**Authority level:** VA works independently. Founder spot-checks 50% of outputs.

**Daily routine:**
- VA completes all SOP tasks independently
- Founder reviews a random sample of 50% of triage decisions and CRM updates
- 15-minute check-in 3 times per week (Mon/Wed/Fri)

**What you're evaluating:**
- Is accuracy improving or are the same error types repeating?
- Are they using the escalation protocol correctly (flagging unsure cases)?
- Are they getting faster without sacrificing quality?

**Target by end of Week 2:** Error rate below 20% on inbox triage. All CRM updates following template correctly. Research briefs complete and sourced.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Week 3: Light Oversight">

**Authority level:** VA is largely autonomous. Founder spot-checks 20% of outputs.

**Weekly routine:**
- VA completes all tasks independently
- Founder reviews a 20% sample during weekly QA session
- 15-minute check-in twice per week

**Signs Week 3 is going well:**
- VA is proactively flagging things you hadn't thought to ask about
- Escalations are appropriate (not too many, not too few)
- CRM data quality is consistent without reminders

**If Week 3 is going poorly:** Do not move to Week 4 autonomy. Identify the specific SOP gap and add clearer guidance before reducing oversight further.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Week 4: Full Autonomy">

**Authority level:** VA fully autonomous with weekly QA.

**Ongoing routine:**
- Weekly 30-minute 1:1 (what went well, what was confusing, what needs SOP update)
- Weekly QA spot-check (10% of outputs, or 5-10 items)
- Monthly SOP review and update session

**Signs Week 4 is successful:**
- QA accuracy above 90%
- Escalations are well-reasoned
- VA is suggesting SOP improvements based on patterns they've noticed

**At this point:** Your VA is a system component, not a management obligation. Weekly 1:1s keep the relationship strong and catch drift before it becomes a problem.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Tool Access: Security Without Friction

Your VA needs access to your tools — but not all tools, and not admin-level access. Here's the principle: **give the minimum access needed to do the job well.**

<InteractiveChecklist
  title="VA Tool Access Checklist"
  persistKey="outsourcing-L6-access"
  items={[
    "CRM: View + Edit access only (not Admin — VA cannot delete records or change pipeline settings)",
    "Email: Delegated access to inbox (or a shared inbox alias like team@yourdomain.com)",
    "Slack: Invite to workspace, add to #va-channel and any relevant project channels",
    "Notion: Share your VA Hub workspace with Edit access",
    "Google Drive: Share your SOP folder with Edit access; share your templates folder",
    "Password manager (1Password or LastPass): Share credentials for tools VA needs — NEVER send passwords in Slack or email",
    "Loom: Ensure VA has access to your training video library"
  ]}
/>

<InsightCard icon="🔐" title="Password Security Non-Negotiable">
Never share passwords via Slack, email, or text. Use 1Password or LastPass to share credentials securely. With a password manager, you can revoke access instantly if the relationship ends. This protects you and protects your VA from liability.
</InsightCard>

## What Good VA Communication Looks Like

<ExampleCard label="Example: VA End-of-Shift Slack Update">
**#va-channel, 5:00 PM**

Hi Mike — here's my end-of-shift update for today:

**Inbox triage:** 47 emails processed. 3 flagged as leads (Priya @ Rowantech, Tom @ Clearfield, and an inbound from a referral by David Chen — brief summary attached). 12 filed as admin. 32 archived as noise.

**CRM updates:** Updated 6 deals with email activity. Flagged 2 deals with no next action date — they're in your HubSpot queue to review. Logged meeting notes for your call with Marcus (formatted notes in HubSpot under his record).

**Escalations:** 1 email I wasn't sure about — it could be a partner inquiry or a sales prospect. Tagged as "Unsure" in Gmail and sent you a Slack message separately.

**Tomorrow:** Starting on the Monday research list (10 prospects). Will have briefs ready by Wednesday EOD.

---

This update takes your VA under 5 minutes to write and gives you full situational awareness in 60 seconds. This is the goal.
</ExampleCard>
