## The Ramp Expectation Problem

Most founders have no framework for what "normal" ramp looks like. So when Month 1 ends and their new SDR has booked 3 meetings instead of 8, they don't know whether to be worried or patient. They wait. They hope. Month 2 ends with 5 meetings. Still below quota, but it's only Month 2. Month 3: 4 meetings. Now it's clear something is wrong — but 3 months of salary have been spent waiting to find out.

The problem wasn't the hire. It was the absence of a ramp framework that would have flagged trouble at Week 4 instead of Month 3.

This lesson gives you the specific numbers, signals, and decision points that let you distinguish "normal ramp" from "silent failure" — early enough to actually do something about it.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Ramp Insight">
The leading indicators of success (activity quality, ICP targeting accuracy, message personalization) are visible in Week 2. The lagging indicators (meetings booked, quota attainment) don't tell you anything useful until Month 3. Manage the leading indicators early, and the lagging indicators take care of themselves.
</InsightCard>

## What Normal Ramp Looks Like

Research from the Bridge Group shows that SDR ramp time is 3–6 months to full productivity, with structured onboarding cutting that to 2–3 months. Here's what the trajectory looks like week by week:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Month 1: Learning and First Outputs">

**Realistic quota:** 50% (4–5 meetings if full quota is 9)

**What you should actually see:**

- Weeks 1–2: Zero meetings booked (they're in onboarding — this is expected)
- Week 3: First outreach sequences live; maybe 1 meeting booked
- Week 4: 2–3 meetings booked; first positive reply patterns emerging

**Green lights at end of Month 1:**
- They can describe the ICP without looking at notes
- They're sending personalized outreach (not just running templates verbatim)
- Reply rates are above 3% (even if meetings are low)
- They're asking good questions in your weekly 1:1 ("Why does this ICP have urgency in Q1?")
- They're reviewing feedback and implementing it (you can see it in their next messages)

**Red flags at end of Month 1:**
- They haven't sent any outreach by Day 15 ("I'm still learning the product")
- They're sending generic template emails without any personalization
- Reply rates are under 1.5%
- They're resistant to message feedback ("I think my version is better")
- They've only booked 0 meetings by Week 4 despite sending 200+ outreach messages

</Slide>
<Slide title="Month 2: Supervised Execution">

**Realistic quota:** 75% (6–7 meetings if full quota is 9)

**What you should actually see:**

- Weeks 5–6: 2–3 meetings per week coming in from their outreach
- Week 7: They're managing replies and scheduling independently
- Week 8: Their first discovery calls (you're observing recordings, not live)

**Green lights at end of Month 2:**
- Reply rates are 5%+ (approaching your benchmark)
- They're reaching the right people (ICP targeting is accurate)
- They're handling "not interested" replies professionally and logging them properly in CRM
- Meeting show rates are 70%+ (they're booking qualified prospects who actually show up)
- They're proactively identifying what's working: "The emails that reference [specific trigger] are getting 10% reply rates"

**Red flags at end of Month 2:**
- They're hitting activity targets but missing outcome targets (many emails, few replies)
- High no-show rates (50%+ of booked meetings don't show) — signals unqualified prospect booking
- Consistently below 5 meetings per month after Month 2
- Still not incorporating feedback on message quality
- Blaming the product, the leads, or the market for low results

</Slide>
<Slide title="Month 3: Full Quota Responsibility">

**Realistic quota:** 100% (9 meetings)

**What you should actually see:**

- They're consistently booking 7–10 meetings per month
- They're managing replies, objections, and scheduling independently
- Their message quality is indistinguishable from your approved templates
- They're suggesting playbook improvements ("I noticed X type of company responds better to Y opener")

**Green lights at end of Month 3:**
- Quota attainment at 80%+ (7+ meetings)
- Reply rate at 6%+ (SDR is personalizating and targeting effectively)
- Minimal management time required from you (they come to 1:1s with their own pipeline analysis)
- They're self-diagnosing: "My reply rates dropped this week — I think the subject line was too long"

**Red flags at end of Month 3:**
- Below 50% of quota (4 or fewer meetings) with no clear improving trend
- Still relying on you to review messages before sending
- Low conversion from meeting to SQL (they're booking meetings that don't qualify)
- Zero self-diagnosis — they can only say "I'm working on it" without specifics

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## The Leading vs. Lagging Indicator Framework

Don't wait for quota misses to manage performance. The signals that predict success or failure appear weeks before they show up in your meeting count.

<FlipCard
  front="Leading Indicator: Message personalization rate"
  back="Pull 10 random messages from their queue. What % have a specific, prospect-relevant first line vs. a generic opener? Under 50% personalized is a red flag. Fix: return to voice transfer kit and add a 'personalization check' to their pre-send process."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Leading Indicator: ICP targeting accuracy"
  back="Pull 20 contacts they've prospected this week. What % match your ICP criteria exactly? If they're off-ICP (wrong company size, wrong role, wrong industry), meetings that do get booked won't convert. Fix: joint ICP review — go through their prospect list together and requalify."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Leading Indicator: Reply rate trend"
  back="Is their reply rate improving week-over-week, flat, or declining? An SDR who goes from 1.5% in Week 3 to 3.5% in Week 6 is learning and improving. Flat or declining reply rates after 6 weeks signal a message quality or targeting problem that isn't self-correcting."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Leading Indicator: Feedback implementation speed"
  back="You give them a specific feedback note on Tuesday. Do you see it implemented by Thursday? Or does the same pattern appear again next week? Implementation speed is the #1 predictor of whether coaching will work. Slow implementers don't get faster with time."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Lagging Indicator: Meetings booked per month"
  back="This is the number most founders track, but it tells you what happened 30 days ago, not what's happening now. By the time you see 3 consecutive months of under-quota, the leading indicators have been blinking red for 6 weeks. Don't manage only by this number."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Lagging Indicator: Quota attainment %"
  back="Useful for Month 3+ evaluation. Meaningless in Month 1–2 because the ramp schedule exists for a reason. A Month 1 SDR at 40% of ramp quota is doing fine. The same SDR at 40% of full quota in Month 4 is in trouble."
/>

## The Week-by-Week Management Rhythm

Managing a ramping SDR requires a specific weekly cadence. Here's what to do each week.

<ProgressiveReveal title="Week-by-Week Management Actions" persistKey="scale-L7-rhythm">

<RevealSection title="Week 1–2 (Onboarding): Daily 15-Minute Check-In">
Not a performance review — a progress check. "What did you learn from the calls today? What's still unclear? What's your plan for tomorrow?" The goal is to surface confusion before it turns into bad habits. 15 minutes, same time each day. Not a meeting — a quick Slack or Zoom touchpoint.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Week 3–4 (First Outreach): Message Review + Metrics Check">
Pull 5 messages they've sent each week. Review personalization, targeting, and CTA quality. Share specific written feedback by end of day. Also check: Are they hitting their daily activity targets (emails per day, LinkedIn messages per day)? The first sign of a low-effort problem is activity below 60% of target.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Month 2 (Supervised Execution): Weekly 30-Minute 1:1">
Structured agenda: (1) Wins — what's working (2 min). (2) Pipeline review — what meetings are booked, what's in progress (5 min). (3) Metrics — reply rate, show rate, meetings vs. target (5 min). (4) One coaching point — specific call recording or message example (10 min). (5) Next week targets (5 min). (6) Blockers — what do they need from you (3 min).
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Month 3+ (Full Quota): Weekly 1:1 + Recording Review">
Same 1:1 structure, but they now bring the agenda. You review 2 call recordings per week — you pick which ones (they don't know which) and give written feedback asynchronously. The 1:1 shifts from "reporting" to "coaching" — they should be diagnosing their own performance by now, and your job is to challenge their diagnosis and suggest higher-level strategy.
</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## The Ramp Expectation Conversation You Must Have on Day 1

Don't wait until Month 3 to tell your hire what success looks like. Have this conversation on their first day.

  persistKey="scale-L7-roleplay"

Month 2: You're running sequences independently with me reviewing your first 2 weeks of messages. Target is 6–7 meetings. Month 3: Full quota — 9 meetings. I'll be listening to 2 call recordings per week and giving you written feedback. At the end of Month 3, if you're at 80%+ of quota and your message quality is strong, I'll extend and invest in your development. If you're below 50% of quota with no improving trend, we'll have a different conversation. Does that feel clear and fair?'"
