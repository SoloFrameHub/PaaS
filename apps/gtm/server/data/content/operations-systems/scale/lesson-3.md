## Why Most Sales Job Descriptions Fail

Open LinkedIn right now and search for SDR positions at early-stage startups. You'll find descriptions that look like they were written by someone who's never met an SDR.

"Proven track record of exceeding quota." (It's their first SDR. What quota?)

"10+ years of B2B sales experience." (For a $45K base role.)

"Self-starter who thrives in fast-paced environments." (Every job description says this.)

"Rockstar sales ninja with a passion for disruption." (Run.)

The result: you get 200 applications from people who copy-paste the same application to 50 jobs, and zero applications from the person who would actually be great.

Your job description is your first sales pitch — for the role. It needs to be specific, honest, compelling, and clear. Everything you've learned about writing for your ICP in Courses 2 and 7 applies here.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Job Description Mindset">
You are not writing a requirements document. You are writing a sales page for a job. The candidate is the buyer. The job is the product. Your JD needs to create desire, establish credibility, and give clear next steps — just like a great sales email.
</InsightCard>

## The Six-Part JD Structure

A great sales job description has exactly six sections. Each has a specific purpose. Here they are with examples:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Section 1: The Hook (2–3 sentences)">

**Purpose:** Answer "why would a talented person want this job?" before they even know what it is.

**Bad hook:**
"We are a SaaS company looking for an ambitious sales development representative to join our growing team."

**Good hook:**
"We've closed our first 25 customers with $0 in marketing spend. Every deal came from founder-led outbound — and we've documented every step. Now we're looking for our first SDR to scale what's already working, with real equity and a direct line to the founder."

**Why it works:** Specificity creates credibility. "Documented every step" signals the candidate won't be guessing. "Real equity" and "direct line to the founder" signal startup-worthy compensation and access.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Section 2: About Us (3–4 sentences)">

**Purpose:** Establish context — what you do, who you serve, traction, and stage.

**Bad about us:**
"We are a cutting-edge B2B SaaS platform focused on innovation and disruption."

**Good about us:**
"We help e-commerce founders track inventory costs across multiple Shopify stores. We're 18 months old, at $24K MRR, and growing 15% month over month. Our customers are 5–50 person D2C brands that sell on Shopify and Amazon. We're remote-first, 4 people, and pre-Series A."

**Why it works:** Real numbers create credibility. Stage and size help the candidate self-select. "Pre-Series A, 4 people" tells the right candidate this is a real build opportunity, not a corporate job.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Section 3: The Role — Daily Activities (5–7 bullets)">

**Purpose:** Tell the candidate exactly what they'll do on a Tuesday. Not vague "responsibilities" — specific, honest activities.

**Bad role description:**
- Drive pipeline through strategic outbound initiatives
- Collaborate with cross-functional teams
- Exceed monthly and quarterly targets

**Good role description:**
- Run 5-touch cold email sequences to VP Finance and CFO at 20–200 person SaaS companies using Apollo and HubSpot
- Send 30–40 personalized LinkedIn messages per day using our proven templates
- Book 8–10 qualified discovery calls per month directly onto the founder's calendar
- Research new prospect lists weekly using LinkedIn Sales Navigator against our ICP criteria
- Update HubSpot after every activity — we track sequence performance, not just output
- Join weekly 30-min 1:1 with the founder for call coaching and performance review

</Slide>
<Slide title="Section 4: Requirements (5 maximum)">

**Purpose:** List must-haves only. Anything beyond 5 requirements reduces applications by 40%.

**Format:** 3–4 must-haves, 1–2 nice-to-haves (labeled clearly).

**SDR must-haves:**
- 1–3 years of outbound sales or prospecting experience (email, LinkedIn, or cold call)
- Demonstrated ability to write compelling cold emails (we'll test this in the application)
- Comfortable with high autonomy — you manage your own activity levels, we review outputs
- Based in a time zone within 3 hours of EST

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Experience with HubSpot or Apollo (we'll train you if not)
- Familiarity with SaaS or e-commerce companies

</Slide>
<Slide title="Section 5: Compensation (3–4 lines)">

**Purpose:** Be completely transparent. Job posts with salary ranges get 30–50% more qualified applicants.

**Format:**
- Base salary range
- Variable comp structure (how it's earned, not just the number)
- Equity (range or "we offer equity")
- Perks worth mentioning

**Example:**
"Base salary: $42,000–48,000/year, paid monthly. Variable: $75 per qualified meeting booked (target: 9/month = $675/month at target), with an accelerator to $112/meeting above 11/month. OTE at target: $50,100–56,100. Equity: 0.1–0.25% with 4-year vest, 1-year cliff. Perks: fully remote, $1,000/year learning budget, quarterly team offsite."

</Slide>
<Slide title="Section 6: How to Apply (the filter)">

**Purpose:** Give a specific application task that screens for writing ability, initiative, and the ability to follow instructions.

**The classic filter:** "To apply, send your resume plus a 3-email cold outreach sequence targeting [specific ICP] as if you were selling [your product]. You have 48 hours from the time you see this posting."

**Why this works:**
- Filters 40% of low-effort applicants who won't complete the task
- The quality of the sequence tells you immediately whether they can write
- Candidates who do it demonstrate initiative and ability to follow specific instructions
- You have real work product to evaluate before you schedule a single call

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Common JD Mistakes to Avoid

<FlipCard
  front="Mistake: Too many requirements"
  back="Every requirement beyond 5 reduces applications by ~8%. List only true must-haves. 'Nice-to-have' requirements belong in a separate labeled section. If you'd hire someone without it, it's not a requirement."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Mistake: Hiding the compensation"
  back="Job posts with transparent comp ranges get 30–50% more qualified applicants and attract people who are serious about the role. Candidates who apply without knowing comp waste your time and theirs. Always show the range."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Mistake: 'Responsibilities' instead of activities"
  back="'Drive pipeline development' tells a candidate nothing about their day. 'Send 35 LinkedIn messages and 20 cold emails per day using our Apollo sequences' tells them everything. Be specific about the actual work."
/>

<FlipCard
  front="Mistake: No application filter"
  back="Generic applications (resume only, no task) attract people who applied to 50 other jobs today. A specific task — 'write a 3-email sequence' — filters for candidates who read carefully, write well, and follow instructions."
/>

## Startup JD Advantages You Should Lead With

You can't compete with enterprise comp. But you have advantages that enterprise jobs can't offer. Use them.

<InsightCard icon="🚀" title="What Startup-Savvy Candidates Actually Want">
According to RepVue surveys, 60% of sales candidates prioritize autonomy and growth over base salary. The right candidates for your first hire are motivated by: real equity with upside, direct access to the founder (coaching and visibility), building something from scratch (ownership, not order-taking), and the ability to shape how the team grows. Lead with these in your hook and throughout the JD.
</InsightCard>

## Your Final JD Quality Check

Before you post, run your JD through this filter:

<InteractiveChecklist
  title="JD Quality Checklist"
  persistKey="scale-L3-quality"
  items={[
    "Hook is specific — includes real numbers, not vague promises",
    "About Us includes MRR, customer count, or growth rate",
    "Role section lists daily activities, not vague 'responsibilities'",
    "No more than 5 requirements total (3–4 must-haves + 1–2 nice-to-haves)",
    "Salary range is transparent and includes variable comp and OTE",
    "Equity is listed (even as a range)",
    "Application requires a specific task that tests the #1 skill for this role",
    "JD contains zero of these words: rockstar, ninja, guru, guru, fast-paced, synergy, disruption"
  ]}
