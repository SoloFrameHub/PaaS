## From First Hire to Repeatable System

You've made it through nine lessons. You know when to hire, who to hire, how to write the JD, how to interview, how to set comp, how to onboard, how to manage, and what to do when it doesn't work.

Now comes the last step: packaging everything you've built into a single Hiring Playbook that makes the next hire faster, cheaper, and more likely to succeed.

Here's why this matters: the average time to fill an SDR role is 30–45 days. The average time a founder spends on the process — writing a JD from scratch, building interview questions, designing comp from scratch — is 20–30 hours per hire. With a documented Hiring Playbook, your second hire takes 8–10 hours. Your third takes 4–5. By the time you're hiring your fourth sales rep, it's nearly a routine operation.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Compounding Value of Documentation">
The work you did in Lessons 1–9 was for this hire. The playbook you build in Lesson 10 is for every hire after this one. A founder who documents the hiring process creates a repeatable system. A founder who doesn't starts from scratch every time — and makes the same mistakes every time.
</InsightCard>

## The Complete Hiring Playbook: What It Contains

Your Hiring Playbook is a single living document that compiles all nine lesson artifacts. Here's what it should include:

<ProgressiveReveal title="Hiring Playbook Table of Contents" persistKey="scale-L10-toc">

<RevealSection title="Section 1: Hiring Readiness Checklist (from Lesson 1)">
The seven prerequisites that must be true before you hire: deals closed, meetings held, playbook documented, MRR threshold met, documentation complete. This section prevents you from hiring too early ever again.

Update this section each time you consider a new hire — the thresholds change as the company grows.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 2: Role Decision Matrix (from Lesson 2)">
The comparison framework for fractional vs. PT SDR vs. FT SDR vs. FT AE. Your current matrix captures your decision for this hire. As revenue grows, the right role will shift — update the matrix at each hiring decision.

Include: the financial thresholds for each role, the bottleneck each role solves, and your specific rationale for this hire's role type.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 3: Job Description Templates (from Lesson 3)">
Store your completed JDs (SDR and AE) with performance notes on each: "This version of the JD generated 34 applications and 2 strong finalists. The application task filtered well."

Over time, you'll iterate on the JD based on what attracts the best candidates. The playbook tracks those iterations.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 4: Interview Scorecard + Question Bank (from Lesson 4)">
The complete 3-stage interview process: async writing task evaluation criteria, the 8 behavioral questions with scoring notes, the roleplay rubric, and the reference check script.

Add to the question bank after every hiring process: "These 3 questions predicted performance best. This question revealed nothing useful."
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 5: Compensation Model Templates (from Lesson 5)">
Your comp plans for each role type (SDR and AE), including base ranges, variable structures, quota targets, ramp schedule, and accelerators. Updated for current market rates each year.

Include the ROI calculation that justified each hire's comp structure.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 6: 30-60-90 Day Onboarding Plan (from Lesson 6)">
The week-by-week onboarding schedule, call library details, voice transfer kit, and milestone checklist. This is the most important section for the first 90 days of each new hire.

Update after every onboarding: "Week 2 needed more time on CRM — add an extra day for tool training."
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 7: Ramp Benchmarks and Milestones (from Lesson 7)">
The specific targets for Months 1, 2, and 3. The leading indicators to track. The decision checkpoints. Updated for each hire based on your market and ICP.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 8: Management Cadence Template (from Lesson 8)">
The weekly 1:1 agenda, call coaching framework, metrics dashboard structure, and the full 90-day management cadence. Reusable for every hire with minor customization.
</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Section 9: Performance Review Framework (from Lesson 9)">
The red flag / yellow flag taxonomy, the 90-day review template, the PIP structure, the separation checklist, and the post-hire retrospective. The most important section for making hard decisions quickly.
</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## The 14-Day Hiring Sprint

You've made the hire decision. Here's how to go from decision to Day 1 in exactly two weeks.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Days 1–2: Post and Activate">

**Day 1:**
- Finalize your JD (using the template from Lesson 3)
- Post on Bravado (free, vetted sales candidates)
- Post on LinkedIn Jobs (basic listing or sponsored)
- Post on AngelList/Wellfound (free, startup-savvy)

**Day 2:**
- Set up your screening folder: email filter for applications, shared doc for tracking candidates
- Write your evaluation rubric for the async writing task (what does a 4/5 response look like vs. a 2/5?)
- Set a calendar block for application review: 30 minutes every morning, Days 3–7

**Target:** JD is live on 3 platforms by end of Day 2.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Days 3–7: Application Screening">

**Daily 30-minute application review:**
- Evaluate resume (2 min): relevant experience, specific metrics, startup exposure
- Evaluate writing task (3 min): followed instructions? ICP-specific? Would you open these emails?
- Decision: Advance, Decline, or "Evaluate more closely"

**By end of Day 7:**
- 30–50 applicants screened
- 5–8 advances to Stage 2 (video interview)
- Decline emails sent to everyone else (respectful, prompt — you're building a reputation)

**Template decline message:** "Thank you for applying to [Company]. After reviewing your application, we're moving forward with other candidates whose backgrounds more closely match our current needs. We wish you the best in your search."

</Slide>
<Slide title="Days 8–10: Video Interviews">

**Schedule 4–5 Stage 2 interviews (45 minutes each):**
- Minutes 0–5: Intro
- Minutes 5–25: Behavioral questions (use your 8-question bank from Lesson 4)
- Minutes 25–45: Live roleplay (you play your ICP prospect)
- Score every candidate on the scorecard immediately after the call — don't wait

**By end of Day 10:**
- 1–2 candidates with scorecard 30+ (out of 40)
- 2–3 candidates declined (send decline emails same day)
- Reference checks initiated for your finalists

</Slide>
<Slide title="Days 11–13: References, Offer, Start Date">

**Day 11:** Call 2 references for each finalist (30 min each). Use your reference check script from Lesson 4. Prioritize: "On a scale of 1–10, would you hire them again?"

**Day 12:** Make the decision. If you have a clear 1st choice: make the offer. If it's close: schedule a 30-minute final interview to clarify one remaining concern.

**Day 12 offer conversation:** "I'm excited to make you an offer. Here's the full package: [base], [variable structure], [OTE], [equity]. Can you give me your decision by Thursday?"

**Day 13:** Confirm signed offer. Schedule Day 1. Send onboarding materials (playbook, ICP document, call recording library) with a note: "Start reading these before Day 1 — it'll make Week 1 more productive."

</Slide>
<Slide title="Day 14: Day 1">

**Your Day 1 agenda (fully prepared by Day 12):**

9:00am — Welcome call (45 min): Company story, why you built this, your vision for the team
10:00am — ICP deep dive (60 min): Walk through the ICP document, quiz them at the end
11:00am — Product walkthrough from customer POV (60 min): Not a sales demo — a customer's-eye view
12:00pm — Lunch break
1:00pm — First call recordings (120 min): Listen to 3 calls together, debrief each one
3:00pm — Tools setup (60 min): CRM, email, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, sequencing tool
4:00pm — Tomorrow's plan (15 min): Their reading assignments for Days 2–3

**End of Day 1 goal:** They know what you do, who you serve, and why customers buy. They've heard your voice in 3 real calls. They're set up in all tools. They're excited for Day 2.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## The Hiring Pipeline: What to Expect

<InsightCard icon="📊" title="Hiring Pipeline Benchmarks">
Plan your funnel before you start. Here's what the typical pipeline looks like for an SDR hire:

- Applicants: 20–50
- Pass resume screen: 8–15 (30–40%)
- Complete async task: 4–8 (50–60% of screened)
- Interview: 3–4 (50% of task completers)
- Reference check: 1–2 (50% of interviewers)
- Offer accepted: 1

This means to hire 1 person, you need to process 20–50 applicants. If you're only getting 10 applications, the JD isn't reaching enough candidates — expand your platform coverage or sponsor the LinkedIn post.
</InsightCard>
