# The Weekly Workflow: How to Run This in 3 Hours/Week

You have learned the tactics: Welcome Sequences, Soap Opera Nurtures, Bridge Offers, Segmentation, and Analytics.
You possess a Ferrari engine.
But if you don't know how to drive it, it will sit in the garage.

The #1 reason founders quit email marketing is **Burnout.**
They try to write "perfect" essays every week. They stare at blank Google Docs. They get behind schedule, miss a week, then two, and then they ghost their list forever.

You need a **Production System.**
A boring, repeatable workflow that allows you to produce high-level content without relying on "inspiration."

In this final lesson, we will build your "Email Factory."

---

## 1. The "Input" Problem: Why You Have Writer's Block

You don't have writer's block. You have **Idea Deficiency.**
It is impossible to cook a meal if your fridge is empty. It is impossible to write an email if your brain is empty.

**The Fix:** Separate "Idea Capture" from "Writing."

<InsightCard icon="🧠" title="The Real Problem">
You don't have writer's block. You have an empty swipe file. It's impossible to write when you have nothing to say.
</InsightCard>

**The "Swipe File" Habit:**
Create a folder (Notion, Apple Notes, Slack) called "Email Ideas."
Every time you:
1.  Have a heated debate with a client.
2.  Fix a bug that was annoying you.
3.  Read a tweet that makes you angry.
4.  Answer a question for the 3rd time in Slack.

...add it to the folder.
*   *Entry:* "Client asked about pricing again. Explained the 'Cost of Inaction' concept. Good topic."

By Friday, you should have 5-10 bullet points. When you sit down to write, you aren't "thinking of ideas." You are choosing one.

## 2. The 3-Hour Weekly Routine

Do not try to write daily. Batch it.
Block out **One Morning** per week (e.g., Tuesday 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM).

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Hour 1: The Ugly Draft (8:00-9:00 AM)">

**Goal:** Get words on paper. No editing allowed.

- **Step 1:** Open your "Swipe File." Pick the idea that excites you most.
- **Step 2:** Set a timer for 45 minutes.
- **Step 3:** Write like you are talking to a friend at a bar.
  - *Prompt:* "I want to tell you about [Topic]. Everyone thinks [Myth], but actually [Truth]. For example, [Story]. So you should [Action]."
- **Status:** At 9:00 AM, the draft is done. It is messy, full of typos, and probably too long. That is perfect.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Hour 2: The Editor's Scalpel (9:00-10:00 AM)">

**Goal:** Narrative structure and clarity.

- **Step 1:** **Wait.** (Take a 10 min walk). You need to reset your brain.
- **Step 2:** Read it aloud. (Seriously). If you stumble over a sentence, delete it.
- **Step 3:** Apply the "Soap Opera" check.
  - Is there a Hook in the first line?
  - Is there an Open Loop?
  - Is there a Super-Signature at the bottom?
- **Step 4:** Write 5 different subject lines. Pick the one that creates the most curiosity.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Hour 3: The Technician (10:00-11:00 AM)">

**Goal:** Scheduling and Operations.

- **Step 1:** Load it into your ESP (ConvertKit/HubSpot).
- **Step 2:** Send a "Test Email" to yourself. Check it on Mobile. (Most people ruin this step).
- **Step 3:** Schedule it for your standard time (e.g., Thursday 9:00 AM).
- **Step 4 (Optional):** Check last week's metrics (Lesson 9) and log them in your dashboard.

**Total Time:** 3 Hours.
**Result:** Your marketing is done for the week.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## 3. The "Emergency Archive" (Repurposing)

What happens if you get sick? Or go on vacation?
You need an "Emergency Archive."

**The Strategy:**
Every 4th week, write **two** emails instead of one.
Schedule one. Save the other in a "Ready to Ship" folder.
Repeat until you have 3-4 evergreen emails banked.

**Repurposing Old Hits:**
If you have written 20+ emails, you are sitting on gold.
Go to your analytics. Find the email with the highest Reply Rate from 6 months ago.
*   Copy it.
*   Change the subject line.
*   Update the intro slightly.
*   **Re-send it.**

*Result:* New subscribers haven't seen it. Old subscribers forgot it. It performs just as well the second time.
*(Note: Do this sparingly. Maybe once a quarter).*

## 4. The "List Cleaning" Ritual (Quarterly)

Once a quarter (Jan, April, July, Oct), you must clean your house.
Having 10,000 cold subscribers hurts your deliverability (Google sees low open rates -> sends you to Spam).

**Step 1: Identify Cold Subscribers**
*   Create a segment: "Has not opened an email in 90 days" AND "Sign up date is > 90 days ago."

**Step 2: The Re-Engagement Campaign**
*   Send the "9-Word Email" (Lesson 5) to this segment.
*   *Subject:* [Name]
*   *Body:* "Are you still interested in [Topic]? - Mike"

**Step 3: The Purge**
*   Wait 7 days.
*   Anyone who did not open or click: **Delete them.**
*   *(Yes, delete them. Unsubscribe them. Get them out).*

**Result:** Your total list size drops (ego hurt). Your Open Rates skyrocket (business win). Your bill goes down (wallet win).

## 5. Dual Context Examples

### Scenario A: B2B SaaS (The CTO)
*   **Swipe File:** Screenshots of bad code, interesting GitHub repos, customer support tickets about "legacy tech."
*   **Routine:** Writes on Sunday night (quiet time).
*   **Workflow:**
    *   *Draft:* Rants about "Technical Debt."
    *   *Edit:* Tones down the anger, adds a helpful framework.
    *   *Tech:* Schedules for Tuesday 10 AM (when devs are checking email).
*   **Repurpose:** Takes the email content and turns it into a LinkedIn thread on Wednesday.

### Scenario B: Creator (The Art Teacher)
*   **Swipe File:** iPhone photos of student art, questions from Instagram DM.
*   **Routine:** Writes on Friday afternoon.
*   **Workflow:**
    *   *Draft:* "Here is why your shading looks muddy."
    *   *Edit:* Adds photos to illustrate the point.
    *   *Tech:* Schedules for Saturday Morning (when people have time to draw).
*   **Repurpose:** Takes the email and records a 60-second Reel for Instagram.
