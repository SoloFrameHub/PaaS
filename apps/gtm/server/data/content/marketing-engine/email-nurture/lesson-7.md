# Launch and Promotion Sequences: The Cash Injection

Throughout this course, we've focused on "Nurture"—building relationships, giving value, and being patient.
But there comes a moment when you need to cash in that social capital.
A moment when you need to shift from "Helpful Educator" to "Commercial Guide."

This is the **Launch.**

Whether you are launching a new product, opening a cohort, or running a Black Friday promo, the rules of the game change during a Launch.
*   **Tempo** increases (Daily emails).
*   **Tone** changes (More direct).
*   **Urgency** becomes the primary driver.

In this lesson, we will break down the Psychology of a Launch and give you the exact **7-Day "Cash Injection" Sequence** that generates 80% of a creator's annual revenue.

---

## 1. The Psychology of Launch: Why People Buy

Why do people who ignored you for 6 months suddenly buy on Friday at 11:00 PM?
Two words: **Scarcity** and **Urgency.**

### The Law of Inertia
Your customers are busy. They might *want* your product, but they will postpone buying it until "tomorrow" forever.
A Launch forces them to decide *today*.

**The "Deadline" is the Product.**
Without a deadline, you are just making a suggestion. With a deadline, you are forcing a choice.

### Ethical Scarcity vs. Fake Scarcity
*   **Fake Scarcity:** "This PDF won't be available tomorrow!" (When it clearly will be). This destroys trust.
*   **Real Scarcity:**
    *   **Price:** "The price goes up by $100 on Friday."
    *   **Quantity:** "Only 20 spots in the cohort."
    *   **Bonus:** "The 'Implementation Call' bonus disappears on Friday."

You must have **Real Scarcity** for a launch to work.

## 2. The 7-Day Launch Sequence (The "Product Launch Formula" Lite)

This sequence is battle-tested. It follows a narrative arc from "Curiosity" to "Logic" to "Fear of Missing Out."

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Phase 1: The Warm-Up (Days 1-2)">

**Goal:** Build anticipation. Do NOT sell yet.

**Day 1: The Announcement ("The Whisper")**
*   **Subject:** "Something big is coming..."
*   **Script:** "I've been working on something for 6 months. It solves [Big Problem]. I'm going to open the doors on Wednesday. Stay tuned."
*   **Purpose:** Open a "Mental Loop."

**Day 2: The Problem ("Agitation")**
*   **Subject:** "Why most people fail at [Topic]..."
*   **Script:** Discuss the "Villain" (The old way). *"Most people try to solve X by doing Y. It fails because of Z. Tomorrow, I'll show you the new way."*

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 2: The Open Cart (Day 3)">

**Goal:** Make the Offer.

**Day 3: The Launch ("Cart Open")**
*   **Subject:** "We are live! (The [Product] is here)"
*   **Script:**
    *   Announce it's open.
    *   Explain the Benefit (The Transformation).
    *   Explain the Offer (What they get).
    *   **State the Scarcity:** "This price/bonus ends Friday at Midnight."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 3: The Logic & Proof (Days 4-5)">

**Goal:** Rationalize the decision.

**Day 4: Social Proof ("The Case Study")**
*   **Subject:** "How Jane hit $10k in 30 days..."
*   **Script:** "You might be wondering if this works for *you*. Meet Jane. She was [Before State]. She used this system. Now she is [After State]."

**Day 5: The Logic ("FAQ")**
*   **Subject:** "Is this right for you?"
*   **Script:** "I've been getting a lot of questions." (Answer the top 3 objections: Time, Money, Difficulty).
*   **The Pivot:** Turn objections into reasons to buy. *"I don't have time" -> "That's exactly why you need this; it saves time."*

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 4: The Close (Days 6-7)">

**Goal:** Pure Urgency.

**Day 6: The Warning ("24 Hours Left")**
*   **Subject:** "Closing tomorrow."
*   **Script:** Short and sweet. "Just a reminder that the $[Amount] discount ends in 24 hours."

**Day 7: The Double Tap (The Final Day)**
*   **Email A (9:00 AM):** "Last Day."
*   **Email B (6:00 PM):** "6 Hours Left."
*   **Email C (10:00 PM):** "Final Call." (Optional but highly effective).
*   *Note:* You will get 50% of your sales on the final day. Do not skip this.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

<InsightCard icon="⚡" title="The Final Day Phenomenon">
Most founders underestimate Day 7. You will get 50% of your total launch revenue in the final 24 hours—and 30% in the final 4 hours. The multiple-email strategy on the last day isn't spam; it's meeting buyers where their psychology actually works.
</InsightCard>

## 3. Dealing with "Launch Fatigue" (Unsubscribes)

During a launch, you will send ~8 emails in 7 days.
You usually send 1 email a week.
**Your unsubscribe rate will triple.**

**This is Good.**
*   People who unsubscribe during a launch were never going to buy. They are "Freebie Seekers."
*   Let them go. You are pruning the tree so the fruit can grow.
*   *Warrior Mindset:* You are not spamming. You are offering a solution to a painful problem. If they don't have the problem, they should leave.

**The "Opt-Out" Link:**
To be kind, add a link at the top of your emails:
*"Click here if you don't want to hear about this Launch anymore, but want to stay on the newsletter."*
(Use your ESP's automation to tag them and exclude them from the rest of the sequence).

## 4. Dual Context Examples

### Scenario A: B2B SaaS (The "Black Friday" Deal)
*   **The Scarcity:** "Get 2 Months Free + Concierge Onboarding if you upgrade to Annual Plan by Friday."
*   **Day 1:** Tease the feature roadmap for next year.
*   **Day 3:** Open the deal.
*   **Day 5 (FAQ):** "Will this work with my current Tech Stack?"
*   **Day 7:** "The onboarding slots are almost full."

### Scenario B: Creator/Coach (The "Cohort" Launch)
*   **The Scarcity:** "Class starts Monday. We cap at 50 students."
*   **Day 2:** Talk about the pain of learning alone vs. in a group.
*   **Day 4:** Show screenshots of the Slack community winning.
*   **Day 7:** "We have 4 spots left." (Only say this if true).

---

## 5. Post-Launch: The "Hangover" Cure

On Day 8, silence.
Do not email them for at least 3-4 days. Let the list breathe.
Then, return to your regular scheduled programming (Newsletter) as if nothing happened.
**Do not sell anything for at least 2 weeks.**

**The "Downsell" (Advanced):**
For people who clicked but didn't buy (High Intent), wait 1 week, then offer a "Lite" version.
*"I saw you checked out the Course but didn't join. Was it the price? I have a self-paced PDF version for $50."*
