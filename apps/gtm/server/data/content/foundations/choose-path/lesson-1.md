# The Four Founder Contexts: Choosing Your Battlefield

You've spent the last few weeks learning to define your ICP (Course 1) and craft your positioning (Course 2).
Now comes the question that paralyzes most founders: *Which acquisition tactics should I actually use?*

The advice you'll find online is a conflicting mess.
*   One guru swears by cold email.
*   Another insists "Content is King."
*   A third says referrals and partnerships are the only sustainable path.

They are all right—for *their* context. And they are all potentially dead wrong for *yours*.

<FlipCard front="The Context Paradox" back="Every tactic works brilliantly in the right context — and fails spectacularly in the wrong one. The tactic is never the problem; the mismatch is." />

The strategy that builds a $20k/mo newsletter business will destroy a B2B SaaS startup. The cold outbound motion that scales an enterprise software company will feel like "spam" if you are a life coach.

I learned this the hard way. Early in building SoloFrameHub, I tried to do everything: cold email, daily LinkedIn content, networking events, and SEO. The result? Scattered effort, scattered results, and near burnout.

The breakthrough came when I accepted a counterintuitive truth: **Focus beats comprehensiveness.** Solo founders who try to master every channel master none. The founders who win pick the **one** channel that matches their context and go tragically deep.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Core Principle">
Focus beats comprehensiveness. Solo founders who try to master every channel master none. Pick the ONE channel that matches your context and go tragically deep.
</InsightCard>

In this course, we stop pretending there is a "Universal Playbook" and instead give you the specific playbook for **your** business model.

## 1. Why "Best Practices" Fail

Here is the dirty secret of business advice: **Context is everything.**

Imagine a founder building a connection tool for therapists.
*   **Advice A:** "Send 100 cold DMs a day!" (Result: Therapists feel invaded and block her).
*   **Advice B:** "Run Facebook Ads!" (Result: Therapists don't click ads for professional tools).
*   **The Truth:** Therapists trust other therapists. The winning channel is **Referral Partnerships** with supervision groups.

Now imagine a founder selling high-frequency trading software to hedge funds.
*   **Advice A:** "Post on TikTok!" (Result: Laughable failure).
*   **The Truth:** Hedge fund managers don't scroll TikTok for software. They respond to hyper-targeted, data-backed **Cold Email**.

If you use the Creator playbook for Enterprise Sales, you fail. If you use the Enterprise playbook for Creator Sales, you fail.

## 2. The Four Contexts Defined

After working with hundreds of founders, I’ve identified four distinct "Gravity Wells." You likely fall into one of these.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Context 1: Zero-to-Ten Sprint (Validation)">

**Who:** You have fewer than 10 paying customers. You are pre-product market fit.

**The Reality:** This isn’t a business model; it’s a *phase*. Every founder starts here.

**The Goal:** Buying customers, not "users." You need validation, not scale.

**The Trap:** Building scalable systems (SEO, Ads) before you know who buys.

**The Winning Tactic:** **Manual Outbound & Network Hustle.** You do things that don’t scale until they break.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Context 2: B2B SaaS (The Engine)">

**Who:** You sell software to businesses.

**Deal Size:** $50/mo to $50k/year.

**The Reality:** Even if you talk to one person, you are selling to a "Committee" (User + Buyer + IT).

**The Goal:** Predictable pipeline.

**The Winning Tactic:** **Cold Email & LinkedIn Sales Navigator.** You need volume to find the "Active Buyers."

</Slide>
<Slide title="Context 3: Creator/Coach (The Brand)">

**Who:** You sell expertise, transformation, or courses.

**Deal Size:** $500 to $10k.

**The Reality:** People buy *you*. Trust in your personal brand is the primary driver of conversion.

**The Goal:** Audience depth and "Fan" conversion.

**The Winning Tactic:** **Content & Community.** You "Attract" rather than "Hunt." Note: Cold outreach feels invasive here unless it’s very warm.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Context 4: Service Business (The Agency)">

**Who:** You sell done-for-you services or high-end consulting.

**Deal Size:** $5k to $100k+.

**The Reality:** Capacity is your constraint. You don’t need 1,000 leads; you need 5 great clients.

**The Goal:** High-trust relationships.

**The Winning Tactic:** **Referrals & Strategic Partnerships.** One partner can feed you for a year.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## 3. The Universal Psychology

Here's what stays constant across all four contexts: **You are always selling to a human.**

*   The VP of Engineering buying your SaaS is worried about looking incompetent to their boss (Status Fear).
*   The Aspiring Entrepreneur buying your coaching is worried about wasting money on another "scam" (Trust Fear).
*   The CEO hiring your agency is worried about you ghosting them (Reliability Fear).

**DISC Theory Applies Everywhere:**
*   A **High D** buyer is impatient, whether they are buying a $50k server or a $50 fitness plan.
*   A **High C** buyer wants data and proof, regardless of the product.

The *channel* changes. The *psychology* remains universal.

## 4. Identifying Your Battlefield (The Hybrid Trap)

Most founders know their context immediately. However, some fall into the "Hybrid Trap."

> *"I'm building a SaaS tool, but I also do consulting to pay the bills, and I want to launch a course next year."*

**Stop.**

You cannot fight a war on three fronts. You must pick a **Primary Context**.
*   If you want to build a product company, SaaS is your primary. Consulting is just a funding mechanism. Treat it as secondary.
*   If you want to be a thought leader, the Content/Creator path is primary. The software is just a monetization tool.

**The Test:**
1.  **Where does 80% of your revenue come from today?**
2.  **Where do you *want* 80% of your revenue to come from in 3 years?**
3.  **Are you selling to individuals or logos?**

If you are &lt;10 customers, your context is **Zero-to-Ten**. Ignore everything else until you graduate.
