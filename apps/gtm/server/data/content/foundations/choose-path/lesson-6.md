## Your Personal Academy Roadmap

Congratulations. You've identified your context and explored your specific tactical playground. Now comes the most important decision: **What will you actually do for the next 90 days?**

The Solo Frame Hub Academy is designed to be a "Choose Your Own Adventure" system. You shouldn't complete courses in order from 1 to 33. That's a recipe for burnout and information overload. Instead, you need a **Personal Roadmap**—a curated path through the curriculum that solves your *immediate* acquisition bottlenecks.

### The Theory of Constraints for Founders

In any system, there is one bottleneck that limits your growth. For a solo founder:

1. If you have no one to talk to, your bottleneck is **Prospecting/Visibility**.
2. If you're talking to people but no one is buying, your bottleneck is **Offer/Positioning**.
3. If people are buying but you're losing money or burnt out, your bottleneck is **Pricing/Systems**.

Identifying your bottleneck tells you exactly which course to take next.

### Creating Your 90-Day Schedule

Most courses in the academy take 2-4 hours to complete "deeply" (including exercises). My recommendation: **One course every two weeks.**

This gives you 10 days to *implement* what you learned before starting the next course.

**Example Schedule:**
- **Weeks 1-2:** Course A (Learn + First Exercise)
- **Weeks 3-4:** Course B (Learn + First Exercise)
- **Weeks 5-6:** Implementation Intensive (Go deep on A and B results)
- **Weeks 7-8:** Course C (Learn + First Exercise)
- **Weeks 9-10:** Course D (Learn + First Exercise)
- **Weeks 11-12:** Quarter Review & Roadmap Adjustment

### The "Sunk Cost" Trap

As you progress through your roadmap, you might realize your context has changed. Maybe you started building a SaaS (Context 2) but discovered your audience really wants coaching (Context 3).

**Don't keep taking SaaS courses out of habit.**
Stop. Update your roadmap. Your roadmap is a living document, not a contract. The moment a course no longer solves your primary bottleneck, archive it and move to what matters now.

## Common Roadmap Mistakes

### Mistake #1: The "Completionist" Trap
**The Trap:** Trying to finish every course in order (1 to 33).
**The Reality:** You will burn out before you make a sale.
**The Fix:** **Just-in-Time Learning.** Only learn what you need to solve *today's* bottleneck.

### Mistake #2: The "Platform Hopping" Trap
**The Trap:** Trying to do LinkedIn + Twitter + YouTube + Cold Email all at once.
**The Reality:** You do all of them poorly.
**The Fix:** **The Rule of One.** One Avatar, One Channel, One Offer. Master it before adding a second.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring the Prerequisites
**The Trap:** Jumping straight to "Cold Email templates" (Course 8) without doing Positioning (Course 2).
**The Reality:** You send 1,000 emails and get 0 replies because your message sucks.
**The Fix:** **Foundations First.** Never skip Courses 0, 1, and 2. They make everything else work.

### Mistake #4: The "Optimization" Trap
**The Trap:** "I need to optimize my CRM before I start selling."
**The Reality:** You can run a million-dollar business on a spreadsheet.
**The Fix:** **Revenue First, Systems Second.** Only build systems when the manual work becomes unbearable.

## Your First 90 Days: The Commitment

This is not a university degree. You don't get a diploma for watching videos. You get a business for taking action.

**The "Do/Learn" Ratio:**
For every 1 hour you spend learning (watching lessons), you must spend 4 hours doing (executing the exercises).

*   Did you watch the Cold Email lesson (1 hour)?
*   Then spend 4 hours writing and sending emails.

If your ratio is off, you are **procrastilearning**.
