# Account Tiering: The Sniper vs. The Shotgun

Let's talk about "The Peanut Butter Mistake."

Most founders approach sales like they are making a sandwich. They have a limited amount of time and energy (the peanut butter), and they have a massive list of prospects (the bread). What do they do? They spread their effort as thin and as evenly as possible. They treat the CEO of a $100M company exactly the same way they treat the owner of a 2-person design shop.

**This is a fatal error.**

<InsightCard icon="💡" title="Stop Spreading Thin">
When you spread your effort thin, you lose the ability to cut through the noise of high-value prospects, and you waste precious energy on prospects who will never pay you more than a pittance. The solution is **Account Tiering** — a Resource Allocation Protocol that ensures you spend your "manual gold" on Tier 1 and your "automated silver" on Tier 3.
</InsightCard>

---

## 1. The Sniper, The Rifle, and The Shotgun

To master tiering, you must internalize the three primary modes of acquisition. Each has a different cost, a different speed, and a different expected outcome.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Tier 1: The Sniper">

### "Must-Win" Accounts

*   **The Target:** These are your "Dream 100." They are a perfect fit, have the budget to pay your highest rates, and their name alone would provide massive social proof.
*   **The Effort:** 100% Manual. No templates. No automation.
*   **The Investment:** You spend 15-30 minutes per account *before* sending a single word. You are looking for "The Deep Hook" — something they said in a podcast 20 minutes in, or a specific engineering challenge mentioned in a technical blog post.
*   **Goal:** A high-quality conversation.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Tier 2: The Rifle">

### "Core Performance" Accounts

*   **The Target:** Great fits that align with your ICP but might have a lower Lifetime Value (LTV) than Tier 1, or they are slightly less urgent.
*   **The Effort:** The Hybrid Approach.
*   **The Investment:** You use a polished template for the body of the message, but you **hand-write the first sentence** (the "personalized hook"). You spend 3-5 minutes per account.
*   **Goal:** Volume with a human touch.

</Slide>
<Slide title="Tier 3: The Shotgun">

### "Efficiency & Volume" Accounts

*   **The Target:** These are the bulk of your market. They are "good enough" to sell to, but if you spend even 5 minutes researching them, you are losing money.
*   **The Effort:** 100% Automated.
*   **The Investment:** You rely on data-driven personalization (e.g., using their industry or tech stack in the template).
*   **Goal:** Maximum coverage for the lowest possible energy.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## 2. The 80/20 of Sales Energy

<InsightCard icon="⏱️" title="The Sales Calorie Budget">

If you are a solo founder with 10 hours a week for sales, your "Sales Calories" should be consumed like this:
*   **5 Hours (50%):** Tier 1 (Sniper). You are deep-diving into 5-10 accounts.
*   **3 Hours (30%):** Tier 2 (Rifle). You are processing 20-30 accounts with hybrid touches.
*   **2 Hours (20%):** Tier 3 (Shotgun). You are setting up and monitoring automated sequences for 100+ accounts.

</InsightCard>

If you reverse this — if you spend 8 hours on automation and 2 hours on research — you will feel "busy" because you see lots of digits in your dashboard, but you will find that your pipeline is filled with "low-quality" deals that eat your time but don't pay the bills.

## 3. The Advanced Scoring Matrix

Do not tier your accounts based on "vibe." Use a scoring system to remove emotional bias. Assign 0-3 points for each of the following:

| Criteria | 0 Points | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Potential** | &lt; $1k/mo | $1k-$5k/mo | $5k-$15k/mo | $20k+/mo |
| **Tech Alignment** | No info | Generic Tech | Needs your specific niche | They are struggling with their current stack |
| **Growth Signal** | Flat/Legacy | Hiring 1-2 people | Recent Funding | Expansion into your specific territory |
| **Influence Potential** | Unknown | Local player | Known in the niche | Industry Icon (Thought Leader) |

<FlipCard front="How do you use the scoring matrix?" back="Score 10-12 = Tier 1 (Manual Research). Score 6-9 = Tier 2 (Hybrid sequence). Score 1-5 = Tier 3 (Volume bucket). Score 0 = Disqualify entirely. Remove gut feelings — let the data decide your allocation." frontIcon="📊" />

**The Decision Protocol:**
*   **Score 10-12:** **Tier 1.** Move to your "Manual Research" folder.
*   **Score 6-9:** **Tier 2.** Move to your "Hybrid" sequence.
*   **Score 1-5:** **Tier 3.** Add to the "Volume" bucket.
*   **Score 0:** **Disqualify.** Save your soul; do not market to these people.

<PersonalizedExample generic="For [industry], your Tier 1 accounts might be companies actively using [terminology] with [buyer role] who has budget authority. Tier 2 could be similar companies but smaller or in adjacent verticals.">

Your Tier 1 accounts are companies with the highest pain + accessibility + right-to-win scores. Tier 2 are solid fits but missing one dimension.

</PersonalizedExample>

## 4. The "Tier Zero" Strategy: The Multi-Touch Dossier

For your Top 3 prospects (the absolute giants), you don't just send an email. You build a **Dossier**.
*   **What it is:** A 1-page document summarizing why they specifically need you *today*.
*   **The Content:** "3 Gaps I found in your current [Marketing/Software/Operations] and exactly how I'd fix them."
*   **The Delivery:** This might be a physical gift, a custom Video (Loom), or a message sent to their right-hand person (the "Executive Assistant Strategy").

### 6. The 'Anti-Tiering' Trap: Avoid the Busy-Work Loop

<InsightCard icon="⚠️" title="Research Procrastination Is Still Procrastination">

There is a specific trap that solo founders fall into: **Research Procrastination.**
*   **The Symptom:** You spend 3 hours researching one Tier 1 account, finding out the CEO's favorite dog breed and the color of their first car.
*   **The Reality:** This is just a sophisticated form of procrastination.
*   **The Rule:** Research should take no more than 20-30 minutes for even your best accounts. If you can't find a "Hook" in 20 minutes, they aren't Tier 1 — or you're looking for the wrong things. Stick to business-relevant signals (hiring, funding, tech, pains).

</InsightCard>

### 7. The Tier 1 'Dossier' Example: What a Win Looks Like

<ExampleCard label="Case Study: The Tier 1 Dossier in Action">

What does a "Dossier" actually contain? Imagine a single PDF or a well-structured email with these three headers:

<StepCard number={1} title="The Context">
*"I see you are launching [Product X] to compete with [Competitor Y]. Usually, this means [Specific Challenge Z] is top of mind."*
</StepCard>

<StepCard number={2} title="The Proof">
*"I recently helped [Similar Company] solve [Challenge Z] by [Your specific methodology]."* (Attach a 1-page case study).
</StepCard>

<StepCard number={3} title="The Low-Friction Offer">
*"I've drafted a 3-step technical roadmap for how you could apply this to [Product X]. Would you like me to send that over?"*
</StepCard>

This isn't a "Sales Pitch"; it's a **Value Delivery**. When you treat Tier 1 accounts like this, your reply rates will often hit 30%+, compared to the 1-2% from Tier 3 automation.

</ExampleCard>

## 8. Dual Context Strategy

<ExampleCard label="Case Study: B2B SaaS — Expansion Tiering">

*   **Strategy:** Tier based on **Modern Tech Stacks**. If a company just switched from a legacy tool to a modern one (e.g., Zendesk to Intercom), they are in a "Migration Period." This is a high-intent Tier 1 signal for software consultants.
*   **Tool:** Use **BuiltWith** or **StoreLead** to identify these transitions.

</ExampleCard>

<ExampleCard label="Case Study: Creator/Coach — Relationship Tiering">

*   **Strategy:** Tier based on **Audience Overlap**. If a creator has a massive audience but low engagement, or if they just launched a product that failed, they are Tier 1 for your coaching or agency services.
*   **Signal:** Look for "Tired" founders who are posting about "rethinking their direction." This is a Tier 1 psychological trigger.

</ExampleCard>
