# LinkedIn Sales Navigator Mastery: Pro-Level Prospecting

Let’s talk about the "Golden Filter."

Imagine you are standing on a beach, and someone tells you there is a single gold coin buried somewhere in the sand. If you start digging with your hands, you might find it in three years. If you use a generic metal detector, you might find it in three months. But if you have a satellite-guided ground-penetrating radar that can see exactly where 24-karat gold is located, you’ll find it in three minutes.

For the solo founder, **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** is that radar.

Most people use LinkedIn as a social network (the beach). Amateurs use the basic "Search" bar (the hands). Professionals use Sales Navigator to filter out the 900 million "non-buyers" and zero in on the exact 500 people who have the problem, the budget, and the intent to buy *today*.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Precision Mindset">
Sales Navigator isn't a social network tool — it's a revenue engine. Your goal is to find the search string that gives you between 50 and 200 "High Signal" results. More is too broad (spammy). Less is too narrow (starving).
</InsightCard>

In this lesson, we are moving past basic clicks. We will master the advanced filters, "Spotlight" triggers, and Boolean logic that transform LinkedIn from a social feed into a high-precision revenue engine.

## 1. Tier Selection: Why "Core" is Your Sweet Spot

LinkedIn is a master of the upsell. They will try to convince you that you need "Advanced" or "Advanced Plus" (costing significantly more). **Ignore them.**

For 99.9% of solo founders, the **Sales Navigator Core ($99.99/mo)** tier is exactly what you need. 

*   **Advanced Lead Filters:** 30+ ways to find people.
*   **50 InMail Messages/Month:** More than enough for a high-touch, quality-first strategy.
*   **Saved Searches:** The engine of your weekly prospecting.
*   **Custom Lists:** Essential for organizing your Tier 1 (Whales) and Tier 2 (Core) accounts.
*   **Smart Links:** A "Hidden" feature that lets you track exactly who clicks your links and how long they spend reading your PDFs.

The higher tiers are designed for large sales teams that need to sync data with enterprise CRMs like Salesforce or manage seat sharing. As a solo founder, the Core features provide 100% of the extraction power you need to generate millions in revenue.

## 2. Boolean Search: The Language of Precision

Boolean search allows you to combine or exclude keywords to find "The Needle in the Haystack." If you aren't using Boolean, you are wasting 50% of your time looking at irrelevant leads.

### The Basic Operators
*   **AND:** Narrows your focus. `Founder AND SaaS` finds people with BOTH words.
*   **OR:** Broadens your reach. `SaaS OR Software` finds people with EITHER word. Use this for different ways to say the same thing.
*   **NOT:** The "Noise Filter." `Founder NOT "Co-Founder"` removes the latter from your view.
*   **Quotes:** Finds exact phrases. `"VP of Marketing"` is better than `VP of Marketing` (which might find a 'VP' of 'Sales' who worked in 'Marketing' 10 years ago).
*   **Parentheses:** Groups complex logic. `(VP OR Director) AND (Marketing OR Growth)`.

### Advanced "Recipe" Examples
**B2B SaaS Context (The Technical Buyer):**
`("VP of Engineering" OR "Head of Infrastructure") AND (SaaS OR "Cloud Services") AND (Python OR AWS) NOT Recruit`
*This finds high-level technical decision-makers at software companies who use your specific tech stack, while filtering out recruiters who might clog your results.*

**Creator/Coach Context (The High-Ticket Student):**
`(Founder OR Author OR Consultant) AND (Business OR "High Performance") AND ("Mastermind" OR "Course") NOT "Junior"`
*This targets established experts who are already comfortable with educational investments and have the budget to hire a premium coach.*

## 3. The "Spotlight" Filters: Your High-Intent Goldmine

Most people skip the "Spotlight" tab. For a solo founder with limited time, this is where you should spend **80% of your effort.**

<Slide title="Changed Jobs in Past 90 Days (The New Sheriff)">

This is the single most powerful filter in B2B sales. New executives are **3x to 5x more likely** to buy a new product or service in their first 90 days.

- **The Mandate:** They were hired to "Fix things."
- **The Budget:** They often negotiated a "slush fund" for new tools during their hiring.
- **The Tabula Rasa:** They haven't built loyalties to the existing (old) vendors yet.

This isn't a pitch; it's a peer-to-peer acknowledgement.

## 4. The Account-Based Pivot (ABM for Solo Founders)

Amateur founders search for **People**. Professional founders search for **Accounts (Companies)** first, then find the people within them. This is called **Account-Based Marketing (ABM).**

### Step 1: Filter by Account
Find companies that match your "Golden Company" criteria:
*   Industry: "Software Development."
*   Headcount Growth: "20%+" (Signals they are profitable and expanding).
*   Tech Stack: "Uses HubSpot" (If you sell a HubSpot service).

### Step 2: "View Employees"
Once you find a "Golden Account," open the company page and filter the employees for your specific decision-makers. This ensures you aren't just finding a "Marketing Manager" in a vacuum, but one who works at a company you *know* is a perfect fit.

## 5. Seniority Nuance: Decision Makers vs. Titles

In large enterprises, "VP" is the buyer. In small startups, a "Manager" might have more power than a "VP" at a dinosaur company.

*   **The Flat Organization Trap:** If you search only for "Chief [X] Officer," you will miss thousands of high-growth founders who use the title "Operator" or "Lead."
*   **The Years of Experience Filter:** Instead of just filtering by "Title," use **Years of Experience (10+)** combined with a keyword. This often finds senior, authoritative thinkers who haven't updated their job title in years.

6. **The 'InMail' Etiquette: Avoiding the 'Connection-Pitch' Trap**

One of the biggest mistakes founders make on LinkedIn is the "Pitch-Slap"—sending a connection request and immediately following it with a 5-paragraph sales pitch. This is the fastest way to get blocked.

*   **The Connection Phase:** Your connection request should be 100% focused on **Context**. *"I saw your post on X and wanted to follow your work here."* No pitch. 
*   **The Nurture Phase:** Engage with 2-3 of their posts over the next week. Like, comment with a thoughtful insight.
*   **The InMail Phase:** When you finally send an InMail, it should feel like a continuation of a conversation, not a cold start. *"We were chatting briefly on your post about [Topic]—I had a thought about how that relates to [Outcome]..."*

By slowing down the process, you increase your reply rates significantly. In the world of high-ticket sales, **Slow is Smooth, and Smooth is Fast.**

## 7. The "Saved Search" System: Automating Your Pipeline

Don't manually search every day. Use the **Saved Search** feature to create an "Evergreen Alert."

1.  Craft your "Perfect Lead" search string.
2.  Save the search.
3.  Set "Email Frequency" to **Daily** or **Weekly**.
4.  **The Result:** Every Monday morning, Sales Navigator will email you a list of every new person who just entered your target ICP (e.g., "New VPs of Sales at YC companies"). You are now prospecting while you sleep.

## 7. Dual Context Strategy

### B2B SaaS Founder: The "Tech Stack" Hunter
*   **The Filter:** Use the "Technologies Used" filter (under Accounts) to find companies using your competitor's software.
*   **The Play:** Outreach to their Head of Product with a "Negative Contrast" angle (Lesson 6 of Course 8) focused on the specific weakness of that competitor.

### Creator/Coach Founder: The "Groups & Keywords" Hunter
*   **The Filter:** Use the "Groups" filter to find people who belong to high-ticket industry associations.
*   **The Play:** Outreach to members of a "Mastermind" group. They have already proven they are willing to pay for expertise; now you just have to prove your expertise is the right fit.
