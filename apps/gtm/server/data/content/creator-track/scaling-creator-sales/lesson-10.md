# Your Scaling Playbook

You have spent nine lessons learning the components of scaling: the ceiling, the setter/closer model, hiring, training, group programs, authenticity, compensation, and systems. Now it is time to assemble these pieces into a cohesive plan that fits your specific situation.

This lesson gives you a 12-month scaling roadmap, clear revenue milestones with corresponding actions, a framework for deciding when to hire versus automate, and a diagnostic tool -- the Scale Readiness Audit -- that you can use today to determine your exact next step.

---

## The 12-Month Scaling Roadmap

Scaling is not a single event. It is a sequence of phases, each building on the previous one. Trying to skip phases is the most common reason creator businesses stall or collapse during growth.

<InsightCard icon="⚠️" title="The Phase-Skipping Trap">
The most common scaling failure? Hiring a closer at $15K/month when your offer isn't validated, or launching a group program at $25K/month when you don't have consistent lead flow. Each phase must be solid before moving to the next.
</InsightCard>

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)">

**Objective**: Document what works and prepare the infrastructure.

**Key actions:**
- Record every sales call for the next 90 days. Build your training library (Lesson 4).
- Write your Sales Playbook: qualification criteria, objection responses, offer presentation, follow-up sequences. This does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist.
- Set up your CRM with proper pipeline stages (Lesson 9). Migrate existing leads from spreadsheets, sticky notes, and memory into the system.
- Define your Ideal Client Profile with specific qualification and disqualification criteria.
- Calculate your current metrics: close rate, average deal value, refund rate, lead sources, and revenue per hour of selling time.

**Milestone**: By end of Month 3, you should have a documented, measurable sales process and at minimum 15 recorded calls in your training library.

**Common mistake**: Spending all three months perfecting the playbook instead of starting to hire. The playbook is a living document -- it will improve with use. Version 1 is good enough.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 2: First Hire (Months 4-6)">

**Objective**: Bring on your first setter and begin reclaiming your time.

**Key actions:**
- Hire a setter using the sources and process from Lesson 3. Start with a commission-per-booking model to minimize risk.
- Run the setter through a 2-week training and shadowing period.
- Implement the daily standup (even with just two people -- you and the setter).
- Track speed-to-lead as your primary metric. Your goal: first contact within 5 minutes of a new lead entering the system.
- Begin designing your group program curriculum (Lesson 6) while your setter handles lead qualification.

**Milestone**: By end of Month 6, your setter should be booking 10-15 qualified calls per week, and your speed-to-lead should be under 5 minutes during business hours.

**Revenue target**: Maintain or slightly increase current revenue ($30-50K/month) while reducing your sales-related time commitment by 30-40%.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 3: Closer Integration (Months 7-9)">

**Objective**: Bring on your first closer and begin removing yourself from sales calls.

**Key actions:**
- Hire a closer using the four-stage process (Lesson 3).
- Run the full 90-day onboarding: immersion, shadowing, assisted closing, independent with review.
- Implement call recording and the review scorecard (Lesson 9).
- Set up compensation with base + commission and quality bonuses (Lesson 8).
- Launch your group program pilot with 5-10 beta members while the closer ramps up.

**Milestone**: By end of Month 9, your closer should be handling 70-80% of sales calls at a close rate within 80% of your personal rate. Your group program pilot should have generated actionable feedback.

**Revenue target**: $50-80K/month, with the closer responsible for 50-60% of closed revenue.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Phase 4: Scale and Optimize (Months 10-12)">

**Objective**: Remove yourself from routine sales, launch the group program fully, and build the machine.

**Key actions:**
- Transition to taking only VIP, high-value, or exception sales calls. Your closer handles the rest.
- Launch the full group program using the Hybrid Bridge approach (Lesson 5).
- Implement the complete performance dashboard (Lesson 9) and run weekly pipeline reviews.
- Conduct the first Authenticity Audit (Lesson 7) and adjust your visible founder practices.
- Evaluate whether a second setter or closer is needed based on lead volume.

**Milestone**: By end of Month 12, your sales operation runs with less than 5 hours per week of your time (call review, coaching, and strategy). Group program is live and enrolling.

**Revenue target**: $80-150K/month, with potential for continued growth without your direct involvement in day-to-day sales.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Revenue Milestones and Corresponding Actions

Not everyone starts at the same place. Here is a milestone-based action guide so you can find your entry point:

| Monthly Revenue | Your Priority | Sales Team Status | Key Action |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| $10-20K | Perfect your offer and close rate | Solo | Record calls, document process, prepare for setter hire |
| $20-30K | Speed to lead and qualification | Hire setter | Setter handles qualification, you close all calls |
| $30-50K | Begin removing yourself from calls | Setter + first closer | Closer in training, handling 30-50% of calls |
| $50-80K | Launch group model | Setter + closer running | Group program pilot, closer handles 70%+ of calls |
| $80-120K | Optimize and expand | Full sales team operating | Second setter or closer, full group program live |
| $120K+ | Scale the machine | Multiple closers possible | Team management, content focus, product expansion |

## When to Hire vs. Automate

Not every problem requires a human. Some scaling constraints are better solved with technology. Here is a decision framework:

### Hire a Human When:

- The task requires judgment, empathy, or real-time adaptation (closing calls, handling complex objections, coaching)
- The quality of the interaction directly impacts revenue or retention (qualification conversations, onboarding)
- The task varies significantly from case to case (custom proposals, high-value negotiations)
- Speed of response is critical and the interaction is conversational (live chat, DM selling)

### Automate When:

- The task is repetitive and follows a predictable pattern (follow-up email sequences, appointment reminders)
- Consistency matters more than personalization (CRM updates, pipeline stage changes, billing)
- The task is time-sensitive but does not require human judgment (speed-to-lead notifications, no-show texts)
- Data collection and reporting (dashboard updates, metric tracking, lead source attribution)

### The Hybrid Approach

Many tasks benefit from automation-assisted humans:

- **Setter + automated follow-up**: The setter makes the initial qualification call. If the prospect does not book, automated texts and emails follow up at defined intervals, and the setter re-engages personally after 3-5 days.
- **Closer + automated proposals**: The closer presents the offer verbally. After the call, an automated system sends a summary, payment link, and social proof follow-up -- all triggered without manual effort.
- **Community + automated onboarding**: New group members receive an automated welcome sequence (access instructions, community tour, first assignment) while you send the personal video message.

The rule: automate the logistics, humanize the moments that matter.

### Scoring Guide

**80-100 points**: You are ready to scale now. Start Phase 2 (first hire) this month.

**60-79 points**: You are close. Identify the categories where you scored lowest and spend 30-60 days strengthening those areas before hiring.

**40-59 points**: You have meaningful gaps. Focus on Phase 1 (foundation) for the next 90 days. Document your process, record your calls, and stabilize your lead flow before adding team complexity.

**Below 40 points**: Scaling is premature. Your priority is product-market fit, offer validation, and building consistent revenue. The earlier courses in this track address these fundamentals.

## Building Your Personal Roadmap

With your audit score and the 12-month roadmap as guides, build a concrete plan:

**Step 1**: Identify your current phase based on revenue and audit score.

**Step 2**: List the 3-5 highest-priority actions for the next 30 days.

**Step 3**: Define the milestone that marks completion of your current phase.

**Step 4**: Set a calendar date to re-take the Scale Readiness Audit (recommended: every 90 days).

**Step 5**: Share your plan with an accountability partner, mentor, or community. Plans that live in private documents have a lower execution rate than plans with social commitment.

## The Long View

Scaling is not a destination. It is a capability. The systems, team, and processes you build in the next 12 months are not the final version of your business. They are the platform from which the next evolution launches.

<FlipCard 
  front="What is the real purpose of scaling?" 
  back="Not to get big — to get free. Free from the income ceiling, free from the time treadmill, and free to direct your creative energy toward whatever you choose next." 
/>

Some creators use this platform to reach $500K and stay there comfortably, working 25 hours a week with a small team. Others use it to push to $1M+ and build a company that operates independently of their daily involvement. Others use the financial freedom to invest in new creative projects, write books, or build adjacent businesses.

The point of scaling is not to get big. It is to get free -- free from the ceiling that limited your income, free from the treadmill that consumed your time, and free to direct your creative energy toward whatever you choose next.

That freedom starts with the first step. Use the audit, pick your phase, and begin.

## Lesson Summary

- The 12-month roadmap has four phases: Foundation (months 1-3), First Hire (4-6), Closer Integration (7-9), and Scale/Optimize (10-12)
- Revenue milestones from $10K to $120K+ each have specific team configurations and priority actions
- Hire humans for judgment, empathy, and adaptation; automate logistics, reminders, and data tasks
- The Scale Readiness Audit (100 points across 4 categories) diagnoses your exact starting point
- Scores of 80+ mean scale now; 60-79 strengthen gaps; 40-59 build foundation; below 40 focus on product-market fit
- Re-take the audit every 90 days to track progress and recalibrate your plan
- The purpose of scaling is freedom -- freedom of income, time, and creative direction
