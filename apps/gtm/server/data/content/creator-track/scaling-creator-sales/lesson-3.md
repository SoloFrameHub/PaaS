# Hiring Your First Closer

Hiring your first closer is the highest-leverage and highest-risk decision you will make in scaling your creator business. Get it right and you unlock revenue growth that was previously impossible. Get it wrong and you lose money, damage your brand, and burn leads that took months to generate.

This lesson gives you a practical, step-by-step system for finding, evaluating, and onboarding your first closer.

## Where to Find Sales Talent

Forget job boards like Indeed or LinkedIn Jobs for this role. The creator economy has its own talent ecosystem, and the best closers for your business are rarely found through traditional channels.

### The Five Best Sources

**1. Closer communities and training programs.** Programs like Remote Closing Academy, Cole Gordon's Closers program, and similar creator-economy sales training produce candidates who already understand high-ticket selling. These communities often have job boards or placement services. The candidates speak the language and understand the model.

**2. Your own audience.** Some of your best followers might be natural salespeople who deeply believe in what you teach. They already know your content, your voice, and your values. Post a role description in your community, email list, or social channels. The conversion: fan-to-employee can be powerful when it works.

**3. Referrals from other creators.** Ask peers in your niche who they use or have used. Closers often work with multiple non-competing creators, which means they already understand the business model. A warm referral from a fellow creator is worth more than 50 cold applications.

**4. Sales talent agencies.** Companies that specialize in placing closers with coaches and creators. They typically charge a placement fee or ongoing percentage. More expensive, but they pre-vet candidates and often provide replacement guarantees.

**5. Contractor platforms.** Platforms like Belay, FreeUp, or even specialized Upwork searches can surface experienced closers. Filter for candidates with coaching, consulting, or creator-economy experience specifically.

## The Interview Process

Do not wing the interview. A structured process is the difference between hiring someone who transforms your business and hiring someone who burns your leads for two months before you fire them.

### Stage 1: Application Screening (5 minutes per candidate)

Require applicants to submit:
- A 2-minute video introduction explaining why they want the role
- Their last 12 months of sales metrics (close rate, average deal size, total revenue closed)
- Two references from previous employers or clients

The video is the most important element. You are hiring someone to talk to your prospects. If their video is sloppy, low-energy, or generic, they will be sloppy, low-energy, and generic on your sales calls.

### Stage 2: Initial Phone Screen (15 minutes)

This is a quick conversation to assess communication skills and basic fit. Questions to ask:

- "Walk me through how you run a typical sales call from open to close."
- "Tell me about a deal you lost recently. What happened and what would you do differently?"
- "What do you know about our brand, and why do you want to work with us specifically?"

Red flags at this stage: cannot articulate a sales process, blames external factors for lost deals, has not researched your brand at all.

### Stage 3: Role-Play Call (30 minutes)

This is the critical stage. Give the candidate your offer details and have them sell it to you while you play a realistic prospect. Brief them 24 hours in advance with your offer one-pager, a prospect persona description, and common objections.

**What to evaluate:**
- **Discovery skills**: Do they ask questions before pitching, or do they launch into features?
- **Listening ability**: Do they reference what you said, or run through a memorized script?
- **Objection handling**: Do they acknowledge and address concerns, or bulldoze through them?
- **Closing ability**: Do they ask for the commitment confidently, or trail off at the end?
- **Tone and energy**: Do they sound like someone your audience would trust?

Score each dimension on a 1-5 scale. Anyone below a 3 average is a pass, regardless of their resume.

### Stage 4: Paid Trial (2-4 weeks)

Never commit to a long-term arrangement without a trial period. Structure it as:

- **Week 1**: Shadow your calls. They listen, take notes, debrief with you afterward.
- **Week 2**: Co-close calls. They lead the conversation; you are on the line as backup.
- **Week 3-4**: They handle calls independently while you review recordings.

Pay them during the trial -- typically their base rate plus commission on any deals they close. A paid trial attracts better candidates and gives you a legally cleaner arrangement than "unpaid auditions."

Top closers have options. Offering a paid trial signals you're serious, professional, and respect their time. It also creates a real working relationship from day one rather than an awkward "audition" dynamic.

## Compensation Structures

Compensation is where most creators either overpay (destroying margins) or underpay (attracting bottom-tier talent). Here are the three standard models for creator-economy closers.

### Model 1: Commission-Only

**Structure**: 10-20% of revenue on deals they close, no base salary.

**Pros**: Zero fixed cost. You only pay when they produce. Attracts self-motivated, confident salespeople.

**Cons**: Higher commission percentage required to attract talent. Less control over their time and priorities. They may cherry-pick easy leads and neglect harder ones.

**Best for**: Testing the model with your first closer before committing to fixed costs.

### Model 2: Base + Commission

**Structure**: $3,000-$5,000/month base + 8-12% commission on closed deals.

**Pros**: Attracts higher-quality candidates. Gives you more control over their schedule and process. Creates stability that leads to better performance.

**Cons**: Fixed cost exists whether they perform or not. Need enough deal flow to justify the base.

**Best for**: Established creators at $40K+/month who have consistent lead flow and want a dedicated closer.

### Model 3: Tiered Commission

**Structure**: Base rate + escalating commission tiers based on performance.

Example:
- $3,000/month base
- 8% commission on first $50K closed in a month
- 12% commission on revenue between $50K-$100K
- 15% commission on revenue above $100K

**Pros**: Incentivizes high performance. Aligns closer's interests with business growth. The marginal cost of additional revenue decreases as a percentage.

**Best for**: Mature operations where the closer has proven themselves and you want to incentivize growth.

## Red Flags to Avoid

### During Hiring

- **"I can close anyone."** Top closers know that not every prospect should be closed. Beware of people who see sales as a conquest rather than a matching process.
- **Vague metrics.** If they cannot tell you their close rate to within a few percentage points, they either do not track it or do not want you to know.
- **Badmouthing previous employers.** The creator economy is small. Closers who trash former clients will eventually trash you.
- **Resistance to recording calls.** Call recording is non-negotiable for quality control. If they push back, they have something to hide.
- **Overselling themselves in the interview.** Ironic but true: closers who use high-pressure tactics on you in the interview will use them on your prospects. Your brand cannot afford that.

### After Hiring

- **Close rate drops significantly in month 2-3.** A small dip during ramp-up is normal. A sustained decline means they either front-loaded their best performance or cannot adapt to your specific audience.
- **Refund rate increases.** If your refund rate jumps after bringing on a closer, they may be using pressure tactics that create buyer's remorse. Track this metric closely.
- **Prospect complaints.** Even one prospect reporting a negative experience on a sales call is a serious signal. Two is a pattern. Three is a termination.

## The First 90 Days

Your first closer's success or failure is largely determined by how you manage their first 90 days. Here is a timeline:

**Days 1-7**: Immersion. They consume your content, go through your program as a student, study your recorded calls, and memorize your offer details. No live calls yet.

**Days 8-14**: Shadow phase. They sit in on your calls silently. After each call, you debrief: what worked, what the prospect was really asking, how you read the situation.

**Days 15-30**: Assisted closing. They take the lead on calls with you listening. You intervene only if they are about to lose a deal or damage the relationship. Debrief every call.

**Days 31-60**: Independent with review. They handle calls solo. You listen to every recording and provide written feedback. Weekly one-on-one coaching sessions.

**Days 61-90**: Performance evaluation. At this point, you should have enough data to know if this is working. Key benchmarks: close rate within 80% of your personal rate, zero prospect complaints, refund rate at or below your historical average.

If they are not meeting these benchmarks by day 90, have the honest conversation. Sometimes the fit is wrong, and extending a bad fit helps no one.

<Slide title="Days 1-7: Immersion">

**No live calls yet.** This week is pure learning.

**Activities:**
- Consume your flagship content (course, book, podcast episodes)
- Go through your program as a student
- Study 10+ of your recorded sales calls
- Memorize offer details, pricing, guarantees
- Learn your prospect personas and common objections

**Your role:** Provide all materials upfront. Schedule a 30-minute Q&A at end of week.

**Success metric:** They can articulate your offer and core methodology without notes.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Days 8-14: Shadow Phase">

**They observe, you perform.**

**Activities:**
- Sit in on all your sales calls silently
- Take detailed notes on your process
- After each call: 15-minute debrief
- Start to identify patterns in objections and closes

**Your role:** Narrate your thinking during debriefs. "I asked that question because..." or "I sensed hesitation when..."

**Success metric:** They can predict your next move during calls with 70%+ accuracy.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Days 15-30: Assisted Closing">

**They lead, you're the safety net.**

**Activities:**
- They run the call from open to close
- You're on the line but silent unless needed
- Intervene only if deal is at risk or relationship damage imminent
- Debrief every single call immediately after

**Your role:** Listen actively. Take notes. Provide specific, actionable feedback. Celebrate wins.

**Success metric:** They close at least 50% of your personal close rate. Zero prospect complaints.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Days 31-60: Independent with Review">

**They fly solo, you review everything.**

**Activities:**
- Handle all calls independently
- You listen to every recording within 24 hours
- Provide written feedback on each call
- Weekly 1-on-1 coaching session

**Your role:** Shift from real-time coaching to pattern recognition. Identify recurring gaps and create training around them.

**Success metric:** Close rate reaches 70%+ of your personal rate. Refund rate stable or improving.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Days 61-90: Performance Evaluation">

**Decision time.**

**Key benchmarks:**
- Close rate within 80% of your personal rate
- Zero prospect complaints
- Refund rate at or below your historical average
- Consistent performance (not front-loaded)

**Your role:** Collect data, have the honest conversation. If benchmarks aren't met, discuss whether it's a training gap or a fit issue.

**Success metric:** Clear go/no-go decision by day 90. If go, transition to standard operating rhythm.

</Slide>

## Lesson Summary

- Source closers from creator-economy communities, your audience, peer referrals, agencies, and contractor platforms -- not traditional job boards
- Use a four-stage interview: application screening, phone screen, role-play call, and paid trial
- Three compensation models: commission-only (10-20%), base + commission ($3-5K + 8-12%), and tiered commission
- Watch for red flags: vague metrics, resistance to recording, badmouthing former clients, and overselling in the interview
- The first 90 days follow a structured ramp: immersion, shadowing, assisted closing, independent with review, and performance evaluation
- Benchmark success at day 90: close rate within 80% of yours, no complaints, stable refund rate
