# Evergreen Webinar Systems

Live webinars are powerful, but they do not scale. You cannot be live every day. You cannot run the same presentation three times a week without burning out. And you cannot serve prospects in different time zones or those who discover you at 2 AM on a Sunday. At some point, the question becomes: can I take what is working live and make it run on autopilot?

The answer is yes -- but only if you do it right. Evergreen webinars (also called automated webinars or on-demand webinars) are one of the most profitable funnel systems in the creator economy when implemented correctly, and one of the most reputation-damaging when implemented poorly. This lesson covers when to make the transition, how to set up an authentic evergreen system, and the hybrid models that combine the best of live and automated.

## When to Go Evergreen

The rule is simple: **do not automate what you have not validated live.**

<InsightCard icon="⚠️" title="The Golden Rule">
Do not automate what you have not validated live. Automation scales what works — and what doesn't.
</InsightCard>

Go evergreen only after you have:

1. **Run the webinar live at least 10 times.** This number is not arbitrary. The first 3-5 runs are where you find the timing, the stories that land, the objections that surface, and the content that confuses people. Runs 6-10 are where you refine the pitch, tighten the transitions, and optimize the close. By run 10, you have a presentation that reliably converts.

2. **Achieved a consistent live conversion rate of 5%+ (attendee to buyer).** If your live webinar does not convert, automating it does not fix the problem. It just scales the failure. Fix the presentation first, then automate.

3. **Validated the offer.** You should have enough sales data to know that the offer resonates, the price is right, and the refund rate is acceptable (under 10%).

4. **Built a backlog of proof.** Evergreen webinars lack the social proof of live chat, live Q&A, and "X people are watching right now." You need strong testimonials, case studies, and results to compensate.

### The Premature Automation Trap

One of the most common mistakes in the creator space is automating too early. A founder runs one live webinar, gets 3 sales, and thinks: "Great, now I will put this on autopilot and it will print money." Then they spend $500/month on evergreen webinar software, drive traffic to it, and get zero conversions.

Why? Because that first live webinar benefited from the energy of a live audience, real-time social proof in the chat, and the urgency of "this is happening right now." None of those elements exist in an automated version unless you deliberately recreate them.

<ExampleCard label="Case Study: The $6,000 Mistake">
Marcus ran his webinar live once, got 8 sales from 120 attendees (6.7% conversion), and immediately automated it with EverWebinar. He spent $2,000 on ads over the next month, driving 340 registrants to the evergreen version. Result: 2 sales (0.6% conversion). The difference? His live webinar had 40+ people in chat creating energy and social proof. The automated version felt empty and scripted. He went back to live webinars, ran 8 more iterations, then re-recorded with deliberate energy and storytelling. The second evergreen attempt converted at 4.2%.
</ExampleCard>

## The Evergreen Webinar Tech Stack

### Platform Options

**EverWebinar** (by WebinarJam): The most established platform. Allows simulated live experiences with pre-recorded video, simulated chat, and customizable scheduling. $499/year.

**WebinarKit**: A more affordable alternative. Simulates the live experience with scheduled "sessions" that registrants can choose from. Integrates with major email platforms. $497 one-time.

**Demio (Automated Events)**: Clean, modern platform that supports both live and automated events. Good for creators who want to maintain a professional feel. $59-$109/month.

**Stealth Seminar**: The original evergreen platform. Highly customizable, supports on-demand and scheduled viewing options. $69-$199/month.

**Simple option (no specialized software)**: Pre-record the webinar, host it on Vimeo (password-protected), and build a simple funnel page that gives access after registration. This lacks the "live simulation" features but is the most affordable way to start.

### Which Setup to Choose

For most solo creators, I recommend starting with the simple option or Demio. The simulated live features of platforms like EverWebinar (fake chat, fake attendee counts) worked well in 2018, but modern audiences are increasingly savvy and may feel deceived if they realize the "live" event is pre-recorded. Authenticity matters more than simulation.

## Setting Up an Authentic Evergreen System

The key word is "authentic." Your evergreen webinar should not pretend to be live if it is not. Instead, frame it as what it is: a pre-recorded training that delivers tremendous value.

### The "On-Demand Masterclass" Model

Instead of scheduling fake live sessions, offer the webinar as an on-demand masterclass:

**Registration page headline:** "Watch the Free Masterclass: [Outcome]"
**Framing:** "I recorded this 60-minute training to share the exact system I use with my private clients. It is available for a limited time."

This framing is honest and still creates urgency. The "limited time" is legitimate -- you can rotate the training, update it quarterly, or limit access based on your launch calendar.

### The On-Demand Funnel Flow

1. **Registration page** -- collects name and email
2. **Watch page** -- the pre-recorded webinar plays immediately or at a scheduled time the registrant selects
3. **Offer page** -- after the webinar, redirect to a sales page with the full offer and a countdown timer
4. **Follow-up sequence** -- automated emails over the next 3-7 days

### Creating the Recording

Do not just record your Zoom screen from a live webinar and use the raw footage. Create a dedicated recording.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Remove Date References">
Do not say "today is March 15th" or reference current events. Keep content timeless. Instead of "Last month's election," say "Recent political shifts." Instead of "This week's news," say "A common scenario."
</Slide>

<Slide title="Remove Live Interaction">
Do not say "type in the chat" or "I see a question from Sarah." These expose the recording as non-live. Instead, anticipate questions: "You might be wondering..." or "A common question here is..."
</Slide>

<Slide title="Keep Energy High">
Without a live audience, your energy naturally drops. Counteract this by standing while recording, using hand gestures, and varying your vocal pace. Record when you're naturally energized, not at the end of a long day.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Record in Sections">
You do not need to nail the entire 60 minutes in one take. Record in 10-15 minute blocks and edit them together. This allows you to maintain peak energy and fix mistakes without re-recording everything.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Invest in Production">
A dedicated microphone ($100-200), good lighting, and a clean background make a meaningful difference in perceived authority. The difference between a $50 USB mic and a $150 XLR setup is immediately noticeable.
</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Maintaining Authenticity

The backlash against fake evergreen webinars has been significant. Audiences have caught on to the tricks: fake chat, "only 47 spots remaining" that never decreases, simulated urgency timers that reset every time you visit. This erodes trust.

### Authenticity Principles

**Principle 1: Be transparent about the format.** You can say "This is a pre-recorded masterclass" and still have people watch. The content and the offer are what matter, not the illusion of liveness.

**Principle 2: Use real urgency, not fake urgency.** Instead of an evergreen countdown that resets for every visitor, use cohort-based deadlines: "The next cohort starts on [date]. Enrollment closes [date]." This is real, and it creates legitimate urgency.

**Principle 3: Offer live elements alongside the evergreen content.** Pair your automated webinar with a live Q&A session once a month: "Watch the masterclass anytime, and join my live Q&A every third Thursday to get your questions answered." This gives prospects the human interaction they crave while keeping the core content on autopilot.

**Principle 4: Update the recording regularly.** Re-record your evergreen webinar every 6-12 months with fresh examples, updated results, and current references. This keeps the content relevant and prevents the "this feels outdated" reaction.

## Hybrid Models

The most sophisticated creators do not choose between live and evergreen. They use hybrid models that combine the strengths of both.

### Hybrid Model 1: Evergreen-to-Live

The automated webinar serves as the top of the funnel. Anyone can watch it anytime. At the end, instead of (or in addition to) a direct checkout, the CTA is to register for the next live event -- a workshop, Q&A, or challenge.

**Flow:** Evergreen webinar → Live workshop registration → Live event → Pitch → Sale

This model works because the evergreen webinar does the heavy lifting of education and belief-building, while the live event provides the energy, social proof, and urgency to close the sale.

### Hybrid Model 2: Challenge-to-Evergreen

You run live challenges quarterly (3-4 times per year). Between challenges, an evergreen webinar captures leads and makes sales on autopilot. When the next live challenge is announced, the evergreen funnel switches to promoting the challenge instead.

**Flow (between challenges):** Evergreen webinar → Automated sales sequence → Direct checkout
**Flow (during challenge launch):** Evergreen webinar → Challenge registration → 5-day challenge → Pitch → Sale

### Hybrid Model 3: The "Application Evergreen"

The evergreen webinar runs continuously. At the end, instead of a checkout page, viewers are invited to apply. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, and qualified prospects book calls that you conduct live.

This is ideal for high-ticket offers ($5,000+) where the evergreen content generates demand and the live call closes the sale.

## Evergreen Webinar Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Registration rate | 25% | 35% | 45%+ |
| Watch rate (start watching) | 40% | 55% | 70% |
| Watch-through rate (finish) | 30% | 45% | 60% |
| Conversion rate (viewer to buyer) | 2% | 5% | 8%+ |
| Revenue per registrant | $5 | $15 | $30+ |

**Revenue per registrant** is the metric that matters most for scaling. If you know every registrant is worth $15 on average, you can calculate exactly how much to spend on advertising to acquire registrants profitably.
