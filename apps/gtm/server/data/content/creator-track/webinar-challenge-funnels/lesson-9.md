# Post-Event Follow-Up Sequences

Here is a statistic that should change how you think about webinar and challenge funnels: 60-80% of total sales from a launch event happen after the event ends, not during the live presentation. The pitch creates the desire. The follow-up sequence converts that desire into action.

<InsightCard icon="💰" title="The Follow-Up Reality">
If you nail the webinar but have no follow-up, you are leaving the majority of your revenue on the table. If you run a phenomenal 5-day challenge but go silent on Day 6, you lose the momentum that took you a week to build. The post-event follow-up is not an afterthought. It is the second half of your launch.
</InsightCard>

## The 7-Day Follow-Up Window

Research across the creator economy consistently shows that the vast majority of post-event sales happen within 7 days. After Day 7, conversion rates drop to near zero for that cohort of prospects. People move on, the emotional peak fades, and "I'll think about it" becomes "I forgot about it."

Your follow-up sequence should be a 7-day campaign that begins the moment your live event ends. Every email in the sequence has a specific job. Let us walk through each one.

## The Post-Webinar Sequence (7 Emails)

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Email 1: The Replay Email">

**Subject line:** "Replay: [Webinar Title] + special offer inside"
**Timing:** Immediately after the event (or within 30 minutes)

**Job:** Get non-attendees to watch the replay and remind live attendees of the offer.

**Structure:**
- Thank everyone who attended live
- Acknowledge that many could not make it and that the replay is available
- Include 3-4 key takeaways or "aha moments" from the presentation to create curiosity
- State the offer clearly with a link to the sales page
- Mention the deadline: "This special offer expires on [date]"

**Example opening:** "If you were on tonight's training, you already know -- we covered a LOT. If you could not make it, no worries. The replay is live right now, but only for [48/72 hours]."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 2: The Social Proof Email">

**Subject line:** "What [Name] said after the training..." or "They started already..."
**Timing:** Day 1 post-event

**Job:** Show that other people are already taking action. This combats the "I'll wait" mindset.

**Structure:**
- Share 2-3 testimonials, screenshots, or messages from attendees who bought or had breakthroughs during the event
- If you have live chat screenshots showing enthusiasm, include those
- Include a brief FAQ or address one common objection
- CTA: Watch the replay (if they have not) or enroll now

**Example:** "Within 20 minutes of the training ending, 23 people enrolled in [Program Name]. Here is what Sarah said: '[Testimonial].' The momentum is real, and if you felt something during the training, trust that feeling."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 3: The Objection Crusher">

**Subject line:** "The #1 reason people don't join (and why it's wrong)"
**Timing:** Day 2 post-event

**Job:** Directly address the most common objection you hear. For most creator offers, this is either "I can't afford it" or "I don't have time."

**Structure:**
- Name the objection openly: "I know many of you are thinking [objection]"
- Reframe it: "Let me share a different way to think about this"
- Tell a story of someone who had that exact objection and overcame it
- Show the cost of inaction: "What does it cost you to stay where you are for another 6 months?"
- CTA: Enroll with link

**For the "I can't afford it" objection:** "The question is not whether you can afford to invest in this. The question is whether you can afford not to. If this program helps you sign just 2 additional clients in the next 90 days, you have more than covered the investment. And if you do nothing, where will you be 90 days from now?"

**For the "I don't have time" objection:** "You do not need to find more time. You need to stop spending time on things that do not work. That is exactly what this program does -- it replaces 20 hours of unfocused hustle with 5 hours of focused, proven strategy."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 4: The Case Study Email">

**Subject line:** "How [Name] went from [Before] to [After] in [Timeframe]"
**Timing:** Day 3 post-event

**Job:** Provide a detailed success story that the reader can see themselves in.

**Structure:**
- Tell the full story of a past client: where they started, what they struggled with, what they did inside your program, and the results they achieved
- Be specific with numbers, timelines, and details
- Connect their story to the reader: "If [client name]'s story sounds familiar, you are in the exact right place to make the same shift"
- CTA: Enroll with link

The case study email often converts the highest of any email in the sequence because it provides the most complete picture of what is possible.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 5: The FAQ / Logistics Email">

**Subject line:** "Your questions about [Program Name], answered"
**Timing:** Day 4 post-event

**Job:** Remove friction by answering practical questions that prevent people from clicking "Buy."

**Structure:**
- Address 5-7 frequently asked questions:
  - What is included?
  - How much time does it require each week?
  - What if it does not work for me? (Guarantee details)
  - Is there a payment plan?
  - When does it start?
  - How long do I have access?
  - Can I get a refund?
- Keep answers concise and confidence-building
- CTA: Enroll with link

This email is deceptively important. Many prospects are 90% convinced but have a logistical question they are too embarrassed to ask. Answering it proactively removes the last barrier.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 6: The Urgency Email">

**Subject line:** "Closing in [X] hours -- your last chance"
**Timing:** 24-48 hours before the deadline

**Job:** Create real urgency that pushes fence-sitters to decide.

**Structure:**
- State the deadline clearly: "Enrollment for [Program Name] closes at midnight on [date]"
- State what they lose: "After [date], the price goes up to [higher price] / the bonuses are removed / enrollment is closed until [next date]"
- Briefly recap the offer and the key transformation
- CTA: Enroll now with link

**Urgency must be real.** If you say the price goes up, it must actually go up. If you say bonuses expire, they must actually expire. If you say enrollment closes, it must actually close. Fake urgency destroys trust and reputation faster than almost anything else.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Email 7: The Cart Close Email">

**Subject line:** "Final hours. [Program Name] closes tonight."
**Timing:** Morning of the deadline + a final email 2-4 hours before close

**Job:** Last push. This is your highest-converting email for fence-sitters.

**Structure (morning):**
- This is it. Share a personal note about why you created the program and who it is for.
- Remind them of the guarantee: "You have nothing to lose and everything to gain."
- CTA: Enroll before midnight with link

**Structure (final hours, 2-4 hours before close):**
- Short and direct: "There are [X] hours left. The link is below. If you have been thinking about it all week, now is the time to decide."
- CTA: Enroll with link

The cart close email typically generates 20-40% of total post-event sales. People procrastinate. The deadline forces a decision.

</Slide>

## The Post-Challenge Sequence

Challenge follow-up differs from webinar follow-up because you have 5 days of shared experience to reference.

### Key Differences

1. **Reference specific challenge moments.** "Remember on Day 3 when we walked through the framework and you created your first draft? That was just the beginning."
2. **Reference community energy.** "The community was on fire this week. 347 people shared their homework. 89 people said it was the most productive week they have had in months."
3. **Leverage peer pressure (ethically).** "52 of your fellow challengers have already enrolled. They are starting Module 1 this weekend."
4. **Shorter window.** Because the challenge is more intensive, your follow-up can be compressed to 5 days instead of 7. The emotional peak decays faster.

## Re-Engagement for Non-Buyers

Not everyone will buy during the launch window. That does not mean they are lost forever. It means they were not ready yet.

### The Non-Buyer Nurture Strategy

**Week 2-4 after the event:** Go silent on the offer. Return to pure value content. Emails, blog posts, social media content. Rebuild goodwill.

**Month 2:** Send a "check-in" email: "How is your progress on [goal they expressed during the event]? I have a new [free resource] that might help." No pitch. Just value.

**Next launch:** When you run your next webinar or challenge (ideally 2-3 months later), invite non-buyers back with a personal email: "I noticed you attended our last [event] but did not join the program. I am running an updated version on [date] with [new content/bonuses]. Would you like to join?"

### The "Change of Mind" Sequence

Some creators use a 30-day follow-up sequence for non-buyers that includes:
- Week 1: Additional free value related to the webinar topic
- Week 2: A new case study or testimonial
- Week 3: A "last chance" offer (different from the original -- perhaps a payment plan, a downsell, or a limited-access version)
- Week 4: A genuine "moving on" email that removes sales pressure and offers to keep providing free value

<ExampleCard label="The 'Moving On' Email That Converts">
The "moving on" email is counterintuitively effective: "I wanted to let you know that I will not be emailing you about [Program Name] anymore. If it was not the right fit or the right time, I completely respect that. I will keep sending you my weekly [newsletter/tips/content] because helping you grow is what I am here for. And if you ever want to explore the program in the future, just reply to any email and we will talk."

This email often generates a spike in replies and late enrollments because it removes pressure and restores trust.
</ExampleCard>

## Sequence Metrics to Track

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Email open rate (sequence average) | 35-50% |
| Click-through rate (sequence average) | 3-8% |
| Sales from replay email (Email 1) | 15-25% of total post-event sales |
| Sales from cart close (Email 7) | 20-40% of total post-event sales |
| Unsubscribe rate (sequence average) | Under 2% per email |
| Total post-event sales as % of all launch sales | 60-80% |
