# Your Launch Playbook

You now have every piece of the puzzle. You understand the difference between webinars and challenges, how to structure them, how to deliver them, how to follow up, and how to evolve them over time. What you need now is a plan -- a concrete, day-by-day playbook that takes you from "I have an offer" to "I completed my first launch."

This lesson gives you that plan. We will cover the launch calendar, the three phases of a launch (pre-launch, launch, post-launch), the specific tasks for each phase, and a starter template for your first webinar or challenge.

## The Launch Calendar

Most creators fail at launches not because of bad content or weak offers, but because of bad timing. They decide to launch on a Monday and scramble to create everything that week. Or they spend 3 months preparing and lose momentum before they ever go live.

### Ideal Timelines

**For your first webinar launch:** 3-4 weeks from decision to live event.
**For your first challenge launch:** 5-6 weeks from decision to Day 1 of the challenge.
**For subsequent launches (with existing assets):** 2-3 weeks for a webinar, 3-4 weeks for a challenge.

### The Annual Launch Rhythm

Once you have your system working, most creators settle into one of these rhythms:

**The Quarterly Launcher:** 4 live launches per year (one per quarter), with evergreen systems running in between. This is the most common model for creators earning $200K-$1M.

**The Bi-Annual Launcher:** 2 big launches per year (typically January and September), with the rest of the year focused on delivery and content. This works for high-ticket offers ($5,000+) where each launch generates significant revenue.

**The Monthly Launcher:** Monthly webinars feeding an evergreen offer. This is the most aggressive model and works best for offers under $1,000 with proven funnels.

Choose the rhythm that matches your energy, your offer price, and your audience size. You can always adjust after your first two launches.

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (2-4 Weeks Before the Event)

The pre-launch phase is about building anticipation, growing your registration list, and preparing all the assets you need.

### Week 1: Foundation

**Day 1-2: Offer finalization**
- Confirm your offer details: what is included, the price, payment options, bonuses, and guarantee
- Write the sales page (or at minimum, the sales page outline)
- Decide: webinar or challenge? (Use the decision matrix from Lesson 1)

**Day 3-4: Content creation**
- For a webinar: Create your slide deck using the Perfect Webinar Framework (Lesson 2). Draft your origin story, three secrets, stack, and close.
- For a challenge: Outline all 5 days of content. Write the teaching outline and homework assignments for each day.

**Day 5-7: Tech setup**
- Set up your registration page (Lesson 3)
- Set up your email reminder sequence (Lesson 3)
- Set up your webinar platform (Zoom, WebinarJam, etc.) or challenge community (Facebook Group, Circle, Skool)
- Test everything: registration flow, email delivery, platform access

### Week 2: Registration Push

**Day 8-10: Email promotion**
- Send 2-3 emails to your existing list promoting the event
- Email 1: Announcement ("I'm hosting a free training on [topic]")
- Email 2: Value-add ("Here's why you need to attend -- [specific benefit]")
- Email 3: Social proof or urgency ("200+ people registered -- save your spot")

**Day 11-14: Social media and outreach**
- Post daily on your primary social platforms about the event
- Reach out to 5-10 peers or affiliates who might promote to their audiences
- Consider a small ad budget ($100-300) for Facebook or Instagram ads driving to the registration page
- Create a short-form video (Reel, TikTok, Short) about the topic you are covering

### Week 3 (Challenges Only): Community Warm-Up

**Day 15-17: Community preparation**
- Open the challenge community space
- Post a welcome message and introduction thread
- Encourage early registrants to introduce themselves and share their goals

**Day 18-21: Pre-challenge engagement**
- Share a "pre-work" assignment: something simple that gets participants thinking before Day 1
- Post a "what to expect" overview with the daily schedule
- Go live for 10 minutes in the community to introduce yourself and build excitement

## Phase 2: The Launch Event

### Webinar Launch Day Checklist

**Morning of:**
- [ ] Test internet connection, audio, and video
- [ ] Load slides and test screen sharing
- [ ] Send the "we start today" reminder email
- [ ] Close unnecessary applications and silence notifications
- [ ] Place water, notes, and timer within reach

**1 hour before:**
- [ ] Open the webinar room
- [ ] Send the "1 hour" reminder email
- [ ] Do a final audio/video check
- [ ] Review your pacing guide (timestamps for each section)

**During the webinar:**
- [ ] Start 2-3 minutes late to let people arrive (but address the early birds)
- [ ] Run the chat storm opening (Lesson 3)
- [ ] Deliver the presentation (Lessons 2 and 4)
- [ ] Make the transition to pitch naturally (Lesson 4)
- [ ] Deliver the stack and close
- [ ] Run Q&A for 15-20 minutes
- [ ] End with a final CTA and the buy link

**Immediately after:**
- [ ] Send the replay email (Lesson 9, Email 1)
- [ ] Activate the 7-day follow-up sequence
- [ ] Post a thank-you in any community space
- [ ] Screenshot any live chat testimonials or positive comments for future use

### Challenge Launch Week Checklist

**Before Day 1:**
- [ ] All daily emails pre-written and scheduled
- [ ] Community space open and seeded with content
- [ ] All daily teaching outlines finalized
- [ ] Homework assignments ready for each day
- [ ] Day 5 pitch deck and stack slides prepared

**Each day of the challenge (Days 1-4):**
- [ ] Send morning email with context and homework setup
- [ ] Go live at the scheduled time
- [ ] Deliver the teaching session
- [ ] Post the homework assignment in the community
- [ ] Engage with homework submissions in the evening
- [ ] Highlight 2-3 "wins of the day" in the community

- [ ] Send morning email building anticipation for the final session
- [ ] Deliver the Day 5 session (celebration, recap, pitch -- Lesson 6)
- [ ] Open enrollment

- [ ] Engage heavily in the community for the rest of the day

## Phase 3: Post-Launch (7 Days After the Event)

### The Follow-Up Sprint

This phase is entirely about executing your 7-day email sequence (Lesson 9). Your daily tasks:

**Day 1 post-event:** Send Email 2 (social proof). Respond to any DMs or emails from prospects with questions.

**Day 2:** Send Email 3 (objection crusher). Check sales numbers. Adjust urgency messaging if needed.

**Day 3:** Send Email 4 (case study). Post a community update thanking participants and sharing results.

**Day 4:** Send Email 5 (FAQ). Address any new objections that surfaced in DMs or community posts.

**Day 5-6:** Send Email 6 (urgency). Increase posting frequency on social media. Consider a live Q&A or "office hours" for prospects who have questions.

**Day 7:** Send Email 7 (cart close -- morning + final hours). Close enrollment at midnight. Do NOT extend.

### The Post-Launch Debrief

Within 48 hours of closing enrollment, conduct a thorough debrief. This is how you improve for next time.

**Metrics to record:**
- Total registrants
- Show-up rate (% of registrants who attended)
- Engagement rate (for challenges: homework completion, community posts)
- Conversion rate (% of attendees/completers who bought)
- Total revenue
- Average order value
- Refund rate (track for 30 days)
- Email sequence performance (open rates, click rates, unsubscribes)
- Ad spend and cost per registrant (if applicable)

**Questions to answer:**
- What worked best? (Which content resonated, which emails converted, which moments created energy?)
- What fell flat? (Where did you lose people? Which objections were not addressed?)
- What would you change for next time?
- What feedback did participants share?

### The Post-Launch Transition

After the debrief, you have three paths:

**Path 1: Deliver and iterate.** If this was your first or second launch, focus entirely on delivering an outstanding experience for the customers you just enrolled. Their results become the social proof for your next launch.

**Path 2: Plan the next launch.** If your funnel is proven and you are doing quarterly launches, begin planning the next one. What will you change? When will it run?

**Path 3: Go evergreen.** If you have 10+ successful live events and consistent conversion data, begin transitioning to an evergreen system (Lesson 8). Keep running live events periodically to generate fresh content and energy.

## The Compounding Effect of Launches

Here is what nobody tells you about launches: the first one is always the hardest and usually the smallest. But each subsequent launch compounds on the last.

Your first launch might generate $5,000. But during that launch, you collected 300 new email subscribers, generated 15 testimonials, identified your strongest content angles, and learned what objections to address. Your second launch, armed with all that data, might generate $15,000. Your third, $35,000. By launch five or six, you have a repeatable system that generates consistent, predictable revenue.
