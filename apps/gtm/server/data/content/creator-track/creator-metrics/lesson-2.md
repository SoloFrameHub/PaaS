# Show-Up Rate Optimization

You ran the ads. You wrote the landing page. People registered. You prepared a killer webinar, workshop, or live training. And then the day arrived, and **less than a third of registrants actually showed up.**

This is one of the most demoralizing experiences in the creator business -- and one of the most fixable. Show-up rate is a metric that responds dramatically to targeted optimization. The difference between a 25% show-up rate and a 45% show-up rate on a webinar with 500 registrants means 100 additional people watching your pitch. At a 10% close rate on a $997 offer, that is $9,970 in additional revenue from the same registration list.

This lesson breaks down exactly what drives show-up rates and how to systematically improve yours.

## Industry Benchmarks: What "Good" Looks Like

Before optimizing, you need to know where you stand. Here are current benchmarks for creator-led events:

### Live Webinars
- **Below 25%:** Poor -- something is broken in your registration-to-attendance pipeline
- **25-35%:** Average -- this is where most creators land without optimization
- **35-45%:** Good -- indicates strong pre-event engagement and a qualified audience
- **45-55%:** Excellent -- typical of creators with warm audiences and strong reminder systems
- **55%+:** Elite -- usually only achieved with small, highly targeted audiences or paid registrations

### Live Workshops (multi-hour or multi-day)
- **Day 1:** 40-60% of registrants
- **Day 2:** 60-75% of Day 1 attendees
- **Day 3:** 50-65% of Day 1 attendees

### Challenges (5-day format)
- **Day 1:** 35-50% of registrants
- **Day 3:** 20-30% of registrants
- **Day 5:** 15-25% of registrants

### Coaching Discovery Calls
- **Without reminder sequence:** 50-60%
- **With optimized reminder sequence:** 75-85%
- **With same-day confirmation text:** 85-92%

<InsightCard icon="📊" title="The Compound Effect">
Improving show-up rate by just 2-3% per event compounds dramatically. Over 10 events, you can move from 30% to 50%+ -- that's 67% more attendees from the same registration effort.
</InsightCard>

## The 7 Factors That Kill Show-Up Rates

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Factor 1: Too Much Time Between Registration and Event">

Every day between registration and the event costs you approximately 3-5% of your attendees. A 14-day registration window means you are losing 40-70% of early registrants before the event even happens.

**Fix:** Keep the registration window to 7 days maximum for webinars. For discovery calls, book within 48 hours of the lead expressing interest. The data is clear: leads booked same-day or next-day show up at 85%+. Leads booked 5+ days out show up at 55-65%.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 2: Weak Registration Commitment">

If someone registers in 3 seconds while scrolling their phone, their commitment level is near zero. They gave you an email address, not a promise.

**Fix:** Add a "commitment micro-step" immediately after registration:
- Ask them to add the event to their calendar (provide a one-click calendar link)
- Ask them to reply to the confirmation email with their biggest question
- Ask them to complete a 2-minute pre-event survey

Each micro-step increases show-up rates by 5-10%. Stacking two of these can move your rate by 15-20%.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 3: Generic Reminder Emails">

"Don't forget! Our webinar is tomorrow!" is not a reminder -- it is noise. Your registrants are getting dozens of similar emails every week.

**Fix:** Use the PACE reminder framework:

- **P - Personal:** Use their first name and reference something specific. "Sarah, you mentioned struggling with pricing -- tomorrow's session covers exactly that."
- **A - Anticipation:** Build excitement about what they will learn. Share a specific stat or insight they will get.
- **C - Consequence:** Remind them what staying stuck costs. "Every week without this system is another week of guessing."
- **E - Easy:** Remove friction. "Click here to join. No downloads needed. Takes 60 minutes."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 4: Wrong Day and Time">

Optimal webinar times vary by audience, but the data shows consistent patterns:

- **B2B creators:** Tuesday-Thursday, 11am-1pm ET performs best
- **B2C creators:** Tuesday-Wednesday, 7pm-9pm ET or Saturday 10am-12pm ET
- **International audiences:** Multiple time slots or recorded options with live Q&A

**The worst times:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, Sunday evenings. Show-up rates drop 15-25% compared to optimal slots.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 5: No Same-Day Engagement">

The hours before your event are critical. Most creators go silent between their last reminder email (usually the day before) and the event itself. This is a missed opportunity.

**Fix:** Implement same-day touchpoints:
- **Morning email** (if event is afternoon/evening): "Today's the day. Here's what we're covering."
- **60-minute SMS/text reminder:** "Starting in 1 hour. Here's your link: [link]"
- **15-minute buzz message:** "We're live in 15 minutes. 347 people are already in the room." (Social proof drives last-minute attendance.)

SMS reminders alone can increase show-up rates by 10-15%. If you are not collecting phone numbers at registration, start.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 6: No Penalty for No-Show">

When there is zero consequence for not showing up, people treat your event like a Netflix queue -- something they will "get to eventually."

**Fix:** Create perceived scarcity or loss:
- "This training will not be available as a replay" (if true)
- "Attendees get a bonus worksheet/template that won't be sent to no-shows"
- "Live attendees can ask questions directly -- the replay won't include Q&A"

These are not manipulative tactics. They are accurate descriptions of the value of live attendance. Use them honestly.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Factor 7: Registration Source Quality">

Where your registrants come from dramatically affects show-up rates:

| Source | Typical Show-Up Rate |
|--------|---------------------|
| Existing email list (warm) | 45-60% |
| Organic social media | 35-50% |
| Partner/affiliate traffic | 30-45% |
| Facebook/Instagram ads | 20-35% |
| YouTube ads | 25-40% |
| Cold outreach/DMs | 40-55% |

If 80% of your registrants come from paid ads, a 30% show-up rate is actually normal. If 80% come from your warm email list and you are at 30%, something is wrong with your reminder sequence.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## The Optimized Reminder Sequence

Here is the exact sequence that consistently produces 40-50% show-up rates for live webinars with mixed traffic sources:

### Immediately After Registration
- Confirmation email with calendar link (one-click add)
- Thank-you page with pre-event engagement question
- If collecting phone numbers: opt-in confirmation for SMS reminders

### 3 Days Before
- Email: Share a valuable insight related to the topic. End with "I'll go deeper on this during our live session on [Day]."

### 1 Day Before
- Email: "Tomorrow at [time] -- here's exactly what we'll cover" (bullet-point agenda). Include the join link prominently.

### Morning Of (for afternoon/evening events)
- Email: Short, personal, excitement-building. "I just finished my final prep for today's session. I added a section on [specific topic] because so many of you asked about it."

### 60 Minutes Before
- SMS: "Starting in 1 hour! Join here: [link]"
- Email: Subject line "We're live in 60 minutes"

### 15 Minutes Before
- SMS: "[First name], we're about to start. [link]"

### 5 Minutes After Start
- Email: "We're live now -- jump in: [link]" (catches latecomers who forgot)

## Measuring and Iterating

Track these numbers for every event:

1. **Total registrants**
2. **Registrants who added to calendar** (if trackable)
3. **Email open rates on each reminder**
4. **SMS click-through rates**
5. **Peak live attendance**
6. **Show-up rate** (peak attendance / total registrants)

After each event, identify the weakest link. If calendar adds are low, improve your thank-you page. If email opens are dropping, rewrite subject lines. If SMS clicks are low, shorten the message.

The goal is to improve show-up rate by 2-3% per event. Over 10 events, that compounds from 30% to 50% or higher.
