# Group Enrollment Events

Everything we have covered so far has focused on one-to-one enrollment calls. These are the gold standard for high-ticket creator sales, and you should master them first. But at some point, one-to-one calls become a bottleneck. You can only take so many calls per week. If each call takes 45-50 minutes and you have a 30% close rate, you need 10 calls to get 3 clients. That is nearly 8 hours of calls per week, plus prep and follow-up time.

<InsightCard icon="📊" title="The One-to-One Ceiling">
At 30% close rate, getting 10 clients requires 33 calls. That's 25+ hours of call time alone—before prep, follow-up, or delivery. Group events break this ceiling.
</InsightCard>

Group enrollment events are how you scale beyond the one-to-one ceiling. They allow you to sell to many people simultaneously while maintaining the enrollment energy that drives conversions.

In this lesson, we will cover the three main types of group enrollment events, how to structure each one for maximum conversion, and the key differences between selling to an individual and selling to a group.

## The Three Types of Group Enrollment Events

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Type 1: Live Workshop to Enrollment">

This is the most common and often the highest-converting group enrollment format. You deliver a 60-90 minute live workshop that provides genuine value, then transition into an enrollment pitch at the end.

**Structure:**

- **Minutes 0-5: Welcome and frame setting.** Welcome attendees, set expectations for what they will learn, and create urgency: "I am going to teach you [specific skill] today, and at the end, I will share how you can take this much further if you want to."
- **Minutes 5-50: The teaching portion.** Teach one complete, actionable framework. Not surface-level overview. Not a teaser. Teach them something they can go implement today. Counterintuitively, the more value you give away, the more people want to pay for more.
- **Minutes 50-60: The bridge.** This is the transition from teaching to selling. "So you now have [the framework]. And if you implement just this one piece, you will see results. But here is the challenge: this is one piece of a bigger system. Let me show you what the full system looks like."
- **Minutes 60-80: The enrollment presentation.** Present the transformation, not the features. Use the same principles from Lesson 3: sell the destination, use before/after framing, and lead with identity-level change.
- **Minutes 80-90: Q&A and close.** Answer questions (most of which will be pre-buying questions like "How does the payment plan work?"), share the enrollment link, and create urgency with a time-bound bonus or limited spots.

**Key principle for workshops:** The teaching portion must be genuinely valuable. If attendees feel like the workshop was a bait-and-switch disguised as free content, you will destroy trust and convert nobody. The teaching should be so good that they think, "If the free workshop is this valuable, the paid program must be incredible."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Type 2: Webinar-to-Call Hybrid">

This format combines a group webinar with individual enrollment calls. The webinar does the heavy lifting of education and desire-building, and the enrollment call handles the personal concerns and close.

**Structure:**

- **The webinar (60-75 minutes):** Similar to the workshop format, but instead of closing directly at the end, you make an offer for a free enrollment call: "If what I shared today resonated and you want to explore what working together looks like, I have a limited number of strategy calls available this week. Book yours at [link]."
- **The enrollment call (20-30 minutes):** Because the webinar already provided education, built trust, and generated desire, the enrollment call can be much shorter. You do not need to spend 10 minutes on pain amplification because the webinar already did that. The call focuses on: "What resonated from the workshop? What is your specific situation? Here is how the program addresses that. Here is the investment. Ready to go?"

**When to use this format:** This works well when your offer is $3,000+ and requires a personal conversation to close, but you do not want to spend 45 minutes on each call. The webinar pre-warms the prospect so the call is a quick closing conversation, not a full enrollment presentation.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Type 3: Community Enrollment Events">

This is the newest and fastest-growing format. Instead of running a separate webinar or workshop, you run enrollment events inside your existing community: a Facebook group, Circle community, Skool group, or even an email list.

**Structure:**

A community enrollment event typically runs over 3-5 days:

- **Day 1: Awareness.** Post about a challenge or frustration your audience faces. Start a conversation. Get people commenting and sharing their struggles.
- **Day 2: Education.** Share a valuable piece of content that addresses one aspect of the problem. This could be a live video, a detailed post, or a mini-training.
- **Day 3: Social Proof.** Share success stories from past members. Let members who have gone through your program share their experience. User-generated social proof converts better than anything you can say yourself.
- **Day 4: Enrollment Opens.** Announce the program, share the enrollment page, and present the offer. Use the transformation selling framework from Lesson 3.
- **Day 5: Deadline.** Enrollment closes at midnight. Create urgency by closing the cart or removing a bonus.

**Why community enrollment events work:** They leverage social dynamics. When people see others in their community enrolling, it creates a bandwagon effect. Comments like "Just enrolled!" or "See you inside!" generate social proof in real-time and create FOMO for those on the fence.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Selling to a Group: Key Differences

When you are on a one-to-one call, you can tailor your message to the specific individual. You know their situation, their objections, and their goals. In a group setting, you cannot personalize to that degree. Here is how to adjust.

**Speak to the archetype, not the individual.** Before your group event, identify 2-3 audience archetypes. For a creator business program, these might be: "The Overwhelmed Expert" (has tons of knowledge but cannot monetize it), "The Plateau Creator" (has some clients but is stuck at a revenue ceiling), and "The New Builder" (just starting and does not know where to begin). Throughout your presentation, explicitly call out each archetype: "If you are someone who has been creating content for years and you know you should be charging more, this next part is for you."

**Use "raise your hand" moments.** In a live event, ask questions that prompt engagement: "Raise your hand if you have ever felt like you are giving away too much for free." "Type 'yes' in the chat if you have ever had someone tell you your content changed their life but they never bought anything from you." These moments make individuals feel seen within the group and create momentum.

**Address objections preemptively.** On a one-to-one call, you address objections as they arise. In a group, you preemptively address them in your presentation because you will not get to have individual conversations with every attendee. Build slides or talking points that address the four core objections from Lesson 4.

**Create urgency with structure, not pressure.** In a group setting, urgency comes from:
- Limited spots: "We only take 20 people per cohort."
- Time-bound bonuses: "If you enroll by Friday, you get a free 1:1 strategy call with me."
- Price increases: "The price goes up by $500 after this enrollment window closes."
- Social momentum: "We already have 12 people enrolled from today's event."

## The Enrollment Presentation Framework for Groups

Here is a 20-minute enrollment presentation structure you can use at the end of a workshop, webinar, or community event.

<ProgressiveReveal title="20-Minute Group Enrollment Presentation" persistKey="creator-sales-conversations-L8-presentation">
<RevealSection title="Minutes 0-3: The Bridge">

Transition from the teaching content to the enrollment presentation.

*"So everything I just taught you is powerful on its own. But here is the reality: knowing what to do and having a system for doing it are two different things. Let me show you what having the full system looks like."*

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 3-8: The Transformation">

Paint the before-and-after picture. Use the three-level transformation approach from Lesson 3.

*"Imagine: six months from now, you have a consistent flow of ideal clients reaching out to you. You are charging premium prices and feeling confident about it. You are working fewer hours because your system handles the heavy lifting. That is not a fantasy. That is what this program is designed to create."*

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 8-13: The Offer">

Walk through your transformation stack. Each element maps to an outcome, not a feature.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 13-16: Social Proof">

Share 2-3 specific success stories. Include a mix of archetypes so different audience members can see themselves.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 16-18: The Investment">

Deliver the value stack and price using the framework from Lesson 5. Present pay-in-full and payment plan options.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 18-20: The Call to Action">

Share the enrollment link. Create urgency with a deadline or bonus. Tell them exactly what to do next: "Click the link in the chat, choose your payment option, and you will get your onboarding email within 5 minutes."

</RevealSection>
</ProgressiveReveal>

## Conversion Rates: What to Expect

Group enrollment events have lower conversion rates than one-to-one calls, but they reach more people per hour, so the total revenue per hour can be higher.

**Benchmarks:**

- **Live workshop to direct enrollment:** 5-15% of attendees enroll. If 50 people attend and you convert 10%, that is 5 enrollments.
- **Webinar to enrollment call:** 10-20% of attendees book a call. 30-50% of those calls close. If 100 people attend, 15 book calls, and 7 enroll.
- **Community enrollment events:** 3-8% of community members enroll per event. If your community has 500 members, that is 15-40 enrollments per launch.

These numbers vary enormously based on the warmth of your audience, the quality of your event, and the price point. But they give you a starting frame of reference.

## Combining Group and Individual

The most effective enrollment strategy for most creators is a hybrid: use group events to generate warm prospects, then use individual enrollment calls to close the ones who need a personal conversation.

**The funnel:**
1. Free community (ongoing nurture)
2. Live workshop or webinar (monthly or quarterly event)
3. Enrollment page (for self-closers who are ready)
4. Enrollment call (for interested prospects who have questions)

This approach lets self-motivated buyers enroll directly (saving you call time) while still offering a personal touch for those who need it.
