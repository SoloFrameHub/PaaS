# The Enrollment Call Script

You have learned the C.L.O.S.E.R. framework, transformation selling, objection handling, and pricing psychology. Now it is time to put everything together into a complete, repeatable enrollment call script.

This is not a script you read word-for-word like a telemarketer. It is a conversation map: a structured flow that ensures you hit every important beat while still sounding natural and human. Think of it like a jazz musician who knows the chord changes. They improvise within the structure, but the structure ensures the song works.

A well-run enrollment call takes 40-50 minutes. Here is the full flow, broken down minute by minute.

## Phase 1: Rapport and Frame Setting (5 Minutes)

**Goal:** Make them feel safe, set expectations for the call, and establish that you are both there with a shared purpose.

**Opening (first 60 seconds):**

*"Hey [Name]! Great to meet you. Thanks for taking the time. Before we jump in, I want to set some expectations so we both get the most out of this call. I am going to ask you some questions about where your business is right now and where you want it to go. Then, if it feels like a fit, I will share how I might be able to help. And if it is not a fit, I will tell you that too. No pressure either way. Sound good?"*

This frame does several things. It establishes that you will lead the conversation (not them). It sets the expectation that you might share a solution. It makes it safe by removing pressure. And it positions you as a professional who is selective, not desperate.

**Rapport building (next 3-4 minutes):**

Ask something genuinely curious about them. Not forced small talk, but real interest.

*"I saw on your application that you are a [type of creator/coach]. Tell me a little about that. What do you do and who do you help?"*

Let them talk. Be genuinely interested. Nod, react, and ask follow-up questions. This is not filler. You are learning about their business, building connection, and letting them feel heard before you go deeper.

**Transition to the situation phase:**

*"That is really cool. Okay, so I have a good sense of what you do. Let me ask you this: what specifically made you book this call today? What is going on that made you say, 'I need to talk to someone about this'?"*

## Phase 2: Situation Deep-Dive (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their current reality in detail. Where are they now? What are they doing? What is working? What is not?

**Key questions to ask (select 4-5 that feel relevant):**

*"Walk me through how you currently get clients. What does that process look like?"*

*"How many clients are you working with right now, and what are you charging?"*

*"What are you doing for marketing and lead generation? What does a typical week look like for you in terms of business development?"*

*"What is your revenue right now, and what do you want it to be?"*

*"What have you already tried to solve this problem? Courses, coaches, programs, DIY?"*

*"On a scale of 1-10, how frustrated are you with where your business is right now?"*

**What you are listening for:** Pain, frustration, wasted effort, and the gap between where they are and where they want to be. Take notes on their exact words. You will mirror these back later.

**The labeling moment (from C.L.O.S.E.R.):**

After they have shared their situation, reflect it back and label it:

*"Okay, so if I am hearing you right, you are really good at what you do. Your clients get great results. But the challenge is on the front end: you do not have a reliable way to get new clients in the door. You are kind of cobbling it together month to month, and it feels unpredictable. Is that accurate?"*

When they confirm, you have earned their trust. They feel understood. Now you can go deeper.

## Phase 3: Pain Amplification (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Help them feel the full weight of their current situation. Not to manipulate, but to create enough emotional urgency that change feels necessary.

This is the most misunderstood phase. "Pain amplification" does not mean twisting the knife. It means helping someone who has been tolerating a bad situation actually confront the cost of continuing.

**Questions that amplify pain naturally:**

*"How long have you been dealing with this? A few months? A year? Longer?"*

*"What does this uncertainty cost you emotionally? Not just financially, but how does it make you feel when you do not know if you will have enough clients next month?"*

*"If nothing changes and you are in this exact same spot 12 months from now, how does that feel?"*

*"What is the ripple effect? How does this business stress show up in the rest of your life? Your relationships? Your health? Your creative energy?"*

*"You said you have been trying to solve this for [X months/years]. At what point does trying on your own become more expensive than getting help?"*

**The critical nuance:** Your tone during pain amplification should be empathetic, not aggressive. You are not saying "Wow, that sounds terrible." You are saying "I can hear how frustrating that is, and I want you to know you are not alone in this. I talk to creators every week who describe the exact same thing."

**The transition to aspiration:**

*"I really appreciate you sharing all of that with me. Now let me ask you the fun question. If we could wave a magic wand and fix all of this, what does your business look like a year from now?"*

## Phase 4: Solution Overview and Transformation (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Present your offer as the bridge between their current pain and their desired future.

**The bridge statement:**

*"So here is what I think. Based on everything you have told me, you have two main challenges: [Challenge 1] and [Challenge 2]. The good news is these are both very solvable. And they are exactly what my program is designed to address. Can I walk you through how?"*

**Present the transformation, not the features:**

Use the Transformation Stack from Lesson 3. Walk through 3-4 key outcomes your program creates, briefly mentioning the mechanism.

*"The first thing we do is build your enrollment pipeline. Right now, you are relying on word-of-mouth and random referrals. We are going to replace that with a system where your content consistently brings qualified people into conversations with you. The second thing we do is give you a proven enrollment conversation framework so that when you get on those calls, you know exactly what to say and you close at 30-40% instead of 5-10%. The third thing is accountability and community. You will be in a group of other creators going through the same process, and I am there every week to troubleshoot and guide you."*

**Social proof injection:**

*"This is the same system that helped [Name] go from zero clients to [result] in [timeframe]. And [Name] who was [similar to this prospect] and achieved [specific result]."*

**Ask for their reaction:**

*"How does that sound? Can you see how this would address what you have been struggling with?"*

## Phase 5: The Close (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Present the price, handle the logistics, and invite them to make a decision.

**The value stack and price delivery (from Lesson 5):**

Walk through your value stack. Anchor to the outcome, stack the components, total the value, and reveal the price.

*"So the investment for the full program is $2,997. There are two options: you can pay in full, which is the best value, or there is a 6-month payment plan at $597 per month. Which option works better for you?"*

**After you state the price, stop talking.** Count to five in your head. Let them process.

**If they say yes:** Move immediately to logistics. "Amazing. Let me send you the enrollment link right now while we are on the call. You will get an email within five minutes with your onboarding materials."

**If they hesitate:** This is normal. Do not panic. Ask: "What is coming up for you right now?" This opens the door for them to share their real concern, which you can then address using the frameworks from Lesson 4.

**If they say "I need to think about it":** This gets its own entire lesson (Lesson 7), but the short version is: "I totally understand. Can I ask what specifically you want to think about? Is it the money, the timing, or whether the program is right for you?"

## Phase 6: Handling Concerns and Reinforcement (5 Minutes)

**Goal:** Address any remaining concerns and, if they say yes, reinforce their decision.

If they raised a concern in Phase 5, address it using the frameworks from Lesson 4. Then ask again: "Does that address your concern? Are you ready to get started?"

**If they say yes, reinforce immediately:**

*"I am genuinely excited to work with you. The fact that you made this decision today tells me a lot about your commitment. Most people stay stuck. You did not. Here is what happens next: [specific next steps]. I want you to complete [first action] by [specific day]. We are going to hit the ground running."*

**If they need to go away and decide:** Set a specific follow-up. "I understand. Let us set a specific time to reconnect. How about tomorrow at [time]? That gives you time to process, and I can answer any questions that come up."

Never end a call without a specific next step. "I will follow up" is not a next step. "I will call you Wednesday at 3 PM" is.

## The Full Call Timing Map

| Phase | Time | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| Rapport & Frame | 0:00-5:00 | Build connection, set expectations |
| Situation | 5:00-15:00 | Understand current reality |
| Pain Amplification | 15:00-25:00 | Create urgency for change |
| Solution & Transformation | 25:00-35:00 | Present offer as the bridge |
| Close | 35:00-45:00 | Price, payment, decision |
| Concerns & Reinforcement | 45:00-50:00 | Address remaining concerns, confirm |

## Call Environment and Logistics

A few practical considerations that matter more than you think:

**Use video.** Always do enrollment calls on video (Zoom, Google Meet). Being able to see each other builds trust and allows you to read body language. If someone turns off their camera, gently ask them to turn it on: "Would you mind turning on your camera? I find these conversations are so much better when we can see each other."

**Eliminate distractions.** Close every tab except your notes document and the call window. Put your phone in another room. Give this person your complete attention for 45 minutes.

**Have your price and payment link ready.** When they say yes, you should be able to send them the payment link within 30 seconds. Do not fumble around looking for it. Have it copied and ready.

**Take notes.** Write down their exact words, especially during the Situation and Pain phases. You will use these phrases throughout the call and in your follow-up.

## Practice Exercise

1. Write out your complete enrollment call script using the six phases above. Customize every question, transition, and presentation to your specific offer and audience.

2. Do a full role-play with a friend or colleague. Set a timer. Run the complete 45-50 minute flow. Record it.

3. Listen back and evaluate:
   - Did each phase take roughly the right amount of time?
   - Where did you rush? Where did you linger too long?
   - How did the price delivery feel? Did you hesitate?
   - Did you successfully transition between phases, or did the conversation feel choppy?

4. Run the script with at least three real prospects before you evaluate whether it "works." One call is not a statistically valid sample. Three calls will show you patterns.
