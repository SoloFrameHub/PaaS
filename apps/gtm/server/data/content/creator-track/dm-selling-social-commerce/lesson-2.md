# Comment-to-DM Automation

The single most powerful growth mechanism in social commerce right now is the comment-to-DM flow. A viewer comments a specific word on your post, and within seconds, they receive a direct message from you with exactly what they asked for. No landing page. No email opt-in form. No friction. Just a comment, a DM, and a conversation.

This lesson teaches you how to build these automations from scratch, stay within platform rules, and design trigger words that maximize both engagement and sales.

---

## How Comment-to-DM Works

The mechanic is simple, but the psychology is sophisticated.

**Step 1:** You create a piece of content -- a reel, a carousel, a story, or a live video -- that delivers genuine value and ends with a specific call-to-action: "Comment the word GROWTH to get my free framework."

**Step 2:** When someone types "GROWTH" in the comments, an automation tool (ManyChat, Chatfuel, or a similar platform) detects the keyword and instantly sends that person a DM.

**Step 3:** The DM delivers the promised value (a PDF, a link, a video) and opens a conversation that can lead to qualification and eventually a sale.

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="Why This Works So Well">
Every comment boosts your post algorithmically. You're turning your sales mechanism into a growth mechanism — prospects self-select AND increase your reach simultaneously.
</InsightCard>

Why does this work so well? Three reasons:

1. **The comment boosts your post algorithmically.** Every comment signals engagement to the platform. More comments mean more reach. You are turning your sales mechanism into a growth mechanism.
2. **The prospect self-selects.** Someone who takes the time to comment a specific word is far more interested than someone who passively scrolls past your content.
3. **The DM arrives while attention is hot.** Unlike email (which people check hours later), the DM arrives within seconds, while the prospect is still on the platform and still thinking about your content.

## Setting Up ManyChat (Step by Step)

ManyChat is the dominant automation tool for Instagram and Facebook comment-to-DM flows. Here is how to set it up:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Initial Setup">

1. Go to manychat.com and create an account using your Facebook login.
2. Connect your Instagram Business or Creator account. (You must have a Business or Creator account -- personal accounts cannot use automation.)
3. Connect your Facebook Page if you want Messenger automation as well.
4. Navigate to "Automation" in the left sidebar, then select "New Automation."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Trigger Configuration">

**Trigger Configuration:**
- Select "Instagram Comment" as your trigger
- Choose "Specific Post" or "All Posts" (start with specific post)
- Set your trigger keyword. Example: `GROWTH`
- Enable "Comment reply" -- this is the public reply the bot posts under their comment. Keep it natural: "Sent it to your DMs! Check your inbox."

</Slide>

<Slide title="DM Message Sequence">

**DM Message Sequence:**
- Message 1 (Immediate): Deliver the promise. "Hey! Here is the Growth Framework I mentioned. [Link or attachment]. Quick question -- what is the biggest growth challenge you are facing right now?"
- Message 2 (If they respond): Acknowledge their answer and ask a qualifying question. "That makes sense. Are you currently doing [X] or still figuring out where to start?"
- Message 3 (Based on their answer): Present your offer naturally. "Based on what you shared, I think [offer] could help. Want me to send you the details?"

</Slide>

<Slide title="Key Settings">

**Key Settings:**
- Set a delay of 30-60 seconds before the DM sends (immediate DMs can feel robotic)
- Enable "exclude if already in flow" to prevent sending the same sequence twice
- Set conversation timeout to 24 hours (after which the automation stops and you follow up manually)

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

### ManyChat Pricing

The free plan allows up to 1,000 contacts and basic automation. The Pro plan ($15/month) unlocks unlimited contacts, advanced triggers, and integrations. For most solo creators, the Pro plan pays for itself within the first week.

## Chatfuel as an Alternative

Chatfuel is ManyChat's primary competitor and offers a few advantages:

- **Simpler interface** for beginners who find ManyChat overwhelming
- **Better Messenger support** if your audience is primarily on Facebook
- **AI-powered responses** that can handle more varied keyword inputs

The setup process is similar: connect your accounts, create a flow, set a keyword trigger, and design your message sequence. Chatfuel's free tier is more limited (50 conversations/month), so ManyChat is generally the better starting point for most creators.

## Choosing Your Trigger Words

Your trigger word is more important than you think. It serves three functions: it must be easy to type, relevant to the offer, and psychologically activating.

### The Rules of Trigger Word Design

**Rule 1: One word, all caps.** "Comment GROWTH" is better than "Comment 'I want the growth framework.'" The simpler the action, the more people do it.

**Rule 2: The word should relate to the desired outcome, not the deliverable.** "Comment REVENUE" works better than "Comment PDF." People want results, not file formats.

**Rule 3: Avoid common words.** If your trigger word is "YES" or "HELP," you will accidentally trigger the automation when someone makes a normal comment. Choose distinctive words: GROWTH, BLUEPRINT, SYSTEM, LAUNCH, READY.

**Rule 4: Test variations.** Run the same offer with different trigger words on different posts and track which word generates more comments. Some words feel more exciting or exclusive than others. "Comment INSIDER" often outperforms "Comment INFO."

## The "Comment [WORD] to Get..." Strategy

This is the complete content-to-automation playbook. Here is how to structure it:

### In a Reel or TikTok

End your video with: "I put everything I just talked about into a free [framework/template/checklist]. Comment the word [TRIGGER] and I will send it straight to your DMs."

Reinforce the CTA in your caption: "Want the [Name] Framework? Comment [TRIGGER] below and I will DM it to you."

### In a Carousel Post

Make your last slide the CTA slide. Design it clearly: "Get the full [resource name]. Comment [TRIGGER] below." Include the trigger word in your caption as well.

### In a Story

Use the question sticker or poll sticker as the trigger instead of a comment. ManyChat can detect specific story replies and trigger a DM sequence from those as well.

### During a Live

Verbally ask viewers to comment the keyword. Lives are especially powerful because the real-time interaction feels personal, and comments during a live boost the live's visibility to even more viewers.

## Compliance and Platform Rules

Automation is powerful, but platforms actively police abuse. Here are the rules you must follow:

### Instagram Rules (as of 2025)

- You must use an **approved automation partner** (ManyChat, Chatfuel). Custom-built bots that use unofficial APIs will get your account banned.
- You cannot send **promotional DMs to people who did not initiate the conversation.** The comment trigger counts as initiation.
- You are limited to approximately **50-80 new DM conversations per day** from automation. Exceeding this triggers rate limiting or temporary restrictions.
- Your automated DMs **cannot include external links in the first message** on newer accounts. Warm up your automation by starting with text-only DMs.

### Facebook Messenger Rules

- The **24-hour window rule**: you have 24 hours after someone's last interaction to send them messages freely. After that, you can only send messages using "Message Tags" (which have strict allowed categories) or pay for Sponsored Messages.
- You must provide a clear way for users to **opt out** of further messages.
- Messenger bots must disclose that they are automated if the user asks.

### General Best Practices

- Never buy or scrape lists of usernames to cold-DM
- Always deliver the promised value before asking for anything
- If someone does not respond to your first DM, do not send automated follow-ups more than once
- Treat automation as a conversation starter, not a replacement for human interaction

The sale happens in the conversation that follows -- and that requires a different skill set entirely.
