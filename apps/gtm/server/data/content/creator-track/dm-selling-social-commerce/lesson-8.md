# Your DM Selling System

You now have the strategies, scripts, automations, and social proof techniques to sell through DMs. But strategies without a system are just good intentions. This final lesson turns everything you have learned into a repeatable daily system -- a machine that generates conversations, qualifies prospects, and closes sales consistently, without requiring you to reinvent the process every day.

---

## The Daily DM Routine

Consistency beats intensity in DM selling. A creator who spends 45 focused minutes on DMs every day will outsell one who spends 6 frantic hours once a week. Here is the daily routine that produces results.

### Morning Block (20 minutes) -- 9:00 AM

**Step 1: Triage your inbox (5 minutes).** Open your DMs and sort every conversation into three categories:

- **Active (respond now):** People who replied overnight, asked a question, or are mid-conversation. These are your highest priority.
- **Automated (check delivery):** People who triggered your comment-to-DM automation since yesterday. Verify the automation delivered correctly. If they replied to the automated message, move them to Active.
- **Follow-up (schedule for later):** People you need to check in with who have gone silent for 24-48 hours.

**Step 2: Respond to Active conversations (10 minutes).** Work through your active conversations using the 5-Message Framework from Lesson 3. Prioritize people who are further along in the conversation (closer to a decision) over people who just entered.

**Step 3: Send follow-ups (5 minutes).** For people in the Follow-up category, send a brief, non-pushy check-in. One message per person. If they do not respond to this follow-up, move them to a weekly review list.

### Evening Block (25 minutes) -- 5:00 PM

**Step 4: Content engagement (10 minutes).** Respond to comments on your content from the day. If anyone left a thoughtful comment, send them a DM: "Hey, thanks for your comment on my post. Really appreciated your point about [specific detail]. How is [topic] going for you?"

**Step 5: Respond to new DMs (10 minutes).** Handle any new conversations that started during the day. Follow the same triage process.

**Step 6: Outreach (5 minutes -- LinkedIn only).** Send 3-5 personalized connection requests or value-first messages on LinkedIn. This is proactive outreach, separate from inbound DMs.

### Total daily time: 45 minutes.

This is not a casual suggestion. Block these 45 minutes in your calendar and treat them like client calls -- non-negotiable. The compounding effect of 45 minutes per day is approximately 300 conversations per month, which at even a modest conversion rate will transform your revenue.

## Tracking Conversations in a CRM

Your DM inbox is not a CRM. It is a chaotic stream of conversations at different stages, mixed with personal messages, spam, and customer support requests. As soon as you are managing more than 10 active conversations, you need a system to track them.

### The Minimum Viable DM CRM

You do not need expensive CRM software. A simple spreadsheet or Notion database with the following columns is sufficient:

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Name | The prospect's name |
| Platform | Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, etc. |
| Date Entered | When the conversation started |
| Entry Point | What triggered the DM (comment keyword, story reply, outreach) |
| Stage | Where they are in the 5-Message Framework (1-5) |
| Key Details | What you know about their situation (problem, budget, urgency) |
| Next Action | What you need to do next (respond, follow up, send offer, book call) |
| Next Action Date | When you need to take the action |
| Outcome | Won, Lost, or Active |
| Revenue | If won, how much |

### How to Use the CRM

**At the end of each DM session:** Update the CRM. Add new conversations, update stages, and note next actions with dates.

**At the start of each DM session:** Check the CRM for any next actions due today. This ensures no conversation falls through the cracks.

**Weekly review (15 minutes, Sunday):** Look at your full pipeline. How many conversations are at each stage? Where are people dropping off? Are there conversations you forgot to follow up on?

### Upgrading Later

Once you are consistently managing 30+ conversations, consider upgrading to a proper CRM. Options that work well for solo creator DM selling:

- **ManyChat CRM (built-in):** If you are already using ManyChat, it tracks conversations and allows tagging and segmentation.
- **HubSpot Free:** A free, fully-featured CRM that can track contacts and deals. Overkill for most solo creators but useful if you want pipeline visualization.
- **Notion or Airtable:** Flexible databases that you can customize to match your exact workflow. Many creators prefer these because they can design the interface themselves.
- **Close CRM:** Built specifically for selling. Better suited for creators with higher-ticket offers who are doing significant volume.

## Follow-Up Cadences

The majority of sales happen after the follow-up, not after the first conversation. Research consistently shows that 80% of sales require at least 5 touchpoints, yet most sellers give up after 1-2. In DM selling, your follow-up cadence is your safety net.

### The Standard Follow-Up Cadence

**Day 1 (Conversation):** Complete the 5-Message Framework. If they say they need to think about it, confirm when you should check in.

**Day 3 (First Follow-Up):** A brief, value-added check-in.
> "Hey [Name], just wanted to circle back. I was thinking about what you mentioned about [their specific challenge] and remembered this [resource/tip/article] that might help. No pressure on the [offer] -- just wanted to share."

**Day 7 (Second Follow-Up):** Reference their situation and gently remind them of the offer.
> "Hi [Name], how is [their project/challenge] going? I have had a couple people start [your offer] this week and it reminded me of our conversation. Still open if you want to chat more about it."

**Day 14 (Third Follow-Up):** A final check-in before moving them to a longer-term nurture.
> "Hey [Name], I know timing is everything. I wanted to let you know I am here whenever you are ready. In the meantime, I thought you might find [recent content you posted] useful. Rooting for you."

**After Day 14:** Move them to a long-term nurture list. Continue to engage with their content (like, comment) and share valuable content periodically. Do not send more direct follow-ups about your offer.

### Follow-Up Principles

- **Always add value in every follow-up.** Never send a message that is only "just checking in." Attach something useful: a resource, an insight, a relevant piece of content.
- **Reference something specific.** Show that you remember their situation. This is easy if you are maintaining your CRM.
- **Three strikes and nurture.** After three unanswered follow-ups, stop sending direct messages about your offer. Shift to long-term nurture through content.
- **If they say no, honor it.** A clear "no" is not an invitation for more follow-ups. Thank them, offer your free resources, and move on.

## Scaling With Templates While Staying Personal

As your DM volume grows, you will face a tension: you need efficiency (templates), but you also need personalization (authenticity). Here is how to balance both.

### The 70/30 Rule

Every DM you send should be approximately 70% template and 30% personalized. The template provides the structure and key messaging. The personalization makes it feel human.

**Example:**

Template (70%):
> "Hey [Name]! Here is the [resource] you asked about. I am curious -- what specifically caught your eye about it? Are you currently working on [topic] or still in the planning phase?"

Personalization (30%):
> "Hey Marcus! Here is the pricing framework you asked about. I noticed from your profile that you are a UX consultant -- this should be especially relevant for service-based pricing. I am curious -- are you currently working on restructuring your rates, or still in the figuring-it-out phase?"

The template saved you 60 seconds. The personalization (referencing his name, profession, and a relevant detail) ensures the message does not feel mass-produced.

### Building a Template Library

Create a document with templates for every stage of the DM selling process:

1. **Opener templates** (3-5 variations) -- for the initial DM after someone triggers the automation
2. **Diagnostic question templates** (5-8 variations) -- for qualifying conversations
3. **Insight templates** (3-5 variations) -- for sharing expertise
4. **Offer presentation templates** (2-3 variations) -- for presenting your offer
5. **Follow-up templates** (3-4 variations) -- for the follow-up cadence
6. **Objection response templates** (5-8 variations) -- for common objections (price, timing, uncertainty)
7. **Close templates** (2-3 variations) -- for both yes and no outcomes

Rotate your templates regularly. If you find yourself sending the exact same Message 1 to 50 people in a week, the language will start to feel stale -- and savvy prospects will notice.

## Measuring Your DM Selling Performance

You cannot improve what you do not measure. Track these metrics weekly:

### Top-of-Funnel Metrics

- **DM triggers per week:** How many people entered your DM automation or messaged you organically?
- **Automation delivery rate:** What percentage of triggered DMs were actually delivered? (Check for failures)
- **Response rate to Message 1:** What percentage of people reply to your initial DM?

### Mid-Funnel Metrics

- **Conversation depth:** On average, how many messages deep do your DM conversations go?
- **Qualification rate:** What percentage of conversations reach Message 3 (the insight/qualification stage)?
- **Offer presentation rate:** What percentage of qualified conversations receive your offer (Message 4)?

### Bottom-of-Funnel Metrics

- **Call booking rate:** What percentage of people who receive your offer book a call?
- **Direct purchase rate:** What percentage purchase directly from the DM conversation?
- **Close rate:** Of everyone who received an offer, what percentage became paying clients?
- **Revenue per conversation:** Total DM-attributed revenue divided by total conversations started.

### Benchmark Targets

For a well-optimized DM selling system, aim for:

- Response rate to Message 1: **40-60%**
- Qualification rate: **30-50%** of responders
- Offer presentation rate: **50-70%** of qualified conversations
- Close rate: **15-30%** of offers presented
- Revenue per conversation: **$5-50** (varies widely by offer price)

If any metric is significantly below these benchmarks, it tells you where to focus your optimization efforts.

## Putting It All Together: Your Weekly Rhythm

| Activity | Frequency | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Morning DM block (triage, respond, follow up) | Daily | 20 min |
| Evening DM block (engage, respond, outreach) | Daily | 25 min |
| CRM update | Daily (end of evening block) | 5 min |
| DM bait content creation (Lesson 6) | 2-3x per week | 30 min each |
| Social proof collection (Lesson 7) | Weekly (Friday) | 10 min |
| Pipeline review and metrics tracking | Weekly (Sunday) | 15 min |
| Template library update | Monthly | 30 min |

**Total weekly investment: approximately 6-7 hours.**

This is the reality of DM selling as a system. It is not a one-time hack or a quick tactic. It is a disciplined, daily practice that compounds over time. The creator who follows this system for 90 days will have a fundamentally different relationship with revenue than the one who does it sporadically.
