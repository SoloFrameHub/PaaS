## The Weekly LinkedIn AI Workflow (5 Hours Total)

This is your **complete operating rhythm**. Every activity is time-boxed, AI-assisted, and designed to generate pipeline without burning you out.

### Monday: Content Planning & Drafting (60 min)

**Goal:** Generate 3-5 posts for the week, schedule them, and prep 1 carousel.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Step 1: Topic Selection (10 min)">

Open Taplio's AI Post Generator. Input 3-5 topics from your content backlog:
- A recent client win or case study
- A contrarian take on your industry
- A tactical how-to (3-5 steps)
- A personal story related to your niche
- A question to drive engagement

**AI Prompt (in ChatGPT):**
```
Based on my voice guide and these 5 topics, generate 5 LinkedIn post drafts (150-250 words each) in my style. Use the following archetypes: Story, Contrarian, Tactical, Social Proof, Question.

Topics:
1. [Topic 1]
2. [Topic 2]
3. [Topic 3]
4. [Topic 4]
5. [Topic 5]
```

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: AI Draft + Edit (30 min)">

ChatGPT generates 5 drafts. For each:

1. **Facts Check:** Verify any statistics, claims, or examples
2. **Voice Check:** Read aloud. Does it sound like you? Edit awkward phrasing
3. **Sales Linter Check:** Does it move people toward your offer? Add a subtle CTA if missing

**The 3-Pass Edit:**
- Pass 1: Fix factual errors and add specificity
- Pass 2: Adjust tone and voice (remove AI-isms like "In today's fast-paced world")
- Pass 3: Strengthen the hook (first 2 lines) and CTA (last 2 lines)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: Schedule in Taplio (10 min)">

Copy edited posts into Taplio. Schedule:
- Monday: Story or Social Proof post
- Wednesday: Tactical or Contrarian post
- Friday: Question or engagement driver

**Optimal Posting Times (based on your audience):**
- B2B SaaS: 7-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM (local time)
- Coaches/Consultants: 6-8 AM, 11 AM-1 PM
- Creators: 8-10 AM, 7-9 PM

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 4: Carousel Prep (10 min)">

Choose 1 post to expand into a carousel (5-10 slides). Use ChatGPT:

```
Turn this LinkedIn post into a 7-slide carousel. Each slide should have:
- A headline (6-8 words)
- 2-3 bullet points or a short paragraph (30-50 words)
- A visual suggestion (icon, chart, or image type)

Post: [paste post]
```

Export slide text to Canva. Use a LinkedIn carousel template. Design in 15-20 min (or delegate to a VA for $10-15).

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

### Tuesday: Prospect Research & Saved Search Review (60 min)

**Goal:** Identify 10-15 high-fit prospects, generate AI research briefs, and prep for engagement.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Step 1: Review Sales Nav Saved Searches (15 min)">

Open your 3 primary saved searches (from Lesson 6):
1. **"Changed Jobs in Past 90 Days"** — High-intent prospects
2. **"Posted in Past 30 Days"** — Active on LinkedIn
3. **"Shares Connections"** — Warm intro potential

Sort by relevance. Export top 25 from each search (75 total).

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: AI Scoring Against ICP (15 min)">

Paste the 75 prospects into ChatGPT with this prompt:

```
I have 75 prospects from LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Score each 1-10 against my ICP criteria:
- [ICP criterion 1]
- [ICP criterion 2]
- [ICP criterion 3]

Return a table: Name | Company | Score | Reason

Prospects:
[paste list]
```

ChatGPT returns a scored table. Filter for 8+ scores. You should have 10-20 prospects.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: Generate Research Briefs (30 min)">

For the top 10-15 prospects, generate 1-page research briefs using the template from Lesson 7.

**Batch Prompt (in ChatGPT):**
```
Generate a 1-page prospect research brief for each of the following prospects. Use this template:

**Prospect Overview:** [Name, role, company, tenure]
**Company Context:** [Size, stage, recent news, funding]
**Content Analysis:** [What they post about, their positions]
**Connection Points:** [Mutual connections, shared interests]
**Outreach Angle:** [Recommended approach]

Prospects:
1. [Name, LinkedIn URL, recent post headline]
2. [Name, LinkedIn URL, recent post headline]
...
```

Save briefs in HubSpot CRM as notes on each contact record.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

### Wednesday-Thursday: Strategic Engagement (60 min total, 30 min/day)

**Goal:** Comment on 10-15 prospect posts per day, building visibility and warmth before outreach.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Step 1: Identify Engagement Targets (5 min/day)">

From your researched prospect list, find 10-15 who posted in the last 24-48 hours.

**Sales Nav Filter:**  
Saved Search → Filter by "Posted in Past 7 Days" → Sort by "Recent Activity"

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: AI-Assisted Comment Drafting (20 min/day)">

For each post, use ChatGPT to draft a SERVE-framework comment:

**Prompt:**
```
Draft a LinkedIn comment for this post using the SERVE framework:
- Specific reference to their point
- Experience or example I can share
- Relevant question to continue dialogue
- Value-add (data, link, framework)
- Encourage (genuine appreciation)

Post: [paste post text]
My context: [your expertise/experience relevant to the post]
```

ChatGPT generates a 3-5 sentence comment. Edit for authenticity. Post manually.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: Track Engagement in CRM (5 min/day)">

Log each comment in HubSpot:
- Contact record → Activity → "Commented on LinkedIn post"
- Note: [Post topic + your comment angle]

This creates a warm touchpoint history before you send a connection request.

</Slide>

### Friday: Connection Requests & DM Follow-Up (90 min)

**Goal:** Send 10-15 personalized connection requests to engaged prospects. Follow up with 5-10 accepted connections via DM.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Step 1: Send Connection Requests (30 min)">

For prospects you've engaged with 2-3 times this week, send connection requests with personalized notes.

**AI Prompt (ChatGPT):**
```
Write a 250-character LinkedIn connection request note for this prospect:

Prospect: [Name, role, company]
Context: I commented on their posts about [topic 1] and [topic 2] this week
Mutual connection: [Name, if applicable]
Reason to connect: [Your value prop in 1 sentence]
```

**Example Output:**
"Hi Sarah, enjoyed your posts on attribution challenges this week. I work with Series B SaaS teams on content ROI frameworks. Would love to connect and share insights. (Mutual: John Smith)"

Send 10-15 requests. LinkedIn limit: ~50/week for established accounts.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: Follow Up with Accepted Connections (30 min)">

For connections accepted in the past 7 days, send a value-first DM (not a pitch).

**AI Prompt (ChatGPT):**
```
Write a LinkedIn DM for a newly accepted connection. The goal is to start a conversation, not pitch.

Prospect: [Name, role, company]
Their recent activity: [Post topic or company news]
Value I can offer: [Framework, resource, intro, insight]

Keep it under 100 words. End with a low-friction question.
```

**Example Output:**
"Thanks for connecting, Sarah! Saw your post on attribution struggles — that's a pain point I hear constantly from Series B marketing leaders. I put together a simple framework that maps content touches to revenue (no attribution tool required). Would you find a 1-pager on this useful? Happy to send it over."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: Voice Note Follow-Up (Optional, 30 min)">

For high-priority prospects who haven't replied to your DM in 3-5 days, send a 30-60 second voice note.

**Script Template (AI-generated):**
```
"Hey [Name], [Your Name] here. I sent you a note earlier this week about [topic]. I know you're busy, so I wanted to quickly share [one specific insight or example]. [30-second explanation]. If this resonates, let me know — happy to send over that framework. If not, no worries at all. Either way, appreciate the connection."
```

Record in LinkedIn's native voice note feature (60-second max). No automation risk.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

### The 10 Commandments of LinkedIn AI Safety

1. **Never automate connection requests.** Every request must be manually sent.
2. **Never automate DMs.** Every message must be manually sent (voice notes are native, so they're safe).
3. **Never exceed 50 connection requests per week** (100/week max for established accounts with high SSI).
4. **Never exceed 75 DMs per day** (50/day is safer for new accounts).
5. **Never use tools that inject code into LinkedIn's DOM** (Expandi, Dripify, Linked Helper, Octopus CRM).
6. **Never scrape profile data at scale** (Phantombuster, unapproved browser extensions).
7. **Never use fake profiles or profile networks** (instant permanent ban).
8. **Never auto-like or auto-comment** (even "smart" automation is detectable).
9. **Always use approved LinkedIn Marketing Partners** for scheduling (Taplio, Buffer, Hootsuite).
10. **Always keep a human in the loop** — AI suggests, you execute.
