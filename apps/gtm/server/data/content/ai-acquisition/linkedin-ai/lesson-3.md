Now what?

Most solo founders post it once on LinkedIn with "New blog post is live 👇" and wonder why it gets 12 views and 2 pity likes from their mom and a former coworker.

**The difference?** They understand the Content Repurposing Pyramid. You're about to master it.

## The Content Repurposing Pyramid

Think of your content creation like a pyramid:

**Top (10% of effort):** One long-form piece — blog post, podcast episode, newsletter, video tutorial, workshop recording

**Middle (20% of effort):** AI-powered extraction and reformatting

**Bottom (70% of output):** 8-12 LinkedIn-native pieces that feel original, not recycled

The math is brutal: creating 8 original LinkedIn posts from scratch = 4-6 hours. Extracting 8 posts from existing long-form content with AI = 45-90 minutes.

## AI-Powered Extraction: The 4-Step Workflow

Here's how to turn one long-form piece into a week's worth of LinkedIn content:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Step 1: Feed the AI">

Copy your long-form content into ChatGPT or Claude with this prompt:

```
Extract the following from this [blog post/newsletter/transcript]:
1. The 3 most contrarian or surprising insights
2. The 5 most tactical, actionable steps
3. Any data points, statistics, or case study results
4. 3-5 quotable one-liners
5. The core framework or model (if any)

Format each as a bullet point with context.
```

The AI will give you a structured breakdown in 30 seconds. This is your raw material.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: Choose Your Formats">

Now decide which LinkedIn formats to use. The 5 highest-performing formats:

**Text Posts (150-300 words):**
- Story version (personal experience)
- Tactical version (how-to with steps)
- Contrarian version (challenge common belief)
- Data version (stats + insight)

**Carousels (5-10 slides):**
- Framework breakdown
- Before/after transformation
- Common mistakes + fixes

**Polls:**
- Test assumptions from your content
- Engage audience on key decision points

**Video Clips (30-90 seconds):**
- Pull the most quotable 60 seconds
- Add captions with Opus Clip or Descript

**Comment Hooks:**
- Questions you can ask on others' posts
- Insights you can add to relevant discussions

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: AI Reformatting">

For each format, use AI to translate your content. Example prompt for a text post:

```
Turn this insight: [paste insight from Step 1]

Into a 200-word LinkedIn post that:
- Opens with a hook (question, bold statement, or mini-story)
- Uses short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- Includes line breaks for readability
- Ends with a question or call-to-action
- Matches this voice: [paste your voice guide from Lesson 2]
```

The AI will draft it in your style. You edit for authenticity.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 4: The 3-Pass Review">

Before scheduling, run each piece through:

**Pass 1: Facts Check**
- Are statistics accurate?
- Are examples real and verifiable?
- Did AI hallucinate any details?

**Pass 2: Voice Check**
- Would I actually say this?
- Does it sound like me or like generic AI?
- Are there any AI-isms to remove? ("In today's fast-paced world," "It's important to note," "At the end of the day")

**Pass 3: Sales Linter**
- Does this move people toward my offer?
- Is there a clear next step?
- Would my ICP find this valuable?

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Format Translation: Same Insight, Different Angles

The secret to repurposing without feeling repetitive: **change the angle, not just the format**.

Let's say your long-form piece includes this insight:

> "Most founders target 'everyone' because they're afraid of leaving money on the table. But diffuse targeting means diffuse messaging, which means nobody resonates."

Here's how to remix it across 4 different angles:

Now try the other angles:

**Story Angle:** "I spent 6 months targeting 'B2B SaaS founders.' Got 2% reply rates. Then I narrowed to 'B2B SaaS founders doing $10K-50K MRR in HR tech.' Reply rate jumped to 18%. Same offer. Different specificity."

**Contrarian Angle:** "Unpopular opinion: Your TAM is too big. If your ICP is 'small businesses,' you don't have an ICP. You have a guess. Narrow until it hurts, then narrow more."

**Data Angle:** "We analyzed 10,000 cold emails. Generic targeting ('small business owners'): 1.2% reply rate. Specific targeting ('agencies with 10-50 employees in healthcare'): 12.8% reply rate. 10x difference. Same effort."

If someone who read your original long-form piece sees your LinkedIn post and thinks "Oh, that's from the blog," you didn't remix enough. Each piece should feel standalone.
</InsightCard>

## The Freshness Window

Here's a critical insight most creators miss:

**Repurposed content performs best within 7-14 days of original publication.**

Why? Because:
1. The topic is still fresh in your mind
2. Your audience may have seen the original and wants more depth
3. You can reference "I wrote about this last week" to create continuity
4. SEO and social signals are still active

After 30 days, you need to add a new angle or updated data to make it feel current.

## Cross-Platform Syndication Strategy

LinkedIn is not Twitter. Twitter is not your newsletter. Each platform has different expectations:

**LinkedIn:**
- Prefers 150-300 word posts
- Line breaks are essential
- Professional tone, but personal stories work
- CTAs should be soft ("What's your experience?") not hard ("Buy my course")

**Twitter/X:**
- Shorter (280 characters or thread)
- More casual, punchy
- Threads perform well for tactical content
- Direct CTAs work better

**Newsletter:**
- Long-form (800-2,000 words)
- Deep dives, not surface-level
- Can be more promotional
- Include multiple CTAs

**The Rule:** Adapt tone, length, and CTA for each platform. Don't just copy-paste.

## Common Repurposing Mistakes

Avoid these traps:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Mistake 1: Copy-Paste Syndrome">

**The mistake:** Posting the exact same text on LinkedIn that you published in your newsletter.

**Why it fails:** LinkedIn's algorithm can detect duplicate content. More importantly, your audience can tell you're phoning it in.

**The fix:** Reformat for the platform. Add line breaks. Change the hook. Adjust the CTA.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Mistake 2: Generic AI Voice">

**The mistake:** Using AI-generated posts without editing for your voice.

**Why it fails:** Your audience can spot AI-isms a mile away. "In today's fast-paced world" screams "I didn't write this."

**The fix:** Always run the 3-pass review. Edit ruthlessly. Add personal details only you would know.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Mistake 3: No Angle Variation">

**The mistake:** Extracting 5 posts that all say the same thing in slightly different words.

**Why it fails:** Your audience sees repetition, not consistency.

**The fix:** Use the 4 angles framework: Story, Tactical, Contrarian, Data. Each should feel distinct.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Mistake 4: Ignoring the Sales Linter">

**The mistake:** Posting valuable content that doesn't move people toward your offer.

**Why it fails:** Engagement without conversion is just vanity metrics.

**The fix:** Every post should have a subtle next step. "DM me if you want the template." "Comment with your biggest challenge." "Link to full framework in comments."

</Slide>

<Slide title="Mistake 5: Batch-and-Forget">

**The mistake:** Scheduling 2 weeks of content and never engaging with comments.

**Why it fails:** LinkedIn rewards engagement. If you don't reply to comments, the algorithm assumes your content isn't worth showing.

**The fix:** Set a daily 10-minute block to reply to comments on your posts. Use AI to draft replies if needed, but always personalize.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>
