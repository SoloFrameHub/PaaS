## The Voice-First Drafting Workflow

Most people use AI backwards. They ask ChatGPT to "write a LinkedIn post about positioning" and get generic slop that sounds like every other AI-generated post.

The pros do it differently: **they train the AI on their voice first.**

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Voice Training Principle">
AI is a ghost-drafter, not the author. Feed it 10-20 of your best posts as style samples BEFORE asking it to generate new ones. The AI learns your sentence rhythm, vocabulary, and tone — then drafts in YOUR voice, not its default corporate-speak.
</InsightCard>

### The 4-Step Voice Training Protocol

<ProgressiveReveal title="Voice Training Steps" persistKey="linkedin-ai-L2-voice-training">

<RevealSection title="Step 1: Collect Your Best Posts (10-20 samples)">

Go to your LinkedIn profile. Find your top 10-20 posts by engagement (likes + comments). Copy the full text of each into a document.

**What to look for:**
- Posts where people replied with "This is exactly what I needed"
- Posts that led to DMs or meeting requests
- Posts that feel most "you" when you read them back

**Avoid:**
- Posts that went viral but feel off-brand
- Posts you wrote when trying to sound like someone else
- Generic tips or listicles that could be anyone's

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Step 2: Identify Your Voice Patterns">

Read through your collected posts and note:

- **Sentence length:** Do you write short, punchy sentences? Or longer, flowing ones?
- **Vocabulary:** What words do you use repeatedly? What jargon do you avoid?
- **Tone:** Conversational? Authoritative? Provocative? Encouraging?
- **Structure:** Do you start with stories? Questions? Bold claims?
- **Hooks:** What first lines made people click "see more"?

<ExampleCard label="Voice Pattern Example: Technical Founder">
**Sentence length:** Mix of 1-sentence paragraphs and 2-3 sentence explanations  
**Vocabulary:** "Ship," "iterate," "signal," "noise" — avoids "synergy," "leverage," "ecosystem"  
**Tone:** Direct, slightly skeptical, data-driven  
**Structure:** Often starts with a contrarian claim, then backs it with data  
**Hooks:** "Everyone says X. The data says Y."
</ExampleCard>

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Step 3: Write Your Voice Guide (200 words)">

Synthesize your patterns into a 200-word instruction set for the AI. This becomes your "voice DNA."

**Template:**

```
My LinkedIn voice:
- Sentence structure: [short/long/mix, rhythm patterns]
- Vocabulary: [words I use often] / [words I never use]
- Tone: [conversational/authoritative/provocative/etc.]
- Perspective: [first-person stories, second-person advice, data-first]
- Hooks: [question/story/contrarian claim/data point]
- Avoid: [corporate jargon, buzzwords, generic phrases]
- Signature phrases: [2-3 phrases I use repeatedly]
```

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Step 4: Test and Refine">

Generate 5 test posts using your voice guide. Read each aloud. Ask yourself:

1. **Could only I write this?** (Or could it be anyone in my space?)
2. **Does it contain a specific example from my experience?**
3. **Would I say this on a sales call?**
4. **Does it avoid generic AI-isms?** ("In today's fast-paced world...")

If any post fails these tests, refine your voice guide and regenerate.

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## The 5 LinkedIn Post Archetypes

Not all posts serve the same purpose. The best LinkedIn creators rotate through 5 core archetypes, each designed to trigger different engagement patterns.

<SlideNavigation>

<Slide title="1. Story Posts">

**Purpose:** Build relatability and trust through personal experience

**Structure:**
- Hook: Set the scene (time, place, situation)
- Conflict: What went wrong or what you struggled with
- Resolution: What you learned or how you solved it
- Takeaway: The lesson readers can apply

**Engagement driver:** People love seeing the human behind the expertise

<ExampleCard label="Story Post Example">
**Hook:** "Three months ago, I sent 500 cold emails and got 2 replies."

**Conflict:** "I was doing everything the 'gurus' said: personalization, value-first, multi-touch sequences. But my inbox was a graveyard."

**Resolution:** "Then I realized: I was targeting everyone. I narrowed to 50 agencies with 10-50 employees in fintech. Same message quality, 10x better targeting."

**Takeaway:** "Next 50 emails: 18 replies, 6 meetings. Outreach works fine. Targeting 'everyone' is what failed."
</ExampleCard>

**When to use:** 1-2x per week, especially when you have a recent lesson learned

</Slide>

<Slide title="2. Contrarian Posts">

**Purpose:** Challenge conventional wisdom and position yourself as an independent thinker

**Structure:**
- Hook: "Everyone says X. But..."
- The conventional wisdom (what most people believe)
- Why it's wrong (data, experience, logic)
- What to do instead
- The result you've seen

**Engagement driver:** Controversy and debate in comments

<ExampleCard label="Contrarian Post Example">
**Hook:** "Everyone says 'post daily on LinkedIn.' But that's terrible advice for solo founders."

**Conventional wisdom:** "The algorithm rewards consistency. More posts = more reach."

**Why it's wrong:** "You're not a content creator. You're a founder who needs customers. 7 mediocre posts/week < 3 excellent posts that actually move prospects."

**What to do instead:** "Post 3x/week. Spend the other 4 days commenting on prospect posts and sending DMs. That's where the meetings come from."

**Result:** "My clients who switched: 40% less time on content, 2x more inbound meetings."
</ExampleCard>

**When to use:** 1x per week max (overuse damages credibility)

</Slide>

<Slide title="3. Tactical Posts">

**Purpose:** Provide step-by-step value that readers can implement immediately

**Structure:**
- Hook: Promise a specific outcome
- Context: Why this matters / when to use it
- Steps: 3-7 numbered steps with brief explanations
- CTA: Invite questions or shares

**Engagement driver:** Saves/shares (people bookmark for later reference)

<ExampleCard label="Tactical Post Example">
**Hook:** "How to write a cold email that gets 15%+ reply rates (5-step framework)"

**Context:** "Most cold emails fail because they're about you, not them. This framework flips that."

**Steps:**
1. Research: Find one specific thing about their business (recent hire, product launch, content topic)
2. Hook: Reference that thing in the first line
3. Problem: Name the pain point that thing suggests
4. Proof: One sentence of social proof (client result, data point)
5. CTA: Ask for 15 minutes, not a demo

**CTA:** "What's your biggest cold email challenge? Drop it in the comments."
</ExampleCard>

**When to use:** 2-3x per week (your content workhorse)

</Slide>

<Slide title="4. Social Proof Posts">

**Purpose:** Build credibility through results and data

**Structure:**
- Hook: Lead with the result (number, outcome, transformation)
- Context: What was the situation before
- Method: What you did (high-level, not full tutorial)
- Lesson: The principle behind the result
- Offer: Soft CTA to work together or learn more

**Engagement driver:** Aspirational (people want that result)

<ExampleCard label="Social Proof Post Example">
**Hook:** "Client update: $0 → $40K MRR in 90 days with zero paid ads."

**Context:** "They came to me with a great product, no customers, and a $500/month marketing budget."

**Method:** "We skipped ads entirely. Built a 200-person ICP list, sent 10 personalized emails/day, posted 3x/week on LinkedIn. That's it."

**Lesson:** "You don't need a big budget. You need a narrow target and relentless consistency."

**Offer:** "If you're stuck at $0-10K MRR and want a similar roadmap, DM me 'roadmap' and I'll send you the framework."
</ExampleCard>

**When to use:** 1x per week (overuse feels braggy)

</Slide>

<Slide title="5. Question Posts">

**Purpose:** Drive engagement and learn about your audience

**Structure:**
- Hook: Ask a specific, relevant question
- Context: Why you're asking / why it matters
- Options: Give 2-3 possible answers (if multiple choice)
- Invitation: Ask people to comment with their answer

**Engagement driver:** Low barrier to participate (just comment)

<ExampleCard label="Question Post Example">
**Hook:** "Quick poll: What's your biggest LinkedIn challenge right now?"

**Context:** "I'm planning my content for Q2 and want to focus on what you're actually struggling with."

**Options:**
A) Finding time to post consistently
B) Writing posts that don't sound generic
C) Turning engagement into actual conversations
D) Something else (comment below)

**Invitation:** "Drop your letter (or 'D' + your challenge) in the comments. I'll create content around the top 3."
</ExampleCard>

**When to use:** 1x per week (great for Fridays)

</Slide>

</SlideNavigation>

## The 5-2-1 Content Mix

Now that you know the archetypes, here's how to combine them into a sustainable weekly rhythm:

<InsightCard icon="📊" title="The Weekly Content Formula">
**5 value posts** (teach/share) + **2 story posts** (personal/relatable) + **1 CTA post** (offer/invite) = 8 posts per week

For solo founders with limited time: **3 value posts + 1 story + 1 CTA = 5 posts per week**
</InsightCard>

**Why this mix works:**

1. **Value posts** (Tactical, Contrarian, Question) build authority and attract your ICP
2. **Story posts** build trust and make you memorable
3. **CTA posts** (Social Proof with soft offer) convert lurkers into conversations

**The mistake most founders make:** All value, no stories, no CTAs. You become a free educational resource, not a business.

## Carousel Creation with AI

Carousels (multi-slide posts) get **1.6-2.2x more reach** than text-only posts on LinkedIn. They're also easier to create with AI than you think.

### The 3-Step Carousel Workflow

**Step 1: Choose Your Source Content**

Carousels work best when adapted from:
- A blog post or newsletter section (500-1000 words)
- A framework or process you teach (3-7 steps)
- A case study or client result (before/after/method)
- A contrarian argument (conventional wisdom → why it's wrong → what to do instead)

**Step 2: AI Extraction**

Paste your source content into ChatGPT/Claude with this prompt:

```
Convert this content into a 6-slide LinkedIn carousel:

Slide 1: Hook (attention-grabbing title + subtitle)
Slides 2-5: Core content (one key point per slide, max 30 words)
Slide 6: CTA (next step or call to action)

Make each slide punchy and visual-friendly. Avoid walls of text.

[PASTE CONTENT]
```

**Step 3: Visual Design**

Option A: **Canva** (more control, more time)
- Use LinkedIn carousel template
- Copy AI-generated text into each slide
- Add simple visuals (icons, charts, images)
- Export as PDF, upload to LinkedIn

Option B: **Gamma.app** (faster, less control)
- Paste AI-generated carousel text
- Gamma auto-generates slides with visuals
- Edit colors/fonts to match your brand
- Export and upload to LinkedIn

## Batch Content Production with AI

Here's the secret to consistent posting without burning out: **batch creation.**

Instead of writing one post per day (7 hours/week), you generate 5-10 posts in one 60-90 minute session, then schedule them across 1-2 weeks.

### The 60-Minute Batch Session

<ProgressiveReveal title="Batch Content Workflow" persistKey="linkedin-ai-L2-batch-workflow">

<RevealSection title="Minutes 0-10: Topic Selection">

Open a doc and list 5-10 topics from:
- Recent client questions or objections
- Your own recent lessons learned
- Industry news or trends you have a take on
- Frameworks or processes you teach
- Results you or clients achieved

**Pro tip:** Keep a running "content ideas" note on your phone. When a client asks a great question or you have an insight, add it immediately.

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 10-40: AI Drafting">

For each topic:

1. Choose the archetype (Story, Contrarian, Tactical, Social Proof, Question)
2. Paste this prompt into ChatGPT with your voice guide:

```
Write a LinkedIn post in my voice about [TOPIC].

Archetype: [Story/Contrarian/Tactical/Social Proof/Question]

Voice guide:
[PASTE YOUR VOICE GUIDE]

Topic details:
[2-3 sentences about what you want to say]
```

3. AI generates draft
4. Copy into your content doc
5. Move to next topic

**Goal:** 5-10 rough drafts in 30 minutes

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 40-55: The 3-Pass Edit">

For each draft, do 3 quick passes:

**Pass 1: Facts Check (2 min/post)**
- Are the claims accurate?
- Are the numbers/stats correct?
- Is the advice sound?

**Pass 2: Voice Check (2 min/post)**
- Does this sound like me?
- Would I actually say this?
- Remove any AI-isms ("In today's fast-paced world," "game-changer," "unlock")

**Pass 3: Sales Linter (1 min/post)**
- Does this move people toward my offer?
- Is there a clear next step (even if subtle)?
- Does it attract my ICP or random people?

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Minutes 55-60: Schedule & Format">

- Paste each post into AuthoredUp or Taplio
- Add line breaks for readability (every 1-2 sentences)
- Schedule across the next 7-14 days
- Add to your content calendar

**Done.** You just created 1-2 weeks of content in 60 minutes.

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

## The Authenticity Edit: Your Quality Control System

AI can draft fast. But speed without quality is just spam.

Here's your 3-pass quality control system to ensure every post maintains your voice and moves prospects forward:

### Pass 1: Facts Check

<InteractiveChecklist 
  title="Facts Check Questions"

    "Have I verified any client results or case studies mentioned?",

  ]} 
/>

**Why this matters:** One factual error can destroy credibility you spent months building. AI hallucinates. You verify.

### Pass 2: Voice Check

Ask yourself: **"Would I actually say this?"**

- Specific examples: "Last Tuesday, a client asked me..."
- Direct language: "This works" instead of "This can be a game-changer"
- Your actual vocabulary: The words you use on sales calls

Every post should move prospects closer to your offer — even if subtly.

   (Even if subtle: "If you're struggling with X, here's how I help clients solve it...")

<InsightCard icon="⚠️" title="The Over-Selling Trap">
Don't end every post with "DM me to work together." The ratio should be: **5 value posts → 1 soft CTA post → 1 direct offer post** per week. Too much selling = people unfollow.
</InsightCard>
