## The $40K Mistake

Maria ran a content marketing agency. After 18 months, she had 12 clients paying $2,500/month each — $30K MRR, stable but stuck.

Then she noticed something: three clients had mentioned hiring new team members. Two had launched new products. One had tripled their blog traffic using her framework.

**She'd been so focused on delivering the current scope that she missed the expansion signals hiding in plain sight.**

When she finally asked one client — "Now that your traffic has tripled, what's your next challenge?" — the answer was immediate: "We need help converting all this traffic. Can you build us a funnel?"

That conversation turned into a $15K project. Then a $5K/month retainer upgrade. Within 90 days, Maria had added $40K in expansion revenue just by **learning to recognize outcome-based triggers**.

For services and coaching businesses, expansion doesn't come from usage metrics. It comes from **client outcomes that signal readiness for the next level**.

---

## Why Outcome Triggers Are Different

<FlipCard 
  front="Usage Triggers vs. Outcome Triggers" 
  back="SaaS: 'They hit 80% of seat limit' → upgrade. Services: 'They achieved their first milestone' → what's next? Outcomes unlock expansion conversations naturally." 
/>

In SaaS, you track product usage. In services and coaching, you track **client progress and results**.

The expansion signal isn't "they used 15 hours this month" — it's "they just hired their first salesperson" or "they closed their biggest deal ever using your framework."

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Core Insight">
Every client outcome is a fork in the road: celebrate and move on, or celebrate and ask "what's next?" The second path is where expansion lives.
</InsightCard>

### The Data Behind Outcome-Based Expansion

<ExampleCard label="Research Finding">
Service businesses that upsell at outcome milestones convert at **45-60%** vs. 15-25% for time-based upsells. Coaching clients who complete a program purchase the next program at **30-50%** when offered at completion.

The "What's Next?" question generates **3x more expansion revenue** than waiting for clients to ask.
</ExampleCard>

**Why this works:** You're not selling — you're solving the natural next problem that success creates.

## The 5 Outcome-Based Triggers

Let's build your trigger detection system. Each trigger has a specific signal, a natural conversation opener, and a typical expansion path.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Trigger 1: First Success Milestone">

### The Signal
Your client reports a **quantifiable win** tied to your work:
- "We got our first 10 customers using your system"
- "That blog post you helped with got 50 leads"
- "I closed a $20K deal using your pitch framework"

### Why It's a Trigger
Success proves the system works. Now they want **more of that success** or **the next level of sophistication**.

### The Conversation
> "Congratulations on [specific result]! That's exactly what we were aiming for. Now that [milestone] is achieved, most clients find they need [logical next step]. Would that be helpful for you?"

### Typical Expansion Paths
- **For agencies:** First campaign success → ongoing campaign management retainer
- **For coaches:** First client win → help scaling to 10 clients
- **For consultants:** First implementation → rollout to other departments

<Slide title="Trigger 2: Goal Completion">

### The Signal
You've **delivered everything in the original scope**:
- Project complete, all deliverables handed off
- Coaching program finished, all modules done
- Retainer scope fully executed

### Why It's a Trigger
The relationship is at a natural decision point: renew as-is, end, or **expand to new scope**. This is your window.

### The Conversation
> "We've accomplished everything we set out to do — [list 2-3 key results]. I'm really proud of what we built together. What's the biggest challenge you're facing now?"

**Then listen.** Don't pitch. Let them articulate the next problem. If it's something you can help with: "That's actually something I help clients with. Want me to put together a proposal?"

### Typical Expansion Paths
- **For agencies:** Website project → ongoing SEO retainer
- **For coaches:** Group program → 1:1 intensive
- **For consultants:** Strategy project → implementation support

<Slide title="Trigger 3: Business Growth Signal">

### The Signal
Your client's business has **visibly grown**:
- Revenue increase (they mention hitting a milestone)
- Team expansion (hired new people)
- New product/service launch
- Office expansion, new market entry

### Why It's a Trigger
Growth creates new complexity. The systems that worked at $10K/month break at $50K/month. They need **upgraded infrastructure**.

### The Conversation
> "I noticed [specific growth signal — e.g., your team grew from 5 to 12 people]. That's fantastic! The systems we built were designed for your previous scale. As you grow, you'll likely need [upgraded capability]. Want to talk about what that would look like?"

### Typical Expansion Paths
- **For agencies:** Solo founder → team of 5 → need team training/SOPs
- **For coaches:** $5K/month → $20K/month → need advanced sales systems
- **For consultants:** Single location → multi-location → need scalable processes

<ExampleCard label="Real Example: The Team Growth Trigger">
A marketing consultant worked with a founder at $15K MRR. Six months later, the founder mentioned hiring a marketing manager.

The consultant's response: "That's great — having a dedicated marketer changes everything. The strategy we built was for you executing solo. Now that you have a team member, you'll need delegation frameworks and a content calendar system. I have a 30-day onboarding package that sets that up. Want to see it?"

**Result:** $8K project + upgraded retainer from $3K to $5K/month.
</ExampleCard>

</Slide>

<Slide title="Trigger 4: New Challenge Emergence">

### The Signal
In conversation (email, call, Slack), your client **mentions a new problem**:
- "We're struggling with [X]"
- "I wish we had a system for [Y]"
- "Do you know anyone who does [Z]?"

### Why It's a Trigger
They're already thinking about the problem. If it's adjacent to your expertise, **you're the trusted advisor** — the easiest path to solving it.

### The Conversation
> "You mentioned [problem]. That's actually something I help clients with. It's a bit outside our current scope, but I could put together a proposal for how we'd tackle it. Would that be useful?"

**Key:** Don't oversell. Acknowledge it's new scope. Give them the choice.

### Typical Expansion Paths
- **For agencies:** Content marketing → email marketing
- **For coaches:** Sales coaching → leadership coaching
- **For consultants:** Operations → hiring/recruiting

<InsightCard icon="💡" title="The Adjacent Expertise Rule">
Only expand into problems where you have **proven expertise**. If you don't, refer them to someone who does. Trust compounds; failed deliverables destroy it.
</InsightCard>

</Slide>

<Slide title="Trigger 5: Time-Based Milestone">

### The Signal
You hit a **calendar milestone** in the relationship:
- 90 days (first quarter review)
- 6 months (mid-year check-in)
- 12 months (annual review)

### Why It's a Trigger
Milestones create natural reflection points. "How far have we come? Where are we going next?" These questions **invite expansion conversations**.

### The Conversation
> "It's been [timeframe] since we started working together. Let's do a quick review: here's what we've accomplished [list 3-5 wins]. Here's what I see as the next phase [opportunities]. Does that align with where you want to go?"

### Typical Expansion Paths
- **For agencies:** 6-month review → scope expansion or rate adjustment
- **For coaches:** End of program → next-level program or 1:1 upgrade
- **For consultants:** Annual review → strategic advisor retainer

## The "What's Next?" Framework

The most powerful expansion tool for services and coaching is deceptively simple: **ask what's next**.

<ComparisonBuilder
  title="Practice Your 'What's Next?' Conversation"
  persistKey="expansion-L3-whatsnext-practice"

  expertExample="Congratulations on hitting 100 email subscribers in 60 days — that's exactly what we targeted.   criteria={[

  ]}
/>

### The Anatomy of a Great "What's Next?" Conversation

1. **Celebrate the win** (30 seconds) — Be specific. Reference the metric or outcome.
2. **Acknowledge the milestone** (15 seconds) — "This is a big deal. You've proven [X]."
3. **Name the pattern** (30 seconds) — "Most clients at this stage find that [next challenge] becomes the priority."
4. **Ask the question** (5 seconds) — "Is that true for you? What's the biggest challenge now?"
5. **Listen** (2 minutes) — Let them articulate it. Don't interrupt.
6. **Offer the path** (1 minute) — "I can help with that. Here's what it would look like..."

<ContextualNote showWhen={{ founderType: "coach" }} variant="personalized" title="For Coaches">
Your clients are buying **transformation**, not hours. The "What's Next?" conversation is about **the next level of transformation**, not more of the same. Frame expansion as "You've achieved X. The next level is Y. Here's how we get there."
</ContextualNote>

<ContextualNote showWhen={{ founderType: "technical" }} variant="personalized" title="For Technical Founders Offering Services">
You might feel uncomfortable with this conversation — it can feel like "selling." Reframe: you're **diagnosing the next technical challenge** and offering a solution. It's the same as recommending the next feature to build, just in a client context.
</ContextualNote>

## The Services Expansion Ladder

Not all expansions are created equal. Here's how to think about **progression** in your service/coaching business:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Level 1: Single Project">

**What it is:** One-time engagement with defined scope and deliverables.

**Price range:** $2K-10K (varies by service type)

**Trigger to next level:** Project completion + client satisfaction + ongoing need

**Example paths:**
- Website design → monthly maintenance retainer
- Sales training workshop → ongoing coaching
- Content strategy → content production retainer

**Your job at this level:** Deliver exceptional results and **plant seeds for ongoing work** during the project.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Level 2: Monthly Retainer">

**What it is:** Recurring monthly engagement with defined scope (hours, deliverables, or outcomes).

**Price range:** $2K-5K/month

**Trigger to next level:** Client needs **more scope** than the retainer covers (consistent overages, new requests)

**Example paths:**
- 10 hours/month → 20 hours/month
- Content only → content + email marketing
- 1:1 coaching → team coaching

**Your job at this level:** Track scope carefully. When you consistently deliver beyond the retainer, that's your expansion signal.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Level 3: Premium Retainer">

**What it is:** Expanded scope, higher commitment, often includes strategic advisory.

**Price range:** $5K-15K/month

**Trigger to next level:** You become **indispensable** to major business decisions

**Example paths:**
- Execution retainer → strategy + execution
- Single-channel → multi-channel
- Group coaching → 1:1 + group

**Your job at this level:** Position yourself as a **strategic partner**, not just a service provider.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Level 4: Strategic Advisor">

**What it is:** High-touch, high-trust relationship where you're consulted on all major decisions in your domain.

**Price range:** $15K-25K/month (or equity for startups)

**Trigger to next level:** This is often the ceiling for solo founders. Next level = hiring a team or transitioning to product.

**Example paths:**
- Fractional CMO
- Board advisor
- Executive coach

**Your job at this level:** Protect your time fiercely. At this level, you're selling **judgment and experience**, not hours.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Retainer Expansion: The Conversation

Let's practice the **retainer upgrade conversation** — one of the highest-value skills for service businesses.

### The Setup

You're approaching a **90-day or 6-month milestone** with a client on a monthly retainer. You've delivered solid results. You've noticed scope creep (they're asking for things beyond the original agreement) or their business has grown significantly.

**This is the moment to upgrade.**

<MiniRoleplay
  scenario="Your client says: 'We're really happy with the work, but we need more hours each month. Can we just add 5 hours to the retainer?'"
  role="You are the service provider responding"
  persistKey="expansion-L3-retainer-roleplay"
  modelResponse="I'm glad it's working well! Adding hours is definitely an option. Before we do that, let me ask: what's driving the need for more time? Is it more of the same work, or are there new projects/challenges coming up? [Listen] Based on that, I'd recommend we look at restructuring the retainer to include [new scope] rather than just adding hours. That way, we're solving the underlying need, not just patching it. Let me put together a proposal for what that would look like — probably in the $X-Y range. Does that make sense?"
/>

### The Retainer Review Script

Use this structure for **every milestone review**:

1. **Results Review** (5 min)
   - "Let's look at what we've accomplished: [list 3-5 specific wins with metrics]"
   - "Here's the ROI based on our work: [calculate if possible]"

2. **Future Vision** (5 min)
   - "Here's what I see for the next phase: [opportunities and challenges]"
   - "Based on where your business is heading, here are the gaps I'm noticing: [specific needs]"

3. **Upgrade Proposal** (5 min)
   - "To deliver [next-level outcomes], here's what I recommend: [upgraded scope/rate]"
   - "This would look like [specific deliverables] at [new price]"
   - "The ROI based on our results so far would be [calculation or estimate]"

4. **Permission Ask** (5 min)
   - "What do you think? Should I put together a formal proposal?"
   - **Then stop talking.** Let them respond.

<InsightCard icon="⚠️" title="The Trust Tax">

Before every expansion conversation, ask yourself:

## Productized Consulting: The Expansion Accelerator

One of the best ways to make expansion **repeatable and scalable** is to **productize** your consulting/coaching offerings.

### What Is Productized Consulting?

Instead of custom proposals for every engagement, you create **standardized packages** with:
- Fixed scope (specific deliverables)
- Fixed timeline (X weeks)
- Fixed price (not hourly)
- Clear boundaries (what's included and what's NOT)

<ExampleCard label="Example: The 'Content Audit + Strategy' Package">
**What's included:**
- Audit of last 90 days of content (blog, social, email)
- Competitive analysis (3 competitors)
- Content strategy document (topics, formats, distribution)
- 60-minute strategy presentation

**Timeline:** 3 weeks

**Price:** $4,500

**What's NOT included:** Content production, ongoing management, paid ad strategy

**Expansion path:** After delivery → monthly content production retainer at $3K-5K/month
</ExampleCard>

### Why Productized Consulting Converts Better

<FlipCard 
  front="Why does productized consulting convert at 30-40% higher rates?" 
  back="Clear scope = clear value. Clients know exactly what they're buying and what it costs. No 'let me think about it' — just yes or no." 
/>

**The data:** Productized consulting closes at **30-40% higher rates** than custom proposals because:
1. **Clarity** — No ambiguity about deliverables or price
2. **Speed** — No back-and-forth on scope; just "yes" or "no"
3. **Perceived value** — Fixed price feels more valuable than hourly (even if the math is the same)

## Tracking Outcome Triggers (Without Fancy Tools)

You don't need Gainsight to track outcome-based expansion triggers. Here's the **solo founder system**:

### The Simple Tracker

Create a **Google Sheet** or **Notion database** with these columns:

| Client Name | Current Engagement | Last Win/Milestone | Date Achieved | Next Challenge (Mentioned?) | Expansion Opportunity | Status |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------|---------------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Acme Corp | $3K/mo retainer | Launched new blog | 2025-01-15 | "Need help with email" | Email marketing add-on | Proposal sent |
| Beta Inc | Sales coaching | First $10K month | 2025-01-10 | Not yet discussed | Team training program | Schedule call |

**Update frequency:** Weekly, during your client review time (15 minutes)

### The Weekly Review Ritual

Every Friday (or Monday), spend **15 minutes** reviewing:

1. **Wins this week** — Did any client achieve a milestone? Add to tracker.
2. **Conversations** — Did any client mention a new challenge? Flag for expansion.
3. **Time-based milestones** — Are any clients approaching 90 days, 6 months, or 12 months? Schedule review.
4. **Scope creep** — Did you deliver beyond the retainer scope this week? That's a signal.
