## The $18,000 Kickoff Call That Never Happened

Sarah, a business coach, landed her biggest client yet: a $6,000 quarterly retainer. She sent a "Welcome! Let's get started" email with a Calendly link. The client booked a call... three weeks later.

By the time they finally connected, the client had already formed opinions about what "wasn't working," had tried implementing things on their own (badly), and was quietly wondering if they'd made a mistake.

Sarah spent the entire first month doing damage control instead of delivering value.

**The brutal truth:** In services and coaching, onboarding isn't separate from delivery. The relationship IS the product. And if you don't structure the first 90 days, your clients will structure it for you — usually in ways that lead to scope creep, mismatched expectations, and quiet churn at renewal time.

<InsightCard icon="⚡" title="The Services Onboarding Paradox">
SaaS products can recover from a bad first week. Service businesses can't. Your client's perception of value is set in the first 14 days, and changing it later requires 10x the effort.
</InsightCard>

This lesson gives you the exact 90-day rhythm that turns new clients into long-term relationships — without burning you out or letting scope creep destroy your margins.

## Why Services Onboarding Is Different (And Harder)

Let's be clear about what makes service/coaching onboarding fundamentally different from SaaS:

<FlipCard 
  front="SaaS Onboarding Goal" 
  back="Get the user to complete actions inside the product that predict retention (TTFV = Time to First Value)" 
/>

<FlipCard 
  front="Services Onboarding Goal" 
  back="Build trust, set expectations, and deliver the first meaningful outcome — all while establishing a working rhythm that prevents burnout" 
/>

**The core difference:** In SaaS, the product delivers value. In services, YOU deliver value. That means:

1. **Onboarding IS delivery** — There's no separation between "getting started" and "doing the work"
2. **Relationship quality = retention** — 68% of service churn stems from "feeling like the provider doesn't care," not poor quality work
3. **Expectations must be explicit** — What's "obvious" to you is invisible to the client until you say it out loud
4. **The first deliverable sets the bar** — Whatever you deliver in Week 2 becomes the baseline expectation for quality and speed

## The Kickoff Call Framework: Your First 30-45 Minutes

The kickoff call is the single highest-leverage hour in your entire client relationship. Get it right, and everything else flows. Get it wrong, and you'll spend months course-correcting.

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Pre-Call: The Intake Form">

**Before the kickoff call,** send a 5-7 question intake form. This isn't busywork — it's your research phase.

**Essential questions:**
- What's the primary outcome you want from our work together?
- What have you already tried? What worked/didn't work?
- What does success look like 90 days from now?
- What's your biggest concern or fear about this process?
- How do you prefer to communicate? (email, Slack, Voxer, etc.)

<Slide title="The Call: 30-45 Minute Structure">

Here's the exact structure that works:

**Minutes 0-5: Rapport + Context**
- "Thanks for filling out the intake — I read through your answers and I'm excited about [specific thing from their form]"
- "Before we dive in, tell me: what made you decide to work with me right now?"
- Listen for: urgency, pain level, decision-making context

**Minutes 5-15: Goals and Desired Outcomes**
- "You mentioned your primary goal is [X]. Walk me through what that looks like in detail."
- "What changes in your business/life when you achieve that?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you that this is achievable in 90 days?"

**Minutes 15-20: Communication Preferences and Availability**
- "You said you prefer [channel]. Here's how I typically use that..."
- "My response time is usually [X]. If something's urgent, here's how to flag it..."
- "Let's lock in our meeting cadence now — I recommend [weekly/biweekly] for the first month, then [cadence] after that."

**Minutes 20-30: Scope and Expectations Review**
- "Here's what's included in our work together: [list deliverables/sessions]"
- "Here's what's NOT included: [common scope-creep items]"
- "If something comes up that's outside scope, here's how we handle it: [process]"

**Minutes 30-40: First Assignment / Next Step**
- "Between now and our next session, here's what I need you to do: [specific, concrete task]"
- "This should take about [time estimate]. If it's taking longer, message me."
- "Our next session is [date/time]. We'll review [specific outcome]."

**Minutes 40-45: Questions**
- "What questions do you have for me?"
- "Anything I didn't cover that you were expecting?"

## The 90-Day Delivery Rhythm for Services

Now let's map the full 90-day journey. This rhythm works for consulting, done-for-you services, and hybrid models.

<ProgressiveReveal title="Services 90-Day Rhythm" persistKey="onboarding-L3-rhythm">

<RevealSection title="Week 1: Kickoff + Foundation">

**Goals:**
- Complete kickoff call
- Send working agreement
- Deliver first "quick win" (something tangible within 7 days)

**Activities:**
- Kickoff call (45 min)
- Intake form review
- First assignment given
- Working agreement sent

**Deliverable:**
A small, concrete output that proves value immediately. Examples:
- **Consultant:** Initial audit/assessment document
- **Coach:** Personalized action plan
- **Done-for-you:** First draft or prototype

<ExampleCard label="Case Study: The 7-Day Quick Win">
Marcus, a positioning consultant, used to spend Week 1 on "discovery." Clients felt like nothing was happening.

He restructured: Week 1 now includes a **Positioning Audit** — a 3-page document highlighting gaps in their current messaging with 5 specific fixes.

Result: Clients feel immediate value, and Marcus has a concrete artifact to reference throughout the engagement.
</ExampleCard>

**Time investment:** 3-4 hours (1 hour prep, 45 min call, 1-2 hours on quick win deliverable, 30 min follow-up)

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Weeks 2-4: First Deliverable Cycle">

**Goals:**
- Deliver primary first-month outcome
- Establish working rhythm
- Build trust through consistent communication

**Activities:**
- Weekly sessions (if applicable)
- Iterative feedback loops
- Mid-cycle check-in (Week 3)

**Deliverable:**
The first major output from your engagement. This sets the quality bar.

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ Waiting until Week 4 to show anything
- ❌ Delivering everything at once with no feedback loop
- ❌ Over-delivering to "prove value" (creates unsustainable expectations)

**Best practice:**
- ✅ Deliver in 2-3 iterations (draft → feedback → final)
- ✅ Set explicit "this is 80% done, I need your input on X" expectations
- ✅ Use async updates between sessions (Loom videos work great)

**Time investment:** 6-8 hours/week (sessions + deliverable work + communication)

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Weeks 5-8: Momentum + Iteration">

**Goals:**
- Deliver 2-3 more outputs/sessions
- Adjust approach based on what's working
- Deepen the relationship

**Activities:**
- Continue session cadence (may shift to biweekly)
- Mid-point review call (Week 6-7)
- Course corrections based on feedback

**The Mid-Point Review:**
This is a 20-30 minute call specifically about the process, not the work.

**Questions to ask:**
- "What's working well so far?"
- "What would you change about how we're working together?"
- "Are we on track to hit your 90-day goal? If not, what needs to shift?"
- "How's the communication cadence feeling — too much, too little, just right?"

<MiniRoleplay
  scenario="It's Week 7. The client says: 'I feel like we're moving slower than I expected.'"
  role="You are the consultant responding"
  persistKey="onboarding-L3-roleplay-pace"
  modelResponse="I appreciate you bringing that up. Let's look at what we've accomplished: [list specific outputs]. That said, I hear you — let's talk about what 'faster' looks like. />

**Time investment:** 5-6 hours/week (cadence may reduce slightly)

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Weeks 9-10: Results Review + Renewal Conversation">

**Goals:**
- Document results achieved
- Celebrate wins
- Discuss next phase (renewal, expansion, or graceful conclusion)

**Activities:**
- Results documentation session
- ROI/impact summary
- Renewal conversation (if applicable)

**The Results Review Document:**

Create a 2-3 page summary:
1. **Starting Point:** Where they were at Day 0
2. **What We Did:** Key deliverables and sessions
3. **Results Achieved:** Concrete outcomes (revenue, time saved, clarity gained, etc.)
4. **What's Next:** Recommended next steps (with or without you)

**The Renewal Conversation:**

Don't wait until Day 89 to bring this up. Week 9-10 is the right time.

**Script:**
"We're coming up on the end of our 90 days together. Let's talk about what's next. Based on where you are now, here are three options:

1. **Continue at current pace:** [Describe what next 90 days would focus on]
2. **Shift to maintenance mode:** [Lower-touch option]
3. **Wrap up and revisit later:** [Graceful exit with door open]

Which of these feels right to you?"

**Time investment:** 3-4 hours (results doc + renewal call + follow-up)

</RevealSection>

<RevealSection title="Weeks 11-12: Transition or Continuation">

**Goals:**
- Smooth handoff (if ending)
- Clear next-phase kickoff (if continuing)
- Request testimonial/referral

**Activities:**
- Final deliverable/session
- Transition documentation
- Testimonial request
- Referral ask (if appropriate)

**The Graceful Exit:**

If the engagement is ending, make it clean:
- Deliver all final assets in organized format
- Provide "maintenance guide" for sustaining results
- Offer 30-day email support for questions
- Ask for testimonial and referral

**The Continuation Kickoff:**

If renewing, treat it like a mini-kickoff:
- Review what worked/didn't in first 90 days
- Set new 90-day goal
- Adjust cadence/scope if needed
- Update working agreement

**Time investment:** 2-3 hours

</RevealSection>

</ProgressiveReveal>

Unlike services, coaching needs deeper psychological/behavioral context.

**Assessment areas:**
- Current state vs desired state (the gap)
- Past attempts and why they failed
- Internal blockers (mindset, beliefs, habits)
- External constraints (time, resources, support)
- Motivation level and commitment readiness

**2. The Weekly Accountability Rhythm (Weeks 2-4)**

Coaching without accountability is just expensive conversation.

**Structure:**
- **Weekly session (45 min):** Review progress, troubleshoot blockers, set next week's commitments
- **Between-session accountability:** Daily or 3x/week check-ins via Voxer/Slack/email
- **Commitment tracker:** Shared doc or tool where client logs daily actions

**Example commitment structure:**
- "This week, you're committing to: [3 specific actions]"
- "I'll check in on [days]. If I don't hear from you by [time], I'm texting you."
- "If you miss a commitment, we troubleshoot why in our next session — no judgment, just data."

**3. The Mid-Point Progress Review (Week 6-7)**

Same as services, but coaching-specific questions:

- "What's the biggest shift you've noticed in yourself?"
- "What's still hard? What's getting easier?"
- "Are the commitments we're setting the right size, or do we need to adjust?"
- "How's the accountability level — too much, too little, just right?"

**4. The Celebration + Next-Level Offer (Week 11-12)**

Coaching clients need explicit celebration of progress, not just results documentation.

**The Celebration Session:**
- Review starting point vs current state
- List specific wins (even small ones)
- Acknowledge effort and growth
- Discuss what's now possible that wasn't before

**The Next-Level Offer:**
- "You've built [foundation]. The natural next step is [next level]."
- "Here's what that would look like: [structure, cadence, investment]"
- "Or, if you want to consolidate what you've learned and fly solo for a bit, here's my recommendation: [maintenance plan]"

## Managing Expectations: The #1 Churn Prevention Tool

Let's address the elephant in the room: **68% of service/coaching churn stems from mismatched expectations, not poor quality work.**

Translation: You can be amazing at what you do and still lose clients if expectations aren't aligned.

<FlipCard 
  front="What causes mismatched expectations?" 
  back="Assuming the client knows what you know. They don't. Everything that's 'obvious' to you is invisible to them until you make it explicit." 
/>

### The 5 Expectations You Must Set Explicitly

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="1. Scope: What's Included vs Not Included">

**The mistake:** Assuming the client understands what's in scope.

**The fix:** List both what IS and what ISN'T included in the working agreement.

**Example (Consultant):**
- ✅ Included: Strategy sessions, deliverable reviews, email feedback within 48 hours
- ❌ Not included: Implementation work, technical setup, unlimited revisions

**Example (Coach):**
- ✅ Included: Weekly sessions, Voxer accountability, monthly progress reviews
- ❌ Not included: Daily check-ins, emergency calls outside scheduled sessions, business plan writing

</Slide>

<Slide title="2. Communication: Channels, Response Times, Urgency">

**The mistake:** Not defining how and when you'll communicate.

**The fix:** Explicit communication protocol in the working agreement.

**Template:**
- **Quick questions:** [Slack/email] — Response within [24 hours on weekdays]
- **Urgent issues:** [Text/call] — Response within [2 hours during business hours]
- **Non-urgent updates:** [Weekly email summary]
- **Off-limits:** [Weekends, after 6pm, holidays]

</Slide>

<Slide title="3. Timeline: When Things Happen">

**The mistake:** Vague timelines like "We'll get to that soon."

**The fix:** Specific dates and milestones in the working agreement.

**Example:**
- Week 1: Kickoff + intake
- Week 2: First draft of [deliverable]
- Week 4: Final version of [deliverable]
- Week 7: Mid-point review
- Week 12: Results review + renewal conversation

</Slide>

<Slide title="4. Effort: What You Need From Them">

**The mistake:** Assuming the client will "just know" what to do between sessions.

**The fix:** Explicit effort requirements and time estimates.

**Example:**
"Between our sessions, I'll need you to:
- Complete [specific task] — estimated time: 2-3 hours
- Review [deliverable] and provide feedback — estimated time: 30 minutes
- Implement [action item] — estimated time: 1 hour

If any of these are taking significantly longer, let me know immediately so we can adjust."

</Slide>

<Slide title="5. Success Metrics: What 'Good' Looks Like">

**The mistake:** Not defining success criteria upfront.

**The fix:** Concrete, measurable outcomes in the working agreement.

**Example (Consultant):**
"Success for this engagement means:
- Positioning statement that clearly differentiates you from competitors
- Messaging framework you can use across all channels
- 3 case studies that demonstrate ROI
- Confidence score of 8+ (out of 10) in your ability to articulate your value"

**Example (Coach):**
"Success for this 90 days means:
- Consistent $10K/mo revenue for 2+ consecutive months
- 5-7 hours/week on acquisition (down from current 15+)
- Confidence score of 8+ in your sales conversations
- 3+ client testimonials"

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Time Management: Delivering Without Burnout

Here's the hard truth: If you're doing 1:1 services or coaching, you can't scale infinitely. Your time is the constraint.

**The math:**
- 10 clients × 2 hours/week each = 20 hours/week on delivery
- Add 5 hours for admin, communication, prep = 25 hours/week
- That's already half your available work time (assuming 50-hour work week)

**The solution:** Ruthless efficiency in the 90-day rhythm.

### The Efficiency Strategies

**1. Batch Your Sessions**

Don't scatter sessions throughout the week. Batch them into 2-3 focused blocks.

**Example:**
- Tuesday: 9am-12pm (3 sessions back-to-back)
- Thursday: 1pm-4pm (3 sessions back-to-back)

**Why this works:** Reduces context-switching, keeps you in "coaching mode," and protects deep work time on other days.

**2. Standardize Your Prep**

Create a pre-session prep template:
- Review last session notes (5 min)
- Check client's progress tracker (5 min)
- Prepare 2-3 discussion points (5 min)

Total: 15 minutes per session, not 30-60.

**3. Use Async for Low-Stakes Communication**

Not everything needs a live call. Use:
- **Loom videos** for feedback on deliverables
- **Voice memos** (Voxer) for quick check-ins
- **Shared docs** for async collaboration

**4. Reduce Cadence After Month 1**

Most clients don't need weekly sessions forever. After the first 4 weeks:
- Shift to biweekly (cuts time in half)
- Or shift to monthly + async support
- Or offer group coaching for ongoing support

**5. Protect Your Non-Delivery Time**

Block time for:
- Acquisition (5-7 hours/week)
- Product/content work (5-10 hours/week)
- Admin (3-5 hours/week)

If delivery is eating into these, you're either:
- Overservicing (doing more than scope requires)
- Undercharging (can't afford to limit client count)
- Not automating/systematizing enough
