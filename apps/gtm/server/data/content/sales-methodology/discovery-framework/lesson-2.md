# The BANT Framework: The Classic Fast-Filter

If you have ever read a sales book from the last 70 years, you have heard of **BANT** (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline). Invented by IBM in the 1950s, it was designed for corporate armies selling mainframe computers. (2025 State of Sales).

Many "modern" sales gurus say BANT is dead. They are wrong. For a solo founder selling solutions in the $500 to $50,000 range, BANT is the ultimate **Fast-Filter**. When implemented correctly, it delivers **23% faster sales cycles** and **53% higher win rates** by ruthlessly disqualifying "tourists." (2025 State of Buyer Behavior).

The problem isn't the framework; it's the **Interrogation Style** founders use to apply it.

## 1. Deconstructing the 4 Pillars

### B - Budget: Qualifying Financial Fit
*   **The Old Way:** *"Do you have a budget for this?"*
*   **The 2026 Way:** *"Have you set aside resources to solve this [Pain], or are you just in information-gathering mode right now?"*
*   **Solo Insight:** For deals under $5k, "Budget" is often created on the fly via credit card. For deals over $25k, failing to find the budget line-item is the #1 cause of "No Decision." (2025 State of Sales).

### A - Authority: Mapping the Decision
*   **The Old Way:** *"Are you the decision-maker?"*
*   **The 2026 Way:** *"Who else would be upset if we launched this project without their specific input?"*
*   **Solo Insight:** Identifying the "Hidden Buyer" (e.g., the CFO or the spouse) early prevents the "I need to ask X" objection at the finish line.

### N - Need: Identifying Real Pain
*   **The Old Way:** *"What features do you need?"*
*   **The 2026 Way:** *"If you don't solve this problem in the next 90 days, what is the hard cost to your revenue or team morale?"*
*   **Solo Insight:** If the prospect can't quantify the pain, it's a "Vitamin," not a "Painkiller." Disqualify.

### T - Timeline: Understanding Urgency
*   **The Old Way:** *"When do you want to start?"*
*   **The 2026 Way:** *"Why now? You've had this problem for a year—why is today the day you decided to fix it?"*
*   **Solo Insight:** "Why now?" is the most diagnostic question in sales. If they can't answer it, they are a "Researcher," not a "Buyer." (2026 Acquisition Trends).

## 2. The Solo Adaptation: The MVQ Protocol

Since you don't have an army of SDRs, you must use **Minimum Viable Qualification (MVQ)**. (2026 Acquisition Trends).

1.  **Motor First:** Use the first 3 minutes to diagnose their **Velocity**. Are they talking fast and focused on results? (D/I Type).
2.  **Binary Filter:** You don't need all 4 BANT criteria to stay on the call, but you need at least **two** to move to a demo.
3.  **Low Friction:** Start with Need and Timeline. Discuss Authority and Budget only after you've quantified the value of the "Prescription."

## 3. Disqualification as a Competitive Advantage

<InsightCard icon="🎯" title="The Disqualification Advantage">Rigorous disqualification reduces forecast variance from 50% to under 15%. Every minute spent on a "Maybe" deal is a minute stolen from your "Yes" leads.</InsightCard>

The greatest skill of a high-leverage founder isn't knowing who to sell to—it's knowing who to **stop** talking to. (2025 State of Buyer Behavior).
*   Rigorous disqualification reduces forecast variance from 50% to **under 15%**.
*   Every minute spent on a "Maybe" deal is a minute stolen from your product or your "Yes" leads.

**The Golden Rule:** Be as willing to disqualify a prospect as you are to qualify them. (Sandler Research).
