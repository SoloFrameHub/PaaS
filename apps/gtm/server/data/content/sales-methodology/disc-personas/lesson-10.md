# Practice: DISC Roleplay — Stress-Testing Your Chameleon Mode

You now know the theory of DISC. You know that High Ds want ROI and High Cs want Documentation. But knowing it intellectually is different from doing it live, under pressure, while a prospect is interrupting you or yawning on Zoom. (2026 Acquisition Trends).

To win, you must resolve the internal **Identity Conflict** between the Maker and the Seller. Roleplay is not about "acting"; it is about training your brain to switch **Linguistic Interfaces** without triggering your own dACC pain response.

---

## 1. Scenario A: The Aggressive Commander (The High D)

**Role:** Busy CEO of a Fortune 500 branch.
**Mood:** Impatient, skeptical, "Just tell me the price."
**The Goal:** Anchor the price to a massive ROI number in under 120 seconds.

<MiniRoleplay scenario="A Fortune 500 CEO interrupts you: 'Look, skip the slides. Just tell me what this costs and what it does.'" role="You are the founder. Respond with BLUF — give a price range and immediately link it to an ROI outcome. Do not apologize or small-talk."
  persistKey="disc-L10-roleplay-d" modelResponse="Fair. The investment is $3-5k/month depending on scale. What it does: we automate your entire billing reconciliation. Based on your team size, that's roughly $40k/year in recovered engineering hours. Want me to show you the 2-minute dashboard walkthrough, or should we go straight to the contract terms?"
/>

*   **The Drill:** Your partner (or AI) interrupts you immediately with: *"Look, skip the slides. Just tell me what this costs and what it does."*
*   **The Pass Condition:** You respond with **BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)**. You give a price range and immediately link it to an objective business result.
*   **The Fail Condition:** You apologize for being direct or try to "socially lubricate" the call after they've told you to stop.

## 2. Scenario B: The Scattered Socializer (The High I)

**Role:** VP of Brand / Marketing.
**Mood:** Enthusiastic, high-energy, constantly going on tangents.
**The Goal:** Harness their energy and convert it into a **Social Obligation** (The People Anchor).

<MiniRoleplay scenario="VP of Marketing says: 'Oh my god, I just got back from this amazing conference in Austin. The content marketing sessions were incredible! Have you been? We should totally go together next year!'" role="You are the founder. Indulge the story briefly, then use a Linguistic Bridge to pivot to business and lock in a People Anchor."
  persistKey="disc-L10-roleplay-i" modelResponse="That sounds incredible — Austin has the best energy. Speaking of content innovation, that's exactly why I wanted to show you this. Imagine being the first marketing VP in your space to automate your entire content pipeline. Your team lead, Sarah, would love this. Can we get her on a 15-minute call tomorrow so she sees the vision too?"
/>

*   **The Drill:** Your partner spends 4 minutes talking about a conference they attended.
*   **The Pass Condition:** You indulge the story for 60 seconds, then use a **Linguistic Bridge** to pivot back to how the product makes them a visionary leader.
*   **The Fail Condition:** You allow the meeting to end with *"We should totally do something!"* without a confirmed meeting for "Dave from Marketing."

---

## 3. Scenario C: The Reluctant Guardian (The High S)

**Role:** HR Director / Operations Manager.
**Mood:** Polite but terrified of the "Implementation Lift."
**The Goal:** Move the deal forward without triggering a "Fight or Flight" response.

*   **The Drill:** Your partner says, *"It looks interesting, but my team is already at max capacity. I don't think we can handle a change right now."*
*   **The Pass Condition:** You validate their stress, lower your volume, and offer the **Safety Anchor** (e.g., a Parallel Pilot).
*   **The Fail Condition:** You push harder on "Growth" or "Speed," causing them to retreat further into their shell.

---

## 4. Scenario D: The Skeptical Analyst (The High C)

**Role:** CTO / Security Lead.
**Mood:** Emotionless, detail-oriented, listening for a single inaccuracy.
**The Goal:** Pass the "Internal Audit" and prove technical competence.

*   **The Drill:** Your partner asks a very specific question about your data encryption or API rate limits.
*   **The Pass Condition:** You provide an exact data point or admit *"I don't have that specific spec in front of me; let me send you the whitepaper by 4 PM."*
*   **The Fail Condition:** You use marketing adjectives like *"Revolutionary"* or you guess a technical answer and get caught. (2025 State of Buyer Behavior).
