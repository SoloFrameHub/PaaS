# Selling to the High D: The Commander

The **Dominant (D)** personality is the most intimidating type for a technical founder to sell to. They are often Executives, Founders, or VPs. They are paid to make hard decisions fast and their primary fear is losing **Control** or wasting time. (2026 Acquisition Trends).

When you get on a call with a High D, you will feel it immediately. The air pressure in the Zoom room changes. They are not there to make friends; they are there to solve a problem and move to the next task.

**The Coordinates:**
*   **Pace:** Fast / Assertive
*   **Focus:** Task / Results
*   **Motto:** "Be brief, be bright, be gone."

## 1. Recognizing the Commander Signals

You can spot a D within the first 60 seconds of a call using digital and behavioral body language: (2026 Acquisition Trends).

### Signal 1: The "BLUF" Opener
Most people start with "social lubrication." A High D will skip it. They prefer **BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)**.
*   *D:* "Hey. Doing well. Look, I've got a hard stop at the half-hour, so let's skip the slides. Show me the dashboard."

### Signal 2: Linguistic Markers
Listen for high-intensity vocabulary. They use words like: *"Result," "Bottom line," "Now," "Action," "Options,"* and *"Decision."*

### Signal 3: The Interruption Power-Play
They interrupt not to be rude, but to signal they have "gotten" the point and want to move faster.
*   **Founder Mistake:** Taking this as a personal rejection.
*   **Reality:** This is a buying signal. If they were bored, they would multitask. Interrupting means they are filtering for utility.

## 2. Trading in the D-Currency

To sell to a D, you must trade in their currency. If you try to sell them "Process" or "Feelings," the deal dies.

### Value 1: Results > Mechanism
They do not care *how* your code works. They care *what it produces*. (2025 State of Sales).

  criteria={["Leads with a measurable business result", "Uses plain language a CEO would understand", "Focuses on what it produces, not how it works"]}
/>

*   *Mechanism:* "Our NLP engine uses transformer models to parse headers."
*   *Result:* "We cut customer support costs by 30% in Month 1."

### Value 2: Speed as Proof
If you can save them time *during the sales process*, you prove you can save them time *with the product*. 
*   **The Tactic:** End the demo 10 minutes early. Say: *"I finished early to give you back 10 minutes for your next meeting."* This earns massive trust.

### Value 3: The Illusions of Autonomy
D-types hate being "pushed" but love being "the decider." 
*   **The Option Close:** Never give one recommendation (which feels like a command). Give two options: *"Option A is the fast-start for $2k. Option B is the full-scale for $5k. Which fits your quarterly goals better?"*

## 3. Managing Conflict and Dissonance

D-types test vendors like wolves test prey. They will challenge you to see if you have conviction. (2026 Acquisition Trends).

<MiniRoleplay scenario="The prospect (High D CEO) says: 'This is 20% more expensive than [Competitor]. That's way too high.'" role="You are the founder handling this price challenge from a Commander personality. Stand firm and reframe."
  persistKey="disc-L2-roleplay" modelResponse="We are more expensive because our uptime is 4x higher. If you want cheap, use [Competitor]. If you want your system to stay up, use us. The math is simple: one hour of downtime costs you $15k. We've had zero unplanned outages in 18 months."
/>

### The Pricing Test
*   *D:* "This is 20% more expensive than [Competitor]. That's way too high."
*   **Avoid Submitting:** If you immediately offer a discount, you create **Cognitive Dissonance**. The D thinks: *"If they cut the price that fast, they were overcharging me or their product isn't worth it."*
*   **The Power Response:** Stand firm. *"We are more expensive because our uptime is 4x higher. If you want cheap, use [Competitor]. If you want your system to stay up, use us."*
*   **The Result:** The D respects the pushback and buys.

## 5. Key Takeaways

<InteractiveChecklist title="Commander Selling Checklist" persistKey="disc-L2-actions" items={["Practice BLUF: give the conclusion first, then the evidence", "Respect the Hard Stop: end calls 2 minutes early", "Stand firm on pricing — conflict is a trust test", "Always use the Option Close: give 2 choices, not 1 recommendation", "End a demo 10 minutes early to prove you value their time"]} />

1.  **Use BLUF.** Give the conclusion first, then the evidence.
2.  **Respect the Hard Stop.** If the call is 30 mins, end at 28.
3.  **Conflict is a Test.** Respect is earned through technical conviction, not submission. (2026 Acquisition Trends).
4.  **Autonomy over Influence.** Let them feel like they are "buying," not being "sold."
