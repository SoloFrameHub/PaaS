# Handling Guarded Prospects

Not every prospect is going to open up to you in the first five minutes. In the real world, you will frequently encounter the **Guarded Prospect.** These are the people who give one-word answers ("It's fine," "Maybe"), and deflect deeper questions by saying, *"Can you just send me some info?"* 

It's tempting to label these prospects as "uninterested." But usually, they aren't hostile—they're just protecting themselves. (2025 State of Sales). The #1 mistake solo founders make when facing a guarded prospect is pushing harder. This triggers the **Amygdala Threat Response**, making them even more defensive. (2026 Acquisition Trends).

<InsightCard icon="🛡️" title="The Real Problem">
Guarded prospects aren't uninterested—they're protecting themselves. Pushing harder activates their threat response and makes them MORE defensive, not less.
</InsightCard>

In this lesson, you will practice the **"Pull Back" Principle** to warm up the **High-S (Steady)** prospect—the cautious, trust-oriented buyer who needs safety before disclosure.

## 1. Techniques for Breaking the Status Quo

### Permission-Based Questions
Don't demand information; ask for permission to be curious. (2025 State of Buyer Behavior).
*   **The Script:** *"To ensure I'm not wasting your time with irrelevant tech specs, would it be okay if I asked two questions about your current workflow to see where the real friction is?"*

### The "I May Be Wrong" Frame
People love to correct wrong assumptions. Use this to your advantage.
*   **The Script:** *"I might be off-base here, but in my experience with other [Industries], the team usually feels significant pressure around [Pain Point]. Is that what you're seeing, or am I missing the mark?"*

### Intent Throttling
When a guarded prospect says, "Just tell me the price," you must **Throttle the Intent** to maintain your Expert Frame.
*   **The Script:** *"I'm happy to give you a ballpark. But without knowing your [X] and [Y], the number I give you will be meaningless for your CFO. Can we take 5 minutes to map the requirement first?"*

**Why It Works:** People love to correct others. This lowers their guard while giving you real information.

<Slide title="Intent Throttling">

**The Pattern:** Redirect premature price questions back to discovery.

**Example:** *"I'm happy to give you a ballpark. But without knowing your team size and current tool stack, the number I give you will be meaningless for your CFO. Can we take 5 minutes to map the requirement first?"*

**Why It Works:** You maintain expert frame while showing you care about accuracy, not just closing.

## 2. The High-S Profile (Emotional Compass / Slow Motor)

When configuring your practice session:
*   **Tone:** Cautious, polite, and avoids conflict.
*   **The Strategy:** slow down your **Motor**. If you talk too fast or too "Salesy," they will shut down. 
*   **The Benchmark:** Maintain the **Expert Frame** with a 54-58% talk time. Use your time to summarize their (limited) input and provide "Safe" social proof examples. (2025 Benchmarks).

## 3. Simulation Drills: Warming the Guard

### Drill 1: The "Silent Wait"
*   **Scenario:** You ask a deep question and the prospect says "It's fine."
*   **Your Task:** Count to 4 in your head. Do not speak. Watch for the prospect to fill the silence with the "real" answer.

### Drill 2: Value-First Discovery
*   **Scenario:** A prospect is skeptical of your background.
*   **Your Task:** Provide a **Micro-Insight** before asking a question. *"I noticed your team is using [Tech X]. Usually, that leads to [Problem Y]. How have you been handling that so far?"*
