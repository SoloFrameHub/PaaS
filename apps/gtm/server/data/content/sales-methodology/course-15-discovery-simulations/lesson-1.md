# Setting Up for Effective Practice

I've spent 30 years in enterprise sales, and I've seen the same pattern repeat in almost every technical founder I coach. They study the frameworks. They can recite the qualification criteria for BANT or MEDDIC in their sleep. But the second they get on a real discovery call with a live prospect, the frameworks evaporate. 

Technical founders often treat sales like a subject to be mastered through reading, when it is actually a **Muscle Memory** discipline. (2025 State of Sales). You wouldn't learn to drive a car by reading a manual and then jumping onto a high-speed highway. You'd spend hours in a parking lot, building the reflexes that allow you to shift, brake, and steer without thinking. 

<InsightCard icon="🧠" title="The Practice Paradox">
Knowing the framework intellectually ≠ Executing it under stress. Your amygdala doesn't care that you read the book—it needs neurological grooves built through repetition.
</InsightCard>

---

## 1. The Practice Mindset: Stress Response Training

One of the biggest discovery mistakes founders make is using their real prospects as practice. Every time you "wing it" on a discovery call with a potential customer, you are paying for your education with lost revenue. 

Practice is superior to study for three specific reasons:
1.  **Stress Response Training:** Real sales calls trigger the **Amygdala**, shutting down your pre-frontal cortex (where you store your "scripts"). Practice builds the Neurological Grooves that make your methodology the "Primitive Default." (2026 Acquisition Trends).
2.  **Pattern Recognition:** You need to see the "guarded" response pattern 20 times before you recognize it instantly. Once you recognize it, you don't have to "think" about what to do; your brain already has the response queued up.
3.  **The 10x Compression Principle:** In the real world, you might get one "hostile" prospect every 50 calls. With the simulator, you can run 10 hostile scenarios in an hour.

## 2. Configuring Your 3D Matrix Simulator

To get the most out of these sessions, you must be intentional about the variables. Use the **SoloFrame Hub 3D Matrix** to stress-test your motion:

<SlideNavigation>
<Slide title="Dimension 1: The Motor (Pace/Internal Energy)">

### The Motor (Pace/Internal Energy)
*   **Fast (D/I):** Impatient, results-oriented, or enthusiasm-driven. You must match their velocity or you will bore them.
*   **Slow (S/C):** Cautious, deliberate, and detail-oriented. If you move too fast, you trigger their "Threat Response."

**Practice Tip:** Run the same discovery scenario twice—once with a Fast motor, once with a Slow motor. Notice how your pacing, question density, and patience must flex.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Dimension 2: The Compass (Focus/Orientation)">

### The Compass (Focus/Orientation)
*   **Logical (D/C):** Obsessed with Math, ROI, and Technical Specs. They speak "Excel."
*   **Emotional (I/S):** Focused on Team Morale, Personal Reputation, and Trust. They speak "Story."

**Practice Tip:** For Logical prospects, prepare 3 data points (industry benchmarks, ROI calculations). For Emotional prospects, prepare 2 customer stories that mirror their situation.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Dimension 3: The Resistance Layer">

### The Resistance Layer
*   **Cooperative:** High intent. They give full answers. Use this for baseline flow.
*   **Guarded:** One-word answers. "Everything is fine." You must use **Deep Curiosity** to break the status quo.
*   **Hostile:** Challenges your competence or price early. You must maintain the **Expert Frame**. (2025 State of Buyer Behavior).

**Practice Tip:** Start with Cooperative to nail your baseline flow. Then layer in Guarded (most common in real life). Only practice Hostile after you've mastered the first two.

</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## 3. Metrics That Matter: The 2026 Benchmarks

In 2026, the "Golden Rule" of 30% talk time has been debunked. Top-performing reps actually talk **54-58% of the time**. (2025 Benchmarks).
*   **Why?** Because as a founder, you are the **Expert Diagnostician**. You should be summarizing, providing industry insights, and "prescribing" solutions with authority.
*   **The Target:** Don't measure *how much* you talk; measure the **Question Intensity**. Successful calls feature **39% more questions** than failed ones. (Gartner Research).
