Why We Built BYOK: Attio CRM + Hunter.io Inside the Solo GTM OS

Two integrations launched today. Here's what they do and why we built them this way.

The problem with bundled enrichment tools

Most platforms that offer contact enrichment or CRM sync do one of two things: they build their own (expensive, thin coverage) or they white-label an existing tool and mark it up. Either way you pay for data you may not need, you can't see the underlying source, and your data sits in someone else's system.

We didn't want to do either. So we built BYOK — Bring Your Own Key.

How BYOK works

You connect your own Attio account and your own Hunter.io account inside Settings → Connected Apps. We store a reference to your API key (encrypted) and use it to proxy calls on your behalf. Results come back to your browser. We never persist contact data, email addresses, or firmographic records to our database. When you disconnect, the key is deleted.

You pay Attio's pricing. You pay Hunter's pricing. We charge nothing extra for the integration. That's the deal.

What Attio gives you

  • → Bi-directional pipeline sync. Deals you track in the OS push to Attio automatically. Stage changes you make in Attio (or via Attio automations) flow back into your OS pipeline view.
  • → On-demand contact enrichment. Click "Enrich" on any deal card and the OS pulls Attio's enriched firmographic data — company size, industry, tech stack, funding stage, employee count, LinkedIn URL — inline, without leaving the app.
  • → Free tier works. Attio's free plan includes enrichment and their data coverage is solid for B2B SaaS targets.

What Hunter.io gives you

  • → Domain search inside the OS. Enter a company domain in ICP Builder or the List Building lesson tools and Hunter returns verified email addresses for that company — without opening a separate tab.
  • → Nothing stored. Results exist in your browser session only. We don't write email addresses to any database.
  • → Free plan included. Hunter's free tier gives you 25 searches/month — enough to build a solid first prospect list without paying anything.

Why this combination

Attio enriches companies — revenue range, headcount, tech stack, funding — from their own data graph. Hunter finds individual contacts at those companies. They're complementary, not redundant. You use Attio to qualify the company, Hunter to find the right person at it.

Together they cover the research half of outbound: company fits ICP → find the decision-maker → enrich their context → write the email. The OS then helps with the email and the call.

What's next

Notion export is already live (export your ICP, positioning, and playbook artifacts as Notion pages). We're watching which integrations get used most before building more. If you're connected and have feedback, reply directly to any OS email or post in your cohort pod.
