Certified Solo GTM Practitioner: A Credential That Signals Something Real

Most course certifications are participation trophies. This one isn't. Here's what it takes, how it's issued, and why the gate matters.

Certified
Solo GTM
Practitioner

SoloFrameHub

Why we built a certification

The Solo GTM OS is free during beta. The curriculum is comprehensive. But comprehensive doesn't automatically mean credible — anyone can say they "completed" a course. We wanted a signal that meant something to a potential investor, partner, or hire who's reviewing a founder's LinkedIn profile.

The credential needed to be hard enough to earn that it's worth displaying, but achievable enough that serious members can reach it within 3–4 months of consistent use. The gate we landed on: Track 1 + Track 3 + a live roleplay session scoring 75% or higher.

The criteria

1

Complete Track 1: Foundations

ICP Builder, Positioning & Value Proposition, Acquisition Path, List Building, and the full Foundations quiz set. This is the strategic groundwork.

2

Complete Track 3: Sales Methodology

DISC Buyer Personas, Discovery Framework (BANT/MEDDIC), Discovery Call Simulations, Objection Handling, Proposals & Negotiation, Closing. This is the execution layer.

3

Roleplay session scoring ≥ 75%

A live AI roleplay session evaluated across question quality, talk-time ratio, objection handling, and authority positioning. The score is AI-graded in real time. This is the proof that the skill translated into practice.

How it's issued

We use Badgr, which implements the Open Badges 3.0 standard. When you meet all three criteria, the OS calls the Badgr API and issues a badge assertion to your email. The assertion is a verifiable credential — not an image, but a cryptographically signed record tied to your account.

From the completion screen, one click pre-fills the LinkedIn "Add Certification" form with your credential URL, issue date, and our organization ID. LinkedIn then displays it on your profile under Licenses & Certifications, where anyone can click through to verify it.

We'll migrate to Credly when volume exceeds 1,000 badges — Credly has better employer recognition at scale. For now, Badgr is free, fast, and standards-compliant.

The /certified directory

There's a public /certified page that lists certified founders by name, company, and optional revenue milestone. It's opt-in — you choose whether to appear. The goal is to build a credible reference list of founders who have demonstrably done the GTM work.

Spanish version

The Spanish certification is called Profesional Certificado en Solo GTM. It uses the same criteria but applied to the Spanish curriculum (Track 1 ES is complete; Track 3 ES translation is in progress). The badge is a distinct design with the ES label and issues through the same Badgr infrastructure.
