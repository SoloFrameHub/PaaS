## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
Discovery is where deals are won or lost—yet most technical founders either skip it entirely (jumping straight to demos) or conduct it so poorly they learn nothing useful. This course provides two battle-tested frameworks (BANT and MEDDIC) that transform discovery from awkward interrogation into strategic conversation. You'll learn exactly what questions to ask, when to ask them, and how to use the answers to qualify or disqualify opportunities.

### Why This Course Matters
- **The Demo Trap:** Technical founders default to showing product features instead of understanding buyer needs
- **Qualification Failure:** Without systematic discovery, founders waste time on deals that will never close
- **Information Asymmetry:** Discovery done right gives YOU control of the sales process
- **Framework Power:** BANT/MEDDIC remove the guesswork—you know exactly what to uncover

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Execute a structured discovery call using BANT or MEDDIC methodology
2. Identify the 4 BANT criteria and 6 MEDDIC elements
3. Ask open-ended questions that uncover real pain (not surface complaints)
4. Qualify or disqualify opportunities based on systematic criteria
5. Document discovery findings for deal progression decisions

### Prerequisites
- Completed Course 1: ICP Builder Workshop (know WHO you're targeting)
- Have at least 2-3 prospect conversations scheduled or completed
- Basic understanding of your product's value proposition

### Lesson 2: BANT Framework - The Classic Qualification System
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define all four BANT criteria (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Explain when BANT is most effective (transactional, shorter cycles)
- Apply BANT scoring to qualify/disqualify opportunities

**Content Outline:**
1. **BANT Origins:** IBM's systematic approach to sales qualification
2. **The Four Criteria:**
   - **Budget:** Do they have money allocated? What's the range?
   - **Authority:** Is this person the decision-maker or influencer?
   - **Need:** Is there a genuine problem your product solves?
   - **Timeline:** When do they need to make a decision?
3. **BANT Scoring System:**
   - Strong (explicit confirmation)
   - Weak (implied or uncertain)
   - Missing (unknown or negative)
4. **Qualification Thresholds:** When to proceed vs. disqualify
5. **BANT Limitations:** Where it falls short (complex enterprise deals)

**Practice Exercise:**
- Apply BANT scoring to 3 prospect scenarios
- Identify which would qualify, which need more discovery

**Visual Framework:** BANT Qualification Matrix

### Lesson 3: MEDDIC Framework - For Complex B2B Sales
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define all six MEDDIC elements
- Explain when MEDDIC is superior to BANT (complex, multi-stakeholder)
- Map MEDDIC elements to specific discovery questions

**Content Outline:**
1. **MEDDIC Origins:** Developed at PTC for enterprise software sales
2. **The Six Elements:**
   - **Metrics:** What quantifiable outcomes do they need? (ROI, time saved, revenue gained)
   - **Economic Buyer:** Who controls the budget and can say yes?
   - **Decision Criteria:** What factors will they use to choose a solution?
   - **Decision Process:** What steps do they follow to make a purchase?
   - **Identify Pain:** What specific problem is driving this initiative?
   - **Champion:** Who inside the organization advocates for your solution?
3. **MEDDIC vs. BANT:** When to use which
   - BANT: <$10K deals, single decision-maker, short cycles
   - MEDDIC: >$10K deals, buying committees, longer cycles
4. **The Champion Test:** No champion = no deal

**Visual Framework:** MEDDIC Element Map with sample questions per element

### Lesson 4: The Art of Open-Ended Questions
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between open and closed questions
- Convert closed questions to open alternatives
- Use the "Five Whys" technique to uncover root causes

**Content Outline:**
1. **Closed vs. Open Questions:**
   - Closed: "Do you have budget?" (Yes/No = dead end)
   - Open: "How does your organization typically fund initiatives like this?" (Reveals process)
2. **The Question Conversion Formula:**
   - "Do you..." → "How do you..."
   - "Is there..." → "What is..."
   - "Can you..." → "Tell me about..."
3. **The Five Whys Technique:**
   - Surface answer: "We need better reporting"
   - Why #1: "Our current reports take too long"
   - Why #2: "We're manually compiling data from 5 sources"
   - Why #3: "Our tools don't integrate"
   - Why #4: "We chose them separately without integration planning"
   - Root cause: Data architecture problem, not reporting problem
4. **Question Sequences:** Leading from broad to specific

**Practice Exercise:**
- Convert 10 closed questions to open alternatives
- Practice Five Whys on a sample problem

**AI Coaching Integration:**
- Practice open-ended questioning in AI roleplay

### Lesson 5: BANT Questions That Actually Work
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Ask Budget questions without seeming pushy
- Identify Authority through indirect questions
- Uncover genuine Need vs. mild interest
- Establish Timeline reality vs. wishful thinking

**Content Outline:**
1. **Budget Questions (The Sensitive Topic):**
   - Don't: "What's your budget?"
   - Do: "How have similar investments been funded in the past?"
   - Do: "What range of investment would make sense for solving this problem?"
   - Do: "Is there budget allocated, or would this need to be requested?"
2. **Authority Questions (Finding the Real Decision-Maker):**
   - "Walk me through how your organization typically makes decisions like this"
   - "Who else would need to be involved in evaluating solutions?"
   - "If you found the perfect solution today, what would happen next?"
3. **Need Questions (Separating Pain from Interest):**
   - "What prompted you to look into this now?"
   - "What happens if you don't solve this problem?"
   - "How is this affecting your [revenue/team/customers] today?"
4. **Timeline Questions (Reality-Testing Urgency):**
   - "What's driving the timing on this?"
   - "What needs to happen before a decision can be made?"
   - "If you started today, when would you need to see results?"

**Practice Exercise:**
- Role-play BANT discovery with AI (Lesson ties to AI Roleplay courses)

### Lesson 6: MEDDIC Questions That Uncover Everything
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Ask Metrics questions that quantify the problem
- Map the Decision Process step by step
- Identify and test potential Champions
- Understand Decision Criteria before proposing solutions

**Content Outline:**
1. **Metrics Questions (Quantifying Pain):**
   - "How are you measuring success in this area today?"
   - "What would solving this problem be worth in [time/money/resources]?"
   - "What metrics would need to improve for this to be considered successful?"
2. **Economic Buyer Questions (Following the Money):**
   - "Who owns the budget for this type of initiative?"
   - "Who would sign off on a purchase of this size?"
   - "Have you worked with [Economic Buyer] on similar projects?"
3. **Decision Criteria Questions (Understanding How They'll Choose):**
   - "What factors are most important in your evaluation?"
   - "How will you compare different solutions?"
   - "What would make you choose one solution over another?"
4. **Decision Process Questions (Mapping the Journey):**
   - "Walk me through the steps from here to a signed contract"
   - "Who else will be involved in the evaluation?"
   - "What's the typical timeline for decisions like this?"
5. **Pain Questions (The Heart of MEDDIC):**
   - "What specific problem is driving this initiative?"
   - "How long has this been an issue?"
   - "What have you tried before, and why didn't it work?"
6. **Champion Questions (Finding Your Internal Advocate):**
   - "Who stands to benefit most if this problem is solved?"
   - "Who's been championing this initiative internally?"
   - "What happens for you personally if this succeeds?"

### Lesson 7: The Discovery Call Structure
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Structure a 30-minute discovery call with clear phases
- Balance question-asking with value delivery
- Know when to pivot from discovery to next steps

**Content Outline:**
1. **The 30-Minute Discovery Structure:**
   - **Opening (3 min):** Rapport, agenda setting, permission to ask questions
   - **Context (5 min):** Their role, company, current situation
   - **Pain Exploration (12 min):** BANT or MEDDIC deep dive
   - **Value Alignment (5 min):** Brief positioning against their stated needs
   - **Next Steps (5 min):** Clear commitment to advance or close
2. **Talk Time Ratio:** Target 30-40% you, 60-70% prospect
3. **The Permission Frame:** "Would it be okay if I asked some questions to understand your situation better?"
4. **Pivot Points:** When to stop asking and start advancing
5. **Red Flags That Kill Discovery:**
   - Prospect gives monosyllabic answers
   - Keeps asking about pricing (skipping value)
   - Can't articulate the problem

**Visual Framework:** 30-Minute Discovery Call Timeline

**Practice Exercise:**
- Script your opening 3 minutes
- Plan your 5 most important questions in priority order

### Lesson 8: Active Listening in Discovery
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Practice active listening techniques (reflecting, clarifying, summarizing)
- Avoid common listening failures (interrupting, assuming, planning next question)
- Use silence strategically to encourage deeper answers

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Listening Matters More Than Asking:**
   - The best discovery question means nothing if you miss the answer
   - Prospects reveal more when they feel heard
2. **Active Listening Techniques:**
   - **Reflecting:** "So what I'm hearing is..."
   - **Clarifying:** "When you say X, do you mean...?"
   - **Summarizing:** "Let me make sure I understand..."
3. **Common Listening Failures:**
   - Interrupting with your solution
   - Assuming you know what they'll say
   - Planning your next question instead of listening
   - Filling silence instead of letting it work
4. **Strategic Silence:** The 3-second rule—wait before responding
5. **Note-Taking Without Losing Engagement:**
   - Key phrases, not transcripts
   - Ask permission: "Mind if I take notes?"

**Practice Exercise:**
- AI roleplay focused on listening metrics (talk time ratio, interruptions)

### Lesson 9: Handling Discovery Resistance
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize common discovery resistance patterns
- Respond to "just send me information" and similar deflections
- Re-engage prospects who shut down during discovery

**Content Outline:**
1. **Common Resistance Patterns:**
   - "Just send me pricing/information"
   - "We don't have time for a long call"
   - "I'm just researching options"
   - Short, uninformative answers
2. **Why Prospects Resist:**
   - Fear of sales pressure
   - Don't see value in the conversation
   - Wrong person (not actually the decision-maker)
   - Not ready to buy (tire-kicking)
3. **Response Strategies:**
   - "Just send info": "Happy to—what specifically would be most useful? Understanding your situation helps me send relevant information."
   - "No time": "Understood. What's the one thing you need to know to decide if this is worth exploring further?"
   - Short answers: "Tell me more about that" / "Help me understand..."
4. **When to Walk Away:** Some resistance signals disqualification

**Practice Exercise:**
- Role-play handling 3 resistance scenarios

**Learning Objectives:**
- Create discovery documentation that drives deal decisions
- Use BANT/MEDDIC scorecards to track qualification
- Share discovery findings with future stakeholders (co-founders, team)

1. **Why Documentation Matters:**

3. **What to Capture:**

### Lesson 11: From Discovery to Demo - The Transition
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Decide when discovery is sufficient to proceed
- Frame the demo around discovery findings
- Set up the demo for success based on uncovered pain

**Content Outline:**
1. **The Qualification Decision:**
   - When to proceed to demo
   - When to ask for another discovery call
   - When to disqualify and exit gracefully
2. **Qualification Thresholds:**
   - BANT: Need 3 of 4 elements confirmed
   - MEDDIC: Need Pain, Economic Buyer, Champion identified
3. **The Discovery-to-Demo Bridge:**
   - "Based on what you shared about [specific pain], I'd like to show you how we address that"
   - Reference their words, not your features
4. **Demo Setup for Success:**
   - Send pre-demo summary of discovery findings
   - Ask: "Did I capture this accurately?"
   - Agenda the demo around their priorities

**Assessment:**
- Given a discovery scorecard, decide: Demo, More Discovery, or Disqualify?

### Lesson 12: Discovery for Different Deal Sizes
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Adapt discovery depth to deal size
- Know when to use abbreviated vs. full discovery
- Scale discovery for enterprise vs. SMB

**Content Outline:**
1. **Discovery Depth vs. Deal Size:**
   - $500 deal: 15-minute qualification check
   - $5,000 deal: 30-minute BANT discovery
   - $50,000 deal: Multiple MEDDIC sessions
2. **Abbreviated Discovery for Small Deals:**
   - Focus on Need and Timeline
   - Budget often fixed by pricing tier
   - Authority usually the person you're talking to
3. **Extended Discovery for Enterprise:**
   - Multiple stakeholder interviews
   - Formal discovery document
   - Economic Buyer meeting (separate from Champion)
4. **The Solo Founder Trap:** Over-discovering small deals, under-discovering big ones

**Case Study:** How to right-size discovery for your average deal
