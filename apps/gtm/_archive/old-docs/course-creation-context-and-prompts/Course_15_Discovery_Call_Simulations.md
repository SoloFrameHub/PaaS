## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
Reading about discovery is not the same as doing discovery. This course is pure practice—using the platform's AI roleplay system to simulate discovery calls with prospects of different DISC personalities, industries, and resistance levels. You'll complete dozens of practice sessions, receive real-time feedback on your technique, and build the muscle memory that makes discovery feel natural when you're on a real call.

### Why This Course Matters
- **Practice Without Stakes:** Make every mistake on AI, not real prospects
- **Rapid Skill Building:** 10 AI sessions = learning from 100 real calls (compressed feedback)
- **Confidence Building:** Repetition removes anxiety; you've already heard every response
- **Methodology Mastery:** BANT/MEDDIC become second nature through drilling

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Complete 20+ discovery call simulations across all DISC personalities
2. Achieve consistent performance metrics (talk time, questions asked, methodology adherence)
3. Handle cooperative, guarded, and resistant prospects effectively
4. Apply Course 2 (Discovery Framework) concepts in realistic conversations
5. Self-assess discovery performance and identify improvement areas

### Prerequisites
- **Required:** Course 2: Discovery Framework BANT/MEDDIC (concepts before practice)
- **Required:** Course 3: Understanding DISC Buyer Personas (know the personalities)
- Access to AI Roleplay feature with sessions remaining

### Platform Integration Points
- **AI Roleplay Chat Interface:** Primary practice environment
- **Performance Analytics Dashboard:** Track improvement across sessions
- **Session Recording & Playback:** Review and learn from sessions
- **Discovery Playbook:** Reference during practice sessions

## COURSE STRUCTURE (10 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: Setting Up for Effective Practice
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Preparation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Configure AI roleplay for discovery practice
- Understand performance metrics tracked
- Set practice goals and benchmarks

**Content Outline:**
1. **Practice Philosophy:**
   - Deliberate practice > random repetition
   - One skill focus per session
   - Review between sessions critical
2. **Roleplay Configuration:**
   - DISC personality selection
   - Scenario setup (industry, company size, stage)
   - Discovery framework selection (BANT/MEDDIC)
   - Difficulty level (cooperative → resistant)
3. **Performance Metrics:**
   - Talk time ratio (target: 30-40% you)
   - Questions asked (target: 8-12 per call)
   - BANT/MEDDIC elements uncovered
   - Methodology adherence score
   - Resolution outcome (qualified/disqualified/unclear)
4. **Practice Benchmarks:**
   - Beginner: Complete full session, hit 3+ elements
   - Intermediate: 30-40% talk time, 5+ elements
   - Advanced: 70%+ methodology adherence, clear qualification

**Setup Exercise:**
- Configure your first roleplay session
- Set 3 specific goals for your practice arc

### Lesson 2: BANT Discovery with Cooperative Prospects
**Duration:** 60 minutes (includes practice)  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice (The Application)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Complete BANT discovery with an easy prospect
- Practice all four BANT elements systematically
- Build confidence with low-resistance conversations

**Practice Structure:**
1. **Pre-Session Review:**
   - BANT questions refresher (from Course 2)
   - Target: Uncover all 4 elements
   - Focus: Conversation flow, not perfection
2. **Roleplay Session 1:** (15-20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Influential (I) personality—friendly, talkative
   - Scenario: 50-person SaaS company, growth stage
   - Difficulty: Cooperative
3. **Self-Assessment Questions:**
   - Did you uncover Budget? What did you learn?
   - Did you identify Authority? Who decides?
   - Did you validate Need? What's the specific pain?
   - Did you establish Timeline? What's driving urgency?
4. **Roleplay Session 2:** (15-20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Steady (S) personality—patient, consensus-oriented
   - Scenario: 100-person manufacturing company
   - Difficulty: Cooperative
5. **Session Comparison:**
   - How did your approach differ by personality?
   - Which BANT elements were easier/harder to uncover?

**Post-Session Exercise:**
- Complete discovery scorecard for each session
- Identify one improvement for next session

### Lesson 3: MEDDIC Discovery with Cooperative Prospects
**Duration:** 60 minutes (includes practice)  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Complete MEDDIC discovery (more comprehensive than BANT)
- Practice all 6 MEDDIC elements
- Experience the deeper discovery MEDDIC requires

**Practice Structure:**
1. **Pre-Session Review:**
   - MEDDIC elements refresher
   - Target: Uncover 4+ of 6 elements
   - Focus: Metrics and Champion identification
2. **Roleplay Session 3:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Influential (I) personality
   - Scenario: Enterprise software evaluation
   - Difficulty: Cooperative
3. **MEDDIC Scorecard:**
   - Metrics: What outcomes do they need?
   - Economic Buyer: Who controls budget?
   - Decision Criteria: How will they choose?
   - Decision Process: What steps to purchase?
   - Identify Pain: What's the root problem?
   - Champion: Who advocates internally?
4. **Roleplay Session 4:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Conscientious (C) personality—analytical
   - Scenario: Technical evaluation
   - Difficulty: Cooperative

**Reflection Exercise:**
- Compare MEDDIC depth to BANT
- When would you use each in real conversations?

### Lesson 4: Handling Guarded Prospects
**Duration:** 60 minutes (includes practice)  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Navigate prospects who don't volunteer information
- Use open-ended questions to draw out details
- Build rapport before diving into discovery

**Practice Structure:**
1. **Pre-Session Brief:**
   - Guarded prospect signals (short answers, skepticism)
   - Rapport-building techniques
   - Patience emphasis: Don't rush
2. **Roleplay Session 5:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Dominant (D) personality—direct, skeptical
   - Scenario: Time-pressured executive
   - Difficulty: Guarded
   - Challenge: They give short answers
3. **Technique Focus:**
   - Using silence (let them fill gaps)
   - "Tell me more about that..."
   - Value delivery mid-discovery (earn continued engagement)
4. **Roleplay Session 6:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Conscientious (C) personality
   - Scenario: Technical buyer, vendor-weary
   - Difficulty: Guarded
   - Challenge: Skeptical of claims

**Debrief Questions:**
- How did you adjust to short answers?
- What opened them up?
- When did you feel tempted to pitch? What happened?

### Lesson 5: Handling Resistant Prospects
**Duration:** 60 minutes (includes practice)  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize when to persist vs. disengage
- Handle objections during discovery (preview Course 6)
- Maintain composure under pressure

**Practice Structure:**
1. **Pre-Session Brief:**
   - Resistance types: Skeptical, hostile, distracted
   - Goal: Maintain control without aggression
   - When to pivot: Sometimes disqualification is success
2. **Roleplay Session 7:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Dominant (D) personality—challenging
   - Scenario: Prospect doubts your credibility
   - Difficulty: Resistant
   - Challenge: Pushes back on every question
3. **Mid-Session Analysis:**
   - At what point did you feel defensive?
   - What response helped most?
   - Did you maintain talk time balance?
4. **Roleplay Session 8:** (20 minutes)
   - Prospect: Steady (S) personality—resistant to change
   - Scenario: Happy with current vendor
   - Difficulty: Resistant
   - Challenge: "We're fine with what we have"

**Debrief:**
- When is resistance a signal to disengage?
- What phrases helped defuse tension?

### Lesson 8: Speed Discovery Practice
**Duration:** 50 minutes (includes practice)  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Complete effective discovery in compressed time
- Prioritize questions when time is limited
- Adapt for "I only have 15 minutes" scenarios

**Practice Structure:**
1. **Constraint Setup:**
   - Roleplay sessions capped at 10 minutes
   - Must uncover minimum 3 elements (BANT) or 4 elements (MEDDIC)
2. **Speed Session 1:** (10 minutes)
   - Quick qualification BANT
   - Focus: Efficiency without rushing
3. **Speed Session 2:** (10 minutes)
   - Abbreviated MEDDIC
   - Focus: Pain, Economic Buyer, Champion
4. **Speed Session 3:** (10 minutes)
   - Random DISC personality
   - Challenge: Adapt AND move fast
5. **Time Analysis:**
   - Which questions are essential?
   - What can you cut when time is tight?

**Playbook Update:**
- Create "Speed Discovery" question prioritization

### Lesson 9: Session Review and Pattern Analysis
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Evaluation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Review session recordings for improvement insights
- Identify patterns across multiple sessions
- Set targeted improvement goals

**Content Outline:**
1. **Recording Review Process:**
   - Select 3 sessions to review
   - Timestamp key moments (wins and mistakes)
   - Score yourself on each metric
2. **Pattern Analysis:**
   - Which DISC type do you handle best? Worst?
   - Which BANT/MEDDIC element do you uncover most easily? Miss most often?
   - Where does your talk time go off-target?
3. **Improvement Targeting:**
   - Select 1-2 specific improvements
   - Design practice sessions focused on weaknesses
4. **Peer Review Option:**
   - Share session with accountability pod
   - Receive external feedback

**Assessment:**
- Submit improvement plan with specific practice sessions
