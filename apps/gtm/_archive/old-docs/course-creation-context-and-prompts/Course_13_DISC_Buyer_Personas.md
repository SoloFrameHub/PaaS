## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
Every prospect you'll ever talk to has a distinct communication style—and mismatching that style kills deals. This course teaches the DISC behavioral model (Dominant, Influential, Steady, Conscientious) so you can identify buyer types quickly and adapt your selling approach accordingly. More importantly, it's the foundation for the platform's AI roleplay system, where you'll practice with AI prospects exhibiting each personality type.

### Why This Course Matters
- **Roleplay Foundation:** All platform AI roleplays use DISC personalities—understanding them maximizes practice value
- **Adaptability = Close Rate:** Same product, same features, but adapting to personality increases conversion
- **Pattern Recognition:** Once you know the 4 types, you'll recognize them in the first 2 minutes of calls
- **Technical Founder Gap:** Engineers often have one communication style (often C) and struggle with others

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Identify all four DISC types (D, I, S, C) and their behavioral characteristics
2. Recognize DISC signals within the first 2-3 minutes of a conversation
3. Adapt communication style, pace, and content to each personality type
4. Configure AI roleplay sessions to practice with specific DISC personalities
5. Handle the unique objections and concerns each type typically raises

### Prerequisites
- Basic sales conversation experience (or completed Course 2: Discovery)
- Access to AI Roleplay feature
- Openness to behavioral psychology concepts

### Platform Integration Points
- **AI Roleplay Chat Interface:** DISC personality selector
- **Streaming Real-Time Coaching:** Performance metrics per personality
- **Session Recordings:** Analysis of adaptation effectiveness
- **Performance Analytics:** Track improvement across all four types

### Lesson 1: Why Personality Matters More Than Product
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation (The Teaching)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain why the same pitch works for some prospects and fails for others
- Recognize that buying decisions are emotional first, rational second
- Understand the DISC model's origins and validation

**Content Outline:**
1. **Hook:** "The founder who lost 3 identical deals to 3 different competitors—each time for a different reason"
2. **The Communication Mismatch Problem:**
   - You speak in features; they want vision
   - You provide data; they want relationships
   - You move fast; they need time
   - You give options; they want recommendations
3. **DISC Origins:**
   - William Marston (1928) behavioral research
   - Workplace application since 1970s
   - Millions of assessments validating the model
4. **The Four Quadrants Preview:**
   - Task-oriented vs. People-oriented
   - Fast-paced vs. Measured-paced
5. **Why This Matters for Technical Founders:**
   - Most engineers cluster in one quadrant (C)
   - Sales requires fluency in all four

**Assessment:**
- Reflection: "Think of a recent sales conversation that went poorly—could personality mismatch explain it?"

### Lesson 2: The D Personality - Dominant
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the core characteristics of Dominant (D) personalities
- Recognize D signals in conversations and emails
- Understand what D types value and what they avoid

**Content Outline:**
1. **D Profile:**
   - Task-oriented + Fast-paced
   - Results-focused, direct, decisive
   - Values: Efficiency, control, winning
   - Fears: Being taken advantage of, losing control
2. **How to Recognize a D:**
   - Direct communication: "What's the bottom line?"
   - Impatient with small talk
   - Challenges assertions immediately
   - Takes charge of meetings
   - Email signature often shows title prominently
3. **What D Types Want:**
   - Quick, direct answers
   - Results and ROI focus
   - Options to choose from (they like control)
   - Confidence from the seller (they test you)
4. **What D Types Hate:**
   - Long explanations before the point
   - Indecision or uncertainty
   - Being told what to do
   - Excessive relationship-building
5. **D Buying Signals:**
   - "Let's do it" (fast decision)
   - "What's the next step?" (taking charge)
   - "Send me the contract" (no more meetings needed)

**Case Study:** Selling to a D-type CTO (what worked, what failed)

### Lesson 3: The I Personality - Influential
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the core characteristics of Influential (I) personalities
- Recognize I signals in conversations and emails
- Understand what I types value and what they avoid

**Content Outline:**
1. **I Profile:**
   - People-oriented + Fast-paced
   - Enthusiastic, optimistic, relationship-driven
   - Values: Recognition, collaboration, new ideas
   - Fears: Rejection, loss of approval, being ignored
2. **How to Recognize an I:**
   - Warm, personal opener: "How are you? How was your weekend?"
   - Storytelling and tangents
   - Animated communication (hand gestures, emojis in emails)
   - Name-drops other people and companies
   - Social proof matters: "Who else uses this?"
3. **What I Types Want:**
   - Connection before business
   - Vision and excitement
   - Social proof and testimonials
   - To feel special and recognized
4. **What I Types Hate:**
   - Cold, transactional conversations
   - Too much data and details upfront
   - Being rushed or dismissed
   - Negativity or pessimism
5. **I Buying Signals:**
   - "I love this!" (enthusiasm)
   - "Let me tell my team about this" (wants to be the messenger)
   - Lots of questions about experience/relationships

**Case Study:** Selling to an I-type marketing VP

### Lesson 4: The S Personality - Steady
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the core characteristics of Steady (S) personalities
- Recognize S signals in conversations and emails
- Understand what S types value and what they avoid

**Content Outline:**
1. **S Profile:**
   - People-oriented + Measured-paced
   - Patient, reliable, harmony-seeking
   - Values: Stability, support, proven solutions
   - Fears: Change, conflict, pressure
2. **How to Recognize an S:**
   - Slower, thoughtful responses
   - Asks about support, implementation, onboarding
   - Mentions the team frequently: "We need to..." "My team would..."
   - Hesitant to commit without consensus
   - Loyal to existing vendors (hard to switch)
3. **What S Types Want:**
   - Reassurance and risk mitigation
   - Implementation support promises
   - Time to consult others
   - Proof the change won't be disruptive
4. **What S Types Hate:**
   - Pressure tactics ("Sign today or lose the deal")
   - Change without clear justification
   - Being forced to decide alone
   - Aggressive selling styles
5. **S Buying Signals:**
   - "Let me check with my team" (consensus-building)
   - "What does implementation look like?" (planning)
   - "How long have you been doing this?" (stability)

**Case Study:** Selling to an S-type operations manager

### Lesson 5: The C Personality - Conscientious
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the core characteristics of Conscientious (C) personalities
- Recognize C signals in conversations and emails
- Understand what C types value and what they avoid

**Content Outline:**
1. **C Profile:**
   - Task-oriented + Measured-paced
   - Analytical, detail-oriented, quality-focused
   - Values: Accuracy, expertise, proof
   - Fears: Being wrong, making mistakes, criticism
2. **How to Recognize a C:**
   - Asks detailed technical questions
   - Wants documentation, whitepapers, case studies
   - Slow to respond (processing information)
   - Precise language: "Exactly how does that work?"
   - Skeptical of claims without evidence
3. **What C Types Want:**
   - Data, proof, and specifics
   - Time to analyze and compare
   - Expertise from the seller
   - Written documentation
4. **What C Types Hate:**
   - Vague claims: "It's faster" (how much faster?)
   - Hype and marketing language
   - Being rushed to decide
   - Emotional appeals
5. **C Buying Signals:**
   - "Can you send me the technical specs?"
   - "I'd like to run a proof of concept"
   - Detailed comparison questions

**Case Study:** Selling to a C-type technical architect (note: many technical founders ARE this type)

### Lesson 6: Rapid DISC Identification - Reading Signals Fast
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify DISC type within the first 2-3 minutes of conversation
- Recognize signals in email communication
- Adjust approach in real-time based on signals

**Content Outline:**
1. **The 2-Minute Assessment:**
   - **Opening behavior:** Gets to business (D/C) vs. Builds rapport (I/S)
   - **Pace:** Fast/impatient (D/I) vs. Measured/thoughtful (S/C)
   - **Questions asked:** Results-focused (D) vs. Relationship (I) vs. Process (S) vs. Details (C)
2. **Email Signal Patterns:**
   - D: Short, bullet points, direct ask
   - I: Personal opener, enthusiasm, exclamation points
   - S: Polite, mentions team, thanks you
   - C: Detailed, structured, asks clarifying questions
3. **Compound Types:** Most people are dominant in one with secondary in another
4. **Danger Zone:** Selling to your opposite (D↔S, I↔C conflicts)
5. **Real-Time Pivot:** Adjusting mid-conversation when signals clarify

**Practice Exercise:**
- Review 8 email samples, identify DISC type
- Watch/read 4 conversation snippets, identify signals

**Assessment:**
- Given conversation transcript, identify DISC type and evidence

### Lesson 7: Selling to D Types - Direct and Results-Focused
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Adapt pitch structure for D types
- Handle D-type objections
- Close with D types effectively

**Content Outline:**
1. **Opening with D Types:**
   - Get to the point fast: "Here's why I'm calling and what I think we can do for you"
   - Skip the small talk unless they initiate
   - State your ask/agenda upfront
2. **Discovery with D Types:**
   - Direct questions: "What's your biggest challenge right now?"
   - Accept short answers; don't push for elaboration
   - Focus on outcomes: "What would solving this be worth?"
3. **Demo/Pitch with D Types:**
   - Lead with results, not features
   - Competitive positioning: "Here's how we compare"
   - Let them interrupt—answer directly
4. **Handling D Objections:**
   - Price: "What's the ROI threshold you need?"
   - Competitor: "What would we need to show to win?"
   - Timing: "What would accelerate this?"
5. **Closing D Types:**
   - Be direct: "Based on what we've discussed, does it make sense to move forward?"
   - Give options: "We can do X or Y—which works better?"
   - Respect their time: Don't pad the close

### Lesson 8: Selling to I Types - Enthusiasm and Relationships
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Adapt pitch structure for I types
- Handle I-type objections
- Close with I types effectively

**Content Outline:**
1. **Opening with I Types:**
   - Personal connection first: "I saw you posted about [topic]—that resonated"
   - Share enthusiasm: Match their energy
   - Story over stats initially
2. **Discovery with I Types:**
   - Relationship questions: "What's your team trying to accomplish?"
   - Let them talk—they reveal a lot when comfortable
   - Connect personally: Share your own experience
3. **Demo/Pitch with I Types:**
   - Vision-first: Paint the picture of success
   - Social proof heavy: "Here's what [similar company] achieved"
   - Make them the hero: "Imagine bringing this to your team"
4. **Handling I Objections:**
   - They often don't voice objections directly
   - Watch for enthusiasm drop (signal of concern)
   - Ask: "What concerns might your team have?"
5. **Closing I Types:**
   - Help them sell internally: "Here's how to present this to your team"
   - Public commitment: "Would you be willing to introduce me to [stakeholder]?"
   - Watch for: All enthusiasm, no action (they may need Champion support)

### Lesson 9: Selling to S Types - Support and Stability
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Adapt pitch structure for S types
- Handle S-type objections
- Close with S types effectively

**Content Outline:**
1. **Opening with S Types:**
   - Warm but not rushed: "Thanks for taking the time"
   - Don't pressure: "No agenda today other than understanding if we can help"
   - Team focus: "How does this affect your team?"
2. **Discovery with S Types:**
   - Patient questioning: Give time to respond
   - Process questions: "How does your team currently handle this?"
   - Change concern: "What would need to be true for a change to make sense?"
3. **Demo/Pitch with S Types:**
   - Implementation focus: "Here's exactly how onboarding works"
   - Support emphasis: "This is your dedicated support contact"
   - Risk mitigation: Case studies of smooth transitions
4. **Handling S Objections:**
   - Timing: Never pressure—offer to "check back when timing is better"
   - Team concern: "Would it help if I met with your team?"
   - Change fear: "Let me show you how we minimize disruption"
5. **Closing S Types:**
   - Consensus path: "What would your team need to see?"
   - No artificial urgency: It backfires badly
   - Support close: "We'll be with you every step"

### Lesson 10: Selling to C Types - Data and Expertise
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Adapt pitch structure for C types
- Handle C-type objections
- Close with C types effectively

**Content Outline:**
1. **Opening with C Types:**
   - Credential establishment: Brief expertise signal
   - Respect their time: "I know you're analytical—I'll be specific"
   - Questions welcome: "Feel free to interrupt with questions"
2. **Discovery with C Types:**
   - Technical questions: Go deep on their current process
   - Metrics focus: "How do you currently measure success?"
   - Comparison frame: "What are you comparing us against?"
3. **Demo/Pitch with C Types:**
   - Data-first: Lead with numbers and proof
   - Technical depth: Don't simplify too much
   - Documentation offer: "I can send the technical specs after"
4. **Handling C Objections:**
   - "Need more information": "What specific questions do I need to answer?"
   - Skepticism: Provide case studies, reference customers
   - Competitor comparison: Side-by-side analysis
5. **Closing C Types:**
   - Proof of concept: "Would a trial help you evaluate?"
   - Written proposal: They want to analyze
   - Time: Don't rush—but set clear next steps

**Special Note:** Many technical founders are C-types—you're selling to your own personality type. Don't assume similarity means easy sale.
