## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
Every founder hears objections. "Too expensive." "We're happy with our current solution." "Not the right time." The difference between founders who close and founders who crumble is preparation—having a proven response ready for every objection before you hear it. This course teaches the LARA framework for objection handling and guides you in building your own searchable Objection Handling Database that grows smarter with every sales conversation.

### Why This Course Matters
- **Fear of Rejection:** #1 reason technical founders avoid sales—objection mastery removes the fear
- **Pattern Recognition:** 80% of objections fall into 5-6 categories—prepare once, use forever
- **Systematic Response:** Frameworks beat improvisation when stakes are high
- **Institutional Knowledge:** Your objection database becomes an asset you can hand to future salespeople

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Apply the LARA framework (Listen, Acknowledge, Respond, Ask) to any objection
2. Categorize objections into the 6 universal types
3. Build a searchable database of objections and proven responses
4. Track response effectiveness and iterate on what works
5. Handle objections without becoming defensive or aggressive

### Prerequisites
- Experience with sales conversations (even unsuccessful ones)
- Completed Course 2: Discovery Framework (understand qualification)
- Willingness to document and learn from rejection

## COURSE STRUCTURE (12 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: Why Objections Are Opportunities, Not Rejections
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation (The Teaching)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Reframe objections as buying signals (they're engaged enough to push back)
- Identify the psychological barrier that makes objections feel personal
- Understand why prepared responses outperform improvisation

**Content Outline:**
1. **Hook:** "The objection that turned into a $50K deal—after the prospect said 'no' three times"
2. **The Objection Paradox:**
   - Silence is worse than objection (no engagement)
   - Objection = they're thinking about it
   - Questions behind objections reveal real concerns
3. **Why Objections Feel Personal:**
   - Technical founders identify with their product
   - Rejection of product feels like rejection of self
   - Reframe: They're rejecting the pitch, not you
4. **The Preparation Advantage:**
   - Top salespeople have heard every objection before
   - They've practiced responses until fluid
   - You can achieve the same with systematic preparation
5. **Preview of the LARA Framework:**
   - Listen → Acknowledge → Respond → Ask
   - Every objection follows this pattern

**Assessment:**
- Reflection: "What objection do you dread most? Why?"

### Lesson 2: The 6 Universal Objection Categories
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Categorize any objection into one of 6 types
- Recognize that limited categories = manageable preparation
- Understand the root concern behind each category

**Content Outline:**
1. **Category 1: Price/Budget**
   - "It's too expensive"
   - "We don't have budget"
   - "Can you do better on price?"
   - Root concern: Value not established, risk of waste
2. **Category 2: Timing**
   - "Not the right time"
   - "We're too busy right now"
   - "Check back next quarter"
   - Root concern: Prioritization, change disruption
3. **Category 3: Authority**
   - "I need to check with my boss"
   - "I'm not the decision maker"
   - "Our board would need to approve"
   - Root concern: Risk of advocacy, internal politics
4. **Category 4: Need**
   - "We don't really need this"
   - "Our current solution works fine"
   - "This isn't a priority"
   - Root concern: Pain not acute enough
5. **Category 5: Trust**
   - "How do I know this will work?"
   - "We've been burned before"
   - "You're too small/new"
   - Root concern: Risk of failure, credibility questions
6. **Category 6: Competition**
   - "We're already using [competitor]"
   - "We're evaluating other options"
   - "What makes you different?"
   - Root concern: Switching cost, alternative comparison

**Practice Exercise:**
- Categorize 10 objections you've heard
- Identify the root concern for each

### Lesson 3: The LARA Framework - Your Objection Handling System
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply all four LARA components to objection handling
- Understand why each step matters psychologically
- Practice the framework with sample objections

**Content Outline:**
1. **L - Listen:**
   - Let them finish (don't interrupt)
   - Ask clarifying questions if needed
   - Understand the real objection beneath the surface
2. **A - Acknowledge:**
   - Show you heard them: "I understand..."
   - Validate the concern: "That's a fair concern..."
   - Never dismiss: Avoid "but" immediately after acknowledgment
3. **R - Respond:**
   - Address the specific concern
   - Use evidence: case studies, data, testimonials
   - Keep it concise: one key point, not five
4. **A - Ask:**
   - Check if concern is resolved: "Does that help?"
   - Uncover hidden objections: "Is there anything else?"
   - Advance to next step: "Given that, shall we move forward?"
5. **LARA Example:**
   - Objection: "It's too expensive"
   - Listen: "Tell me more—what were you expecting to invest?"
   - Acknowledge: "I hear you—price is an important factor"
   - Respond: "Our customers typically see ROI in 90 days. [Company X] saved $Y monthly"
   - Ask: "If ROI timeline works, would budget be unlocked?"

**Visual Framework:** LARA flow diagram with sample dialogues

### Lesson 4: Handling Price Objections Without Discounting
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Respond to price objections without immediately discounting
- Reframe price conversations around value and ROI
- Know when discounting is appropriate vs. value destruction

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Founders Discount Too Fast:**
   - Fear of losing the deal
   - Don't know how to respond otherwise
   - Undervalue their own solution
2. **Price Objection Responses:**
   - "Too expensive" → "Compared to what?" (understand anchor)
   - "No budget" → "Is budget the only blocker?" (qualification check)
   - "Can you do better?" → "What would we need to do to make this work?" (negotiate, don't cave)
3. **Value Reframe Technique:**
   - Calculate cost of problem (their current pain in $$$)
   - Compare: $X/month vs. $Y problem cost
   - ROI framing: "Most customers see payback in [timeframe]"
4. **When to Discount:**
   - Annual commitment (real value to you)
   - Case study/testimonial permission
   - Referral commitment
   - Never: Just to close the deal
5. **Walking Away:**
   - "If budget is truly the blocker, I understand. Let's reconnect when timing is better."

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write 3 responses to "It's too expensive" using LARA
- Role-play with AI coach

### Lesson 5: Handling Timing Objections - Creating Urgency
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish real timing issues from polite brush-offs
- Create legitimate urgency without pressure tactics
- Set effective follow-up for genuine timing objections

**Content Outline:**
1. **Real vs. Fake Timing:**
   - Real: "We're in the middle of a migration, can't add more change"
   - Fake: "Not the right time" (no specific reason)
2. **Testing for Real Timing:**
   - "Help me understand—what's happening in [timeframe] that changes this?"
   - "If timing weren't an issue, would this be something you'd want?"
3. **Response Strategies:**
   - Cost of delay: "What does waiting cost you?"
   - Competitive angle: "Your competitors are already doing this"
   - Soft close: "Should we at least get you set up so you're ready when timing clears?"
4. **Setting Real Follow-Ups:**
   - Specific date: "Can we reconnect on [date]?"
   - Trigger-based: "What would need to happen for timing to be right?"
   - Calendar hold: "Let me send you a calendar invite for Q2"
5. **Avoiding: False Urgency**
   - No fake deadlines
   - No "price goes up tomorrow"
   - Integrity builds long-term pipeline

**Case Study:** Converting a "not now" to a closed deal 90 days later

### Lesson 6: Handling Authority Objections - Navigating the Org Chart
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify who the real decision-maker is
- Equip your champion to sell internally
- Request introductions without being pushy

**Content Outline:**
1. **Authority Mapping:**
   - Who influences? Who decides? Who blocks?
   - Your contact's role in the buying committee
2. **Response Strategies:**
   - "I'm not the decision-maker" → "Who else would need to be involved? Can we loop them in?"
   - "I need to check with my boss" → "What would help you present this internally?"
   - Equip with ammunition: Executive summary, ROI calculator, comparison doc
3. **Champion Enablement:**
   - Give them the words: "Here's how to explain this to [decision-maker]"
   - Anticipate their objections: "They'll probably ask about [X]—here's the answer"
   - Make them look good: "This could be a win for you internally"
4. **Multi-Threading:**
   - Don't rely on single contact
   - Request introduction: "Would it make sense to include [other stakeholder]?"
   - Cold outreach to decision-maker with champion's name

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write email your champion can forward to decision-maker
- Plan multi-threading strategy

### Lesson 7: Handling Need Objections - When They Don't See the Pain
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Uncover latent pain when prospects say "we're fine"
- Challenge status quo without being aggressive
- Know when "no need" means "wrong ICP"

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why They Say "No Need":**
   - Pain not acute enough (yet)
   - Unaware of better alternatives
   - Status quo bias (change is work)
   - Actually wrong fit (respect this)
2. **Response Strategies:**
   - "We're fine with current solution" → "What would 'better' look like?"
   - "Not a priority" → "What is the priority? How does this relate?"
   - "Works well enough" → "What's the cost of 'good enough' vs. great?"
3. **Pain Amplification (Ethical):**
   - Quantify current state: "How many hours does that take today?"
   - Compare to alternative: "Others in your position save X hours with this"
   - Implication questions: "What would you do with that time back?"
4. **When to Walk Away:**
   - If ICP mismatch, graceful exit
   - "Sounds like timing isn't right—let me check back in 6 months"

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write Five Whys sequence to uncover latent pain

### Lesson 8: Handling Trust Objections - Building Credibility
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Address "too small/new" concerns for early-stage companies
- Use social proof effectively
- Offer risk mitigation without desperation

**Content Outline:**
1. **Trust Objections for Solo Founders:**
   - "You're too new" / "You're too small"
   - "How do I know you'll be around?"
   - "We've been burned before"
2. **Response Strategies:**
   - Social proof: "We work with [similar company] who had the same concern"
   - Risk mitigation: "Here's our guarantee..." / "Start with a pilot"
   - Transparency: Acknowledge size, pivot to advantage ("We're small because we just work with [ICP], which means...")
3. **Building Trust Assets:**
   - Customer testimonials (specific, named)
   - Case studies with metrics
   - Logos (use with permission)
   - Reviews/ratings (G2, Capterra)
4. **Turning Size into Advantage:**
   - "Big vendors deprioritize small customers—we can't afford to"
   - "I'll be your direct contact, not a rotating support team"

**Assessment:**
- Compile your current trust assets
- Identify gaps to fill

### Lesson 9: Handling Competition Objections - Differentiation Under Fire
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Respond to "we're using [competitor]" without bashing
- Articulate differentiation clearly and concisely
- Handle bake-offs and comparisons professionally

**Content Outline:**
1. **Competition Objection Types:**
   - "We're already using [competitor]"
   - "We're evaluating [competitor]"
   - "How are you different from [competitor]?"
2. **Response Principles:**
   - Never bash competitors (looks desperate)
   - Respect their choice (they may have good reasons)
   - Focus on differentiation, not superiority
3. **Response Strategies:**
   - "Already using" → "What prompted you to look at alternatives?"
   - "Evaluating" → "What criteria are most important to you?"
   - "How different" → "Here's what customers say when they compare: [specific difference]"
4. **Competitive Positioning:**
   - Know your advantages vs. top 3 competitors
   - Know where you're weaker (don't lie)
   - Use cases where you win vs. where they win
5. **Bake-Off Strategy:**
   - Offer to participate (confidence signal)
   - Define evaluation criteria (shape the scorecard)
   - Provide comparison guide (your terms)

**Practice Exercise:**
- Create competitive positioning document for top competitor
- Write 3 responses to "[Competitor] is cheaper"

### Lesson 10: Building Your Objection Database
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice (The Application)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Create a structured, searchable objection database
- Document objections, responses, and effectiveness
- Establish the feedback loop for continuous improvement

**Content Outline:**
1. **Database Structure:**
   - Category (6 types)
   - Objection verbatim (exact words they use)
   - Response script (LARA format)
   - Evidence/proof points to include
   - Effectiveness score (1-5, updated over time)
2. **Population Strategy:**
   - Start with objections you've heard
   - Add from this course's examples
   - Update after every sales conversation
3. **Using the Database:**
   - Before calls: Review likely objections
   - During calls: Mental recall of prepared response
   - After calls: Update with new objections, refine responses
4. **Effectiveness Tracking:**
   - Did the response work? (Conversation continued)
   - What variation worked better?
   - Which objections signal disqualification?

### Lesson 11: AI Roleplay - Objection Drilling
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Practice (Application)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Practice objection handling with AI prospects
- Build muscle memory for LARA responses
- Identify which objections need more preparation

**Content Outline:**
1. **Roleplay Setup:**
   - Select objection category to practice
   - Choose DISC personality for added realism
   - Set difficulty (cooperative → resistant)
2. **Drill Structure:**
   - AI raises objection
   - You respond using LARA
   - AI either accepts or pushes harder
   - Cycle continues until resolution or disqualification
3. **Practice Sessions:**
   - Session 1: Price objections (all variations)
   - Session 2: Timing objections
   - Session 3: Competition objections
   - Session 4: Random mix (surprise practice)
4. **Performance Feedback:**
   - LARA adherence score
   - Response quality rating
   - Handling time
   - Resolution success rate
