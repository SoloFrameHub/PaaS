## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
You can't email prospects you can't find. This course teaches systematic list building—the technical foundation for outbound lead generation. You'll master the tools (Apollo, Clay, LinkedIn Sales Navigator), the data enrichment process, and the quality controls that separate spammy mass outreach from targeted, high-response campaigns. This is where your ICP (Course 1) becomes an actual list of names, titles, and email addresses.

### Why This Course Matters
- **ICP → Real People:** Course 1 defined WHO to target; this course finds THEM
- **Quality Over Quantity:** 100 perfect-fit prospects > 10,000 random contacts
- **Tool Mastery:** These platforms have steep learning curves—this course flattens them
- **Deliverability Foundation:** Bad lists kill email deliverability (Course 5 prerequisite)

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Build targeted prospect lists using Apollo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, and Clay
2. Apply ICP filters to generate lists of 100-500 qualified prospects
3. Enrich basic contact data with firmographic and intent signals
4. Validate email addresses before outreach (reduce bounce rates <2%)
5. Organize and maintain a prospect database for ongoing outreach

### Prerequisites
- Completed Course 1: ICP Builder Workshop (targeting criteria defined)
- Free accounts created: Apollo.io, LinkedIn (Sales Navigator trial recommended)
- Spreadsheet proficiency (Google Sheets or Excel basics)

### Platform Integration Points
- **ICP Builder Component:** Exports filtering criteria to tools
- **Email Sequence Builder:** Receives validated lists
- **CRM Interface:** Prospect database management
- **Versioned Artifacts:** List building templates and filters

**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation (The Teaching)

**Learning Objectives:**

### Lesson 6: Email Validation - Protecting Your Sender Reputation
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain why email validation is non-negotiable
- Use validation tools to verify email addresses
- Achieve <2% bounce rate target

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Validation Matters:**
   - Bounce rate impact on deliverability
   - ISP reputation scoring
   - The death spiral: bounces → spam folder → no responses
2. **Types of Invalid Emails:**
   - Hard bounces: Address doesn't exist
   - Soft bounces: Mailbox full, server issues
   - Catch-all domains: Accepts all but may bounce
   - Spam traps: Designed to catch spammers
3. **Validation Tools:**
   - ZeroBounce: Industry standard, $16/1000 emails
   - Hunter.io: Includes validation with lookup
   - NeverBounce: Bulk validation
   - Clearout: Budget option
4. **Validation Workflow:**
   - Export list → Upload to validator → Remove invalid → Re-export
   - Target: >95% valid before outreach
5. **Real-Time vs. Batch:**
   - Real-time API: Validate on entry
   - Batch: Validate entire list at once

**Practice Exercise:**
- Validate your 50-prospect list
- Calculate validity rate
- Remove invalid/risky addresses

### Lesson 8: Intent Signals - Finding Ready-to-Buy Prospects
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify intent signals that indicate buying readiness
- Set up monitoring for trigger events
- Prioritize prospects based on intent signals

**Content Outline:**
1. **What Are Intent Signals?**
   - Events or behaviors indicating purchase likelihood
   - Move prospects from "could buy someday" to "might buy now"
2. **Types of Intent Signals:**
   - Hiring: Posted job for role your product helps
   - Funding: Just raised a round (budget unlocked)
   - Technology: Changed/added relevant tools
   - Content: Researching related topics (Bombora, G2 intent)
   - Trigger events: Leadership change, expansion, acquisition
3. **Monitoring Tools:**
   - Apollo: Funding + hiring alerts
   - Google Alerts: Company news mentions
   - LinkedIn: Job posting alerts
   - Crunchbase Pro: Funding notifications
4. **Intent-Based Prioritization:**
   - High intent: Funding + hiring + tech change
   - Medium intent: One signal present
   - Low intent: No signals (don't ignore—nurture)
5. **Case Study:** How monitoring "Hired Head of Growth" led to 5 conversations in one week

**Practice Exercise:**
- Add intent signals to your prospect list
- Set up 3 monitoring alerts

### Lesson 9: Data Hygiene - Maintaining List Quality Over Time
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Establish processes for ongoing list maintenance
- Handle bounces, replies, and status changes
- Avoid the "dirty data" trap

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Lists Decay:**
   - People change jobs (20%+ annually)
   - Emails become invalid
   - Companies change (mergers, closures)
2. **Hygiene Processes:**
   - Weekly: Process bounces, update statuses
   - Monthly: Re-validate active sequences
   - Quarterly: Full list audit
3. **Status Management:**
   - New → Contacted → Replied → Engaged → Customer/Lost
   - Removing vs. archiving (never delete outreach history)
4. **Duplicate Prevention:**
   - Unique key: Email address
   - Merge vs. skip decisions
5. **Tools for Hygiene:**
   - CRM duplicate detection
   - Automated bounce processing
   - Re-validation services

**Assessment:**
- Create your data hygiene checklist
- Document weekly/monthly processes

### Lesson 10: Segmentation for Personalized Outreach
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Segment lists for targeted messaging
- Create segment-specific value propositions
- Prepare lists for Course 5 (Cold Email Mastery)

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Segment:**
   - One message doesn't fit all
   - Personalization increases response rates 2-3x
   - Easier to write when audience is specific
2. **Segmentation Dimensions:**
   - Firmographic: Industry, size, region
   - Role-based: Title, department, seniority
   - Intent-based: High/medium/low intent signals
   - Use case: Primary problem they'd solve
3. **Segment Size Guidelines:**
   - Too small (<20): Not worth separate sequence
   - Too large (>500): Probably needs further segmentation
   - Sweet spot: 50-200 per segment
4. **Segment-Specific Value Props:**
   - Same product, different angle per segment
   - Reference their specific context

**Practice Exercise:**
- Segment your list into 2-3 groups
- Write one-sentence value prop per segment
