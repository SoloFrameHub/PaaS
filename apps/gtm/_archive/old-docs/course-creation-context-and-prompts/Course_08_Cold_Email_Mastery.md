## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description
Cold email is the most cost-effective way for a bootstrapped founder to generate conversations with potential customers. But 99% of cold emails get ignored—not because cold email doesn't work, but because most cold emails are terrible. This course teaches you to write emails that get replies by focusing on the prospect's problems, not your product's features. You'll learn frameworks, templates, and sequences that consistently achieve 5-10%+ reply rates.

### Why This Course Matters
- **Highest ROI Channel:** $0 cost per send, scales infinitely with time
- **The Great Equalizer:** Same inbox as enterprise sales teams, but you can personalize better
- **Technical Founder Trap:** Engineers write feature-focused emails that get ignored
- **Systematic Approach:** Templates + personalization = repeatable success

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this course, founders will be able to:
1. Write cold emails that achieve >5% reply rates
2. Apply the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) and AIDA frameworks to email copy
3. Create multi-touch email sequences (3-5 emails over 2-3 weeks)
4. Personalize at scale using merge fields and research
5. A/B test subject lines and email body copy

### Prerequisites
- Completed Course 4: List Building (validated prospect list ready)
- Email sending account configured (Gmail or dedicated domain)
- Basic understanding of your value proposition

### Platform Integration Points
- **Email Sequence Builder:** Create and manage automated sequences
- **ICP Builder:** Pull personalization variables
- **Performance Analytics:** Track open rates, reply rates, conversion
- **Versioned Artifacts:** Email templates as evolving documents

### Lesson 1: Why 99% of Cold Emails Fail (And How to Join the 1%)
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation (The Teaching)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the three fatal flaws of most cold emails
- Understand the prospect's perspective (why they ignore emails)
- Set realistic expectations for cold email performance

**Content Outline:**
1. **Hook:** "The CEO who reads 200+ cold emails per month—and replies to 3"
2. **Why Most Cold Emails Fail:**
   - Flaw 1: Me-focused ("We're the leading provider...")
   - Flaw 2: Feature-dumping ("Our platform offers...")
   - Flaw 3: No clear ask ("Let me know if you'd like to chat sometime...")
3. **The Prospect's Perspective:**
   - Inbox overload (100+ emails/day for decision-makers)
   - Pattern recognition (they know cold email when they see it)
   - Trust deficit (why should they believe you?)
4. **What Makes the 1% Different:**
   - Problem-focused, not product-focused
   - Specific and relevant
   - Clear, low-friction call-to-action
5. **Realistic Benchmarks:**
   - Good: 5-10% reply rate
   - Great: 10-20% reply rate
   - Exceptional: 20%+ (highly targeted, warm signals)

**Assessment:**
- Analyze 3 cold emails: Identify fatal flaws in each

### Lesson 2: The PAS Framework - Problem, Agitate, Solution
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply the PAS copywriting framework to cold emails
- Write problem-focused opening lines
- Connect problems to emotional stakes (agitation)

**Content Outline:**
1. **PAS Overview:**
   - **Problem:** Name their specific pain
   - **Agitate:** Amplify the cost of the problem
   - **Solution:** Position your offer as the answer
2. **Problem Section (The Hook):**
   - Specific > Generic: "Most SaaS CTOs waste 10+ hours/week on..." not "Many companies struggle with..."
   - Research-backed: Use their context
   - Question format works: "Are you still manually doing X?"
3. **Agitate Section (The Stakes):**
   - What happens if they don't solve this?
   - Quantify when possible: Time, money, risk
   - Don't overdo it (one sentence max)
4. **Solution Section (The Bridge):**
   - Light touch—don't pitch features
   - Credibility signal: "We helped [similar company] achieve [result]"
   - Transition to CTA
5. **PAS Email Template:**
   - Subject: Reference to problem or result
   - Line 1: Problem statement
   - Line 2: Agitation (cost/consequence)
   - Line 3: Solution bridge
   - Line 4: CTA

**Case Study:** Before/after PAS transformation of a real cold email

### Lesson 3: The AIDA Framework - Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply the AIDA framework to longer-form cold emails
- Write attention-grabbing subject lines
- Build interest and desire before the ask

**Content Outline:**
1. **AIDA Overview:**
   - **Attention:** Subject line + first line (they open)
   - **Interest:** Relevant hook (they keep reading)
   - **Desire:** Value proposition (they want it)
   - **Action:** Clear CTA (they respond)
2. **Attention (Subject Line Mastery):**
   - Short: 3-5 words optimal
   - Specific: Reference their context
   - Curiosity or value: "Quick question about [their challenge]"
   - Avoid spam triggers: No ALL CAPS, no !!!, no "FREE"
3. **Interest (First 2 Lines):**
   - Personalization that proves research
   - Problem recognition
   - Pattern interrupt (don't sound like every other email)
4. **Desire (The Value Bridge):**
   - What outcome can you help them achieve?
   - Social proof: Similar companies, results
   - Risk reversal: Low commitment ask
5. **Action (The CTA):**
   - Single, clear ask
   - Low friction: "Open to a 15-minute call next week?"
   - Binary format: Yes/no answerable

**Practice Exercise:**
- Rewrite your current cold email using AIDA
- Peer review in accountability pod

### Lesson 4: Subject Lines That Get Opened
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Write subject lines that achieve >40% open rates
- Test subject line variations
- Avoid spam filter triggers

**Content Outline:**
1. **Subject Line Psychology:**
   - Curiosity: What will I learn?
   - Relevance: Does this apply to me?
   - Urgency: Should I read this now?
2. **High-Performing Patterns:**
   - Question format: "Quick question about [their initiative]"
   - Reference format: "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
   - Result format: "[Competitor] just achieved [outcome]"
   - Direct format: "[Their company] + [Your company]"
3. **What to Avoid:**
   - Generic: "Great opportunity" (spam trigger)
   - Salesy: "SAVE 50% TODAY" (immediate delete)
   - Deceptive: "Re:" when not a reply (erodes trust)
4. **Testing Methodology:**
   - A/B test 2 subject lines per sequence
   - Sample size: 50+ sends per variation
   - Winner threshold: 10%+ difference
5. **Deliverability Considerations:**
   - Spam words to avoid
   - Character limits (50-60 max)
   - Emoji: Usually hurt in B2B

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write 5 subject line variations for your email
- Predict which will perform best

### Lesson 5: The First Line - Your Only Chance to Hook Them
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Write personalized first lines that prove relevance
- Avoid generic openers that scream "mass email"
- Research efficiently for first-line personalization

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why the First Line Matters Most:**
   - Email preview shows subject + first 5-10 words
   - Decision to read happens in <2 seconds
   - Generic opener = immediate delete
2. **Personalization Levels:**
   - Level 1: Name + company (bare minimum, not impressive)
   - Level 2: Reference specific trigger (funding, hire, news)
   - Level 3: Reference their content (post, podcast, talk)
   - Level 4: Reference mutual connection or shared experience
3. **First Line Formulas:**
   - Trigger-based: "Congrats on the Series A—scaling [function] must be top priority"
   - Content-based: "Your post on [topic] resonated—especially [specific point]"
   - Observation-based: "Noticed [company] just added [role/tool]—curious if [question]"
4. **Research Efficiency:**
   - 2-3 minutes per prospect max
   - LinkedIn: Recent posts, job changes, mutual connections
   - Company: News, funding, hiring
   - Personal: Podcast appearances, blog posts
5. **Batch Personalization:**
   - Segment first: Same trigger, same first line template
   - Fill in specifics with merge fields

**Practice Exercise:**
- Research and write first lines for 10 prospects
- Time yourself: 25 minutes max

### Lesson 6: The Body - Value Without Pitching
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Deliver value in 3-5 sentences without feature-dumping
- Position outcomes instead of capabilities
- Build credibility without sounding desperate

**Content Outline:**
1. **The Anti-Pitch Philosophy:**
   - They don't care what you built
   - They care what changes for them
   - Features = how you do it; Outcomes = why they care
2. **Body Structure:**
   - Sentence 1: Problem acknowledgment (continued from first line)
   - Sentence 2: Outcome you enable
   - Sentence 3: Brief credibility signal
   - Sentence 4: Transition to CTA
3. **Outcome Positioning:**
   - Bad: "We offer automated reporting dashboards"
   - Good: "Our customers cut weekly reporting time from 5 hours to 30 minutes"
   - Better: "We helped [similar company] eliminate their 5-hour weekly report process"
4. **Credibility Without Desperation:**
   - Name-drop: Similar companies, not big logos
   - Results: Specific metrics, not vague claims
   - Social proof: "We work with 50+ [ICP type]" not "trusted by thousands"
5. **Length Guidelines:**
   - Total email: 50-100 words ideal
   - Mobile view: See entire email without scrolling
   - Every word must earn its place

**Assessment:**
- Rewrite your email body to focus on outcomes, not features

### Lesson 7: The Call-to-Action - Making "Yes" Easy
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Write CTAs that get positive responses
- Reduce friction in the ask
- Match CTA to buyer readiness

**Content Outline:**
1. **CTA Psychology:**
   - Friction kills: Every barrier reduces response rate
   - Binary works: Yes/no questions easier than open-ended
   - Low commitment first: Don't ask for 60 minutes on first touch
2. **High-Converting CTA Patterns:**
   - Time-specific: "Open to 15 minutes next Tuesday or Thursday?"
   - Yes/No: "Would it make sense to explore this?"
   - Interest check: "Is this a priority for you right now?"
   - Value offer: "Happy to share the framework we used—want me to send it?"
3. **CTA Progressions (Multi-Touch):**
   - Email 1: Interest check ("Is this relevant?")
   - Email 2: Value offer ("Want me to send [resource]?")
   - Email 3: Direct ask ("Open to a quick call?")
   - Email 4: Breakup ("Should I close your file?")
4. **What to Avoid:**
   - Vague: "Let me know if you want to chat" (no urgency)
   - High friction: "Fill out this form" (too much work)
   - Desperate: "Anytime works for me" (low value signal)

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write 3 different CTAs for your email
- Test in upcoming sequence

### Lesson 8: Email Sequences - The Multi-Touch Approach
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design a 4-5 email sequence with strategic timing
- Vary angles and CTAs across touchpoints
- Know when to stop (avoid being annoying)

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Sequences Work:**
   - Timing: They may be busy, not uninterested
   - Persistence: 80% of deals require 5+ touchpoints
   - Different angles: What didn't resonate might later
2. **Sequence Structure (5-Email Model):**
   - **Email 1:** Primary pitch (PAS or AIDA)
   - **Email 2:** (Day 3) Follow-up + new angle
   - **Email 3:** (Day 7) Value offer (resource, insight)
   - **Email 4:** (Day 12) Social proof or case study
   - **Email 5:** (Day 18) Breakup email
3. **Angle Variations:**
   - Email 1: Problem-focused
   - Email 2: Outcome-focused
   - Email 3: Curiosity-focused (offer insight)
   - Email 4: Proof-focused (case study)
   - Email 5: Scarcity/breakup
4. **Timing Best Practices:**
   - Business days only (avoid weekends for B2B)
   - Tuesday-Thursday optimal
   - Time of day: 7-8am or 4-5pm often work
5. **When to Stop:**
   - After explicit "no" (respect it)
   - After 5 emails with zero engagement
   - Move to nurture, revisit in 3-6 months

- Set up timing and personalization

### Lesson 9: The Breakup Email - Getting Replies from Silence
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Evidence

**Learning Objectives:**
- Write effective breakup emails that generate responses
- Use psychology of loss to prompt action
- Know when and how to re-engage later

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Breakup Emails Work:**
   - Creates closure (people don't like loose ends)
   - Low pressure (you're leaving, not pushing)
   - Sometimes timing is finally right
2. **Breakup Email Patterns:**
   - Direct: "I'll assume the timing isn't right and close your file"
   - Question: "Should I stop reaching out?"
   - Helpful: "Want me to circle back in Q2 instead?"
3. **Breakup Email Examples:**
   - "Haven't heard back—totally understand if timing's off. Should I check back in 6 months, or is this a dead end?"
   - "Going to assume this isn't a priority right now. If anything changes, I'm here."
4. **Post-Breakup Strategy:**
   - Add to nurture list
   - Re-engage on trigger events
   - 3-6 month cool-off before new sequence

**Case Study:** Breakup emails that generated 20%+ reply rates

### Lesson 10: Personalization at Scale - The 80/20 Approach
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Personalize efficiently without spending hours per email
- Use merge fields and segment-specific templates
- Balance personalization with scalability

**Content Outline:**
1. **The Personalization Spectrum:**
   - 0%: Mass email (spam)
   - 20%: Segment-specific template + name
   - 50%: First line personalized + segment template
   - 80%: Research-based personalization throughout
   - 100%: Fully custom (not scalable)
2. **The 80/20 Sweet Spot:**
   - 80% template (body, CTA consistent)
   - 20% personalized (first line, specific reference)
   - Result: Quality + volume
3. **Merge Field Strategy:**
   - First name
   - Company name
   - Trigger event reference
   - Industry-specific pain point
   - Competitive reference
4. **Batch Personalization Workflow:**
   - Segment by trigger (funding, hiring, tech)
   - Write first line template per trigger
   - Fill specifics in bulk

**Practice Exercise:**
- Create template with merge fields
- Personalize first lines for 20 prospects in 30 minutes

### Lesson 11: A/B Testing Your Way to Higher Reply Rates
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design valid A/B tests for cold email
- Interpret test results correctly
- Iterate systematically toward higher performance

**Content Outline:**
1. **What to Test:**
   - Subject lines (highest impact)
   - First lines (second highest)
   - CTA variations
   - Timing (day, time of send)
   - Length (short vs. detailed)
2. **Testing Methodology:**
   - One variable at a time
   - Same segment for both variations
   - Minimum sample: 50 per variation
   - Statistical significance matters
3. **Common Testing Mistakes:**
   - Too many variables (can't attribute results)
   - Too small sample (noise, not signal)
   - Not tracking properly (measure opens AND replies)
4. **Iteration Cycle:**
   - Test → Winner → New test on next element
   - Document learnings (what works for your ICP)

**Platform Tool:** A/B testing setup in Email Sequence Builder

### Lesson 12: Deliverability - Making Sure Emails Arrive
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand email deliverability fundamentals
- Configure sending infrastructure properly
- Monitor and maintain sender reputation

**Content Outline:**
1. **Deliverability Basics:**
   - SPF, DKIM, DMARC authentication
   - Sender reputation scoring
   - ISP filtering algorithms
2. **Technical Setup:**
   - Custom domain (never Gmail for cold outreach)
   - Proper DNS records
   - Warmup process for new domains
3. **Sending Volume Guidelines:**
   - New domain: 10-20/day first week
   - Ramp slowly: +10-20/day per week
   - Max recommended: 100-150/day per domain
4. **Reputation Killers:**
   - High bounce rate (>5%)
   - Spam complaints (>0.1%)
   - Low engagement signals
5. **Monitoring Tools:**
   - Google Postmaster Tools (for Gmail)
   - Mail-tester.com (quick checks)
   - Deliverability platforms (Lemlist, Instantly)

**Assessment:**
- Complete deliverability checklist for your setup

### Lesson 13: Responding to Replies - The Conversion Point
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Teaching Mode:** Explanation + Practice

**Learning Objectives:**
- Respond to positive replies to secure meetings
- Handle objection-laden replies
- Re-engage soft "nos" appropriately

**Content Outline:**
1. **Positive Reply Handling:**
   - Speed matters: Reply within 1-4 hours
   - Calendar link: Remove friction immediately
   - Confirmation: Recap and agenda preview
2. **Objection Replies:**
   - "Not interested": Acknowledge, ask why (learn)
   - "Bad timing": Ask when to follow up
   - "Send more info": Send brief follow-up, re-ask for call
   - "We use competitor": Offer comparison insight
3. **Soft No Handling:**
   - Respectful persistence
   - Add to nurture
   - Trigger-based re-engagement
4. **Common Mistakes:**
   - Slow response (momentum lost)
   - Feature-dumping in response (they wanted to talk, not read more)
   - Not having clear next step

**Practice Exercise:**
- Write response templates for 5 common reply types
