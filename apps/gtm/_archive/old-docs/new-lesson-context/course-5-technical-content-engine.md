## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description

Content marketing is the highest-ROI channel for technical founders because you can leverage your expertise into assets that generate leads for years. Plausible Analytics grew from $400 MRR to $3.1M ARR primarily through content marketing with $0 advertising spend—their strategy was described as "boring" but ruthlessly consistent. This course teaches content pillar architecture, the one-to-many repurposing workflow, and how to create content that demonstrates expertise AND generates leads—not just traffic.

The trap most technical founders fall into is creating content that demonstrates expertise but doesn't generate leads—blog posts that get traffic but no conversions. They spend 80% of time on creation and 20% on distribution when the ratio should be inverted. This course teaches content strategy that intentionally moves readers toward your product, with every piece serving a purpose in the larger marketing system.

Technical founders have a unique advantage: your documentation instincts, systematic thinking, and deep expertise are exactly what content marketing rewards. This course teaches you to leverage those strengths while avoiding the perfectionism trap that keeps founders from publishing.

### Why This Course Matters

**Founder Pain Points Addressed:**
- "I published 20 blog posts and got zero leads"
- "I don't have time to create content AND build product"
- "My content gets traffic but no one converts"
- "I don't know what to write about"
- "Building in public feels narcissistic—who cares about my journey?"
- "Content marketing takes 6 months—I need leads now"

**The Core Insight:** Content that converts isn't about volume or even quality—it's about strategy. Every piece of content should have a job: attract a specific audience segment, address a specific problem, and lead to a specific next step. The one-to-many workflow means you create once and distribute endlessly.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Design a content pillar architecture** with 3-5 core topics organized in pillar/cluster structure
2. **Implement the 80/20 content mix** balancing evergreen content that compounds with timely content for engagement
3. **Execute the one-to-many repurposing workflow** transforming one substantial piece into 5-10 distribution assets
4. **Build in public strategically** sharing progress that attracts customers, not just followers
5. **Create a sustainable content calendar** producing consistent output without burnout
6. **Analyze content effectiveness** using the Sales Linter framework to audit conversion potential
7. **Design lead magnets** that capture email addresses at each funnel stage
8. **Track content performance** connecting traffic to leads to revenue

## SOURCE FRAMEWORKS AND RESEARCH

### From Bootstrap Marketing Curriculum Research

**Content Pillar Architecture:**
- Organize all content around **3-5 core topics** with pillar pages (comprehensive guides) surrounded by cluster content (specific subtopics)
- Builds topical authority with search engines while creating internal linking structures
- The **80/20 content approach**: 80% evergreen educational content that compounds, 20% timely/trending content for social engagement

**Documentation-as-Content:**
- Leverages technical founder's natural tendency to document
- Internal processes, technical decisions, lessons learned become published content
- Buffer's sharing of interview questions and hiring processes exemplifies this approach

**One-Piece-to-Many Workflow:**
- Single substantial piece (blog post, video) transforms into:
  - 5-10 social posts
  - Email newsletter
  - Twitter threads
  - LinkedIn articles
  - Video clips

**The Fatal Mistake:**
> "The biggest mistake is creating without promoting—technical founders often spend 80% on creation and 20% on distribution when the ratio should be inverted."

**TOFU-MOFU-BOFU Pipeline:**
- **Top of Funnel:** Educational blog posts attract audience
- **Middle of Funnel:** Comparison content and case studies nurture interest
- **Bottom of Funnel:** Product demos and free trials convert leads
- Plausible's model: content drives traffic → 30-day free trial (no credit card) → **33.5% trial-to-paid conversion**

### From Community-Led Growth Research

**Building in Public (BIP):**
- "Authenticity beats polish. A post about 'How I lost my first customer' gets more engagement than 'New feature released'"
- Universal content approaches:
  - B2B: "Here is the exact cold email script that got me a 0% reply rate (and how I fixed it)"
  - Creator: "Here is the lesson plan I scrapped because it was too boring"
- Twitter/X and LinkedIn are primary BIP engines

**The "Feedback Loop" Launch:**
- B2B Founder posts: "I'm building a tool to automate API docs. Is this a pain for you, or am I wasting my time?"
  - Result: 50 comments (Market Validation) + 10 Beta users (Acquisition)
- Creator Founder posts: "I'm writing a guide on 'Toddler Tantrums.' What's the one situation that makes you want to pull your hair out?"
  - Result: 100 replies (Content Ideas) + Waitlist (Acquisition)

**Universal Truth:**
> "The person asking for advice gets a customer. The person asking for a sale gets ignored."

### From AEO Research (Content Structure)

**The Inverted Pyramid for AI:**
1. **Direct Answer (First 50 Words):** Start every post with a snippet-ready definition
   - Bad: "In this article, we will explore the many benefits of..."
   - Good: "Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) for solo founders typically ranges from $50–$150 depending on channel..."
2. **Q&A Formatting:** Use H2 headers as questions, immediate paragraph as direct answer
3. **Lists & Tables:** AI models prefer structured data—tables 28% more likely to be cited

### Lesson 3: The 80/20 Content Mix

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Balance evergreen content that compounds with timely content for engagement
- Identify content types appropriate for each category
- Plan content mix based on business stage

**Content Outline:**

1. **The 80/20 Principle for Content** (8 min)
   - 80% evergreen: Educational content that remains relevant
   - 20% timely: Trending topics, news reactions, current events
   - Why this ratio works: Compound returns + social engagement

2. **Evergreen Content Types** (12 min)
   - How-to guides and tutorials
   - Framework explanations
   - Comparison content ("[X] vs. [Y]")
   - "Ultimate guide" pillar content
   - FAQ content optimized for AEO
   - Characteristics: Searchable, referenceable, shareable over time

3. **Timely Content Types** (10 min)
   - Industry news reactions
   - Tool/product launch commentary
   - Conference/event coverage
   - Seasonal content
   - "Hot take" opinion pieces
   - Characteristics: Social engagement, establishes thought leadership

4. **Stage-Appropriate Mix** (10 min)
   - Pre-traction (Zero-to-Ten): 100% evergreen, build foundation
   - Early traction: 90/10 evergreen/timely
   - Established: 80/20 standard mix
   - Growth stage: Can experiment with 70/30
   - Why founders over-index on timely too early

5. **Content Calendar Integration** (6 min)
   - Planning evergreen in advance (batching)
   - Leaving slots for timely content
   - The "evergreen + timely layer" approach
   - Preview: Lesson 7 deep dive

6. **The Compound Effect** (4 min)
   - Month 1: 10 posts, 1,000 visits
   - Month 12: 120 posts, 50,000 visits (not linear)
   - Evergreen content as appreciating assets
   - Traffic from posts you wrote 2 years ago

**Assessment:**

### Lesson 8: Sales Linter for Content

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Audit content for sales effectiveness using the Sales Linter framework
- Identify and fix common content mistakes that kill conversions
- Balance value delivery with conversion optimization

**Content Outline:**

1. **What Is a Sales Linter?** (8 min)
   - Code linters catch syntax errors; sales linters catch conversion errors
   - Content that teaches but doesn't sell
   - The platform's Sales Linter tool (introduction)
   - Manual audit framework for any content

2. **The Sales Linter Checklist** (15 min)
   - **Audience clarity:** Is it clear who this is for? (First 50 words)
   - **Problem acknowledgment:** Does it name a specific pain?
   - **Credibility signals:** Why should they trust you?
   - **Value delivery:** Does it actually help?
   - **Soft CTA:** Is there a natural next step?
   - **Hard CTA:** Is there a conversion opportunity?
   - **Objection handling:** Does it address hesitations?
   - Scoring: 1-5 on each dimension

3. **Common Content Mistakes** (12 min)
   - **Too educational:** Teaches everything, asks for nothing
   - **Too salesy:** All pitch, no value
   - **Wrong audience:** Impresses peers, not prospects
   - **Buried CTA:** Great content, CTA at very bottom
   - **Generic advice:** Could apply to anyone
   - **No credibility:** Claims without evidence
   - Examples and fixes for each

4. **The Value/Ask Balance** (10 min)
   - 90% value, 10% ask (not 50/50)
   - "Soft CTAs": "If you want to go deeper..." / "Related: [your resource]"
   - "Hard CTAs": "Start your free trial" / "Book a demo"
   - Where to place each type
   - The "earned ask" concept

5. **Using the Platform Sales Linter** (6 min)
   - Uploading content for analysis
   - Interpreting results
   - Iteration workflow
   - AI-assisted improvements

6. **Audit Exercise** (4 min)
   - Select a piece of your content
   - Run through Sales Linter checklist
   - Identify top 3 improvements
   - Rewrite key sections

**Assessment:**
Audit one piece of content using the Sales Linter checklist. Score each dimension, identify the 3 weakest areas, and write improved versions of those sections.

5. **Lead Magnet Delivery System** (6 min)
