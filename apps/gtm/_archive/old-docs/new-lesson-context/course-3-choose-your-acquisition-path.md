## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description

Not all founders should use the same acquisition playbook. A B2B SaaS founder selling $200/month subscriptions to VPs of Marketing needs different tactics than a coach selling $5,000 programs to aspiring entrepreneurs, or a service provider landing $50,000 consulting engagements through referrals. This course helps founders identify their primary business context, select the appropriate playbook, and understand which academy courses are highest priority for THEIR situation.

This is a decision-making course—by the end, you'll have a customized learning path through the remaining academy content. The platform's AI will use your context selection to personalize examples, roleplay scenarios, and recommendations throughout all subsequent courses.

The goal is not to limit your options but to focus your execution. Solo founders who try to master every channel simultaneously master none. This course gives you permission to deprioritize the tactics that don't match your context—while ensuring you nail the ones that do.

### Why This Course Matters

**Founder Pain Points Addressed:**
- "I'm doing a little bit of everything and nothing is working"
- "Everyone says LinkedIn is the answer, but my customers aren't there"
- "I wasted 3 months on content marketing when I should have been doing direct outreach"
- "The advice that worked for that SaaS founder is failing for my coaching business"
- "I don't know which academy courses to prioritize—there's too much"

**The Core Insight:** The tactics differ by context, but the psychology is universal. Whether you're selling to "VP of Engineering" or "aspiring course creator," you're selling to an individual making a personal decision. This course clarifies which tactics match your buyer's context while reinforcing that the underlying principles (ICP clarity, discovery, value articulation) remain constant.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Identify your primary founder context** among the four archetypes (B2B SaaS, Creator/Coach, Service Business, Zero-to-Ten) based on deal characteristics, not assumptions
2. **Select the 3-5 highest-priority academy courses** for your context from the 33-course curriculum
3. **Create a 90-day acquisition focus plan** with specific weekly activities aligned to your context
4. **Articulate why certain channels are deprioritized** (not ignored) based on your context
5. **Recognize context overlap** and when to borrow tactics from adjacent playbooks
6. **Set context-appropriate metrics** and activity benchmarks for your first 90 days

## SOURCE FRAMEWORKS AND RESEARCH

### From Book Manuscript (Chapter 10: Playbooks)

**The Four Playbooks:**

1. **Playbook 1: Zero-to-Ten Sprint** (Pre-traction)
   - No customers, no audience, maximum uncertainty
   - "Get to 10 paying customers as fast as possible"
   - Manual, personal, intensive—validation before scale
   - Week 1-2: Define and Find (50-100 prospects manually)
   - Week 3-4: Direct Outreach (individual, personalized)
   - Week 5-8: Discovery and Iteration

2. **Playbook 2: B2B SaaS Founder**
   - $50-500/month self-serve or $2,000-10,000/year sales-assisted
   - Primary channels: LinkedIn, cold email, content, partnerships
   - Weekly rhythm: Mon prospecting, Tue-Thu engagement/outreach, Fri content/admin
   - Must account for buying committee even in individual conversations
   - 30-50 emails/day sustainable per domain

3. **Playbook 3: Coach/Consultant**
   - $500-$25,000+ offers to individuals or businesses
   - Primary channels: Content/audience, referrals, speaking/guesting, communities
   - Content-first approach: "Give away the what and how—people pay for implementation"
   - Product ladder: Free → Low-ticket → Mid-ticket → High-ticket
   - Enrollment conversation focus: Qualification matters more than closing

4. **Playbook 4: Creator**
   - Digital products to audience (courses, templates, communities)
   - Primary channels: Platform content, email list, launches, collaborations
   - Audience first, monetization second
   - Email list is the asset you own
   - Launch windows vs. evergreen (launches create urgency)

**Key Insight from Manuscript:**
> "The playbooks overlap. A B2B SaaS founder might also be building content. A coach might need the zero-to-ten sprint before implementing the service business playbook. A creator might eventually move to B2B. Start with the playbook that matches your primary motion right now. But borrow tactics from other playbooks when they apply."

### From Research: Universal Psychology

**From Sales Psychology Research:**
- Identity-role conflict is universal: "Builder/Creator" identity conflicts with "Seller" requirement
- Technical founders view sales as "inefficiency"—interrupts real work
- Creator founders view sales as "betrayal"—violates authentic relationship
- The intervention is identical: Cognitive reframing from "Selling" to "Serving"

**From DISC Research:**
- DISC applies universally because you're selling to PEOPLE, not job titles
- A "High D" buyer exhibits identical impatience whether VP of Engineering or aspiring entrepreneur
- The same behavioral identification works across both B2B and creator contexts

## COURSE STRUCTURE (6 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: The Four Founder Contexts

**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between the four founder contexts based on deal characteristics, not assumptions
- Identify the primary context that matches your current business reality
- Understand why context selection affects tactical priorities

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Context Selection Problem** (7 min)
   - Why "best practices" advice fails: optimized for wrong context
   - The $200/month SaaS founder vs. $10,000 coach: same psychology, different tactics
   - "Primary motion" concept: you can have secondary contexts, but need one focus

2. **Context 1: Zero-to-Ten Sprint** (8 min)
   - Definition: Pre-traction, no customers, no audience
   - Key signal: "I don't know if anyone will pay for this yet"
   - Primary objective: Validation, not scale
   - Timeline: 8-12 weeks of intensive manual outreach
   - This is a PHASE, not a permanent context—everyone passes through it

3. **Context 2: B2B SaaS** (8 min)
   - Definition: Software/service sold to businesses with buying committees
   - Key signals: Multiple stakeholders, longer cycles, $1K-50K annual deals
   - Primary objective: Navigate organizational buying while selling to individuals
   - The "VP problem": Even when selling to one person, you're selling to a committee

4. **Context 3: Creator/Coach** (8 min)
   - Definition: Expertise/transformation sold to individuals from audience
   - Key signals: Personal brand matters, audience precedes monetization
   - Primary objective: Build trust at scale, convert audience to customers
   - The overlap with B2B: Course creators selling to "aspiring entrepreneurs" are doing B2B

5. **Context 4: Service Business** (7 min)
   - Definition: High-touch delivery, often relationship-based sales
   - Key signals: Custom proposals, referral-heavy, $5K-100K+ engagements
   - Primary objective: Deep qualification, fewer but higher-value relationships
   - The hybrid reality: Many service businesses also have productized components

6. **The Context Decision Matrix** (7 min)
   - Questions to identify your context:
     - What's your average deal size?
     - How do customers find you (outbound/inbound/referral)?
     - How many stakeholders are involved in purchase decision?
     - Is there a "buying committee" or individual decision-maker?
   - Handling context overlap (SaaS founder who also coaches, creator with B2B offers)

**Assessment:**
Complete the Founder Context Assessment (AI-guided questionnaire) that identifies your primary and secondary contexts. Output: Context Profile Card showing your primary context, secondary context (if applicable), and initial course prioritization.

### Lesson 2: B2B SaaS Acquisition Path

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Map the B2B SaaS acquisition funnel specific to solo founder constraints
- Identify the 5-7 highest-priority academy courses for B2B SaaS context
- Create a weekly rhythm template for B2B SaaS acquisition

**Content Outline:**

1. **The B2B SaaS Reality for Solo Founders** (10 min)
   - You don't have SDRs or marketing teams
   - Buying committee complexity: Champion → Influencer → Decision-maker → Procurement
   - Deal cycles: 30-90 days typical, longer for enterprise
   - Revenue math: How many deals do you need at your ACV?

2. **The B2B SaaS Acquisition Stack** (12 min)
   - **Primary channels:**
     - LinkedIn (prospecting + content): 60% of effort
     - Cold email: 25% of effort
     - Content/SEO: 15% of effort (long-term investment)
   - **Secondary channels:**
     - Partnerships/integrations
     - Product-led growth hooks
     - Community presence

3. **The B2B Weekly Rhythm** (10 min)
   - Monday: Prospect identification (20-30 new prospects)
   - Tuesday-Thursday: Engagement and outreach
   - Friday: Content and administration
   - Time allocation: 15-20 hours/week on acquisition
   - The "90 minutes of gold": Morning prospecting block

4. **Academy Course Prioritization for B2B SaaS** (12 min)
   - **Tier 1 (Complete First):**
     - Course 4: List Building & Prospecting Infrastructure
     - Course 8: Cold Email Mastery
     - Course 14: Discovery Framework (BANT/MEDDIC)
   - **Tier 2 (Next Priority):**
     - Course 7: LinkedIn Growth Engine
     - Course 13: Understanding DISC Buyer Personas
     - Course 17: Objection Handling Database
   - **Tier 3 (When Established):**
     - Course 5: Technical Content Engine
     - Course 6: SEO & Answer Engine Optimization
     - Course 20: Sales Pipeline Management

5. **B2B SaaS Metrics That Matter** (7 min)
   - Outreach volume: 30-50 emails/day, 20 LinkedIn touches/day
   - Response rates: 10-20% cold email, 30%+ warm LinkedIn
   - Pipeline velocity: Active deals × win rate × deal size ÷ cycle time
   - Warning signs: >20% deals stalled at proposal

6. **The 90-Day B2B SaaS Focus Plan** (4 min)
   - Days 1-30: Infrastructure + first outreach (50-100 prospects)
   - Days 31-60: Optimize messaging based on responses
   - Days 61-90: Scale what works, cut what doesn't
   - Milestone: First closed deal validates entire approach

   - Content: 1-2 high-quality pieces per week (depth > frequency)

   - The referral ask rhythm: Systematic, not opportunistic

4. **Academy Course Prioritization for Service Business** (12 min)

### Lesson 5: The Zero-to-Ten Sprint

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Execute the "Zero-to-Ten Sprint" methodology for pre-traction founders
- Understand why validation precedes scale
- Create a week-by-week sprint plan for first customer acquisition

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Zero-to-Ten Is Different** (8 min)
   - Maximum uncertainty: You don't know if anyone will pay
   - ICP is a hypothesis, not a fact
   - The goal: 10 paying customers as fast as possible
   - Everything else is distraction
   - "Ten customers is not a business—but it proves someone will pay"

2. **Week 1-2: Define and Find** (10 min)
   - ICP hypothesis: Narrower is better (can always expand)
   - Build first prospect list MANUALLY (no tools, no automation)
   - Find 50-100 prospects who match your hypothesis
   - Sources: LinkedIn, industry directories, community forums
   - Note for each: Why they might be a fit

3. **Week 3-4: Direct Outreach** (10 min)
   - Personal outreach to EVERY prospect (no automation)
   - Not selling—starting conversations
   - The honest message: "I'm building a product to solve [problem]. Would you be open to a brief conversation about how you currently handle it?"
   - This is research that leads to sales, not sales disguised as research

4. **Week 5-8: Discovery and Iteration** (10 min)
   - Every conversation teaches you something
   - Adjust ICP based on what you learn
   - When someone says "I wish this existed"—that's the signal
   - The beta offer: Significant discount in exchange for feedback
   - Your first customers are people willing to take a risk

5. **The Zero-to-Ten Academy Path** (8 min)
   - **Absolute Minimum (Do First):**
     - Course 1: ICP Builder Workshop
     - Course 14: Discovery Framework (simplified BANT)
   - **As You Progress:**
     - Course 8: Cold Email Mastery (if outbound is working)
     - Course 7: LinkedIn Growth Engine (if LinkedIn is working)
   - **NOT YET:**
     - Don't build complex systems until you've validated
     - Don't create content engines until you know what resonates
     - Don't automate until you've done it manually

6. **Transition: When You've Completed Zero-to-Ten** (4 min)
   - Signs you're ready: 10 paying customers, repeat patterns in who buys
   - Choosing your "real" context: B2B SaaS, Creator/Coach, or Service
   - Carrying forward learnings into scaled approach
   - The ICP you thought you had vs. the ICP you actually have

**Assessment:**
Create your Zero-to-Ten Sprint Plan with specific week-by-week targets: Prospect list size, outreach volume, conversation goals, and beta customer targets.

CRITICAL INSIGHT:
