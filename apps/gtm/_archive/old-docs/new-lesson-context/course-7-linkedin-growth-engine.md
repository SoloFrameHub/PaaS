## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description

LinkedIn is the only platform where your "Creator" identity (content) and "Sales" identity (prospecting) live in the same ecosystem. For solo founders, this is a leverage multiplier. You don't need to choose between "building an audience" and "doing sales"—you do both simultaneously to create a "Trust Pincer" maneuver: outbound (Sales Navigator) targets them directly while inbound (content) shows them your expertise in their feed.

Research from late 2024/2025 indicates a major algorithm shift: "Connections First" is back. LinkedIn now prioritizes showing your content to your actual connections rather than viral strangers, making it the most effective channel for "Account-Based Marketing for One" (ABM-1).

This course teaches Justin Welsh's PAIPS content formula, the 4-hour weekly system, and 2025 engagement tactics. You'll build a personal brand that generates inbound leads without daily time commitment.

### Why This Course Matters

**Founder Pain Points Addressed:**
- "I post on LinkedIn but get no engagement"
- "My profile is just a resume—it doesn't generate leads"
- "I don't have time to post every day"
- "I don't know how to turn connections into customers"
- "Sales Navigator seems expensive—is it worth it?"
- "Building in public feels self-promotional"

**The Core Insight:** LinkedIn isn't two separate activities (content vs. sales). It's one integrated system. Your content qualifies prospects (inbound), and your prospecting feeds your audience (outbound). The best performers use Sales Navigator Core to find the right people and "Zero-Click" content to earn their trust before ever asking for the sale.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Position LinkedIn as your primary acquisition channel** understanding why personal brands outperform company pages
2. **Optimize your profile for lead generation** with headline, banner, about, and featured sections that convert visitors
3. **Create content using the PAIPS formula** (Pain→Agitate→Insight→Proof→Solution)
4. **Implement a 4-hour weekly system** for sustainable LinkedIn presence without burnout
5. **Use Sales Navigator Core effectively** for Boolean search, saved searches, and prospect warmup
6. **Execute the "warmup workflow"** engaging before connecting, connecting before pitching
7. **Convert connections to conversations** using permission-based DM strategies
8. **Track and optimize** the metrics that matter for LinkedIn-driven pipeline

## SOURCE FRAMEWORKS AND RESEARCH

### From LinkedIn Research Document

**The "Trust Pincer" Strategy:**
> "LinkedIn is the only platform where your 'Creator' identity (content) and 'Sales' identity (prospecting) live in the same ecosystem. You do both simultaneously."
- Outbound (Sales Navigator): Target them directly
- Inbound (Content): They see your expertise in their feed

**2025 Algorithm Reality:**
- "Connections First" is back—content shows to your network, not viral strangers
- AI-generated content penalized with **30% less reach**
- "Dwell Time" matters—algorithm tracks how long users stop on your post
- "Bro-etry" (one-line paragraphs) is dying; dense carousels and videos winning

**Sales Navigator Pricing (2025):**
- **Core:** $99.99/mo (~$79.99 annual) — The only tier solo founders need
- **Advanced:** $149.99/mo — Overkill for team of one
- **Advanced Plus:** $1,600+/yr — Enterprise, irrelevant for bootstrappers

**Boolean Search (The "Secret Weapon"):**
- **OR** to broaden: `SaaS OR Software OR Tech`
- **AND** to narrow: `Founder AND "New York"`
- **NOT** to clean: `Founder NOT "Co-Founder"`
- **Parentheses** for complex: `(Coach OR Consultant) AND (Fitness OR Wellness) AND "Owner"`

**Spotlight Filters (Low-Hanging Fruit):**
- "Changed Jobs in Past 90 Days": New execs 3x more likely to buy
- "Posted on LinkedIn in Past 30 Days": Filters out zombie accounts

**InMail Reality:**
- Response rates: 18-25% globally, **<10% for generic pitches**
- Fix: Don't pitch—provoke curiosity
- Bad: "I have a lead gen agency..."
- Good: "I saw your post about X. Built a free resource for that. Mind if I share?"

**Content Formats That Convert:**
1. **Carousels (PDF):** 1.9x better than text, demonstrates deep expertise
2. **Short Video (Vertical):** 45% YoY growth, builds "parasocial" trust
3. **"Anti-Hero" Text:** Share mistakes/failures for relatability

**The "Warmup Workflow":**
1. **Identify:** Find prospect in Sales Nav
2. **Engage:** Comment on their post. Wait 24 hours
3. **Connect:** Reference their post: "Saw your post on [Topic]. Connecting to follow."
4. **Wait:** Don't pitch. Let content work for 1-2 weeks
5. **Hybrid:** If they view your profile, then DM: "Saw you stopped by. Hope content was helpful!"

**B2B vs. Creator Tactics:**

| Element | B2B SaaS | Creator/Coach |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Headline** | "Helping [Role] achieve [Result] with [Mechanism]" | "Teaching [Avatar] how to [Result] without [Pain]" |
| **Featured** | Case Study PDF, Calculator, Book Demo | Free Lead Magnet, Testimonial Video |
| **Connection** | "Building network of [Industry] leaders" (peer) | "Loved your journey as [Role]" (fan/peer) |
| **DM Strategy** | Permission-based: "Worth a chat?" | Invitation-based: "Inviting you to [Event]" |

**Zero-Click Content Strategy:**
- Give value IN the feed—don't force external clicks
- LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links
- Post full case study as carousel, full lesson as video
- CTA: "DM me 'Asset' for templates" (boosts engagement + starts conversation)

### From Book Chapter: Reaching Your Customers

**The Math:**
> "100 generic emails with 1% response = 1 conversation. 20 personalized messages with 20% response = 4 conversations."

**Mike's LinkedIn Rhythm:**
- Identify 20-30 prospects Monday
- Engage with content Tuesday-Thursday
- Send connection requests Friday
- Result: Acceptance rate jumped from 20% to 50%+

**Profile Setup for Outreach:**
- Headline: Value proposition, not job title
- Featured: Case study, testimonial, best content

**Connection Request Principles:**
- ~100-150 requests/week before triggering restrictions

**Duration:** 50 minutes

- Decide when (if ever) to invest in company page
- Position yourself as the face of your business

   - Algorithm favors person-to-person content

2. **Why Company Pages Fail** (8 min)
   - Followers don't see posts (algorithm suppression)
   - No relationship-building mechanism
   - "Corporate voice" triggers skepticism
   - Solo founders: Building company page = wasting time

3. **When Company Page Makes Sense** (8 min)
   - After 10+ employees (separate from founder brand)

     - B2B: Founder as product expert

   - What do you want to be known for?

   - Expertise + Personality = Differentiation
   - Authenticity requirement: Be you, not generic "entrepreneur"

6. **Your Brand Statement** (4 min)
   - One sentence: Who you help, what result, how
   - Test: Would your ICP recognize themselves?

### Lesson 4: The PAIPS Content Formula

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Master the PAIPS formula for LinkedIn content
- Create content that demonstrates expertise AND generates engagement
- Build a repeatable system for consistent content creation

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Frameworks Matter** (8 min)
   - Random posting = inconsistent results
   - Frameworks remove decision fatigue
   - PAIPS: Proven formula from Justin Welsh
   - Adaptable for B2B and Creator contexts

2. **P - Pain** (10 min)
   - Open with the problem your ICP faces
   - Be specific, not generic
   - Use their language, not yours
   - Hook in first line—it shows in feed preview
   - Examples from B2B and Creator

3. **A - Agitate** (8 min)
   - Make the pain feel urgent
   - Show consequences of inaction
   - "If you don't solve this..."
   - Emotional connection, not just logic
   - Don't overdo—authentic agitation

4. **I - Insight** (10 min)
   - Your unique perspective or framework
   - What most people get wrong
   - The "aha moment" that positions you as expert
   - This is where you differentiate

5. **P - Proof** (8 min)
   - Evidence that your insight works
   - Options: Data, case study, personal experience
   - "I" statements: "I did X and Y happened"
   - Screenshots, metrics, testimonials

6. **S - Solution (Soft CTA)** (8 min)
   - Don't hard sell
   - Options: "DM me [keyword]", "Follow for more", "Comment if..."
   - Give before you ask
   - Lead magnet offers work well here

7. **Putting It Together** (3 min)
   - Full PAIPS example walkthrough
   - Time to write: 20-30 minutes per post
   - Batch creation for efficiency

**Assessment:**
Write one complete PAIPS post for your LinkedIn. Include: Hook (Pain), Agitate, Insight, Proof, and Soft CTA. Target 150-300 words.

### Lesson 5: Content Formats That Work

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Choose the right format for different content goals
- Create carousels, video, and text posts that get engagement
- Understand 2025 algorithm preferences

**Content Outline:**

1. **2025 Algorithm Reality** (8 min)
   - AI-generated content penalized 30%
   - "Dwell time" matters—how long they stop on your post
   - Connections First: Shown to network, not viral strangers
   - Authenticity > polish

2. **Carousels (PDF)** (12 min)
   - 1.9x better performance than text
   - 5-7 slides breaking down a framework
   - First slide = hook (visible in feed)
   - Last slide = CTA
   - Design: Clean, readable, on-brand
   - Tools: Canva, PowerPoint → PDF

3. **Short-Form Video** (10 min)
   - 45% YoY growth in uploads
   - 60-second "talking head" answering ONE question
   - Builds parasocial trust—they feel they know you
   - Vertical format preferred
   - Caption requirement (most watch muted)

4. **Text Posts** (10 min)
   - Still effective when done well
   - Avoid "bro-etry" (one-line paragraphs)
   - Dense value > empty white space
   - Hook in first line critical
   - 1,300 characters sweet spot

5. **The "Anti-Hero" Format** (8 min)
   - Share failures and mistakes
   - "I lost a $5k deal yesterday. Here's where I messed up..."
   - Authenticity wins—vulnerability builds trust
   - Balance: Failure + Lesson = Value

6. **Format Selection by Goal** (7 min)
   - Teaching a framework → Carousel
   - Building trust/connection → Video
   - Quick insights/opinions → Text
   - Announcements → Text + Image
   - Mix formats for variety

**Assessment:**
Plan your next week's content: 3 posts using at least 2 different formats. Outline each with format choice, topic, and PAIPS structure.

### Lesson 6: The 4-Hour Weekly System

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Build a sustainable LinkedIn rhythm without daily time commitment
- Batch content creation for efficiency
- Schedule engagement strategically

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why 4 Hours Works** (8 min)
   - Daily posting = unsustainable for solo founders
   - Batch creation > reactive posting
   - Consistency > frequency
   - 3 posts/week outperforms inconsistent daily

2. **The Weekly Breakdown** (12 min)
   - **Monday (1.5 hrs):** Content creation—batch 3 posts
   - **Tuesday-Thursday (30 min each):** Engagement—comments on target posts
   - **Friday (30 min):** Performance review + connection requests
   - Flexibility: Adjust to your energy patterns

3. **Content Batching** (10 min)
   - Set aside focused time (no interruptions)
   - Write 3 posts in one session
   - Use PAIPS template to streamline
   - Create visuals at same time
   - Schedule using LinkedIn native or Buffer

4. **Engagement Strategy** (10 min)
   - Comment on prospects' posts (warmup workflow)
   - Comment on peers' posts (visibility)
   - Respond to all comments on your content
   - 15-20 quality comments > 100 generic reactions
   - "Value-add" comments: Share insight, not just praise

5. **The "Ring the Bell" Tactic** (6 min)
   - Follow prospects (they get notified)
   - Engage 2-3 times before connecting
   - Creates familiarity before request
   - Part of prospecting routine

6. **Protecting the Time** (4 min)
   - Block calendar—treat as client meeting
   - No LinkedIn outside scheduled time (avoid scroll trap)
   - Measure: Time invested vs. results generated
   - Adjust as you learn what works

**Assessment:**
Create your 4-hour weekly LinkedIn schedule. Block specific times on your calendar. Include: Content creation slot, daily engagement slots, weekly review slot.

### Lesson 7: Engagement Strategy

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Execute engagement that builds relationships and visibility
- Master the art of valuable commenting
- Build engagement into prospecting workflow

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Engagement Matters** (8 min)
   - Comments on others' posts = visibility to their audience
   - Warmup before connection requests
   - Algorithm rewards active engagement
   - Reciprocity: Engage with them, they engage with you

2. **The "Value-Add" Comment** (12 min)
   - Bad: "Great post!" / "Love this!" / "🔥"
   - Good: Add a perspective, share experience, ask thoughtful question
   - Formula: Agreement + Insight OR Question + Context
   - Length: 2-4 sentences—enough to be noticed

3. **Who to Engage With** (10 min)
   - Tier 1: Prospects you want to connect with
   - Tier 2: Peers in your space (mutual visibility)
   - Tier 3: Industry leaders (visibility to their audience)
   - Balance: 50% Tier 1, 30% Tier 2, 20% Tier 3

4. **DM Strategy: Warm and Cold** (10 min)
   - **Warm DM (after engagement):** Reference your comment/interaction
   - **Cold DM (with caution):** Only if highly targeted
   - Permission-based: "Worth a chat?" vs. pitch
   - Invitation-based: "Inviting you to [event/resource]"
   - Never pitch in first DM

5. **Responding to Engagement on Your Content** (6 min)
   - Reply to every comment (algorithm + relationship)
   - Expand on their point
   - Ask follow-up questions
   - Thank + add value > just thank

6. **Engagement Tracking** (4 min)
   - Note who engages with you consistently
   - Build relationship over time
   - Convert consistent engagers to connections
   - Track prospect engagement as pipeline signal

**Assessment:**
Identify 10 target accounts for engagement this week. For each, note their last post topic and draft a "value-add" comment you'll leave.

### Lesson 10: From Connection to Conversation

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Convert LinkedIn connections into sales conversations
- Execute the warmup-to-pitch progression
- Handle different response types appropriately

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Conversion Pathway** (8 min)
   - Connection ≠ Conversation
   - Most founders: Connect → Pitch → Ignored
   - Better: Connect → Warm → Permission → Conversation
   - Patience required: Days to weeks

2. **The Post-Connection Wait** (10 min)
   - Don't message immediately after connecting
   - Let your content appear in their feed
   - Watch for: Profile views, content engagement
   - Minimum: 3-5 days before DM (unless signal)

3. **The Opening DM** (12 min)
   - NOT a pitch—a conversation starter
   - Options:
     - "Saw you stopped by my profile—hope the content was useful!"
     - "Curious: You've been at [company] X years—what's changed most about [function]?"
     - "Put together a breakdown on [topic]. Happy to share if relevant."
   - Ask for low commitment, not 30-minute call

4. **Permission-Based Selling** (10 min)
   - "Worth a quick chat?" not "Let me show you our demo"
   - Give before asking: Value first, always
   - If interest: "What's the best way to continue—quick call or email?"
   - Respect: No = no (for now)

5. **Handling Responses** (10 min)
   - **Positive:** Book the call, confirm value proposition
   - **Neutral:** Provide value, stay in touch
   - **Negative:** Thank them, move on, don't argue
   - **Silence:** One follow-up okay, then let content do work

6. **The Handoff to Sales Process** (5 min)
   - LinkedIn conversation → Discovery call
   - Transition: "Sounds like it's worth a deeper conversation. Here's my calendar link."
   - Connection to Course 14: Discovery Framework
   - Track: LinkedIn-sourced pipeline

**Assessment:**
Write 3 opening DM templates: One for prospects who engaged with your content, one for new connections in your ICP, one for prospects who viewed your profile.
