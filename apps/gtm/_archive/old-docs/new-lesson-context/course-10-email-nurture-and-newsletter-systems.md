## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description

Your email list is your owned audience—immune to algorithm changes, platform shutdowns, and rising ad costs. While social media followers can disappear overnight (algorithm change, account suspension, platform decline), email subscribers are yours. This is the only marketing asset you truly control.

This course teaches email capture, nurture sequence design, newsletter strategy, and automation that converts subscribers to customers over time. You'll learn to build the bridge between "interested stranger" and "paying customer" through systematic email communication.

Unlike cold email (Course 8) which targets strangers, this course focuses on warming leads who've opted in—people who raised their hand and said "I want to hear from you." The goal is building trust over time until they're ready to buy.

### Why This Course Matters

**Founder Pain Points Addressed:**
- "I have traffic but no way to capture and follow up with visitors"
- "People sign up for my newsletter but never buy"
- "I don't know what to send or how often"
- "My emails feel like I'm just 'staying in touch' without purpose"
- "Which email tool should I use without spending $200/month?"
- "How do I turn subscribers into customers without being pushy?"

**The Core Insight:** Email is the highest-ROI marketing channel (averaging $36-42 return per $1 spent), but only when you understand the journey: Capture → Welcome → Nurture → Convert. Most founders skip the middle steps, going straight from "Thanks for subscribing" to "Buy my thing." The nurture sequence is where trust is built and buying resistance dissolves.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Understand email as owned audience** and why it outperforms social following
2. **Design email capture strategy** with lead magnets, popups, and landing pages
3. **Build welcome sequences** that indoctrinate new subscribers in your first 5 emails
4. **Architect nurture sequences** that progress from education → trust → offer
5. **Develop newsletter strategy** with consistent cadence, format, and content mix
6. **Implement segmentation** based on behavior, interests, and engagement
7. **Create launch/promotion sequences** that generate urgency and action
8. **Select email automation tools** appropriate for bootstrap budgets
9. **Track email analytics** that matter (beyond open rates)
10. **Build your complete email playbook** with templates and maintenance rhythm

- Content drives traffic → 30-day free trial (no credit card) → 33.5% trial-to-paid conversion
- Email captures those who aren't ready to try yet

**The Funnel Problem:**
> "Founders commonly build virtually no middle-of-funnel content—this is the most neglected area."

**TOFU/MOFU/BOFU:**
- **TOFU (Top):** Awareness stage—pain points, impressions, traffic
- **MOFU (Middle):** Consideration—case studies, nurture sequences, cost-per-lead
- **BOFU (Bottom):** Decision—trials, demos, conversion rates

**Lead Magnet Strategy:**
- TOFU: Ebooks and guides
- MOFU: Calculators and assessments
- BOFU: Case studies and free trials
- Key principle: Solve ONE specific, urgent problem related to the larger solution you sell

## COURSE STRUCTURE (10 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: Email as Owned Audience

**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand why email list is your most valuable marketing asset
- Recognize the difference between rented and owned audience
- Commit to email as primary capture mechanism

**Content Outline:**

1. **Rented vs. Owned Audience** (10 min)
   - Social followers: Platform controls access
   - Algorithm changes can destroy reach overnight
   - Platform risk: Accounts suspended, platforms decline
   - Email: Direct line to your audience you control
   - "1,000 email subscribers > 10,000 social followers"

2. **Email Economics** (10 min)
   - ROI: $36-42 per $1 spent (highest of any channel)
   - Conversion rates: Email converts 3x better than social
   - Longevity: Email list compounds over years
   - Cost: Pennies per subscriber per month

3. **The Strategic Role of Email** (8 min)
   - Bridge between content and sales
   - Captures "not ready yet" visitors
   - Nurtures cold leads to warm
   - Enables systematic follow-up at scale
   - The only channel you own

4. **How Email Fits Your Funnel** (8 min)
   - Content/SEO/Community → Traffic
   - Email capture → List building
   - Welcome sequence → Indoctrination
   - Nurture sequence → Trust building
   - Sales sequence → Conversion

5. **Email Mindset** (5 min)
   - Permission-based: They asked to hear from you
   - Value-first: Earn every open
   - Long-game: Relationships over transactions
   - Your newsletter = Your platform

6. **Course Roadmap** (4 min)
   - What we'll build: Complete email system
   - Capture → Welcome → Nurture → Convert
   - Time investment expectations

**Assessment:**
Calculate your current "owned audience" vs. "rented audience" ratio. List all platforms where you have followers/subscribers and categorize each as owned or rented.

### Lesson 2: Email Capture Strategy

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Design effective lead magnets for your ICP
- Implement capture mechanisms (popups, landing pages, content upgrades)
- Balance capture aggressiveness with user experience

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Lead Magnet Framework** (12 min)
   - Purpose: Exchange value for email address
   - Must solve ONE specific, urgent problem
   - Related to your paid offering
   - Format options: PDF guide, template, checklist, mini-course, tool
   - TOFU lead magnets vs. MOFU lead magnets
   - Examples: B2B (ROI calculator) vs. Creator (5-day email course)

2. **Capture Mechanisms** (10 min)
   - **Popups:** Exit intent, timed, scroll-triggered
   - **Embedded forms:** In content, sidebar, footer
   - **Landing pages:** Dedicated pages for lead magnets
   - **Content upgrades:** Specific to each blog post
   - **Homepage capture:** Above fold, clear value prop

3. **Popup Strategy (Without Annoying)** (8 min)
   - Exit intent: Catch them as they leave
   - Scroll depth: Show after 50% engagement
   - Time delay: 30-60 seconds minimum
   - Frequency capping: Once per session/week
   - Mobile considerations: Less intrusive

4. **Landing Page Essentials** (10 min)
   - Single focus: One offer, one action
   - Clear headline: What they get
   - Benefits bullets: Why they want it
   - Social proof: Who else got it
   - Minimal form: Email only (or + first name)
   - Mobile-first design

5. **Content Upgrades** (6 min)
   - Blog post → Specific related download
   - Higher conversion than generic lead magnet
   - Example: "Cold Email Post" → "5 Cold Email Templates"
   - Creates segmentation automatically

6. **Tools for Capture** (4 min)
   - Built-in: ConvertKit, MailerLite, Beehiiv forms
   - Popups: OptinMonster, Sumo (free tier)
   - Landing pages: Carrd ($19/year), email tool built-in

**Assessment:**
Design your primary lead magnet: What is it, what problem does it solve, why would your ICP trade their email for it? Then sketch a landing page structure (headline, benefits, form).

### Lesson 3: Welcome Sequence Design

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Build a 5-email welcome sequence that indoctrinates new subscribers
- Set expectations and build immediate trust
- Segment subscribers based on welcome sequence engagement

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Welcome Sequences Matter** (8 min)
   - Welcome emails: 50-60% open rate (highest you'll ever get)
   - First impression sets engagement pattern
   - Indoctrination: Teaching them who you are, what you believe
   - Missed opportunity: Most founders send generic "thanks for subscribing"

2. **The 5-Email Welcome Framework** (15 min)
   - **Email 1 (Immediately):** Deliver lead magnet + set expectations
   - **Email 2 (Day 2):** Your story—why you do this, credibility
   - **Email 3 (Day 4):** Best content—your top performing piece
   - **Email 4 (Day 6):** Social proof—case study or testimonial
   - **Email 5 (Day 8):** Soft CTA—what's next (free trial, community, etc.)

3. **Email 1: The Delivery Email** (8 min)
   - Subject: Deliver what you promised
   - Content: Here's your [lead magnet], brief intro
   - Set expectations: "Over the next week, I'll share..."
   - Ask: "What's your biggest challenge with X?" (segmentation)

4. **Email 2-4: Building the Relationship** (10 min)
   - Your story (vulnerability builds trust)
   - Best content (prove your value)
   - Social proof (others trust you too)
   - Each email: One clear idea, one CTA

5. **Email 5: The Bridge to Action** (8 min)
   - Recap value provided
   - Introduce next step (not hard sell)
   - Options: Free trial, community, consultation, product
   - "If you're ready" language (permission-based)

6. **Welcome Sequence Metrics** (6 min)
   - Track: Open rate, click rate, reply rate
   - Benchmark against industry averages
   - Segment: Who engaged vs. didn't
   - Optimize: A/B test subject lines, content

**Assessment:**
Write the subject line and first paragraph for all 5 welcome sequence emails. Focus on the progression: Deliver → Story → Value → Proof → Next Step.

### Lesson 4: Nurture Sequence Architecture

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Design nurture sequences that build trust over 4-8 weeks
- Progress subscribers from education to readiness to buy
- Avoid the "newsletter-only" trap

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Nurture Sequence Purpose** (10 min)
   - Bridge between "interested" and "ready to buy"
   - Most founders skip this entirely
   - Newsletter alone isn't enough
   - Systematic progression: Education → Trust → Desire → Action
   - Timeline: 4-8 weeks, 8-12 emails

2. **Education Phase (Emails 1-4)** (10 min)
   - Teach your framework/methodology
   - Demonstrate expertise without selling
   - Address common misconceptions
   - Build vocabulary (teach them your terms)
   - Each email: One concept, applied to their situation

3. **Trust Phase (Emails 5-8)** (10 min)
   - Case studies and results
   - Behind-the-scenes (how you work)
   - Address objections indirectly
   - Social proof and testimonials
   - "What if" scenarios

4. **Desire Phase (Emails 9-12)** (10 min)
   - Paint the transformation picture
   - Cost of inaction
   - Compare options (including doing nothing)
   - FAQ-style objection handling
   - Soft invitations to next step

5. **Nurture Sequence Triggers** (8 min)
   - After welcome sequence completes
   - After specific actions (downloaded X, visited pricing)
   - Re-engagement for inactive subscribers
   - Segment-specific nurtures

6. **Content Sources for Nurture** (7 min)
   - Repurpose blog posts
   - Answer FAQ in depth
   - Share customer conversations (anonymized)
   - Behind-the-scenes of your process
   - Teaching what you learned building your product

**Assessment:**
Outline your nurture sequence: List 12 email topics progressing from Education (4) to Trust (4) to Desire (4). Include the core message and CTA for each.

### Lesson 5: Newsletter Strategy

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Develop newsletter cadence, format, and content mix
- Distinguish newsletter from sequences (ongoing vs. finite)
- Build a sustainable newsletter rhythm

**Content Outline:**

1. **Newsletter vs. Sequences** (8 min)
   - Sequences: Finite, triggered, everyone gets same
   - Newsletter: Ongoing, broadcast, everyone at same time
   - Sequences build the relationship
   - Newsletter maintains the relationship
   - Both are necessary

2. **Cadence Selection** (10 min)
   - Weekly: Best for engagement, most demanding
   - Bi-weekly: Good balance for solo founders
   - Monthly: Minimum—less loses mindshare
   - Consistency > frequency
   - Pick what you can sustain for 52 weeks

3. **Newsletter Format Options** (10 min)
   - **Long-form essay:** One deep topic (example: Justin Welsh)
   - **Curated links:** 3-5 items with commentary
   - **Mixed format:** Insight + links + personal note
   - **Personal journal:** BIP style, what you learned
   - Choose format that matches your strength

4. **Content Mix** (10 min)
   - 60-70% education/value
   - 20-30% personal/connection
   - 10-20% promotion/CTA
   - Never 100% promotional
   - Vary mix by season (launches vs. maintenance)

5. **Newsletter Production System** (8 min)
   - Batching vs. weekly writing
   - Content calendar: Topics planned ahead
   - Swipe file: Ideas collected ongoing
   - Templates: Consistent structure speeds creation
   - Time investment: 2-4 hours/week

6. **Growing Newsletter** (4 min)
   - Quality → sharing
   - Referral programs (Beehiiv, SparkLoop)
   - Cross-promotion with similar newsletters
   - Newsletter → social → newsletter (flywheel)

**Assessment:**
Design your newsletter format: Cadence, format structure, content mix ratio, and a 4-week content calendar with specific topics.

### Lesson 6: Email Segmentation

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Implement behavior-based and interest-based segmentation
- Create targeted sequences for different subscriber types
- Avoid over-segmentation complexity

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Segmentation Matters** (8 min)
   - Same message to everyone = mediocre results for all
   - Right message to right person = higher engagement
   - Segmentation improves: Open rates, click rates, conversions
   - The balance: Benefit vs. complexity

2. **Segmentation Types** (12 min)
   - **Source-based:** How they found you (which lead magnet)
   - **Interest-based:** What they clicked, downloaded, viewed
   - **Behavior-based:** Engagement level, purchase history
   - **Demographic:** Role, company size, industry (if collected)
   - **Stage-based:** Where they are in buyer journey

3. **Simple Segmentation Strategy** (10 min)
   - Start with 2-3 segments max
   - Example: By lead magnet source
   - Example: Engaged vs. unengaged (opened in last 30 days)
   - Add segments only when justified by content/offer differences
   - Avoid: 15 segments you can't serve

4. **Segment-Specific Sequences** (10 min)
   - Different welcome sequences by source
   - Different nurture paths by interest
   - Re-engagement for cold subscribers
   - VIP segment for highly engaged

5. **Implementation Mechanics** (6 min)
   - Tags vs. lists (most tools use tags)
   - Trigger-based tagging (clicked link = add tag)
   - Conditional content (same email, different sections)
   - Testing segmentation impact

6. **Cleaning Your List** (4 min)
   - Why unengaged subscribers hurt you
   - Re-engagement campaign before removal
   - Regular hygiene: Remove bounces, unsubscribes
   - Quality > quantity

**Assessment:**
Design your initial segmentation strategy: What 2-3 segments will you create, what triggers them, and how will content differ for each?

### Lesson 7: Launch and Promotion Sequences

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Create product launch email sequences that generate urgency
- Execute time-limited promotions effectively
- Balance promotion with relationship preservation

**Content Outline:**

1. **Launch Sequence Purpose** (8 min)
   - Concentrated push for action
   - Time-limited by nature
   - Higher email frequency acceptable
   - Generates urgency and decision
   - Not spam if value precedes it

2. **Launch Sequence Structure (7-Day)** (15 min)
   - **Day 1:** Announcement—what's launching, why it matters
   - **Day 2:** The problem—agitate the pain point
   - **Day 3:** The solution—introduce your offer
   - **Day 4:** Social proof—who's already in, results
   - **Day 5:** FAQ/Objections—address hesitations
   - **Day 6:** Deadline reminder—urgency intensifies
   - **Day 7:** Final call—last chance

3. **Creating Urgency (Ethically)** (10 min)
   - Real deadlines (cart closes, price increases)
   - Limited bonuses (first X buyers get Y)
   - Cohort-based (starting date real)
   - Avoid: Fake scarcity, evergreen "closing" funnels
   - Urgency without integrity = short-term wins, long-term loss

4. **Email Frequency During Launch** (8 min)
   - Higher frequency is acceptable
   - 1-2 emails/day in final 48 hours: OK
   - Segment: Opened vs. didn't (more to engaged)
   - Unsubscribes during launch: Expected and OK
   - Recovery: Post-launch value emails

5. **Post-Launch Sequence** (8 min)
   - Thank buyers with clear next steps
   - Non-buyers: No hard feelings, value continues
   - Survey: Why didn't you buy? (feedback)
   - Bridge to next opportunity (waitlist, next launch)

6. **Promotion Without Launches** (6 min)
   - Evergreen sequences for ongoing offers
   - Triggered by behavior (visited pricing page)
   - Less urgent, but consistent
   - Soft CTAs in regular newsletter

**Assessment:**
Outline a 7-day launch sequence: Subject lines and core message for each email. Include what type of urgency you'll use and why it's authentic.

### Lesson 8: Email Automation Tools

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Select the right email tool for your stage and budget
- Set up essential automations
- Avoid over-engineering your email system

**Content Outline:**

1. **Tool Selection Framework** (10 min)
   - Stage 1 (0-1K subscribers): Free tier priority
   - Stage 2 (1-10K): Features matter, price reasonable
   - Stage 3 (10K+): Deliverability and advanced features
   - Switching costs: Start with tool you can grow into

2. **Bootstrap-Friendly Email Tools** (15 min)
   - **ConvertKit:** Free to 10K, tag-based, creator-focused
   - **MailerLite:** Free to 1K, full automation features
   - **Beehiiv:** Free to 2.5K, AI tools, built-in referrals
   - **Buttondown:** Free tier, developer-friendly, markdown
   - **Listmonk:** Self-hosted, free forever, technical setup
   - Comparison matrix for decision

3. **Essential Automations** (10 min)
   - Welcome sequence (triggered by signup)
   - Nurture sequence (triggered by welcome completion)
   - Re-engagement (triggered by inactivity)
   - Purchase follow-up (triggered by conversion)
   - These four handle 80% of needs

4. **Setting Up Automations** (8 min)
   - Trigger → Condition → Action
   - Visual automation builders (most tools)
   - Testing before going live
   - Monitoring for errors

5. **Advanced Features (When Ready)** (4 min)
   - A/B testing
   - Lead scoring
   - Behavioral triggers
   - Integrations with CRM
   - Don't over-engineer early

6. **Deliverability Basics** (3 min)
   - Authentication (handled by tool usually)
   - Clean list hygiene
   - Avoid spam triggers in content
   - Monitor sender reputation

**Assessment:**
Select your email tool based on the comparison framework. Set up or plan your first automation: Welcome sequence with trigger, timing, and email content outline.

### Lesson 9: Email Analytics That Matter

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Track metrics that indicate business impact
- Avoid vanity metrics traps
- Optimize based on data, not assumptions

**Content Outline:**

1. **Beyond Open Rates** (10 min)
   - Open rates: Increasingly unreliable (Apple Mail Privacy)
   - Click rates: Better indicator of engagement
   - Reply rates: Strongest engagement signal
   - Conversion rates: Ultimate metric
   - List growth rate: Net new subscribers

2. **Metrics by Email Type** (10 min)
   - **Welcome sequence:** Open rate (still useful), click rate, reply rate
   - **Nurture sequence:** Click rate progression, unsubscribe rate
   - **Newsletter:** Click rate, reply rate, sharing
   - **Launch sequence:** Click rate, conversion rate, revenue

3. **The Metrics Dashboard** (10 min)
   - Weekly tracking: Opens, clicks, unsubscribes
   - Monthly tracking: List growth, engagement trends
   - Per-sequence tracking: Performance by automation
   - Revenue attribution: Which emails drive purchases

4. **Analyzing Sequence Performance** (8 min)
   - Drop-off points: Where do people stop opening?
   - Click patterns: What topics get action?
   - Unsubscribe triggers: Which emails cause exits?
   - Time analysis: Best day/time for your audience

5. **A/B Testing Strategy** (8 min)
   - Test one variable at a time
   - Subject lines: Easiest, most impact
   - Send time: Morning vs. afternoon
   - Content format: Long vs. short
   - Statistical significance: Minimum 100 per variant

6. **Action on Data** (4 min)
   - Identify underperforming emails
   - Double down on what works
   - Regular review rhythm
   - Iteration > perfection

**Assessment:**
Create your email metrics dashboard: What 5-7 metrics will you track weekly/monthly? Set up tracking in your email tool or spreadsheet.
