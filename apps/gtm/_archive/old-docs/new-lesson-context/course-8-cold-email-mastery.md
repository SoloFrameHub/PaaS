## COURSE OVERVIEW

### Course Description

Cold email is the most cost-effective way for a bootstrapped founder to generate conversations with potential customers. But 99% of cold emails get ignored—not because cold email doesn't work, but because most cold emails are terrible. They're about you, not the prospect. They're generic. They ask for too much too soon.

This course teaches you to write emails that get replies by focusing on the prospect's problems, not your product's features. You'll learn frameworks (PAS, AIDA), sequences that achieve 5-10%+ reply rates, and the technical foundation required in 2024/2025 with Gmail and Yahoo's strict sender requirements.

Cold email complements your content (Courses 5-6) and LinkedIn (Course 7) efforts. While those channels build inbound, cold email lets you proactively reach prospects who match your ICP but haven't discovered you yet. When cold emails generate replies, you'll use DISC (Course 13) and Discovery (Course 14) to convert conversations to opportunities.

### Why This Course Matters

**Founder Pain Points Addressed:**
- "I sent 500 emails and got zero responses"
- "My emails are landing in spam"
- "I don't know what to write in a cold email"
- "Following up feels like pestering"
- "Cold email seems sleazy—I don't want to be 'that person'"
- "The technical setup is too complicated"

**The Core Insight:** The math is simple: 100 generic emails with 1% response = 1 conversation. 20 personalized emails with 20% response = 4 conversations. Volume isn't the answer—relevance is. And in 2024/2025, deliverability is the prerequisite: without SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, your emails won't even arrive.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Diagnose why cold emails fail** and avoid the fatal flaws that get emails ignored
2. **Write using the PAS framework** (Problem, Agitate, Solution)
3. **Write using the AIDA framework** (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
4. **Craft subject lines** that get opened without triggering spam filters
5. **Write personalized first lines** that prove you did your research
6. **Structure email bodies** that deliver value without pitching
7. **Create effective CTAs** that get low-friction responses
8. **Build multi-touch sequences** with 4-5 emails that maintain interest
9. **Personalize at scale** using AI tools while maintaining authenticity
10. **Manage deliverability** to ensure emails actually arrive
11. **Handle replies** converting interest into conversations
12. **Build your cold email playbook** with templates, sequences, and tracking

**The New Reality (2024/2025):**
> "The days of sending 1,000 generic emails/day are over (and legally dangerous). The new standard is 50–100 highly personalized messages that treat the recipient as a partner, not a prospect."

**Mandatory Technical Requirements:**
1. **SPF (Sender Policy Framework):** "I am allowed to send mail from this IP"
2. **DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail):** "This email was not tampered with"
3. **DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication):** "If authentication fails, reject"

- Strict alignment for bulk senders (>5k/day)

- NEVER send cold outreach from primary domain
- If blacklisted, transactional emails (invoices, passwords) fail too
- Buy 2-3 lookalike domains: `getagency.com`, `tryagency.com`
- Warmup: 3-4 weeks minimum

  - Week 3: 30-50 emails/day

## COURSE STRUCTURE (12 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: Why 99% of Cold Emails Fail

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Diagnose the fatal flaws that kill cold email response rates
- Shift mindset from sender-focused to recipient-focused
- Understand 2024/2025 benchmarks and what "good" looks like

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Cold Email Reality Check** (10 min)
   - Average professional receives 121 emails/day
   - Most cold emails: Ignored, deleted, or spam-filtered
   - It's not that cold email doesn't work—it's that most is terrible
   - The 1% who succeed: What do they do differently?

2. **Fatal Flaw #1: It's About You** (10 min)
   - "I'd love to show you how we can help..."
   - This signals sales pitch immediately
   - Recipient's brain categorizes as noise
   - Fix: Lead with THEIR problem, not your solution

3. **Fatal Flaw #2: It's Generic** (8 min)
   - "Hi [First Name], I noticed you work at [Company]..."
   - Merge tags are visible—could have gone to 10,000 people
   - No signal that you know anything about them
   - Fix: Specific, relevant personalization

4. **Fatal Flaw #3: Asks Too Much** (8 min)
   - "Would you have 30 minutes this week to discuss..."
   - Asking stranger for significant time commitment
   - The bar is too high for cold contact
   - Fix: Low-friction asks that start conversations

5. **Fatal Flaw #4: No Earned Right** (8 min)
   - Before asking, you need to give reason to say yes
   - Most emails: All ask, no give
   - The value-before-ask principle
   - Fix: Lead with insight, resource, or question

6. **The Better Math** (6 min)
   - 100 generic @ 1% = 1 conversation
   - 20 personalized @ 20% = 4 conversations
   - Quality over quantity always wins
   - Course roadmap: What you'll learn

**Assessment:**
Audit 3 cold emails you've sent (or received). Identify which fatal flaws they exhibit. Score each 1-5 on: About them (not you), Specific, Appropriate ask, Earned right.

### Lesson 2: The PAS Framework

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Master the Problem-Agitate-Solution copywriting framework
- Apply PAS to cold email structure
- Write emails that connect with prospect pain

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Frameworks Work** (8 min)
   - Structure removes guesswork
   - Proven patterns from direct response copywriting
   - PAS: One of the most effective for cold outreach
   - When to use PAS vs. AIDA (next lesson)

2. **P - Problem** (12 min)
   - Open with a problem they actually have
   - Be specific—generic problems don't resonate
   - Research required: What keeps them up at night?
   - Example B2B: "Struggling to get responses from outbound?"
   - Example Creator: "Finding it hard to fill your coaching program?"
   - Use their language, not yours

3. **A - Agitate** (10 min)
   - Make the problem feel urgent
   - Show the consequences of inaction
   - Emotional engagement, not just logical
   - "Every week that passes, your competitors are..."
   - Don't overdo—authentic agitation

4. **S - Solution** (10 min)
   - Introduce how you can help (briefly)
   - Don't feature-dump—hint at solution
   - Focus on outcome, not process
   - "I helped [similar company] solve this by..."
   - Soft transition to CTA

5. **PAS Email Template** (10 min)
   - Full example walkthrough
   - Subject line considerations
   - Length: 50-125 words optimal
   - B2B example and Creator example

6. **Practice and Variations** (5 min)
   - PAS variations: Sometimes P-A-S-CTA
   - When PAS works best: Known pain points
   - Exercise setup

**Assessment:**
Write one complete cold email using the PAS framework. Include: Subject line, Problem statement, Agitation, Solution hint, and CTA. Keep under 125 words.

### Lesson 3: The AIDA Framework

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Master Attention-Interest-Desire-Action framework
- Understand when AIDA beats PAS
- Write emails that build through logical progression

**Content Outline:**

1. **AIDA Origins and Application** (8 min)
   - Classic marketing framework (100+ years old)
   - Works for slightly longer emails
   - Better when prospect may not know they have problem
   - Logical progression vs. emotional (PAS)

2. **A - Attention** (10 min)
   - Subject line + first line must hook
   - Pattern interrupt: Something unexpected
   - Personalization as attention device
   - "Saw your post about X—I had a different take"
   - Questions that provoke curiosity

3. **I - Interest** (10 min)
   - Build on the hook with relevant information
   - Connect to their situation
   - Share insight they don't have
   - Keep them reading—don't lose them here
   - Credibility without bragging

4. **D - Desire** (10 min)
   - Make them WANT what you're offering
   - Paint picture of better outcome
   - Social proof: "We helped X achieve Y"
   - Address implicit objections
   - Create FOMO without being sleazy

5. **A - Action** (8 min)
   - Clear, specific CTA
   - Low friction (not "30 minute call")
   - Binary question technique: "Worth a conversation?"
   - One CTA only—don't confuse

6. **AIDA vs. PAS Decision** (9 min)
   - PAS: Known pain, emotional connection
   - AIDA: Educating, logical progression
   - Sometimes hybrid works
   - Full AIDA example walkthrough

**Assessment:**
Write one cold email using the AIDA framework targeting a different segment than your PAS email. Include clear examples of each element.

### Lesson 4: Subject Lines That Get Opened

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Write subject lines that achieve 50%+ open rates
- Avoid spam trigger words and patterns
- Test and iterate on subject line performance

**Content Outline:**

1. **Subject Line Psychology** (10 min)
   - 47% of emails opened based on subject alone
   - You have 3-5 words to earn the open
   - Curiosity vs. clarity tradeoff
   - Mobile preview: First 30 characters matter most

2. **Patterns That Work** (12 min)
   - **Question:** "Quick question about [specific thing]"
   - **Mutual connection:** "[Name] suggested I reach out"
   - **Personalized:** "[Company] + [insight]"
   - **Casual/short:** "hey" / "quick thought"
   - **Specific benefit:** "[Result] for [company type]"
   - B2B and Creator examples for each

3. **Patterns That Fail** (8 min)
   - All caps: "AMAZING OPPORTUNITY"
   - Spam words: Free, guarantee, act now
   - Too salesy: "Boost your revenue 500%"
   - Too long: Truncated on mobile
   - Clickbait that doesn't deliver

4. **Deliverability Considerations** (10 min)
   - Spam filters scan subject lines
   - Avoid: Excessive punctuation, ALL CAPS, $$
   - Personalization helps deliverability
   - Consistency: Subject should match body

5. **Testing Subject Lines** (6 min)
   - A/B test with 50-50 splits
   - Track open rates by subject type
   - Sample size matters: 100+ sends per variant
   - Document winners in swipe file

6. **Your Subject Line Library** (4 min)
   - Build a collection that works for you
   - Categorize by type and purpose
   - Artifact introduction

**Assessment:**
Create a subject line library with 10 subject lines: 2 questions, 2 personalized, 2 casual, 2 benefit-focused, 2 curiosity-driven. All under 50 characters.

### Lesson 5: The First Line Hook

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Write personalized first lines that prove you did research
- Balance personalization with efficiency
- Avoid fake personalization that backfires

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why First Lines Matter** (8 min)
   - First line shows in email preview (after subject)
   - Determines if they read the rest
   - Signals: "This was written for YOU"
   - Bad first lines = instant delete

2. **Research-Based Personalization** (12 min)
   - Reference their recent content (LinkedIn post, podcast)
   - Mention company news (funding, launch, hire)
   - Acknowledge their specific challenge
   - Time investment: 2-5 minutes per prospect
   - Tools: LinkedIn, company blog, press releases

3. **Fake Personalization Traps** (10 min)
   - "I noticed you went to [University]..."—obvious automation
   - "Congrats on your recent [generic thing]"
   - Merge fields without context
   - If it could apply to anyone, it's not personal
   - Prospects KNOW when it's fake

4. **First Line Templates** (10 min)
   - "Saw your post about [specific topic]—agreed with your point on X"
   - "Noticed [Company] just [specific event]—congrats"
   - "Your [piece of content] on [topic] resonated because..."
   - "I've been following [Company]'s approach to [specific thing]"
   - Each must be genuinely relevant

5. **Personalization at Scale** (6 min)
   - Tier prospects: Top 20% get manual personalization
   - Rest: Template + segment-specific customization
   - AI research assistance (Clay, etc.)
   - Human review before sending

6. **Efficiency Tips** (4 min)
   - Batch research before writing
   - Keep notes on each prospect
   - Reuse insights across sequence
   - Don't spend 30 minutes per email

**Assessment:**
Research 5 prospects and write personalized first lines for each. Include: The source of your personalization and why it's relevant to your offer.

### Lesson 6: The Body - Value Without Pitching

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Write email bodies that deliver value
- Position outcomes without feature-dumping
- Build credibility without desperation

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Value-First Principle** (10 min)
   - Give before you ask
   - Body should answer: "What's in it for them?"
   - Not about you—about their outcome
   - Credibility through insight, not claims

2. **Outcome Positioning** (12 min)
   - Features: "Our AI-powered platform..."
   - Benefits: "Get responses from cold email..."
   - Outcomes: "Book 5 calls per week without..."
   - Always translate features → outcomes
   - Specific > vague ("5 calls" vs. "more leads")

3. **Credibility Without Desperation** (10 min)
   - Social proof: "Helped [similar company] achieve [result]"
   - Don't brag—let results speak
   - Avoid: "We're the best," "Industry leader"
   - One proof point is enough
   - Make it relevant to THEIR situation

4. **Body Structure Options** (10 min)
   - Short (3-4 sentences): Problem → Solution hint → CTA
   - Medium (5-7 sentences): Add proof point + outcome
   - Avoid: Walls of text, feature lists
   - White space helps readability
   - Mobile-friendly formatting

5. **What NOT to Include** (8 min)
   - Feature lists
   - Company history
   - Multiple links
   - Attachments (spam trigger)
   - More than one CTA
   - Obvious automation

6. **B2B vs. Creator Body Examples** (5 min)
   - Full example: B2B SaaS selling to VPs
   - Full example: Creator selling coaching
   - Same principles, different language

**Assessment:**
Write 2 email bodies (without subject or first line): One using short format, one using medium format. Both for the same offer but different emphasis.

### Lesson 7: The CTA - Getting Responses

**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Write CTAs that get responses (not just clicks)
- Use low-friction asks that start conversations
- Avoid CTAs that kill response rates

**Content Outline:**

1. **CTA Psychology** (10 min)
   - You're asking a stranger to do something
   - High bar = low response
   - Goal: Start conversation, not close deal
   - Make saying "yes" easy

2. **The Low-Friction Ask** (12 min)
   - Bad: "Would you have 30 minutes this Thursday?"
   - Better: "Worth a quick conversation?"
   - Best: "Does this resonate?"
   - Binary questions: Force yes/no, not scheduling
   - "If this isn't relevant, no worries—just hit delete"

3. **CTA Patterns That Work** (10 min)
   - **Permission-based:** "Mind if I send over a resource?"
   - **Interest check:** "Is this on your radar for Q1?"
   - **Value offer:** "I put together X—want me to share?"
   - **Binary:** "Worth a 10-minute call, or not the right time?"
   - **Soft close:** "If this sounds interesting, I can share how we helped [company]"

4. **CTAs That Kill Response** (8 min)
   - Calendar links in first email
   - "Let's schedule a call"
   - Multiple CTAs (confusing)
   - Vague: "Let me know your thoughts"
   - Pushy: "Can you meet tomorrow?"

5. **Matching CTA to Email Type** (6 min)
   - First email: Lowest friction (interest check)
   - Follow-up 2-3: Slight escalation
   - Final follow-up: Clear close
   - Warm lead: Can ask for more

6. **Testing CTAs** (4 min)
   - Track response rate by CTA type
   - A/B test in sequences
   - What works for your audience?

**Assessment:**
Write 5 different CTAs for the same email: 2 permission-based, 2 binary, 1 value offer. Identify which you'd use for first email vs. follow-up.

### Lesson 8: Multi-Touch Sequences

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Build 4-5 email sequences that maintain interest
- Time follow-ups appropriately
- Add value with each touch (not just repeat)

**Content Outline:**

1. **Why Sequences Beat Single Emails** (10 min)
   - Most responses come after 2nd-4th touch
   - Silence ≠ "no"—usually means "buried"
   - Following up isn't pestering—it's persistence
   - One email is not a campaign

2. **Sequence Structure** (12 min)
   - **Email 1:** Initial outreach (PAS or AIDA)
   - **Email 2:** Different angle (3-4 days later)
   - **Email 3:** Value add—share resource (3-4 days)
   - **Email 4:** Social proof (4-5 days)
   - **Email 5:** Breakup email (5-7 days)
   - Total sequence: 2-3 weeks

3. **Adding Value, Not Repeating** (10 min)
   - Each email must offer something new
   - Different angles: Problem focus, outcome focus, proof
   - Share relevant content, insights, resources
   - Bad: "Just following up on my last email"
   - Good: "Saw this article about [challenge]—thought you'd find it useful"

4. **The Breakup Email** (8 min)
   - Final touch after silence
   - Acknowledge you'll stop reaching out
   - Often gets highest response rate
   - "I'll assume [topic] isn't a priority—feel free to reach out if that changes"
   - No guilt, no pressure

5. **Timing and Spacing** (8 min)
   - First follow-up: 3-4 days
   - Subsequent: 4-7 days
   - Avoid weekends for B2B
   - Best days: Tuesday-Thursday
   - Best times: 8-10am recipient's timezone

6. **Sequence Tools** (7 min)
   - Instantly, Smartlead: Built-in sequences
   - Auto-stop on reply
   - A/B testing within sequences
   - Personalization at scale

**Assessment:**
Build a complete 5-email sequence for one segment. Include: Subject lines, body text for each, timing between emails, and what's different about each touch.

### Lesson 9: Personalization at Scale

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Use AI and templates to personalize efficiently
- Segment prospects for targeted messaging
- Maintain authenticity at volume

**Content Outline:**

1. **The Personalization Spectrum** (10 min)
   - Level 1: Name and company (minimum, not enough)
   - Level 2: Industry/role-specific messaging
   - Level 3: Company-specific insight
   - Level 4: Individual-specific (recent activity)
   - Diminishing returns above Level 3 for most

2. **Segmentation Strategy** (12 min)
   - Group prospects by: Role, industry, company size, challenge
   - Each segment gets tailored template
   - Same framework, different specifics
   - B2B example: "VP Marketing at Series A SaaS"
   - Creator example: "Coaches with 1-5K email list"

3. **AI Tools for Personalization** (10 min)
   - **Clay:** Data enrichment, write personalized openers
   - **Instantly/Smartlead AI:** Built-in icebreakers
   - AI finds data—you verify output
   - Rule: AI research, human check for top 20%

4. **Template + Variable Approach** (10 min)
   - Core template stays consistent
   - Variables: First line, company reference, specific outcome
   - Merge fields done right
   - Preview every email before send (spot obvious errors)

5. **When NOT to Scale** (8 min)
   - Whale accounts: Always manual
   - Referral introductions: Manual
   - High-value segments: Semi-manual
   - Better 20 great emails than 100 mediocre

6. **Quality Control** (5 min)
   - Review random samples from campaigns
   - Check: Does it read as personal?
   - Spot broken merge fields
   - Response rate feedback loop

**Assessment:**
Create 3 segment-specific email templates: Same core message, different personalization for each segment. Include what merge fields/variables you'd use.

### Lesson 11: Deliverability Management

**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Ensure emails actually arrive in inbox (not spam)
- Maintain sender reputation over time
- Troubleshoot deliverability issues

**Content Outline:**

1. **Deliverability Fundamentals** (10 min)
   - None of your copy matters if email hits spam
   - 2024 reality: Gmail/Yahoo strict requirements
   - Reputation is earned and lost
   - This is the prerequisite—not optional

2. **Authentication Recap** (10 min)
   - SPF: Which IPs can send for your domain
   - DKIM: Email wasn't tampered with
   - DMARC: What to do when auth fails
   - All three required—check Course 4 for setup details
   - Test: Use mail-tester.com before campaigns

3. **Warmup Protocols** (10 min)
   - New domain: 3-4 weeks warmup required
   - Week 1: 5-10 emails/day (conversational)
   - Week 2: 15-20/day
   - Week 3: 30-50/day
   - Week 4+: Up to 50-100/day per domain
   - Automated warmup: Instantly, Smartlead built-in

4. **Reputation Management** (10 min)
   - Spam complaint rate: Must stay below 0.1%
   - At 0.3%, Google blocks your domain
   - Bounce rate: Keep below 2%
   - Unsubscribe rate: Monitor for signals
   - Volume consistency: Don't spike dramatically

5. **Troubleshooting** (10 min)
   - Emails going to spam: Check authentication first
   - Sudden drops: Did you spike volume?
   - Blacklist check: MXToolbox, Google Postmaster
   - Recovery: May need new domain

6. **Ongoing Monitoring** (5 min)
   - Weekly: Check bounce and spam rates
   - Monthly: Review Postmaster data
   - Quarterly: Audit domains and authentication
   - Keep multiple domains—redundancy matters

**Assessment:**
Complete a deliverability audit: Verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC for your sending domain(s), check current spam rate, and document your warmup status.
