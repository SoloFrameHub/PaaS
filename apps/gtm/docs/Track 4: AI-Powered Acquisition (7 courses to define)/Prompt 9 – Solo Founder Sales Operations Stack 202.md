An optimal 2026 sales‑ops stack under **\$100/month** is one solid **CRM**, one **automation layer**, and a few **point tools**—all tuned for low data‑entry friction and a simple “leads → meetings → proposals → wins” view.[^1][^2]

***

## CRM choices \& tradeoffs (2026, solo-level)

**HubSpot Free**

- Price: Free core CRM + basic email logging and pipelines.[^3]
- Pros: Quick to start, email logging, decent reports, lots of native integrations.
- Cons: Automation, sequences, and advanced reports quickly push you into paid hubs; can become expensive as you scale.
- Fit: Good default for very early **B2B or creator**, if you’re okay with HubSpot’s expansion pull.

**Attio**

- Price: Free plan (up to 3 seats) with real‑time sync and basic enrichment; Plus from **~€36/user/month** (~\$36–40).[^4][^5]
- Pros: Modern UI, AI enrichment, flexible data model, strong automation when paid; excellent for technical founders who want a **system of action** not just a database.[^4]
- Cons: Paid tiers can exceed your \$100 budget if you add seats; setup takes more thought.
- Fit: Strong for **B2B founders** who want AI‑native workflows and are comfortable configuring objects/automations.

**Folk**

- Price: Free tier, then **Standard ~\$20–25/member/month**, Premium ~\$40–50.[^6][^7][^8]
- Pros: Built for relationship‑based selling (consultants, creators, agencies), Gmail/Google Calendar‑friendly, enrichment and light sequences on paid tiers.[^8][^2]
- Cons: Less suited for very high‑volume cold outbound than Close/Pipedrive.
- Fit: Ideal for **creator/coach + high‑touch B2B services** with fewer, deeper deals.

**Close**

- Price: Solo plan from **\$9/month (annual) or \$19/month monthly**; Essentials from **\$35 (annual) / \$49 (monthly)**.[^9][^10][^11]
- Pros: All‑in‑one sales CRM with built‑in calling, SMS, email sequences, and strong pipeline views; great for volume outbound.[^12][^2]
- Cons: Designed around outbound teams; more than you need if you’re doing mostly inbound/relationship sales.
- Fit: Best if you’re a **B2B SaaS/service founder** doing meaningful cold email volume and want email/call logging done for you.

**Pipedrive**

- Price: Generally **~\$15–30/user/month** on lower tiers (not in your files but consistent with 2025–2026 reviews).[^3]
- Pros: Very easy pipeline UI, basic automations, email integration.
- Cons: Advanced automation and reporting require higher tiers; emails less integrated than Close.
- Fit: Good middle ground for **transactional B2B** or hybrid use.

**Notion / Airtable**

- Price: Free to **~\$10–20/user/month**.[^2]
- Pros: Flexible, can be customized into a CRM; great as a **shared brain**.
- Cons: High risk of spending more time *building* a CRM than selling; no native sales automation.[^2]
- Fit: Acceptable as a stopgap when you have <20–30 live deals and strong discipline.


### Explicit recommendations

- **Volume B2B (SaaS/services, cold outbound):**
    - Start: HubSpot Free (if you’re still figuring ICP) or **Close Solo** (~\$19/month).[^10][^9]
    - Upgrade: Close Essentials or Pipedrive once you have consistent outreach and want sequences + better reporting.
- **Relationship \& creator‑led (coaching, consulting, collabs, partnerships):**
    - Start: **Folk Standard** (~\$20–25/month) if you can afford it, otherwise HubSpot Free.[^7][^8]
    - Upgrade: Folk Premium if you need sequences/dashboards.
- **AI‑builder B2B (you want deep automation in CRM):**
    - Start: Attio Free (3 seats) plus external automation.[^5][^4]
    - Upgrade: Attio Plus (~\$36/user/month) once you’re sure you’ll live inside it daily.

## Pipeline \& analytics basics (universal)

**Universal stages (works for B2B and creator/coach):**[^13][^1][^2]

1. Lead (identified, enriched, not yet contacted).
2. Contacted (cold/warm outreach sent).
3. Engaged (reply or meaningful DM; conversation started).
4. Meeting booked (discovery/enrollment scheduled).
5. Proposal / Offer presented (SOW, quote, sales page, or enrollment offer delivered).
6. Verbal yes / Pending payment.
7. Closed–Won.
8. Closed–Lost (with loss reason).

**Minimal dashboards:**

- **Funnel:**
    - Leads → Meetings → Proposals → Wins, with conversion % at each step.[^14]
- **Velocity:**
    - Average days:
        - Lead → First contact.
        - First contact → Meeting.
        - Meeting → Win.[^14]
- **Commit vs upside forecast (binary):**
    - Commit:
        - Clear need + budget + timeline (MVQ/“Pain–Impact–Decision” met) and explicit intent.[^15][^13]
    - Upside:
        - Real opportunity but missing one element (unclear timing, competing priorities, multiple options).
    - Forecast:
        - Next 30 days = sum of **Commit** stage amounts.
        - 60–90 days = Commit + a conservative fraction of Upside (e.g., 30–40%).[^14]

## Automation layer under \$100/month

**Tool options:**

- **Zapier**
    - Pros: Easiest to start, huge integration catalog.
    - Cons: Paid tiers can get expensive quickly once you exceed free tasks.
    - Use when: You need quick wins and low event volume.
- **Make (Integromat)**
    - Pros: More flexible, visual, tends to be cheaper for multi‑step workflows.
    - Cons: Slightly steeper learning curve.
    - Use when: You want to stay under budget but need more complex flows.
- **n8n**
    - Pros: Open‑source, self‑hosted or cloud; near‑infinite workflows with low marginal cost.[^2]
    - Cons: Requires infra and basic DevOps.
    - Use when: You’re technical and want to keep recurring costs low.
- **Trigger.dev**
    - Pros: Code‑first, great for TypeScript backends; scheduled jobs, webhooks, queues.
    - Cons: Developer‑centric; not a no‑code tool.
    - Use when: You already have a Next.js/FastAPI backend and want “jobs as code.”

**3–5 automations that matter most (for any stack):**[^2]

1. **Lead Catcher**
    - Trigger: Form submit, Calendly booking, new lead from enrichment spreadsheet.
    - Action: Create/update contact + deal in CRM, tag source, notify you (Slack/email).[^2]
2. **Meeting Logger**
    - Trigger: Zoom/Meet call ends or calendar event with “Discovery/Enrollment.”
    - Action: Log activity in CRM, attach recording link, set follow‑up task.[^2]
3. **Follow‑Up Reminder**
    - Trigger: Deal moved to “Proposal / Offer” stage.
    - Action: Create task for **Day 3, Day 7, Day 14** follow‑up chain; optionally draft email with AI.[^13][^2]
4. **Contract / Invoice Chaser**
    - Trigger: Proposal accepted / invoice sent.
    - Action: If no payment/signature after X days, create reminder task and draft a polite nudge.
5. **Simple Notifications**
    - Trigger: Commit‑stage deal created or moved forward.
    - Action: Slack/email “deal highlight” so you don’t lose track of high‑value conversations.

## When and how to hire help (VA or SDR vs automation)

**When a VA makes sense:**

- Leading indicator: you’re consistently:
    - Dropping follow‑ups.
    - Spending >5 hours/week on repetitive admin (updating CRM, sending standard docs, basic research).[^16][^13]
- Thresholds:
    - >40–50 active opportunities at once, or
    - >10 sales meetings/week.[^14]
- Typical first VA scope:
    - Inbox triage (tagging leads vs non‑leads).
    - Updating CRM from email/calendar.
    - Basic research (company summaries, pulling LinkedIn URLs, finding decision‑maker names).
    - Document management (organizing proposals, agreements, invoices).[^13]

**When to test part‑time SDR vs more automation:**

- Consider part‑time SDR only after:
    - You have a **proven outbound motion** (50–100+ manual emails/DMs with known reply/booking rates).
    - You can document scripts, ICP, and qualification logic (MVQ).[^15][^2]
- For most solo founders with <\$100/month tool budgets, it’s usually higher‑ROI to:
    - Push automation + VA support first.
    - Add SDR later when you’re closer to the upper end of your MRR goal and have more surplus cash + clear playbooks.

**Delegation playbooks for VA (simple):**

- **Inbox triage SOP:**
    - Tag incoming messages as Lead / Customer / Admin / Noise.
    - Star or label anything matching your ICP or containing buying signals.[^13]
- **CRM updates SOP:**
    - For each meeting or key email, update:
        - Stage, amount, close date, and next step due date.
- **Research SOP:**
    - For new leads without context: find LinkedIn, company site, size, role, and one recent signal; enter into CRM.[^2]

## Legal templates \& sales finance basics

**Solo‑friendly contract templates (not legal advice):**

- Sources:
    - Indie‑friendly template libraries, reputable legal template providers, and open resources (e.g., some law firms and SaaS legal sites publish standard **MSA/SOW/SaaS** templates).[^17][^16]
- What to customize:
    - Scope of work/deliverables.
    - Payment terms (net 7/14/30, late fees).
    - Term/termination, IP ownership, and confidentiality.
- What to leave to counsel for higher stakes:
    - Complex IP assignments, data processing agreements, large‑enterprise security riders, and anything above your risk comfort.

**Sales finance basics:**

- **Invoicing \& collections:**
    - Use Stripe/Chargebee/PayPal/your accounting tool to:
        - Standardize invoice templates.
        - Automate reminders at 7/14/30 days overdue.
- **Revenue tracking:**
    - Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) or trailing 90‑day average.
    - New vs expansion vs churned revenue.[^14]
- **CAC payback targets:**
    - Bootstrapped:
        - Aim for CAC payback **within 1–3 months** when possible; 6–9 months is acceptable if churn is low and cash runway is stable.[^1][^14]
    - VC‑style benchmarks:
        - Often tolerate **12+ month** payback in exchange for faster growth; you generally can’t.[^1][^14]

## Reference stacks under \$100/month

### 1. “Spreadsheet + CRM Lite” (very early, <50 active opps)

| Component | Tool | Approx. cost | Notes |
| :-- | :-- | --: | :-- |
| CRM | HubSpot Free or Attio Free | \$0 | Pipeline, basic email logging; Attio gives AI‑enriched contact view. [^4][^5] |
| Relationship db | Google Sheets / Airtable | \$0–\$10 | Track experiments, simple funnel metrics. [^14] |
| Automation | Free Zapier plan or Make | \$0–\$10 | Lead catcher + follow‑up reminders in low volume. |
| E‑sign | Free DocuSign tier or similar | \$0–\$10 | A few envelopes/month. |

**Total:** ~\$0–\$20/month.
**Use‑case:** validating ICP, <50 open deals, low automation needs, heavy manual learning.[^17][^1]

### 2. “Serious Solo Ops” (50–100 active opps, scaling to \$100K MRR)

**Option A – Volume B2B outbound**


| Component | Tool | Approx. cost | Notes |
| :-- | :-- | --: | :-- |
| CRM + outreach | Close Solo or Essentials | \$19–\$49 | All‑in‑one calling, email, sequences, pipelines. [^9][^10] |
| Automation | Make.com paid tier | ~\$16–\$30 | Lead catcher, meeting logger, follow‑up chains. |
| E‑sign | SignWell / Zoho Sign | ~\$10–\$15 | Low‑volume e‑sign. |
| Analytics | Native CRM reports + simple spreadsheet | \$0–\$10 | Build basic funnel + velocity views. [^14] |

**Total:** ≈ \$45–\$90/month, depending on exact plans.

**Option B – Relationship/creator hybrid**


| Component | Tool | Approx. cost | Notes |
| :-- | :-- | --: | :-- |
| CRM | Folk Standard | \$20–\$25 | Relationship‑centric, enrichment, email support. [^7][^8] |
| Automation | Make / Zapier Starter | \$10–\$30 | Lead catcher, meeting logger, simple notifications. |
| E‑sign | SignWell / similar | ~\$10–\$15 | Contracts for coaching/services. |
| Analytics | Airtable/Sheets dashboard | \$0–\$10 | Custom views of leads → meetings → wins. [^14] |

**Total:** ≈ \$40–\$80/month.

You can comfortably scale from **\$10K → \$100K MRR** with one of these stacks by keeping your tool count low and your **habits + processes** tight. When in doubt, prioritize: one CRM you actually use, a handful of high‑leverage automations, and human‑first conversations over adding more software.[^1][^2]
