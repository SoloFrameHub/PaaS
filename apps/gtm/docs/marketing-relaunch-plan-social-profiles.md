### LinkedIn

"Always be closing."

The worst sales advice ever given.

I've built revenue engines at five companies. One went from struggling startup to nearly $1B market cap. None of the people who made that happen were "closers."

They were diagnosticians.

They asked better questions. They disqualified faster. They walked away from bad-fit deals instead of forcing them through. They treated every conversation like a doctor's visit — what's the problem, is it something I can fix, and is the patient willing to do the work?

Result: higher close rates, bigger deals, happier customers, zero burnout.

When I built the Solo GTM OS with Claude Code, the discovery call simulator was the first feature I built. Not the courses. Not the dashboard. The simulator.

Because this is the skill gap that kills solo founders. You can build a great product. You can find the right prospects. But if you pitch before you diagnose, you lose in the first five minutes.

The simulator uses the DISC behavioral framework. Four buyer personas, each one fights back differently:

The Analytical wants a spreadsheet before they'll commit to a second call.
The Driver wants ROI in the first 30 seconds or they're checking email.
The Expressive wants to hear the vision, not the spec sheet.
The Steady wants case studies and low switching costs.

You practice until the real call feels like the fifth time, not the first.
