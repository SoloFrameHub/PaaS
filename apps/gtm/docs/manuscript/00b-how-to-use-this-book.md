# How to Use This Book

This isn’t just a “how to send emails” manual. It’s a system built on three pillars:

1. **Direct conversations** – you reaching out, running discovery, and closing.  
2. **Retention and referrals** – customers staying, expanding, and sending others.  
3. **Answer Engine Visibility** – AI systems and search describing you as *the* person who solves a specific problem.

Chapter 14 (AEO) sits in that third pillar. Chapter 15 adds a fourth element—community and social proof—that reinforces all three.

Because solo founders have limited time, there are three ways to move through the book.

## Path 1: The Three‑Pillar System (recommended for most readers)

Use this if you want a durable acquisition engine, not just a few quick wins.

1. **Pillar 1 – Direct Conversations (Outreach & Sales)**  
   - Read **Chapters 1–5** in order:  
     identity and fear of selling, ICP, outreach, discovery/qualification, and pricing/presentation.  
   - These chapters get you talking to the right people and having conversations that can actually turn into revenue.

2. **Pillar 2 – Retention & Expansion**  
   - Read **Chapter 6** on onboarding, retention, expansion revenue, and referrals.  
   - The goal here is simple: every customer you win should be worth more and last longer, so your acquisition work compounds.

3. **Pillar 3 – Answer Engine Visibility (AEO)**  
   - Read **Chapter 14** much earlier than most founders would think.  
   - Even if your site is simple, you need a home on the web and in AI systems that:  
     - States clearly who you help and what you do.  
     - Is structured so Google AI Mode, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Claude can understand and cite you.  
   - This chapter shows you how to turn your site from “online brochure” into “canonical reference” that supports every cold email, DM, and intro.

4. **Systems & Scaling**  
   - After you've seen all three pillars once, read **Chapters 7–9** (AI/automation, metrics, objections), **Chapter 10** (playbooks for different stages), **Chapter 11** (diagnosis), **Chapter 12** (sustainable rhythm), **Chapter 13** (your one‑page system), and **Chapter 15** (community and social proof).

On this path, you’re not treating AEO as “advanced SEO” for later—you’re making sure that while you’re doing direct outreach, any person or AI that looks you up sees a coherent, credible story.

## Path 2: Conversations First, AEO in Parallel (if you need deals quickly)

Use this if you need revenue this quarter, but you also accept that search and AI visibility are shifting under your feet.

In your first 1–2 sittings:

- Read **Chapter 1** (why selling feels hard), **Chapter 2** (ICP), and the relevant playbook in **Chapter 10** (your starting situation).  
- Start **executing outreach immediately**: 20–50 messages, calls booked, discovery conversations.

Then, in the same 30‑day window, make **Chapter 14 your secondary focus**:

- Don’t wait until “later” to set up your website and AEO basics.  
- Use Chapter 14 to:
  - Ship a simple but clear site (or improve your existing one).  
  - Add basic schema and answer‑first structure so AI assistants can accurately describe who you are and what you do.  
  - Make sure that when someone you cold‑email Googles you or asks an AI about you, they see the same positioning you used in your message.

Think of this path as: **talk to people now, and at the same time, make sure the internet and AI know who you are.** You’re not trying to “do content marketing” yet; you’re making sure you exist where people and models look.

## Path 3: AEO‑Led Optimization (if you already have traffic or content)

Use this if you:

- Already have a site, content, or audience,  
- Are seeing traffic but not enough leads, or  
- Suspect AI Overviews and answer engines are cannibalizing your old SEO playbook.

On this path:

1. Start with **Chapter 14**. Treat it as a standalone mini‑book on the shift from SEO to AEO:
   - Zero‑click reality  
   - How Google AI Mode, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Claude actually source answers  
   - How to structure pages, schema, and content so you get cited instead of ignored.

2. Then:

   - Use **Chapter 2** to tighten your ICP in light of what your analytics and existing customers tell you.  
   - Use **Chapters 3–5** to align your outreach and sales conversations with the same positioning that’s on your site.  
   - Use **Chapters 6 and 9** to plug leaks in retention and objection handling.

In other words, this path is for founders who already have some motion and want to **align everything—outreach, site, and AI visibility—around a clear, individual‑first position.**

## Feeling Overwhelmed? Start With Three Frameworks

This book has dozens of frameworks. You don't need all of them at once. If you read nothing else, internalize these three — they cover targeting, qualifying, and sustaining:

1. **ICP — Ideal Customer Profile** (Chapter 2): Who exactly you're trying to reach. Write a one-paragraph statement specific enough to filter 100 prospects down to the 10 most likely to buy. Everything else in this book depends on getting this right.

2. **MVQ — Minimum Viable Qualification** (Chapter 4): Three questions — Pain, Impact, Decision — that tell you in under five minutes whether a conversation is worth pursuing. Saves you from spending hours on prospects who were never going to buy.

3. **Weekly Review Rhythm** (Chapter 12): Fifteen minutes every Friday reviewing your pipeline, metrics, and next actions. This is what turns sporadic effort into a system. Without it, everything else decays.

Start here. Add frameworks as you need them. The full set is in the Framework Index appendix whenever you're ready.
