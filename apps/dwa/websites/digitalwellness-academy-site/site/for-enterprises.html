FOR ENTERPRISES & CORPORATE WELLNESS

Past the EAP check-box - into resilience your workforce actually uses

Traditional EAPs post double-digit eligible-population utilization rates in the low single digits. The problem isn't what's offered - it's that a phone line labeled "if you need help" is the last thing a high-performing employee will ever dial. A peak-performance platform, labeled for optimization rather than crisis, and integrated with the resilience literature your people actually want, is a completely different uptake curve.

Why the EAP playbook is stuck

Three structural reasons corporate wellness budgets keep growing and engagement doesn't.

Stigma as gatekeeper

"Call this number if you're struggling" self-selects for people already in crisis - which is the hardest moment to reach out. Everyone who'd benefit before that moment doesn't.

Synchronous-only

EAP sessions require scheduling, a phone call, disclosure. Knowledge workers route around anything that requires those three things.

Opaque outcomes

You pay per eligible life, utilization is low single digits, and outcomes reporting is a satisfaction score. No way to tie the spend to retention, productivity, or the healthcare claims line.

What a corporate deployment looks like

Optimization-led access, clinical-grade escalation path, structured resilience content for specific employee stressors.

Optimization School as the front door

Employees access the Five Pillars (Physical Vitality, Social Connection, Mental Clarity, Emotional Resilience, Purpose) via SSO. No clinical intake. Tracks like Workplace Mental Health, Stress & Challenge Navigation, Healthy Boundaries, Team Sports & Collective Activity, and Family & Parenting are the 2026 equivalent of a modern lunch-and-learn curriculum - except it's self-paced, interactive, and measurable.

Uptake comes from labeling it as performance, not help. The clinical-grade content is the same; the front-door framing isn't.

Quiet clinical escalation path

When an employee engaging with the Optimization content indicates they might benefit from the Therapeutic courses (via assessments, forum activity, or explicit request), the platform offers access - privately, with no flag to their employer. If they're in distress during any text input, the classifier fires and the employee sees 988 + local resources. The employer never sees individual-level anything; they see aggregate engagement and outcome trends.

ERG and manager tooling

Mental-health ERGs and people managers get a light toolkit: discussion guides aligned to platform courses, manager conversation scripts for team members showing signs of burnout, and aggregate team-level (not individual) dashboards for leaders who want to track cultural metrics rather than individual behavior.

How it plugs into your stack

Identity, benefits system, and learning infrastructure - with the privacy walls in the right places.

Identity & SSO

SAML 2.0 / OIDC against Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace. Provision on hire, deprovision on exit via SCIM. Employees authenticate once, access is automatic.

Benefits adjacency

Sits alongside your EAP, not replacing it. Platform is positioned as a performance/resilience benefit. If an employee needs clinical care, EAP referral path stays intact.

LMS integration (optional)

xAPI / SCORM hooks for launching specific courses from within your existing learning platform. Completion writes back to LMS if you want manager-visibility at the development-program level.

Reporting into HRIS

Aggregate, de-identified engagement data only. No individual records cross the boundary. Quarterly cohort reporting mapped to org-level dimensions (function, geography, tenure band).

Measuring whether it's working

Outcome metrics that map to HR and finance categories - not just a net-promoter score on the benefit itself.

Engagement

Active user rate, course completions per active user, repeat-session frequency. Benchmarked to 30–50%+ active use - an order of magnitude above typical EAP utilization.

Wellbeing trajectory

Aggregate movement on burnout assessment, self-esteem check, emotional dysregulation check - cohort-level, de-identified. Mapped by org function and tenure band.

Business-adjacent signals

Optional cross-tabulation with your HRIS on voluntary attrition, internal mobility, and engagement survey scores. Tie the platform spend to something your CFO can read.

Run an ERG pilot or a function-level trial

Most enterprises start with 500–2,000 employees in a single function or ERG before a company-wide rollout. Pilot programs include quarterly outcome reviews and pricing caps against company-wide licensing.
