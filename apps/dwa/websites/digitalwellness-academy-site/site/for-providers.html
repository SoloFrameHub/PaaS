How it fits into a real clinical week

The platform is designed to be the between-session layer a psychiatric or therapy practice has always wanted but never had time to build.

Before intake

Prospective patients land on your SEO-indexed lesson pages, read about their symptoms in your practice's voice, and click through to the intake form. They arrive pre-educated and less stigmatized.

Intake visit

Clinician assigns 2–3 courses at the end of the session (anxiety toolkit, sleep mastery, etc.). Platform sends the patient a branded welcome email with their first lesson queued up.

Between sessions

Patient works through lessons, thought records, and tracking. Distress classifier monitors text inputs continuously. Any crisis signal reaches the provider within minutes.

Session prep (5 min before visit)

Provider opens the patient's dashboard: trend line shows GAD-7 dropped 3 points since last session, mood tracking improving, they completed the exposure hierarchy lesson, and flagged one thought record as unusually distressing.

Follow-up session

Clinician spends zero minutes asking "how was your week?" and goes directly to clinically meaningful questions. The platform did the remembering.

Maintenance phase

Patients who graduate from active treatment can stay on the platform at a lower-tier subscription, continuing skill reinforcement and periodic reassessments - while you free that appointment slot for someone on the waitlist.

The objections we hear - and the honest answers

"My patients won't use another app."

Fair. The fix is not "market harder" - it's that prescription carries weight. When a clinician says "this week, complete lessons 1–3 of the anxiety toolkit and bring me the thought record you flagged as hardest," engagement rates change. The platform is designed as a between-session assignment layer, not an optional sidecar.

"I already use [BetterHelp / Cerebral / Teladoc / etc.]."

Those are parallel care channels - we're not. A licensed practice uses the platform as infrastructure inside their own care, not a separate service to compete with. The branding is yours. The clinician of record is yours. The relationship is yours.

"What's the HIPAA story?"

Zero-knowledge text processing for all free-form inputs. Provider-scoped row-level data access. BAA-compliant cloud deployment as a prerequisite for production licensing. Full audit logging on every PHI-adjacent event. Detailed HIPAA posture →

"What happens when my patient is in actual crisis?"

The DistilBERT classifier flags crisis-level signals within seconds. Patient sees a 988 resource modal immediately. Assigned provider gets alerted in-app and by email with timestamp and context. The platform is not a replacement for emergency services - it's an early-warning layer on top of them.

"How long does rollout take?"

30 days: week 1 branding + customization, week 2 deploy + test, week 3 clinician training, week 4 patient launch. We handle the technical rollout; you handle the patient introduction.
