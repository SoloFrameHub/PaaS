Growth is hard in the wrong three ways

Every practice wants to grow. The problem isn't demand - it's that the tools available for growing a behavioral-health practice compound cost instead of capacity.

The problem: Acquisition
Paid ads are a treadmill

Google and Meta ads for behavioral health run $100-$400+ per patient inquiry. The day spend stops, pipeline stops.

The platform's answer

A compounding content engine. Every lesson atomizes into blog posts, email sequences, and social snippets - branded to your practice, linking to your intake. Acquisition cost trends toward zero as the library ranks.

The problem: Organic reach
Search is locked out

Local mental-health search is dominated by national directories. A practice site with ten pages cannot outrank a platform publishing thousands.

The platform's answer

597 indexable lesson pages on your domain. SEO + AEO + GEO structured data baked in, schema-tagged per condition, mapped to local service area. Your practice becomes the authoritative answer, not a link in a directory.

The problem: Capacity per hour
50 minutes isn't enough

A session is 50 minutes of clinical contact. The rest of the week - 10,030 minutes - is where outcomes cap and churn begins.

The platform's answer

Between-session skill work, monitored 24/7. Each patient moves through assigned lessons, thought records, and assessments. The distress classifier flags crises. Every clinical hour produces more lasting change - the same team serves more patients, better.

Demand comes from the content engine. Capacity comes from the between-session layer. The practice grows - and growth doesn't outrun your ability to deliver care.

24/7 Crisis Detection That Scales

Custom-trained DistilBERT model for mental health-specific distress detection with clinical validation

How It Works

1

Real-Time Analysis

Monitors text across journals, assessments, forum posts, and check-ins. Not generic moderation - mental health-specific distress detection.

2

Three-Level Classification

Crisis (≥85%): Modal with 988 hotline + immediate provider alert
Mild (60-85%): Gentle check-in prompt
None (<60%): No intervention

3

Zero-Knowledge Architecture

Text analyzed and discarded immediately - never stored, never logged. Only metadata (level, confidence, timestamp) persisted for audit.

4

Provider Integration

Crisis alerts sent to assigned provider via email + in-app notification with context (course, lesson, timestamp) but no PHI.
