PLATFORM OVERVIEW

What the platform actually feels like - from onboarding through outcome

43 courses, 592 interactive lessons, 596 quizzes, 22 validated clinical assessments. But a course catalog isn't what makes this work - the engine does. Here's how a patient moves through it, what they see on every screen, and what the clinician sees on the other side.

The learner journey, end to end

Five stages. Designed so that each one feeds the next - nothing is dead-ended, nothing is rote.

1

Onboarding & symptom screening

A 9-step intake captures symptom profile (severity + duration), therapy history, coping strategies, crisis screen, sleep, nutrition, and learning preferences. For clinical patients it runs PHQ-9 and GAD-7 as a baseline.

The output isn't a static profile - it's a ranked course queue. Severe anxiety + elevated GAD-7 routes to the panic disorder and anxiety toolkit sequences. Mild depression + insomnia routes to sleep mastery plus depression action.

2

Dashboard & recommended path

Dashboard shows up to five recommended courses, ranked by symptom match and engagement likelihood. Progress is visible: streaks, XP, courses completed, dimension scores on mood/anxiety/sleep/stress/nutrition.

Wellness alerts surface inactivity, clinical deterioration, or reassessment prompts. Analytics view shows 30-day mood rolling average and PHQ-9/GAD-7 trajectory - the same trend lines the provider sees on their end.

3

Lesson experience

Two-column layout: sticky lesson nav with progress markers on the left, MDX content with embedded interactive components on the right. Each lesson typically uses five or more components.

A representative anxiety-toolkit lesson: body map to identify physical symptoms → interactive breathing pacer → thought record worksheet → exposure hierarchy builder → five-question quiz with immediate feedback and explanation. Context-aware AI coach flyout on every screen.

4

Tracking & outcomes

500+ external-JSON quizzes (five questions each) test retention. 22 standardized assessments including PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5, PDSS-SR, PSQI, SPIN, ISI, and thematic self-checks run at scheduled intervals.

Daily mood tracking (anxiety, sleep, energy, stress). Feedback on every lesson - star rating plus optional verbatim. Both feed the analytics pipeline that flags underperforming content for rewrite.

5

Broader access & reduced stigma

Because the optimization school sits on the same platform, a patient doing CBT for panic disorder can also quietly enroll in Physical Vitality or Purpose & Meaning. The reverse is also true: a marathon trainee using Movement for Mental Performance can discover the anxiety toolkit on their own terms.

No forced pathways. No clinical front door required to access the optimization side. That's the stigma-reduction mechanic.

The component library

30+ interactive component types. Every lesson is composed from these - no passive video blocks, no text-only walls.

CBT

Thought Record

Cognitive restructuring worksheet with situation / thoughts / feelings / evidence / reframe fields. Variations per symptom type.

ERP

Exposure Hierarchy

Drag-rank feared situations by distress intensity. Couples with an Exposure Log and Exposure Plan Worksheet for follow-through.

Somatic

Body Map

Click regions on an anatomical figure to tag sensations. Teaches interoceptive awareness and HPA-axis literacy.

Autonomic

Interactive Breathing

Visual pacer with configurable patterns (box, 4-7-8, diaphragmatic). Session tracking and guided-grounding mode.

Active learning

Interactive Scenario

Branching decision tree with contextual feedback on each choice. Surfaces reasoning mistakes learners wouldn't catch from reading alone.

Measurement

Likert Assessment

Standardized and thematic assessments with scoring, banding, and history charting. Powers the trend lines.

Quizzing

Lesson Quiz

Five questions per lesson from external JSON. Immediate feedback with explanation. Response times + retries logged.

Mindfulness

Mindfulness Timer & Grounding

Structured timers for sitting practice and guided grounding scripts. Session data persisted for consistency tracking.

Skill-building

Coping Strategy Ranker

Patient ranks strategies they've tried by effectiveness. Pairs with tracking log to surface which ones actually correlate with mood improvement over time.

Tracking

Tracking Log & Trend Chart

Daily check-ins (mood, anxiety, sleep, energy) with trend visualization. Same data feeds provider-facing outcome charts.

Presentation

Slide & Flip Card

Structured teaching: flip-card definitions, step-by-step breakdowns, accordion detail layers, lesson diagrams.

Reflection

Checklist & Check-in

Skill-mastery checklists, scheduled check-ins, insight-grid reflections, engagement summaries at course completion.

Full component inventory: 30+ distinct interactive types. Every text-entry component routes through the distress classifier in the therapeutic school.
