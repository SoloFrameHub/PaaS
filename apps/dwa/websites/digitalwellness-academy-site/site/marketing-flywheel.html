THE MARKETING FLYWHEEL

The platform doesn't just serve your patients - it markets your practice

592 clinically-authored lessons atomize into SEO/AEO/GEO-indexed pages on your domain, weekly email sequences, social snippets, newsletter pillars, and printable patient brochures. The first lesson of every course is public. Your practice becomes the answer when someone searches "managing panic attacks in [your city]" - and when they click through, they land on your intake form.

The whole flywheel, in one pass

Content in → search traffic out → qualified inquiries in → patient subscriptions out → more data → better content. A closed loop that compounds.

Every turn of the loop increases the content library's coverage and your practice's organic footprint. Marginal cost of the next turn approaches zero.

SEO, AEO, GEO — three search surfaces, one content library

Optimizing for keyword ranking alone is a 2015 playbook. We optimize for traditional search (SEO), answer engines like Perplexity and ChatGPT (AEO), and location-intent queries (GEO) - all at once.

SEO

Traditional search

Long-tail keyword coverage across every symptom, modality, and life situation in our taxonomy.

  • • 592+ canonical URLs per licensee domain
  • • Schema.org MedicalWebPage + CourseInstance markup
  • • Internal linking graph between related lessons
  • • Fresh content from weekly atomization
  • • Provider-NPI and practice-address microdata
AEO

Answer-engine optimization

Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, Gemini, Claude and the next wave of retrieval-augmented answer engines cite structured, credible sources.

  • • FAQ schema + Question/Answer blocks on every lesson
  • • Clear authorship attribution (clinical reviewer NPI)
  • • Citation-ready evidence callouts (inline sources)
  • • Short, answer-shaped paragraphs under clear H2s
  • • llms.txt / ai.txt conventions for explicit crawl policy
GEO

Geographic / local

"Anxiety therapist near [city]" is where the clinical buying decision actually happens - but most practice websites can't rank.

  • • LocalBusiness schema with practice address + hours
  • • City/region landing pages per major service area
  • • Google Business Profile integration
  • • Review schema + aggregate-rating surface (when real)
  • • Location-specific symptom pages ("anxiety treatment Austin")

Blog / pillar page

SEO-optimized canonical on your domain. H1 matches search intent, schema wrapped, internal links to related courses and the intake form. 1,200–1,800 words from the lesson body.

Email drip

7-day sequence per course, subject lines tested against the topic. Final email routes to a discovery call or the paid subscription signup for the full course.

Social snippets

Platform-specific: LinkedIn long-form for professional audiences, Instagram carousel for pattern-interrupt posts, short-form video scripts you or your team can record in 5 minutes each.

Newsletter pillars

Monthly newsletter auto-drafted from the most-engaged lessons of the prior 30 days. Practice brand voice, your practice's byline. Sent through your existing email tool via API.

Patient brochures

Printable PDFs per symptom (panic disorder, seasonal depression, grief, etc.) for your waiting room and post-session handoff. Branded with your practice, QR-linked to the full lesson.

Podcast / talk outlines

If you or a clinician in your practice does a podcast or community talk, the platform generates structured outlines that match the lesson - no duplicate research effort.

The free-lesson funnel

The first lesson of every course is publicly indexable and free. That's deliberate - it's the lead magnet.

1

Prospect searches a symptom or situation

"Why do I keep having panic attacks in the morning?" "How do I stop ruminating at night?" "Does CBT actually work for social anxiety?"

2

Lands on your domain's lesson page

Clinically accurate, branded to your practice, with interactive components (mini thought record, breathing pacer). They're not reading an article - they're using a thought record.

3

Completes the first lesson → email capture

At the end, they see "Continue to lesson 2" - gated on email. Soft ask. No credit card. 40–60%+ capture rate on qualified traffic.

4

7-day email sequence starts

Drip sequence matched to the course topic. Each email opens with a micro-lesson and ends with a soft CTA - either a discovery call with your practice, a subscription, or the next lesson gated on signup.

5

Converts to subscriber or patient

Two outcomes are both wins: they subscribe (revenue share), or they book an intake visit (new patient). Many do both sequentially.

Promotional calendar

The platform ships with a promo calendar: free-lesson weeks tied to awareness months (Mental Health Awareness in May, Men's Mental Health in June, Suicide Prevention in September, Depression Awareness in October, etc.). Practices can opt in to run synchronized campaigns with co-marketing support.

Content for the general public - not only patients

A big share of the Optimization School's value is that it speaks to people who don't identify as "patients." They get clinically-grounded education framed for growth, not illness.

The marathoner who's burning out

Lands on "Movement for Mental Performance" from a sports-training search. Discovers the overlap with Emotional Resilience. Keeps engaging - without ever having used a word like "therapy" or "anxiety."

The executive Googling "sunday scaries"

Lands on "Workplace Mental Health" from a LinkedIn share. The content is identical in clinical rigor to the therapeutic anxiety courses - just re-framed and without a PHQ-9 intake.

The parent of a teenager

Searches "how do I help my teenager with anxiety" on behalf of someone else. Lands on a psychoeducation lesson they can read themselves - and optionally, forward to their teen to use.

The curious medical professional

PCPs, NPs, and health coaches search specific modalities (ERP, DBT distress tolerance, behavioral activation). The lessons educate them; the citation trail routes them to you for clinical referrals.
