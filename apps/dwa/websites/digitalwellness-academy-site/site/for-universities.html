FOR UNIVERSITIES & COUNSELING CENTERS

Support the students your counseling center can't schedule

Campus counseling centers are running 6–12 week waitlists and 20–30% annual growth in demand. Students in crisis don't wait; they drop out, defer, or do worse. The Digital Wellness Academy platform is what a counseling center looks like when it scales beyond appointment capacity - clinical care extension for the high-need caseload, peak-performance resilience content for the entire student body, and 24/7 distress monitoring so nothing falls through at 2 a.m.

Why counseling centers can't solve this alone

Three structural mismatches define the student mental-health gap - and each one has a platform answer.

Constraint 1

Appointments don't scale linearly

Adding one staff clinician adds about 20 weekly clinical hours. Adding one platform seat adds 168 weekly hours of self-directed access.

Constraint 2

Peak demand is concentrated

Midterm, finals, breakup, family deaths - distress spikes happen on a predictable academic calendar. Counseling centers can't flex staff; platforms can flex content promotion and crisis triage.

Constraint 3

Stigma gates the front door

Many students who'd benefit from counseling don't book because of stigma or scheduling friction. An optimization-labeled resilience track gets them through the same evidence-based content without a clinical intake.

A two-layer deployment

Clinical care extension for the students your counselors see. Peak-performance resilience content for everyone else.

Layer 1 · Counseling center extension

Clinical care between appointments

  • • Therapeutic courses assigned by your counselors (anxiety, mood, trauma, sleep, nutrition)
  • • Standardized assessments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5) administered on schedule
  • • Real-time DistilBERT distress classifier on every text input
  • • Counselor dashboard with trend lines and crisis alerts
  • • 988 escalation path + campus-specific crisis line configurable
  • • Session-prep reports for each student's next appointment
Layer 2 · Whole-campus resilience

Optimization content for the 80% who never book

  • • 19 Five-Pillar courses across physical vitality, social connection, mental clarity, emotional resilience, purpose
  • • Self-check assessments (burnout, self-esteem, perfectionism) without clinical gating
  • • Content themed to academic stress periods (midterms, internship season, finals)
  • • SSO via institutional email; single platform for everyone
  • • Peer-cohort analytics at the institutional level (no individual identification)
  • • Keyword-gate safety layer on public inputs - still escalates serious signals

How a campus rollout actually runs

We've designed around counseling center workflow and academic-year rhythm - not generic SaaS kickoff playbooks.

Week 1–2 · Institutional configuration

SSO wiring to your SIS/identity provider. Counseling center staff accounts provisioned. Campus crisis line + Title IX + Dean of Students escalation paths configured. Brand customization on the student-facing portal.

Week 3 · Clinical team training

Your counselors learn the dashboard, the alert workflow, how to assign courses, how to read trend charts. Trial caseload of 20 students to pressure-test before general launch.

Week 4 · Soft launch to current caseload

Active counseling patients onboarded first. Use the Therapeutic school as between-session assignment. 2 weeks of tuning based on real counselor feedback.

Weeks 5–8 · Whole-campus launch

Optimization School opened to the full student population via SSO. Res life, student affairs, faculty mental-health ambassadors get launch kit. Content tied to the academic calendar (midterm stress, seasonal depression, finals, summer melt).

Ongoing · Outcomes review

Quarterly cohort-level reporting: engagement, assessment trajectories, crisis-alert volumes, counseling center load comparison year-over-year. Signals route into the content improvement pipeline.

FERPA, HIPAA, and the campus privacy reality

Counseling records at universities live in a blended HIPAA/FERPA regime, and platforms that don't understand the overlap get campus counsel into panic mode fast. Ours does.

What the counseling center sees

  • • Only students who are active counseling patients appear in clinician dashboards
  • • Row-level access control at the database - not application-layer filter
  • • Structured outcomes only (scores, trends, completion) - no free-text
  • • Audit log on every read and write of student records

What institutional admins see

  • • De-identified cohort analytics at the residence hall, college, or class-year level
  • • Aggregate engagement - never individual student activity
  • • Volume of crisis alerts, not their content
  • • Content performance data for curriculum/outreach planning
