## Hero paragraph

> A 10-lesson psychoeducation course on bipolar disorder — the distinction between bipolar I, bipolar II, cyclothymia, and mixed features; the role of sleep, social rhythms, activity, and substances as common triggers; and the evidence for structured psychoeducation, mood tracking, and interpersonal and social rhythm therapy (IPSRT) as relapse-prevention tools alongside medication. Bipolar disorder is a chronic, relapsing condition that requires coordinated care with a prescribing clinician. This course is psychoeducation and a self-monitoring scaffold — it does not replace medication or psychiatric care, and it does not tell learners to adjust prescribed medications.

## Safety panel

> **This course is:** psychoeducation about bipolar I, II, and cyclothymia; early-warning-sign awareness; structured mood tracking as a self-monitoring tool.
>
> **This course is not:** a substitute for psychiatric care, a basis to start, stop, or change any medication, or a treatment for acute mania or severe depression. If you have bipolar disorder, self-management must be coordinated with your prescriber.
>
> **If you are in crisis** or thinking about self-harm, call or text **988** (U.S. Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or contact your local crisis line.
