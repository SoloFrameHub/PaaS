## Hero paragraph

> A 12-lesson psychoeducation course on adult attachment — the two underlying dimensions (attachment anxiety and attachment avoidance), the four prototype styles these combine to describe, and the evidence that attachment security is dynamic rather than fixed. The course covers how early adversity and adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) can shape adult attachment, how insecure patterns tend to compound over relationship duration, and how earned security develops through significant relational and therapeutic experience. This is psychoeducation and self-understanding — it is not a replacement for Emotionally Focused Therapy, attachment-based therapy, or trauma treatment.

## Safety panel

> **This course is:** psychoeducation about adult attachment dimensions and patterns; self-understanding and reflection; preparation for attachment-informed or trauma-focused therapy.
>
> **This course is not:** EFT, schema therapy, or trauma treatment; a diagnostic system for labeling partners; or a replacement for clinical care.
>
> **If you are in crisis** or thinking about self-harm, call or text **988** (U.S. Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or contact your local crisis line. If you are experiencing intimate partner violence, "working on your attachment patterns" is not the solution — call the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233.
