## Hero paragraph

> A 10-lesson psychoeducation course introducing the emotion-regulation module from Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) — identifying and labeling emotions (sadness, shame, guilt, anger, fear), the PLEASE skill for reducing emotional vulnerability through sleep, nutrition, and movement, opposite-action practice, and increasing positive emotions. DBT has robust empirical support for borderline personality disorder, chronic suicidality, and self-harm, and growing evidence for mood, eating, PTSD, and substance-use conditions involving emotion dysregulation. This course teaches the emotion-regulation skills at a self-help level as an adjunct to — not a replacement for — standard DBT with a trained clinician.

## Safety panel

> **This course is:** psychoeducation about DBT emotion-regulation skills; self-help practice of PLEASE, opposite action, and emotion labeling.
>
> **This course is not:** standard DBT (which includes skills group, individual therapy, phone coaching, and therapist consultation), a treatment for BPD or chronic suicidality, or a replacement for professional care.
>
> **If you are in crisis** or thinking about self-harm, call or text **988** (U.S. Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or contact your local crisis line.
