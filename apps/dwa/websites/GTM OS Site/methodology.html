The Method · Not the Curriculum

Diagnose before you prescribe. Everywhere else, it's malpractice.

A doctor who writes a prescription before running a diagnosis gets sued. A founder who pitches before they understand the pain calls it a Tuesday. The OS is built to fix that - and to teach you how.

Six frameworks inside the platform, each backed by research, each with a course that teaches it and a live surface that runs it for you on real pipeline. This page is the map. The curriculum is inside.

The Premise
"A doctor who writes a prescription before running a diagnosis commits malpractice. The law agrees. The patient doesn't come back."

Most founder-led sales runs that malpractice in reverse - product pitched before the pain is understood, features listed before the impact is quantified, closes attempted before decision authority is even confirmed.

The platform teaches you to be the doctor. Diagnose first. Prescribe only when the diagnosis supports it. Walk away cleanly when it doesn't. The six frameworks below are the tools that make the diagnostic practice systematic - not art, not luck, not charisma.

Framework 02 · Discovery

The 30-minute diagnostic discovery.

A five-phase call structure that puts the prospect in the room - talking, surfacing pain, naming impact - with you running the diagnosis, not the pitch.

This is the doctor's intake. No prescription is written until the diagnosis is documented - and documenting it rigorously is what separates the closer from the pitchman.

Taught in the platform

Practiced in: the voice roleplay matrix - speak the structure against real DISC-matched buyer personas before you run it on a live prospect.

Framework 04 · Psychology

Three reframes that unblock reluctant founders.

Most founders who "aren't good at sales" are dealing with identity-role conflict: the builder identity clashes with the seller role. Negativity bias makes it worse. The remedy isn't willpower - it's a different mental frame. Three reframes to choose from, matched to your founder category.

Taught in the platform

Operationalized in: the 11-founder-category assessment maps you to the reframe that fits your profile. The coach uses your assigned frame as its operating tone - and avoids the phrases that trigger your category's resistance.

The Principle
Activity
not outcomes

Acquisition is an infinite game. Founders burn out when they tie self-worth to outcomes mostly outside their short-term control. The remedy: commit to activity for 30 days, then evaluate.

Framework 06 · Rhythm

The 30-day rhythm.

A structured first week that produces a usable output in the first 90 minutes, then a sustainable activity cadence for the remaining 23 days. Tracked against 5–7 KPIs split into activity, efficiency, and outcome metrics so you know where to adjust without over-reacting to a single bad day.
