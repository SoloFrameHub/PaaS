The Architecture of the Price

How $29.95/month actually works.

Full platform AI for the price of a midday coffee habit sounds like it shouldn't pencil out. It does - because of three engineering choices that absorb what competitors pass through as price hikes.

01
Tight Integration

One platform replaces a five-tool stack. You pay one vendor tax, not five.

Most founders building a GTM motion stitch together a course platform, a CRM, a coaching chatbot, a community forum, and a roleplay or enablement tool. Each one is $30–$100/month, each one has its own vendor markup, each one adds integration pain. The platform unifies all of it into a single runtime built to share state between layers - your ICP flows into your roleplay, your pipeline feeds your coach, your pod sees your wins as they happen.

You pay one vendor for what used to require five. The savings are architectural - they're baked in before the price sticker.

02
Self-Hosted Infrastructure

No Vercel markup. No abstracted-cloud tax. Every dollar saved goes into capability, not pricing.

Funded AI startups run on Vercel, Render, Supabase, Auth0 - abstracted cloud platforms that bill in seat-counts and request-minutes. Their pricing has to cover those rents. This runtime lives on a single commodity VPS for a small fraction of what abstracted-cloud equivalents cost, with the same functional capability. A second box will join it for redundancy when revenue justifies one.

The infrastructure delta isn't spent on features nobody asked for. It's absorbed into the subscription price - which is why the founder-comfortable number holds up at all.

03
AI Integrated with Cost Discipline

Premium models only where empathy matters. Cheaper models for everything else.

Per-task model routing: roughly 80% of AI calls run on inexpensive models optimized for structured work - ICP validation, quiz reflection, assessment routing, JSON extraction. The 20% where nuance actually matters - coaching conversations, voice roleplay persona realism, roleplay evaluation - run on premium models. Prompt caching where it earns its keep. Embedding reuse across surfaces. Shared inference across all 16 AI task types on the platform.

Most AI products don't bother with this engineering - they just raise the subscription price instead. Every monthly fine-tune cycle (when the GPU budget is there) will further compress per-call cost across every surface on the platform at once.

Those three choices let the subscription cover platform AI rather than passing it through to you as "BYOK every token." You bring your own keys only for third-party integrations you already subscribe to - your CRM, your email-finder, your messaging - no markup on top of what you already pay them.

What's NOT in the Price

Be skeptical of any tool that doesn't tell you what it isn't.

Integration subscription fees

You connect your own Attio / Pipedrive / Hunter / Notion / Brevo / WhatsApp Business accounts. Whatever you pay them is whatever you pay them. The platform routes through your credentials - no SaaS tax on services you already buy.

A sales team calling you

There isn't one. No SDR queue, no "book a demo" gauntlet, no quarterly check-in from an account exec trying to upsell you. Apply for beta; if it's a fit, you're in. If you want help, the context-aware coach handles it first and a human picks up when it can't.

An enterprise tier

The platform is built for solo founders and small GTM teams. There is no enterprise-SSO-audit-log-procurement-cycle tier. If you run a 50-person sales org with SOC-2 mandates, this isn't for you (yet). If you're a founder shipping alone or with a small team, everything here is what you need.

Pricing FAQ

Direct answers.

When does "introductory" end?

No specific end date. $29.95/month will stay intro-priced until the platform is earning enough to warrant a price change - and when that happens, existing subscribers stay on their rate through the following year. Fair warning, not surprise billing.

Is the AI included in the price?

Yes. The platform handles AI inference entirely - no keys to configure, no provider accounts to set up, no surprise bills. The subscription covers it. Per-task model routing (inexpensive models for structured work, premium models only where nuance matters) plus prompt caching keep the per-founder AI cost manageable without passing that complexity to you. Third-party integrations (CRM, email-finding, export, messaging) use your own API keys at vendor rates - no markup.

What integrations are available, and how do they work?

You connect your own Attio / Pipedrive / Hunter / Notion / Brevo / WhatsApp Business accounts via API key. Tokens are encrypted in your profile and used only to proxy calls on your behalf. The platform never stores your contact data, never resells it, and never includes it in any training corpus.

Is there a yearly discount?

Annual billing at $259.95/yr (~$21.66/mo) - roughly 28% off the introductory monthly rate. Available once you're out of beta. No multi-year lock-in.

Team pricing?

For 2–5-person GTM teams, each seat is the same $29.95/month during beta. Shared dashboards, per-role visibility on the pipeline, and pod assignment by DISC complementarity within the team. Full team-specific UX is on the Q2 roadmap.

Refund policy?

Cancel any time from your account. No cancellation fee, no "did you mean to cancel?" retention flows. If a first-month charge somehow lands on someone who didn't mean to subscribe, email and it'll be refunded same-day.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

You own your artifacts. ICP documents, positioning statements, email sequences, playbooks, roleplay transcripts - all exportable (Notion via OAuth, plus plain-text downloads). Cancellation purges personal data on a standard retention clock and you walk out with what you built.
