The Hidden Failure Mode

Vertical startups don't die on product.
They die on go-to-market.

70–80% of digital-health startups fail within 5 years — and the autopsies consistently cite pilot purgatory, 12-month sales cycles, and founder-seller mismatch. Not technical failure. Not clinical failure. Distribution.

The builder-seller gap

Technical founders build the product, then hit a psychological wall the moment they have to sell it. The skills, the identity, and the reps are different — and the clock is ticking on runway.

Course platforms are generic

Kajabi, Thinkific, Teachable ship horizontal tools. They don't know your vertical's psychology, compliance posture, or buyer — so the academy you build on them feels generic and converts like a $29 course.

Chat UIs aren't a product

A prompt window on top of someone else's model isn't defensible. Without retrieval pipelines, fine-tuned domain models, and integration into the daily workflow, the demo ships and then stalls.

sVTech's approach: a vertical-aware academy platform with distribution built in.

Each vertical gets a multi-tenant academy — branded, scoped, and wired into a shared platform of retrieval, fine-tune, and workflow primitives. The customer-acquisition engine isn't a separate product; it's part of what the tenant ships with.

Origin

A cookbook. An execution engine. And the platform underneath both.

The Solo Founder's Dilemma is a detailed cookbook of customer-acquisition frameworks for solo bootstrapped founders — ICP three-circle, MVQ qualification, the Prescription Frame, value anchoring, DISC buyer adaptation, and five playbooks for different starting points. Research-backed, with distinct recipes for B2B SaaS, coaches, and creators.

Solo GTM OS is the execution engine — software that runs the cookbook for the founder. Digital Wellness Academy applies the same platform to a different vertical with its own distribution problem, built with a psychiatric practice as design partner.

One codebase underneath all three. Node.js, PostgreSQL, retrieval-augmented generation, and fine-tune pipelines as the platform primitives — not a chat UI glued to a vendor.
