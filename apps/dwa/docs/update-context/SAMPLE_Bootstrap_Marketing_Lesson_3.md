# Lesson 3: TOFU/MOFU/BOFU - The Funnel Framework

## Introduction

Every customer goes through stages before buying: they become aware of their problem (Top of Funnel), consider solutions (Middle of Funnel), and make a decision (Bottom of Funnel). This TOFU/MOFU/BOFU framework helps you create the right content for each stage—whether you're selling software to marketing directors or coaching programs to entrepreneurs.

Most solo founders make one of two mistakes:
1. **All TOFU, no BOFU:** Creating educational content that attracts attention but never asks for the sale
2. **All BOFU, no TOFU:** Pitching to people who aren't ready, resulting in "I need to think about it"

This lesson teaches you how to map content across all three funnel stages so you're attracting the right prospects AND converting them when they're ready.

---

## The Three Funnel Stages

### TOFU: Top of Funnel (Awareness)

**Goal:** Help people recognize they have a problem
**Audience:** People who don't know they need a solution yet
**Mindset:** Research, education, problem identification

**What TOFU content does:**
- Defines the problem clearly
- Demonstrates expertise
- Builds initial trust
- Captures contact information

**What TOFU content does NOT do:**
- Pitch your solution directly
- Assume they're ready to buy
- Use heavy sales language

**Universal Principle:** TOFU content solves a problem ADJACENT to what you sell, not the core problem your product solves.

**Why?** If your product fully solved their problem in a blog post, they wouldn't need to buy. TOFU content creates awareness that a bigger problem exists.

---

### MOFU: Middle of Funnel (Consideration)

**Goal:** Help people evaluate potential solutions
**Audience:** People actively researching options
**Mindset:** Comparison, evaluation, learning how solutions work

**What MOFU content does:**
- Compares different approaches
- Educates on solution categories
- Demonstrates your methodology
- Qualifies fit

**What MOFU content does NOT do:**
- Hard-sell your specific product
- Bash competitors directly
- Ignore alternatives

**Universal Principle:** MOFU content positions your APPROACH as superior, not just your specific product/service.

**Example:**
- Bad MOFU: "Our CRM is better than Competitor X"
- Good MOFU: "Why lightweight CRMs work better for SMBs than enterprise platforms"

This educates on the category while indirectly positioning you.

---

### BOFU: Bottom of Funnel (Decision)

**Goal:** Help people choose YOU specifically
**Audience:** People ready to buy, comparing final options
**Mindset:** Risk assessment, ROI calculation, final validation

**What BOFU content does:**
- Proves results (case studies, testimonials)
- Overcomes final objections
- Demonstrates product/service directly
- Provides clear next steps

**What BOFU content does NOT do:**
- Educate on the category (they already know)
- Focus on awareness-level problems
- Hide pricing or avoid direct CTAs

**Universal Principle:** BOFU content removes friction and proves you can deliver.

## The Biggest Funnel Mistake: Wrong Content for Wrong Stage

### Mistake 1: BOFU Content for TOFU Audience

**What it looks like:**
- Someone finds your blog post via Google
- They're researching the problem category
- Your post immediately pitches your product/service

**Why it fails:**
They're not ready. You've skipped awareness and consideration.

**Example (B2B):**
- Bad: Blog post "How to Choose a CRM" that only talks about YOUR CRM
- Good: Blog post compares CRM categories, mentions yours as one option

**Example (Creator):**
- Bad: "How to Get Coaching Clients" that's entirely a program pitch
- Good: "How to Get Coaching Clients" with frameworks, THEN mentions program at end

---

### Mistake 2: TOFU Content for BOFU Audience

**What it looks like:**
- Someone books a demo/call (clearly BOFU)
- You spend the call educating on the category
- You never ask for the sale

**Why it fails:**
They already know they need a solution. They're comparing YOU to alternatives.

**Example (B2B):**
- Bad: Demo call that explains "why CRMs matter" for 20 minutes
- Good: Demo that shows YOUR CRM solving THEIR specific problem

**Example (Creator):**
- Bad: Enrollment call explaining "why coaching works"
- Good: Enrollment call showing how YOUR program solves THEIR situation

---

### Mistake 3: Creating Only One Funnel Stage

**All TOFU, No BOFU:**
- Attracts huge audience
- Nobody buys
- Example: 10,000 blog readers, 0 customers

**All BOFU, No TOFU:**
- Pitches constantly
- Small audience
- Example: Great sales page, no traffic

**The fix:** Create content for ALL stages, then connect them

## Funnel Flow: How Content Connects

### The Ideal Flow (Universal)

**Step 1: TOFU → Email Capture**
- Prospect finds blog post via search/social
- Post solves immediate problem
- CTA: "Download the full template" (email required)

**Step 2: Email Nurture → MOFU Content**
- Email sequence delivers value
- Links to comparison guides, frameworks, case studies
- Builds confidence in your methodology

**Step 3: MOFU → BOFU Transition**
- Clear CTA: "Ready to see how this works for you?"
- Links to demo booking, consultation, or sales page
- Qualified prospects self-select into BOFU

**Step 4: BOFU → Conversion**
- Demo/call/sales page answers final questions
- Removes friction (pricing clarity, guarantee, timeline)
- Clear next step: buy, sign, enroll

## Funnel Metrics: What to Measure

Track conversion at each stage to identify leaks:

### B2B SaaS Funnel Metrics

| Stage | Metric | Good Benchmark |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **TOFU** | Blog traffic → email conversion | 2-5% |
| **MOFU** | Email subscriber → demo request | 3-8% |
| **BOFU** | Demo → trial signup | 30-50% |
| **BOFU** | Trial → paid customer | 15-25% |

**Example:** 10,000 blog visitors → 300 emails → 15 demos → 5 trials → 1 customer = 0.01% end-to-end

**Where to optimize:** If demo → trial is 10%, fix the demo. If email → demo is 1%, fix MOFU content.

---

### Creator/Coach Funnel Metrics

| Stage | Metric | Good Benchmark |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **TOFU** | Social/blog → email conversion | 1-3% |
| **MOFU** | Email subscriber → application/call | 2-5% |
| **BOFU** | Call → enrollment | 20-40% |
| **BOFU** | Waitlist → enrollment (launch) | 10-20% |

**Example:** 5,000 Instagram followers → 150 emails → 5 applications → 2 enrollments = 0.04% end-to-end

**Where to optimize:** If call → enrollment is 10%, fix your offer/sales process. If email → application is 0.5%, fix MOFU content.

## Content Distribution by Stage (The 70/20/10 Rule)

**Recommendation for Solo Founders:**

- **70% TOFU:** Consistent educational content that attracts and builds audience
- **20% MOFU:** Frameworks, comparisons, and authority content
- **10% BOFU:** Direct sales content (demos, testimonials, pricing)

**Why this ratio?**
- TOFU content compounds (evergreen, SEO-friendly)
- MOFU content converts warm audience
- BOFU content closes ready buyers

**Most solo founders do the opposite:**
- 10% TOFU (inconsistent blogging)
- 20% MOFU (occasional case study)
- 70% BOFU (constant pitching)

**Result:** Small audience, high pressure, low conversion

## Practice Exercise: Map Your Content Funnel

**Step 1: Audit Your Current Content**
List everything you've created in the last 90 days. Categorize:
- TOFU: Awareness-stage content
- MOFU: Consideration-stage content
- BOFU: Decision-stage content

**Question:** What's your current distribution? 70/20/10? Or inverted?

---

**Step 2: Identify Gaps**
Look at your funnel metrics:
- Lots of traffic, few emails? → Need better TOFU lead magnets
- Lots of emails, few demos/calls? → Need better MOFU content
- Lots of demos/calls, few closes? → Need better BOFU content (or sales skills)

---

**Step 3: Create Missing Content**
Choose ONE gap and create content to fill it:

**If TOFU gap:**
- Create blog post or social content addressing common question
- Add clear lead magnet CTA

**If MOFU gap:**
- Create comparison guide or framework PDF
- Email to your list with CTA to deeper resource

**If BOFU gap:**
- Create customer success story or product walkthrough
- Make it easy to book demo/call or see pricing

## Common Questions

**Q: How long should people stay in each stage?**
**A (B2B):** TOFU = months/years, MOFU = weeks/months, BOFU = days/weeks
**A (Creator):** TOFU = weeks/months, MOFU = days/weeks, BOFU = hours/days

**Q: Can someone skip stages?**
**A:** Yes. Some prospects enter at MOFU (already know the category) or BOFU (direct referral). The funnel serves those who need full education.

**Q: Should every piece of content have a CTA?**
**A:** Yes, but match the CTA to the stage. TOFU → email signup. MOFU → deeper resource. BOFU → demo/purchase.

## Summary

**Key Takeaways:**

1. **TOFU (Awareness)** attracts prospects by solving adjacent problems → Goal: Email capture
2. **MOFU (Consideration)** educates on solutions and builds methodology confidence → Goal: Demo/call booking
3. **BOFU (Decision)** proves you can deliver and removes friction → Goal: Conversion
4. **Wrong content for wrong stage** is the #1 funnel mistake solo founders make
5. **70/20/10 distribution** (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU) builds sustainable pipeline
6. **Track metrics at each stage** to identify and fix leaks
