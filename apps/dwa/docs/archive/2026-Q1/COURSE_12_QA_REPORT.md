### Sample Excellence (Lesson 2, Question 5):

> **Question:** "When is 'serving others' NOT the right recommendation and what should be prioritized instead?"
>
> **Correct Answer:** "When you're already experiencing caregiver overload, moral injury, severe perfectionism, or depletion—priority should be respite, boundaries, and professional support"
>
> **Explanation:** "For people already in caregiving overload (63% of caregivers experience severe burden), those with moral injury from helping in broken systems, those struggling with perfectionism and over-responsibility, or anyone already depleted, the message is NOT 'serve more.' Adding helping activities when you're already over-giving can worsen mental health. The priority in these cases is: finding respite, developing boundaries, reducing current burden, and seeking professional support for perfectionism or moral injury. This is not selfish—it's sustainable and evidence-based."

This demonstrates the quiz quality: testing nuanced understanding, integrating evidence (63% statistic), and reinforcing critical safety principles.
