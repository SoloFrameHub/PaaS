# 5k in gpu credits are meaningless

Completely fair — let me be straight about that.

The \$5K honourable mention isn't the goal. Here's how to recalibrate the target realistically:

**What actually matters from this award:**

The **\$100K in GPU credits** is meaningful — that funds real model fine-tuning on your anonymized assessment dataset. But the more valuable prizes are the **VC introductions** and the **AstraZeneca/NVIDIA partner network access** that come from reaching the finalist stage. For a platform in the licensing phase, one warm introduction to a behavioral health-focused VC from that judge panel is worth more than any credit amount.[^1]

**The honest path to finalist:**

The gap between the platform today and last year's winners is one thing: **a quantified AI outcome**. MetaSight won with 500K participants and a screening accuracy number. You need one equivalent metric — even a small one — before April 30. The strongest option in your stack right now is:[^2]

- Train a distress classifier on publicly available mental health text datasets (CLPsych, Reddit mental health corpus — both open source)
- Validate it against PHQ-9/GAD-7 clinical thresholds
- Report a real AUC or F1 score

That's a 1–2 week build. It gives you a defensible "our safety detection model achieves X% sensitivity at the clinical suicidality threshold" — which is the kind of specific, measurable AI claim that gets past the first review round.[^3]

**Without that, the realistic ceiling is semifinalist** — which still gets you the network. With it, you're genuinely competing for the top prize.
