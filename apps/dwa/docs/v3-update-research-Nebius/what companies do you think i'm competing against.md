# what companies do you think i'm competing against

Here's the honest competitive picture for the Digital Health category specifically.

## Your Direct Competition

The Digital Health category will likely attract these types of players — and here's how you stack up against each:

**The dangerous ones:**

- **Slingshot AI** — the 2025 runner-up, now with a \$53M Series A, building autonomous "agentic" therapeutic AI. They have more funding, more AI depth, and a prior finalist relationship with Nebius judges. This is probably your toughest direct competitor in the mental health space.[^1][^2]
- **Eleos Health** — real-time AI co-pilot for therapists, surfaces patient sentiment and CBT adherence signals during sessions. Already positioned as "analytics infrastructure" for practices — directly overlapping with your provider portal pitch.[^1]
- **Wysa / Woebot** — both clinically validated AI mental health platforms with multi-year datasets and published clinical studies. Woebot is FDA-listed. They won't apply to Nebius (too big/mature), but smaller clones of their model will.[^1]
- **Meru Health** — structured therapist-supported digital programs for anxiety, depression, and burnout with engagement tracking AI. Very similar positioning to yours.[^3]

**Where you have an edge over all of them:**

None of the above are **multi-tenant licensed infrastructure for independent psychiatric practices**. They're all consumer-facing or employer-facing tools. Your specific angle — a licensed clinical platform that practices white-label and deploy to their own patients, with practice-isolated HIPAA compliance and population-level assessment data generation — is genuinely differentiated from everything in that field.[^4]

The companies you're not competing against directly are the BioPharma and genomics applicants (Ataraxis-type cancer prediction platforms, drug discovery AI) — those are a completely different category and the judges evaluate them separately.[^5]

**Bottom line:** You're not competing against Slingshot AI's \$53M budget. You're competing to be the most credible, clearly differentiated *infrastructure play* in the Digital Health category — which is a lane none of the well-funded consumer apps are occupying.
