## Module 1: Foundation (Lessons 1-4)

### Lesson 1: What Boundaries Actually Are — and Are Not
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand boundaries as evidence-based constructs (assertiveness, differentiation of self, interpersonal effectiveness)
- Distinguish healthy boundaries from isolating walls and from cultural/individualistic assumptions
- Recognize what research supports vs. popular "boundaries" overstatements

**Evidence Grade:** STRONG for assertiveness; MODERATE-STRONG for differentiation of self  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 1 (defining boundaries), Section 6 (accurate vs. overstatement), three communication styles

---

### Lesson 2: Types of Boundaries — Mapping Your Personal Territory
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the four boundary dimensions: physical, emotional, mental, and time/energy
- Assess your current boundary health across dimensions
- Understand how boundary types interact and reinforce each other

**Evidence Grade:** EMERGING for boundary typology; STRONG for underlying constructs  
**Key Content:** Personal boundaries as self-regulation (research brief Section 1), boundary assessment framework

---

### Lesson 3: Identifying Boundary Violations — Warning Signs
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize patterns of boundary violations in relationships
- Distinguish boundary violations from disagreement or conflict
- Identify your personal red flags and early warning signs

**Evidence Grade:** EMERGING for violation typology; MODERATE for impact on wellbeing  
**Key Content:** Boundary violations → emotional exhaustion link (research brief), self-assessment tools

---

### Lesson 4: The Psychology of People-Pleasing
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the psychological roots of approval-seeking and people-pleasing
- Recognize how people-pleasing differs from prosocial behavior and empathy
- Identify when people-pleasing becomes maladaptive (resentment, burnout, loss of self)

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (related to assertiveness deficits, low self-esteem, neuroticism)  
**Key Content:** Guilt about limit-setting (research brief Section 3.3), fawning as trauma response (Section 4.5)

---

## Module 2: Core Skills (Lessons 5-8)

### Lesson 5: Know Your Values — The Foundation of Authentic Limits
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Clarify your personal values using ACT-based values identification exercises
- Link values to specific boundary decisions (values-based vs. fear/guilt-driven limits)
- Practice "I value/need" language over "I have a boundary" language

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE-STRONG (ACT psychological flexibility framework)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3.2 (values-based communication), ACT values clarification, motivational sustainability

---

### Lesson 6: The Skill of Saying No
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Master assertive refusal skills using DBT DEAR MAN framework
- Distinguish assertive, aggressive, and passive refusal styles
- Practice clear, direct, value-grounded "no" statements

**Evidence Grade:** STRONG (assertiveness training, internet-based RCTs, ES 0.95–1.73)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3.1 (saying no/limit-setting), Hagberg et al. 2023 RCT, DEAR MAN operationalization

---

### Lesson 7: Communicating Your Needs — I-Statements and Requests
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Use "I" statements to express needs without blame or defensiveness
- Make specific, actionable requests (not vague demands)
- Practice active listening as a boundary-sustaining skill

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (interpersonal effectiveness, NVC validated scale)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3 (core skills), DBT interpersonal effectiveness, NVC principles adapted

---

### Lesson 8: Guilt Tolerance — The Emotional Obstacle
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the sources of guilt about limit-setting (internalized norms, fear of rejection)
- Apply DBT distress tolerance skills to guilt (half-smile, willing hands, radical acceptance)
- Practice self-compassion for your own need-expression

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (DBT distress tolerance, self-compassion framework)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3.3 (guilt tolerance), Neff's self-compassion, trauma-informed caveats

---

## Module 3: Specific Contexts (Lessons 9-16)

### Lesson 9: Digital Boundaries — Connected World Peace
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Set informational, social, and time boundaries with technology
- Manage digital intrusion, notification overload, and online availability expectations
- Apply boundary-setting to social media, email, and messaging

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (workplace boundary theory applied to digital context)  
**Key Content:** Work-family boundary permeability, digital intrusion, intentional vs. passive use

---

### Lesson 10: Workplace Boundaries — Professional Limits
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Set role boundaries and prevent scope creep at work
- Communicate limits with supervisors and colleagues assertively
- Recognize signs of overcommitment, compassion fatigue, and burnout

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE-STRONG (role boundary theory, burnout prevention, APA workplace guidance)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 4.2 (work relationships), work-family boundaries, burnout as WHO ICD-11 syndrome

---

### Lesson 11: Family Dynamics — Differentiation and Connectedness
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand Bowen's differentiation of self: connection without fusion, autonomy without cutoff
- Recognize triangulation and I-position responses vs. emotional reactivity
- Navigate family enmeshment and cultural context of family interdependence

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE-STRONG (Bowen framework: 295 studies, Calatrava et al. 2022)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 4.1 (family relationships), DoS predictor evidence, cultural variability note

---

### Lesson 12: Intimate Relationship Boundaries
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Maintain self while staying emotionally connected in romantic relationships
- Understand attachment patterns and how they predict boundary-setting difficulty
- Apply communication skills to relationship contexts (satisfaction vs. process improvement)

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (communication skills improve process, not always satisfaction longitudinally)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 4.3 (romantic relationships), Lavner et al. communication-satisfaction complexity, attachment theory

---

### Lesson 13: Parenting and Boundaries
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Set developmental boundaries with children (age-appropriate autonomy and limits)
- Model healthy boundary-setting for children's learning
- Balance parental authority with child autonomy needs

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (parenting literature, self-determination theory applied to parenting)  
**Key Content:** Teaching limits as life skills, authoritative parenting, autonomy support

---

### Lesson 14: Privacy, Solitude, and Emotional Presence
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish privacy (legitimate need) from emotional withdrawal (stonewalling, cutoff)
- Understand autonomy as a basic psychological need (SDT)
- Recognize when alone time supports wellbeing vs. when it signals avoidance

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (privacy needs research, SDT autonomy, Gottman stonewalling evidence)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3.6 (privacy/autonomy/withdrawal), Westin's privacy framework, volitional solitude

---

### Lesson 15: Recognizing Coercive and Manipulative Patterns ⚠️ SAFETY CRITICAL
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify coercive control patterns (monitoring, isolation, gaslighting, financial control, DARVO)
- Distinguish coercive control from conflict or poor communication
- Understand when assertiveness training is insufficient and potentially dangerous

**Evidence Grade:** EMERGING-MODERATE (coercive control research expanding; policy-relevant)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 3.5 (coercive control) + Section 4.6 (abusive relationships), AIFS 2024 rapid review, safety escalation

---

### Lesson 16: Caring Without Losing Yourself — Caregiving Boundaries
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize compassion fatigue and burnout in caregiving contexts
- Distinguish empathy from over-identification (self-oriented vs. witnessing)
- Apply boundary-setting to protect both caregiver and care recipient wellbeing

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (compassion fatigue well-studied; boundary-specific intervention limited)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 4.4 (caregiving), compassion fatigue vs. burnout distinctions, female gender risk factor

---

## Module 4: Advanced & Integration (Lessons 17-20)

### Lesson 17: Boundaries with Difficult People
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply advanced assertiveness strategies with high-conflict individuals
- Use fogging, broken record, and negative assertion techniques
- Recognize when relationship patterns are structurally unsustainable

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (assertiveness training applied to difficult contexts)  
**Key Content:** Advanced assertiveness techniques, when to disengage, recognizing structural incompatibility

---

### Lesson 18: Boundary Repair and Maintenance
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Repair crossed boundaries with repair attempts and communication
- Maintain boundaries over time (consistency, follow-through, self-monitoring)
- Adjust boundaries as contexts and relationships evolve

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (relationship repair research, Gottman repair attempts)  
**Key Content:** Repair attempt framework, maintenance strategies, boundary flexibility

---

### Lesson 19: When Limits Feel Impossible — Trauma, Attachment, and Patterns ⚠️ SAFETY CRITICAL
**Duration:** 30 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand how complex trauma affects relational patterns and boundary-setting
- Recognize boundary difficulties as trauma symptoms, not character flaws
- Identify when professional support is decisive (not just helpful)

**Evidence Grade:** CLINICALLY CRITICAL (C-PTSD, attachment, trauma processing)  
**Key Content:** Research brief Section 4.5 (trauma histories), window of tolerance framework, fawning as traumatic adaptation, therapy as necessary

---

### Lesson 20: Living with Boundaries — Your Personal Plan Builder
**Duration:** 35 min  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Synthesize all prior lessons into a personalized boundary action plan
- Identify top 3 boundary domains for development (work, family, relationships, digital, etc.)
- Build sustainable practices with accountability and barrier planning

**Evidence Grade:** STRONG for plan-building as behavior change support  
**Key Content:** 7-step plan framework (Why → Baseline → Goal → Strategy → Barriers → Accountability → 4-Week Plan), synthesis exercises
