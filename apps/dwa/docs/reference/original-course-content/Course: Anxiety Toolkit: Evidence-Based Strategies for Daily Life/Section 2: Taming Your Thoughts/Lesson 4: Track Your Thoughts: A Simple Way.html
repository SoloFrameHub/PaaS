Lesson 4: Your Thought Record

A practical tool to bring all your new skills together.

Putting Knowledge Into Practice

A note from Autumn: "In the past few lessons, we've learned to identify the Thought-Feeling-Action cycle and spot common Thinking Traps. Now, it's time to use those skills in a structured way. A Thought Record isn't about judgment; it's a powerful tool for building awareness and proving to yourself that you can change how you feel by shifting how you think."

Complete all steps below, then submit your completed thought record.

1

Describe a recent situation that triggered an anxious or negative feeling. Be specific: Who, what, where, when?

Tip: Focus on facts rather than interpretations. What actually happened?
2

What emotions did you feel? Rate the intensity of your strongest emotion.

Mild Intense
5/10
3

What was the first thought that popped into your head? Write it down exactly as it occurred.

Tip: Write the thought as if it's speaking to you directly. Use "I" statements.
4

Look at your automatic thought. Which traps might apply here? (Select all that apply)

Catastrophizing
Expecting the worst possible outcome
Mind Reading
Assuming you know what others are thinking
All-or-Nothing
Seeing things in black and white
Emotional Reasoning
"I feel it, so it must be true"
"Should" Statements
Rigid expectations about how things "should" be
Labeling
Defining yourself or others with negative labels
5

Challenge that automatic thought. What's a more realistic, balanced, or compassionate way to see this?

Tip: Look for evidence that supports AND contradicts your automatic thought. What's a more balanced perspective?
🎉

Excellent Work!

You've successfully completed a Thought Record. This is one of the most powerful skills in managing anxiety. Each time you do this, you weaken the power of automatic negative thoughts and strengthen your ability to find balance.

Your balanced thought is now your new mental tool. Try to recall it when similar situations arise.

Keep this practice up. You're building a new mental muscle!
