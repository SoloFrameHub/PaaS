Lesson 2: Spotting "Thinking Traps"

Your field guide to the automatic thoughts that fuel anxiety.

Your Brain's Mental Shortcuts

Anxious thoughts often aren't random; they follow predictable patterns. Think of them like mental shortcuts your brain takes that lead you down a path of worry. We call these **Thinking Traps**. They are faulty filters that distort reality. Learning to spot them is like taking off foggy glasses—suddenly, you can see things more clearly.

Part 1: The Field Guide

Click on each card to flip it over and learn about a common thinking trap. Familiarize yourself with them before heading to the Practice Arena.

🌪️

Catastrophizing

Catastrophizing

Jumping to the absolute worst-case scenario, even when it's highly unlikely.

"My heart is beating fast... what if I'm having a heart attack?"

⚫⚪

All-or-Nothing Thinking

All-or-Nothing Thinking

Seeing things in black-and-white. If it's not perfect, it's a total failure.

"I ate one piece of cake. My diet is ruined, so I've failed."

🔮

Mind Reading

Mind Reading

Believing you know what others are thinking about you, without any real evidence.

"They didn't text back right away. They must be angry with me."

💔

Emotional Reasoning

Emotional Reasoning

Assuming that because you *feel* something, it must be true. Letting feelings dictate reality.

"I feel overwhelmed, therefore I must be incompetent and unable to handle this."

📜

"Should" Statements

"Should" Statements

Holding yourself to a strict, invisible rulebook. "Shoulds" often lead to guilt and frustration.

"I should be able to do this all by myself without asking for help."

🏷️

Labeling

Labeling

Assigning a negative, global label to yourself or others based on a single event.

"I made a mistake on that project. I am such a failure."
