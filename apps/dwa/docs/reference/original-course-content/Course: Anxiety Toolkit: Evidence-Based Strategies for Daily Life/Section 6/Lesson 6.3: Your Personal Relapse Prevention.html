Lesson 6.3: Your Personal Relapse Prevention Plan

Creating a compassionate action plan for tough days.

Setbacks Are Part of the Journey

Managing anxiety is not a linear path. There will be good days and hard days. A "relapse" or setback doesn't mean you've failed; it means you're human. Having a plan in place for these moments removes the fear and uncertainty. It turns a potential crisis into a planned-for event.

The Goal: A Fire Escape Plan for Your Mind

You wouldn't wait until a fire starts to figure out where the exits are. This plan is your fire escape plan. You create it when you are calm so you can use it when you are stressed. It should be simple, clear, and actionable.

Part 1: My Early Warning Signs

What are the first subtle signs that your anxiety is starting to ramp up? Knowing these allows you to intervene early, before the storm hits.

Part 2: My Go-To Toolkit

When you notice your warning signs, what are your top 3-5 most effective tools from this course? Be specific.

Part 3: My Support System

Who are the safe people you can reach out to for support? List their names and how they can best help you.

Your Emergency Skill

What is the **one skill** that works best when you are in a full-blown crisis? For many, this is the **Temperature** part of the TIPP skill (holding cold water on your face). Know what your number one emergency skill is and have it ready.

Keep Your Plan Accessible

This plan is useless if you can't find it when you need it. Consider writing it on a notecard and putting it in your wallet, or saving it as a note on your phone's home screen. Make it easy to find when you're feeling overwhelmed.

You Are Prepared

Congratulations. By creating this plan, you have sent a powerful message to your brain: "Even on my hardest days, I have a plan. I know what to do. I am capable." This sense of preparedness is a powerful antidote to the helplessness that anxiety can create.
