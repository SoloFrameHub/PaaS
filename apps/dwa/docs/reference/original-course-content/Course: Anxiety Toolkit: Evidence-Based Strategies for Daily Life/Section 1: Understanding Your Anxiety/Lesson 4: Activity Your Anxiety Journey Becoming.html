Your Anxiety Journey: Becoming a Curious Observer

A note from David Glenn:

"Hello again. This is one of the most important exercises in this entire course. For so long, you've likely been in the anxiety. Now, we're going to take a step back and simply look at it, like a curious scientist observing a phenomenon."

"There are absolutely no right or wrong answers here. This is about building awareness, not about getting a perfect score. Please be gentle and honest with yourself."

"Take a few deep breaths, and when you're ready, please reflect on the questions below. Aim for just a few sentences for each."

Think back to the most recent time you felt a significant wave of anxiety. Describe what was happening right before and during that moment.

What did you feel in your body? Using the examples from the lessons (or your own words), list the top 2-3 sensations you noticed.

What was your mind telling you? Try to recall any 'what if...' thoughts, worries, or self-critical statements that were present.

What did you do in response to the anxiety? Did you avoid something, distract yourself, push through, or something else?

"Thank you for sharing. Simply writing this down is a huge step in taking back control. You are separating yourself from the anxiety and seeing it for what it is: a pattern of feelings, thoughts, and urges. We will use this information in later lessons. Great work."

A Gentle Reminder: Safety First
