Think of anxiety like a car alarm. A good car alarm goes off when someone's breaking in. But what if your car alarm went off every time a leaf touched it?

Normal vs. Problem Anxiety

Normal Anxiety

Everyone gets anxious sometimes. That's normal. Job interviews, medical tests, first dates - your brain should be a little alert.

Problem Anxiety is different:

Shows up when you're actually safe

Way too intense for the situation

Sticks around too long

Makes you avoid things that matter

The good news? This is fixable. You just need the right tools.

Important: When to Seek Immediate Help

If you're in immediate danger or having thoughts of self-harm, please reach out to these resources:

  • Emergency Services: 911 (or your local emergency number)
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 (24/7 treatment referral service)

When to seek immediate professional help:

  • Thoughts of harming yourself or others
  • Feeling completely unable to function in daily life
  • Panic attacks that feel life-threatening
  • Using substances to cope with anxiety
  • Anxiety that's getting worse despite using these tools

Remember, this course provides education, not direct therapy. If you need immediate support, please use the resources above.
