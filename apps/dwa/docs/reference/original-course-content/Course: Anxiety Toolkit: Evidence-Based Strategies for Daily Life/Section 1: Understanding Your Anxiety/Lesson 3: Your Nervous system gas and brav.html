Your Nervous System

The Gas & The Brake

A note from David Glenn:

"In the last lesson, we learned that anxiety is your body's alarm system. Now, let's look at the two key parts of that system that control how you feel: the 'gas pedal' that revs you up and the 'brake pedal' that cools you down. Understanding how they work is the key to learning how to influence them."

Meet Your 'Gas Pedal' 🚗

The Sympathetic Nervous System

This is the part of your nervous system that screams 'GO!' when your brain senses a threat. It floods your body with adrenaline and cortisol to prepare you for 'fight or flight.' It's all about mobilization and survival.

Think of it as your internal fire alarm: It's designed to keep you safe, but sometimes it goes off when you're just making toast!

Common 'Gas Pedal' Sensations:

Click each one to see why it happens.

  • Racing Heart ❤️
  • Fast Breathing 💨
  • Tense Muscles 💪
  • Sweating 💦
  • Butterflies in Stomach 🦋

Meet Your 'Brake Pedal' 🛑

The Parasympathetic Nervous System

This is the part of your system that says, 'Okay, crisis over. You can relax now.' It's the 'rest and digest' system that actively calms your body down, conserves energy, and helps you recover.

Think of it as your internal spa manager: It's constantly working to restore balance and help you feel calm and centered.

Common 'Brake Pedal' Effects:

Click each one to see how it helps.

  • Slower Heartbeat 💙
  • Deeper Breathing 🌬️
  • Relaxed Muscles 😌
  • Digestion Resumes 🍎
  • Clear Thinking 🧠

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • ✅ Your nervous system has two main parts: gas (sympathetic) and brake (parasympathetic)
  • ✅ Physical symptoms of anxiety are normal responses from your gas pedal
  • ✅ You have the power to activate your brake pedal through intentional actions
  • ✅ The goal isn't to eliminate stress, but to get better at returning to balance
