Lesson 3.3: Riding the Wave of Emotion

Building strength by learning to surf your feelings.

What if You Didn't Have to Fight Your Feelings?

Our natural instinct when we feel an intense emotion is to fight it, suppress it, or run from it. But this often makes the emotion stronger, like trying to hold a beach ball underwater. This lesson introduces a radical new idea: what if you could just let the emotion exist, knowing it will pass on its own?

A note from Phillip: "Emotions are like ocean waves. They build in intensity, they peak, and then they always, always recede. The skill of 'Riding the Wave' is about learning to stay on your surfboard and trust the process, rather than getting tumbled in the surf."

How to "Surf" Your Emotions

1. Notice & Name

Acknowledge the feeling without judgment. Simply say to yourself, "This is anxiety," or "I am feeling a wave of anger." Naming it helps you separate from it.

2. Let Go of the Story

Detach from the thoughts fueling the emotion. Let them float by like clouds. Your only job right now is to notice the physical sensations in your body.

3. Stay in the Present

Observe the feeling with curiosity. Where is it in your body? Is it hot or cold? Does it vibrate or ache? Be a scientist of your own sensation, knowing it is temporary.

4. Breathe Through It

Use slow, paced breathing as your anchor. Breathe into the physical sensation. This reminds your body that you are safe even while the emotion is present.
