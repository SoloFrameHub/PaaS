Lesson 3.2: Ground Yourself: Simple Ways to Feel Present

An anchor for your mind in an emotional storm.

The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Skill

When your mind is spinning with anxious thoughts, it's often focused on the past ("what I did wrong") or the future ("what might go wrong"). Grounding is the skill of pulling your attention back to the present moment—the only place you are truly safe. This exercise uses your five senses to anchor you in the here and now.

A note from Phillip: "Anxiety cannot easily survive in the present moment. This skill is incredibly effective because it's impossible to focus on your five senses and your anxious thoughts at the exact same time. You are intentionally shifting your brain's focus."

Ready to try it? We'll walk you through it step-by-step.

5️⃣ Acknowledge 5 things you can SEE

Look around you right now. No judgment, just notice. List five things you see in your immediate environment. They can be big or small.

4️⃣ Acknowledge 4 things you can FEEL

Turn your attention to the sensation of touch. What are four things you can physically feel right now?

3️⃣ Acknowledge 3 things you can HEAR

Listen carefully. Tune in to the sounds around you, even the subtle ones you might usually ignore.

2️⃣ Acknowledge 2 things you can SMELL

Take a gentle breath in through your nose. What scents can you detect in the air right now?

1️⃣ Acknowledge 1 thing you can TASTE

Focus on your sense of taste. This could be the lingering taste of your last meal, or simply the neutral taste of your own mouth.

Excellent Work.

Take a deep breath. Notice how you feel right now. By engaging your senses, you've successfully anchored yourself in the present moment. This is a skill you can use anywhere, anytime you feel your mind starting to spin. You are in control.
