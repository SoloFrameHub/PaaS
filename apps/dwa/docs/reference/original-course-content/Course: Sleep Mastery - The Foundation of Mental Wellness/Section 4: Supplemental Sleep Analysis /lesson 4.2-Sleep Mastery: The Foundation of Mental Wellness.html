Lesson 4.2: When Sleep Issues Require Professional Help

📹 Video Duration: 10 minutes | 📚 Reading: 6 minutes

Red Flags Requiring Professional Evaluation

While the techniques in this course help with general sleep hygiene, certain symptoms indicate the need for professional sleep medicine evaluation.

Immediate Professional Consultation Needed:

  • Sleep Apnea Symptoms: Loud snoring, gasping, witnessed breathing interruptions
  • Chronic Insomnia: 3+ months of difficulty falling or staying asleep despite good sleep hygiene
  • Excessive Daytime Sleepiness: Falling asleep during activities, work, or driving
  • Parasomnia Behaviors: Sleep walking, sleep talking, night terrors in adults
  • Restless Leg Syndrome: Irresistible urge to move legs, especially at bedtime
  • Shift Work Sleep Disorder: Persistent sleep problems related to work schedule

The Sleep Study Process

Understanding what happens during professional sleep evaluation can reduce anxiety about seeking help.

  • Initial consultation and sleep history review
  • Sleep diary analysis (2-4 weeks of data)
  • Polysomnography (overnight sleep study) when indicated
  • Home sleep apnea testing options
  • Treatment planning and follow-up care
