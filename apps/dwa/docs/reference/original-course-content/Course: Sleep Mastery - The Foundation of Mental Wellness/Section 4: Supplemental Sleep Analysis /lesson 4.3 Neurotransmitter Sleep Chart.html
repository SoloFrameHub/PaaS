⚡ Norepinephrine (Noradrenaline)
Primary FunctionAlertness, stress response, attention
Sleep RoleMust significantly decrease for sleep onset
Peak LevelsDuring stress, exercise, or alertness demands
Sleep ImpactElevated levels prevent deep sleep and REM
Reset ProcessSystems reset during sleep, especially deep stages
Deficiency SignsLow energy, poor focus, difficulty with stress
Medication EffectsSNRIs can affect sleep quality and timing
Optimization TipsStress management, evening relaxation, cool environment
🌙 Melatonin
Primary FunctionSleep timing, circadian rhythm regulation
Sleep RoleSignals body that it's time for sleep
Peak Levels2-3 hours before natural bedtime
Sleep ImpactPromotes sleepiness; timing more important than dose
Reset ProcessProduction cycle resets with light exposure
Deficiency SignsDelayed sleep onset, jet lag sensitivity
Medication EffectsSupplements can help but timing is crucial
Optimization TipsDark evening environment, consistent schedule

📊 Daily Neurotransmitter Cycle

Morning (6 AM - 12 PM)

  • Cortisol peaks for awakening
  • Dopamine rises for motivation
  • Norepinephrine increases for alertness
  • Melatonin suppressed by light
  • GABA stable, moderate levels

Afternoon (12 PM - 6 PM)

  • Cortisol gradual decline
  • Serotonin peaks for mood stability
  • Dopamine maintained for focus
  • Norepinephrine stable
  • Melatonin remains suppressed

Evening (6 PM - 12 AM)

  • Cortisol significant decline
  • Dopamine decreases for wind-down
  • Norepinephrine must drop for sleep
  • Melatonin begins rising
  • GABA activity increases

Night (12 AM - 6 AM)

  • Cortisol lowest levels
  • Dopamine minimal activity
  • Norepinephrine very low except REM
  • Melatonin peak levels
  • GABA highest activity

🏥 Clinical Applications

Medication Timing Considerations

Morning Medications (6 AM - 10 AM)

  • Stimulants (ADHD medications)
  • Activating antidepressants (some SSRIs)
  • Medications that can interfere with sleep

Evening Medications (6 PM - 9 PM)

  • Sedating antidepressants (trazodone, mirtazapine)
  • Anti-anxiety medications (as prescribed)
  • Sleep aids (melatonin, prescribed sleep medications)

Avoid Late Evening

  • Caffeine (affects adenosine)
  • Stimulating medications
  • High-dose B vitamins
  • Tyrosine supplements

🔬 Sleep Stage Neurotransmitter Activity

NREM Stage 1 (Light Sleep)

  • Declining: Norepinephrine, dopamine
  • Stable: GABA activity
  • Beginning: Serotonin regulation

NREM Stage 2 (Deeper Sleep)

  • Low: Norepinephrine, dopamine
  • Active: GABA systems
  • Transitioning: Serotonin patterns

NREM Stage 3 (Deep Sleep)

  • Minimal: Norepinephrine, dopamine
  • Peak: GABA activity
  • Restorative: All neurotransmitter systems

REM Sleep

  • Increased: Acetylcholine (not covered but important)
  • Variable: Norepinephrine (brief surges)
  • Processing: Emotional memories (serotonin)

🛠️ Optimization Strategies by Neurotransmitter

To Support Serotonin

  • Diet: Turkey, eggs, cheese, salmon, tofu
  • Light: Morning sunlight exposure
  • Exercise: Regular moderate activity
  • Timing: Protein breakfast, carbs in evening

To Balance Dopamine

  • Diet: Protein-rich breakfast, limit sugar
  • Activity: Achievement-based tasks in morning
  • Environment: Minimize evening stimulation
  • Timing: Save rewarding activities for daytime

To Enhance GABA

  • Diet: Magnesium-rich foods, herbal teas
  • Activities: Yoga, meditation, deep breathing
  • Environment: Calm, quiet evening space
  • Timing: Progressive relaxation before bed

To Manage Norepinephrine

  • Stress: Evening stress reduction techniques
  • Environment: Cool, dark bedroom
  • Activities: Gentle stretching, reading
  • Timing: Avoid stimulating content before bed

⚠️ Warning Signs of Imbalance

Seek Professional Help If:

  • Sleep medications losing effectiveness
  • New psychiatric symptoms with sleep changes
  • Extreme daytime fatigue despite adequate sleep time
  • Sleep disturbances after medication changes
  • Persistent sleep issues after optimizing hygiene

Emergency Symptoms:

  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Severe mood swings
  • Psychotic symptoms
  • Substance abuse to manage sleep

🧠 Serotonin (5-HT)
Primary FunctionMood regulation, impulse control, sleep-wake cycle
Sleep RolePromotes NREM sleep onset and duration
Peak LevelsDuring wakefulness, decreases before sleep
Sleep ImpactEssential for falling asleep; converts to melatonin
Reset ProcessReceptors restore sensitivity during sleep
Deficiency SignsDifficulty falling asleep, mood instability, impulsivity
Medication EffectsSSRIs can suppress REM sleep, alter sleep architecture
Optimization TipsTryptophan-rich foods, sunlight exposure, exercise
🎯 Dopamine (DA)
Primary FunctionMotivation, reward processing, focus, movement
Sleep RoleMust decrease for sleep onset; regulates sleep timing
Peak LevelsMorning and when anticipating rewards
Sleep ImpactHigh levels prevent sleep; low levels cause fatigue
Reset ProcessReceptor sensitivity restored during sleep
Deficiency SignsLow motivation, difficulty waking, poor focus
Medication EffectsStimulants can delay sleep onset significantly
Optimization TipsProtein breakfast, exercise, limit evening stimulation
🛡️ GABA (Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid)
Primary FunctionPrimary inhibitory neurotransmitter, anxiety reduction
Sleep RoleEssential for sleep onset and maintenance
Peak LevelsIncreases in evening, highest during deep sleep
Sleep ImpactLow GABA = racing thoughts, difficulty sleeping
Reset ProcessReceptor systems recalibrate during sleep
Deficiency SignsAnxiety, restlessness, muscle tension, insomnia
Medication EffectsBenzodiazepines enhance but can create dependence
Optimization TipsMagnesium, yoga, meditation, chamomile tea
