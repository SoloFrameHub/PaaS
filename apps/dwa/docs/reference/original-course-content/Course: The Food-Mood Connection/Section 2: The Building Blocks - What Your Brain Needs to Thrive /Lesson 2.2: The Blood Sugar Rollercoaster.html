Lesson 6: The Blood Sugar Rollercoaster

How Sugar & Refined Carbs Impact Anxiety

The Spike & The Crash

Have you ever had a sugary snack for a quick energy boost, only to feel tired, irritable, or even anxious an hour later? You've just experienced the blood sugar rollercoaster. This lesson uncovers how "fast-acting" carbohydrates directly impact your body's stress response and can be a major hidden trigger for anxiety, mood swings, and brain fog.

When you eat refined sugars or simple carbohydrates (like white bread, pastries, or sugary drinks) on their own, your body digests them very quickly. This causes a rapid spike in your blood sugar. In response, your pancreas releases a large amount of insulin to quickly move that sugar out of your blood and into your cells.

But often, the body overcorrects. This large insulin surge can cause your blood sugar to drop too low, too fast. This "crash" is what feels so terrible. To your brain, low blood sugar is an emergency, so it triggers the release of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline to bring it back up. This stress response is what can feel identical to a wave of anxiety.

💡 Key Insight: The Anxiety Connection

The physical symptoms of a blood sugar crash—a racing heart, shakiness, sweating, irritability, and a sense of unease—are nearly identical to the symptoms of a panic attack. For many people, what they perceive as random anxiety is actually their body's physiological response to this rollercoaster cycle.

"This is the '3 PM anxiety' I see so often. A client like Rachel, the busy professional, might have a light lunch of just a salad or a simple sandwich. By mid-afternoon, her brain has used up that quick fuel, her blood sugar crashes, and her body sends out a stress signal. She feels anxious and immediately craves a cookie or a soda to fix it, which just starts the cycle all over again. The solution wasn't an anti-anxiety pill; it was adding protein, healthy fat, and fiber to her lunch to get off the rollercoaster." - Autumn Persinger, PMHNP-BC
