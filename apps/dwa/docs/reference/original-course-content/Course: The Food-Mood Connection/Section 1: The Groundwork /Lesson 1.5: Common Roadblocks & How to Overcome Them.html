Lesson 1.5: Common Roadblocks & How to Overcome Them

Guidance for when the journey gets tough, from David Glenn, PMHNP-BC

Normalizing the Challenge

Welcome. By now, you've started implementing some of the most powerful techniques in sleep science. And if you're like most people, you may have also discovered that this process isn't always a straight line. This is completely normal and expected.

A Clinical Note from David: In my practice, the first two weeks are often the most challenging. Your body and mind are unlearning old, ingrained habits and building new ones. The initial discomfort is often a sign that the changes are taking hold. My goal in this lesson is to be a voice of reassurance and give you the practical strategies I share with my own patients to navigate these common bumps in the road.

Common Roadblocks: What You Might Be Feeling

Do any of these sound familiar? Click on a card to reveal the strategy for overcoming it.

"I'm even MORE tired since starting my sleep window."

Strategy: Trust the Process

This is the most common feedback! It's a sign that you are successfully building sleep pressure. This intense tiredness is temporary. Stick with your schedule—especially your wake-up time—for at least 7-10 days. The deep, consolidated sleep that follows is worth it.

"My racing thoughts are still keeping me awake."

Strategy: Schedule Your Worry

Don't battle your thoughts in bed. Double down on the "Worry Time" technique from Lesson 1.3. Set aside 15 minutes in the early evening to write down every anxiety. This gives your brain a designated time to process, making it less likely to interrupt you at night.

"I missed a day / stayed up late. Have I ruined my progress?"

Strategy: The "Next Right Choice" Rule

Absolutely not. The goal is progress, not perfection. You cannot fail. The only thing that matters is the next right choice. Did you stay up late? The next right choice is to get up at your scheduled wake-up time. Don't let one off night derail the entire system.

"I get anxious just thinking about having to follow the rules."

Strategy: Focus on ONE Thing

If the whole system feels overwhelming, simplify. For this week, focus on just ONE core rule. Make it your non-negotiable. Is it your wake-up time? Is it the 'digital sunset'? Master one component, build confidence, and then layer on the next when you feel ready.

You Have What It Takes

Building a new skill takes practice, patience, and self-compassion. The fact that you are here, learning how to navigate challenges, shows your commitment. You are building resilience.
