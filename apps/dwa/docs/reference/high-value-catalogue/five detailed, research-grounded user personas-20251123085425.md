# five detailed, research-grounded user personas

Here are **five detailed, research-grounded user personas** tailored specifically for a **non-clinical mental-wellbeing education platform** priced at $19.95/month. # **Persona 1 — “The Self-Improver”**
### **Core Segment:** Adult professionals seeking personal development
### **Age:** 28–45
### **Income:** $60k–$140k
### **Tech Use:** Heavy (mobile-first)
### **Subscription Likelihood:** ★★★★★ (very high)
### **Motivations**
*   Wants to sleep better, reduce stress, and “upgrade” lifestyle habits
*   Prefers self-led learning over therapy
*   Loves structured programs (30-day challenges, habit resets)
*   Trusts expert-led content over influencer health trends
### **Pain Points**
*   Feels overwhelmed by conflicting wellness advice online
*   Has tried meditation apps but found them repetitive
*   Lacks a clear roadmap to improve wellbeing
*   Doesn’t want to commit time/money to therapy
### **Buying Trigger**
*   Finds a high-quality free lesson via Google or an AI chatbot
*   Watches 2–3 public videos and realizes, “This is exactly what I’ve been missing”
### **Content They Want**
*   Sleep improvement pathways
*   Stress & burnout modules
*   Nutrition for energy/mood
*   Short, daily micro-lessons
### **Retention Hooks**
*   Progress tracking
*   Clear transformation paths
*   Email nudges with “Next step” recommendations
### **Why They Pay**
Because they want an **easy, trustworthy, step-by-step program** that fits their busy schedule and doesn’t feel clinical.

# **Persona 2 — “The Quiet Struggler”**
### **Core Segment:** People with mild-to-moderate symptoms who avoid therapy
### **Age:** 22–50
### **Income:** $35k–$90k
### **Tech Use:** High
### **Subscription Likelihood:** ★★★★☆
### **Motivations**
*   Feels anxious, overwhelmed, or stuck
*   Wants help without needing to “talk to someone”
*   Prefers privacy and self-paced education
*   Wants evidence-based tools without feeling like a patient
### **Pain Points**
*   Hesitant to seek therapy due to stigma or cost
*   Doesn’t know where to start
*   Finds self-help books too long
*   Feels daily stress is affecting work or relationships
### **Buying Trigger**
*   Takes a free lesson like “Why Your Brain Overreacts to Stress”
*   Learns something that instantly resonates
*   Realizes the platform feels **non-judgmental and approachable**
### **Content They Want**
*   Anxiety habits modules
*   Cognitive tools (thought reframing, awareness exercises)
*   Guided audio exercises
*   Actionable daily routines
### **Retention Hooks**
*   Tools that make them feel an immediate lift
*   Regularly released content
*   “You’re not alone” tone
### **Why They Pay**
Because the platform feels like **therapy-adjacent learning**, with none of the stigma or cost.

# **Persona 5 — “The Concerned Parent or Partner”**
### **Core Segment:** Adults trying to support a loved one
### **Age:** 35–60
### **Income:** $50k–150k
### **Tech Use:** Moderate
### **Subscription Likelihood:** ★★★☆☆
### **Motivations**
*   Wants to help their child, spouse, or sibling
*   Needs digestible education to understand mental wellbeing
*   Wants to model healthy routines at home
*   Seeking preventive tools, not treatment
### **Pain Points**
*   Doesn’t know what advice is safe
*   Gets overwhelmed reading clinical resources
*   Wants clear actions to support family routines
### **Buying Trigger**
*   Receives a link to a free lesson from a friend/clinic
*   Finds content structured, empathetic, and simple
*   Recognizes the platform can support the _whole household_
### **Content They Want**
*   Family sleep strategies
*   Nutrition for kids/teens
*   Emotional regulation basics
*   Stress and school/work-life balance
### **Retention Hooks**
*   Printable guides
*   Family-oriented modules
*   Monthly “Family Wellbeing” webinars
### **Why They Pay**
Because it helps them support loved ones with **trustworthy, non-clinical guidance.**
