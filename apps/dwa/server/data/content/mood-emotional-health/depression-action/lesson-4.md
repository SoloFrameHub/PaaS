In the previous lesson, you learned the basics of behavioral activation. Now we'll go deeper—moving from random activities to **purposeful engagement** with what truly matters to you.

When depression takes over, we often lose touch with our values—the deeply held beliefs about what makes life meaningful. This lesson will help you rediscover your values and connect them to concrete actions.

## 1. What Are Values?

Values are different from goals. Understanding this distinction is crucial.

<FlipCard front="Values are ongoing directions, not destinations" back="Think of values as a compass direction. 'I value connection' is like heading North. You can always move toward North. You never 'arrive' at North and stop. Even if you go off course, you can always redirect." />

<FlipCard front="Goals can be completed — values cannot" back="'Have dinner with a friend this Friday' is a goal. 'Be a caring, present friend' is a value. Goals are checkboxes. Values are the direction those checkboxes point toward." />

### Values Are NOT:

- Feelings (which come and go)
- Rules imposed by others
- Things you "should" care about
- Fixed or unchangeable

## 2. Why Values Matter for Depression

Depression disconnects us from our values in several ways:

<InsightGrid>
<InsightItem icon="😶" title="Anhedonia">
Makes valued activities feel empty. The things you once loved now bring no pleasure, so you stop doing them.
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🔋" title="Fatigue">
Prevents you from acting on values. Even when you know what matters, exhaustion blocks the path.
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🌑" title="Negative Thinking">
Tells you "what's the point?" Depression is relentless at generating reasons why your values don't matter anymore.
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🚪" title="Avoidance">
Keeps you from meaningful engagement. It's easier to stay withdrawn than to risk the effort and feel nothing.
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🏝️" title="Isolation">
Cuts you off from valued relationships. The people who matter most become the hardest to reach.
</InsightItem>
</InsightGrid>

<Callout type="reflection" title="The Reconnection">
When you act in alignment with your values — even without feeling like it — you create a sense of meaning and purpose. You build a life worth living. You have reasons to get out of bed. You reconnect with your authentic self. You counter the emptiness depression creates.
</Callout>

## 4. Values Clarification Exercise

### Step 1: Rate Current Alignment

For each value you identified, consider:
- **Importance** (1-10): How important is this value to me?
- **Current Action** (1-10): How much am I living this value right now?

The gap between importance and action shows where to focus.

### Step 2: Identify Small Steps

For values with the biggest gaps, brainstorm one small action you could take this week.

<Callout type="tip" title="When Depression Says 'What's the Point?'">
Depression will try to convince you that your values are meaningless. This is the illness speaking, not reality. You don't need to feel the meaning right now — you just need to move in the direction of what matters. The feeling often follows the action.
</Callout>

## 5. Values-to-Activities Mapping

Now translate your values into concrete activities at different levels.

## 7. When Values Feel Empty

Depression can make your values feel hollow or meaningless. Here's how to work with this:

<ScenarioCard title="The Feeling vs. The Action">
You may not **feel** the meaning while doing values-based activities. That's okay — feelings follow action, not the other way around. Do the activity because it aligns with who you want to be. Trust that the feeling may return with time.
Think of it like watering a garden during winter. You can't see growth. The soil looks barren. But underneath, the roots are receiving what they need. When the season changes, the growth will show.
</ScenarioCard>

### Small Steps First

- Don't start with your biggest, most meaningful value
- Start with small, achievable actions
- Build success before tackling harder things
- Momentum matters more than magnitude

### Notice What You Notice

After each activity, pay attention to:
- Any shift in mood, even tiny
- Any sense of accomplishment
- Any connection to meaning, however faint
- How you feel about yourself
