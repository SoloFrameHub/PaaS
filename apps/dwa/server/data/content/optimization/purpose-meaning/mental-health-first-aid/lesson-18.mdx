# Group Facilitation and Peer Support Programs

## Learning Objectives

- Understand peer support principles (lived experience, mutuality, hope) and evidence for effectiveness
- Facilitate safe, supportive group discussions using ground rules, active listening, and conflict management
- Connect to existing peer support programs (NAMI, recovery communities, online groups) and assess program quality

## Research Foundation

Peer support (support from someone with lived mental health experience) shows MODERATE evidence for improving hope, empowerment, social connection, and reducing hospitalizations. More cost-effective than professional services for ongoing support. Group facilitation skills important: clear guidelines, psychological safety, managing dominating participants. Online peer support growing; similar benefits to in-person.

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (peer support effectiveness); EMERGING (online peer support)

<EnhancedAccordion title="Running Safe Support Groups">
  <AccordionItem title="Ground Rules (Essential)">
    1. Confidentiality (what's shared here stays here)
    2. Respect (no judgment, criticism, or interrupting)
    3. "I" statements (share your own experience, don't give advice)
    4. Right to pass (can listen without sharing)
    5. Crisis protocol (if someone in crisis, facilitator connects to 988/professional help)
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Managing Difficult Dynamics">
    **Dominating participant:** "Thanks for sharing. Let's hear from others who haven't had a chance yet."
    
    **Conflict:** "I'm noticing tension. Let's take a breath and remember our ground rules."
    
    **Triggering content:** "That sounds really hard. Let's be mindful that some topics may be triggering for others."
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Connecting to Existing Programs">
    - **NAMI Connection:** Peer-led recovery support groups
    - **SMART Recovery:** Mutual support for addiction
    - **Online:** 7 Cups, r/mentalhealth (Reddit), specialized forums
    
    **Quality check:** Clear guidelines, trained facilitators, focus on support not advice
  </AccordionItem>
</EnhancedAccordion>

## Key Takeaways

- Peer support (from someone with lived experience) has MODERATE evidence for improving hope, empowerment, connection, reducing hospitalizations; cost-effective for ongoing support
- Key principles: lived experience credibility, mutuality (both give/receive), hope ("if they recovered, maybe I can"), non-hierarchical
- Group facilitation essentials: ground rules (confidentiality, respect, "I" statements, right to pass, crisis protocol), psychological safety, managing participation
- Existing programs: NAMI Connection, SMART Recovery, online forums; quality check includes clear guidelines and trained facilitators
- Managing difficult dynamics: redirect dominating participants, address conflict with ground rules reminder, be mindful of triggering content
