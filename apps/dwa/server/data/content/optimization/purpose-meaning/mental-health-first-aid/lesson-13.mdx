# Mental Health in the Workplace

## Learning Objectives

- Recognize workplace mental health challenges including burnout, stress, harassment impact, and presenteeism while respecting professional boundaries
- Implement supportive workplace responses including accommodation discussions, EAP referrals, and anti-stigma messaging without overstepping HR role
- Navigate disclosure decisions, ADA accommodations, and when to involve HR/management while maintaining confidentiality within legal limits

## Research Foundation

Workplace mental health costs US economy $200B+ annually in lost productivity. Burnout affects 50%+ of workers in high-stress professions. Supportive workplace cultures reduce absenteeism, improve retention. ADA requires reasonable accommodations for mental health conditions. EAPs show moderate evidence for improving access to care. Stigma remains primary barrier to workplace disclosure.

**Evidence Grade:** STRONG (workplace mental health prevalence/costs); MODERATE (EAP effectiveness, accommodation benefits)

## Supporting Colleagues

<Callout type="info" title="How to Approach">
"I've noticed you seem stressed lately. Are you okay? Our company has an EAP that provides free confidential counseling if that would help."
</Callout>

<EnhancedAccordion title="Workplace Resources">
  <AccordionItem title="Employee Assistance Programs (EAP)">
    Free, confidential counseling (typically 3-8 sessions). Covers mental health, substance use, family issues, stress.
    
    **How to refer:** "Have you checked out our EAP? It's confidential and free."
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="ADA Accommodations">
    Mental health conditions qualify for reasonable accommodations: flexible schedule, quiet workspace, time off for appointments.
    
    **Process:** Employee requests, HR engages in interactive process.
    
    **Your role:** Suggest they explore accommodations, not provide them yourself.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="When to Involve HR/Management">
    **Must involve if:** Safety risk, performance significantly impaired, colleague requests help navigating process
    
    **Maintain confidentiality:** Share only what's necessary, focus on behaviors/performance not diagnosis
  </AccordionItem>
</EnhancedAccordion>

## Key Takeaways

- Workplace mental health costs $200B+ annually; burn out affects 50%+ of high-stress workers; supportive cultures improve retention/productivity
- EAPs provide free confidential counseling (3-8 sessions); ADA requires reasonable accommodations for mental health conditions
- Your role: notice changes, express concern, refer to EAP/HR, support accommodations — NOT diagnose, provide therapy, or make HR decisions
- Confidentiality limits: maintain privacy except for safety risks; when involving HR, share behaviors/performance not diagnosis
- Stigma remains primary barrier to workplace disclosure; anti-stigma messaging and leadership modeling help
