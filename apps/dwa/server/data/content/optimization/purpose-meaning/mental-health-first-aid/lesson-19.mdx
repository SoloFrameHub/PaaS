# Crisis Response Team Coordination

## Learning Objectives

- Understand mobile crisis team models and when to access vs. calling 911
- Coordinate handoffs between MHFA, crisis teams, and ongoing care to ensure continuity
- Document crisis interactions appropriately while maintaining privacy and dignity

## Research Foundation

Mobile crisis teams (mental health professionals who respond in community) reduce ER visits, hospitalizations, and police involvement compared to 911 alone. Crisis Intervention Teams (CIT) train police in mental health response; reduce use of force. Warm handoffs to ongoing care reduce relapse. 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline connects to local mobile crisis teams in many areas.

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE-TO-STRONG (mobile crisis effectiveness, CIT training benefits)

<EnhancedAccordion title="Effective Crisis Coordination">
  <AccordionItem title="Your Role in Handoff">
    When transferring care to crisis team/988:
    - Stay until they arrive (if safe)
    - Brief them on situation: what happened, warning signs, what helped/didn't help
    - Provide context (if you know person's history, share relevant info)
    - Support person during transition
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Documenting Appropriately">
    **What to document:**
    - Date/time, what observed (behaviors, statements), actions taken, who contacted
    - Focus on facts, not speculation
    - Relevant for follow-up or liability protection
    
    **Maintain privacy:** Secure storage, share only as needed for care/safety
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Following Up After Crisis">
    - Check in next day: "How are you doing? Did the crisis team help?"
    - Support connection to ongoing care
    - Debrief with your supervisor/support system (helper self-care)
  </AccordionItem>
</EnhancedAccordion>

## Key Takeaways

- Mobile crisis teams (mental health professionals responding in community) reduce ER visits, hospitalizations, police involvement vs. 911 alone
- Call mobile crisis/988 for: mental health crisis without violence/weapon, suicidal thoughts without immediate means, psychosis without danger, welfare checks
- Call 911 for: weapon, violence, medical emergency, immediate threat to life
- Effective handoff: stay until team arrives (if safe), brief on situation/context, support person during transition, follow up next day
- Documentation: facts (date/time, observed behaviors/statements, actions taken, who contacted), not speculation; maintain privacy and secure storage
