# Program Development and Community Implementation

## Learning Objectives

- Design community mental health first aid program using needs assessment, stakeholder engagement, and evidence-based planning
- Implement sustainable program infrastructure including training, supervision, evaluation, and continuous improvement
- Create personal MHFA action plan integrating all course learnings into ongoing community mental health support practice

## Research Foundation

Community mental health programs succeed when: grounded in local needs assessment, stakeholder buy-in, sustainable funding/infrastructure, ongoing training/supervision, program evaluation. Implementation science shows fidelity to evidence-based models matters; adaptation needed for cultural fit but core components preserved. Peer involvement strengthens programs. Evaluation enables continuous improvement.

**Evidence Grade:** MODERATE (implementation science, community program best practices)

## Step 4: Commit to Ongoing Learning and Practice

<EnhancedAccordion title="Sustaining Your MHFA Skills">
  <AccordionItem title="Refresher Training">
    **Research shows:** MHFA knowledge decays over time; attitudes return to baseline in 57% of cases within ~4 months without reinforcement.
    
    **Action:** Schedule annual refresher. Re-read key lessons. Attend MHFA community of practice.
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Supervision and Consultation">
    Don't practice MHFA in isolation.
    
    **Find:** Peer consultation group, supervisor, experienced MHFA practitioner for questions/debriefs
    
    **Why:** Prevents errors, provides support, reduces vicarious trauma
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Specialized Training">
    Based on your growth areas and population:
    - Youth Mental Health First Aid (if supporting youth)
    - Cultural competency training
    - Motivational interviewing workshop
    - Trauma-informed care deep dive
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Self-Care (Non-Negotiable)">
    Review Lesson 17. Commit to:
    - Regular debriefing after difficult cases
    - Boundaries (time, emotional, saying no)
    - Monthly vicarious trauma self-check
    - Personal therapy/support
  </AccordionItem>
</EnhancedAccordion>

## Step 5: Define Your MHFA Practice Boundaries

<SlideNavigation>
  <Slide title="Scope Boundaries">
    **I will do:**
    - Notice, approach, listen, support, refer (ALGEE)
    - Connect to resources
    - Follow up to ensure connection
    - Be present in non-crisis support
    
    **I will NOT do:**
    - Diagnose, provide therapy, recommend medications
    - Become someone's sole/ongoing support system
    - Violate confidentiality except for safety
    - Exceed my training
  </Slide>
  
  <Slide title="Time Boundaries">
    - MHFA available during: [your hours/contexts]
    - NOT available 24/7 (except crisis, then refer to 988)
    - Response time expectations: [realistic timeframe]
    - When to say "I can't take this on right now"
  </Slide>
  
  <Slide title="Emotional Boundaries">
    - I care about people I help AND I'm not responsible for their outcomes
    - I can't save everyone
    - Stepping back when needed is self-care, not abandonment
    - Signs I need to step back: [from Lesson 17 self-check]
  </Slide>

## Community Program Development (Optional Advanced Application)

For those developing formal MHFA programs in organizations/communities:

<EnhancedAccordion title="Program Development Framework">
  <AccordionItem title="Needs Assessment">
    - Survey community mental health challenges
    - Identify gaps in existing services
    - Assess readiness and resources
    - Engage stakeholders (consumers, providers, community leaders)
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Program Design">
    - Define target population and goals
    - Adapt MHFA curriculum to cultural context while preserving core components
    - Secure funding and partnerships
    - Develop training infrastructure
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Implementation">
    - Train MHFA instructors
    - Conduct community trainings
    - Establish supervision/consultation system
    - Create resource network
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Evaluation and Improvement">
    - Track outcomes: people helped, resources connected, satisfaction
    - Monitor program fidelity
    - Gather participant feedback
    - Iterate based on data
  </AccordionItem>
</EnhancedAccordion>

## Key Takeaways

- Community programs need: needs assessment, stakeholder engagement, cultural adaptation with core fidelity, training infrastructure, evaluation/continuous improvement
