## Progress Tracking System

**Track enough to notice patterns, not so much it becomes burdensome.**

**Minimal tracking (recommended for beginners):**
- **Consistency:** Mark calendar when you create (streak = motivation)
- **Subjective wellbeing:** Weekly scale rating ('Overall, how am I feeling this week? 1-10')

**Moderate tracking (for data-driven people):**
- **Practice log:** What you did, how long, before/after mood ratings
- **Pattern notes:** Every 2 weeks, write 2-3 sentences on what you notice ('Journaling in morning seems more helpful than evening')

**Advanced tracking (for deepening practice):**
- **Emotion trends:** Track which emotions surface during creating (anger, sadness, joy)—reveals patterns
- **Technique effectiveness:** Compare practices ('Movement regulates anxiety better than journaling for me')
- **Life context:** Note external stressors and how creative practice buffers or doesn't

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Perfectionist Tracking">

**Don't track:**
- Quality of work ('Is my art getting better?'—irrelevant for wellbeing practice)
- Detailed time logs that create pressure ('I HAVE to hit 30 min or I failed')
- Every single emotional nuance (this becomes rumination)

**Track to learn and adjust, not to judge yourself.** If tracking feels stressful, do minimal (just mark calendar when you create).

</Callout>

**Your assignment:** Choose your tracking method (minimal, moderate, or advanced). Set up system NOW (calendar, notebook, app).

## Long-Term Sustainability: Beyond the Honeymoon Phase

**Initial enthusiasm fades. Sustainable practice outlasts motivation.**

**Strategies for long-term maintenance:**

1. **Community and Accountability:**
   - Find creative accountability partner (check in weekly: 'Did you create this week?')
   - Join ongoing group (L15): Parallel work sessions, monthly creative circles
   - Share (selectively) with trusted people who support your practice

2. **Ritual and Identity:**
   - Build ritual around creating: Light candle, make tea, set up space (signals 'this is my time')
   - Shift identity: 'I am someone who creates' (not 'I'm trying to create more')—identity drives behavior

3. **Flexible Goals:**
   - Seasons of depth vs. maintenance: Some months you go deep, others you just sustain minimum
   - Permission to pause: Life crises require triage—you can stop practice temporarily and return later

4. **Integration with Other Wellbeing Practices:**
   - Creative practice complements therapy, exercise, sleep, relationships (not replaces them)
   - Build holistic wellbeing strategy—creativity is one pillar, not the only one

5. **Celebrate Milestones:**
   - 30 days consistent? Celebrate. 6 months? Acknowledge it. 1 year? Mark the anniversary.
   - Track cumulative impact: Re-read old journal entries, look at early art—see growth over time

**Long-term success = systems + flexibility + self-compassion.**
