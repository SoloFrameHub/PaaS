# Personal Digital Wellness Assessment

## Learning Objectives

- Complete a **comprehensive baseline assessment** of your current digital habits, problem areas, and values alignment
- Use **validated assessment frameworks** (Mindful Technology Use Scale, PSU indicators, sleep/attention/comparison patterns)
- Identify **personal patterns and triggers** for compulsive or problematic use
- Assess **readiness for change** and clarify motivation for building healthier digital habits

## Research Foundation

This lesson synthesizes assessment tools and frameworks used throughout digital wellness research. The Mindful Technology Use Scale (24-item validated instrument) measures intentional, values-aligned technology engagement — which predicts better wellbeing outcomes independent of screen time. Problematic Smartphone Use (PSU) is characterized by compulsive use, loss of control, negative consequences, and interference with daily functioning — affecting approximately 25% of children and young people. Self-assessment frameworks evaluate multiple domains: sleep disruption (blue light exposure, nighttime use), attention fragmentation (notification interruptions, multitasking), social comparison (body image, FOMO, upward comparison), passive vs. active use, work-life boundaries, and values alignment. This is the PRE-plan lesson: honest self-assessment creates the foundation for your personalized Digital Wellness Action Plan (Lesson 20). Without baseline data, you can't measure progress or target interventions effectively.

**Evidence Grade:** Assessment frameworks validated across multiple studies

## Why Self-Assessment Matters

**You can't change what you don't measure.**

Most people:
- **Underestimate their screen time** by 50%+ (self-report vs. actual use)
- Don't recognize **specific patterns** (nighttime scrolling, compulsive checking, comparison triggers)
- Can't identify **which apps are problematic** (assume "all screens are bad" vs. targeted reduction)
- Don't connect digital habits to **specific symptoms** (poor sleep, anxiety, poor focus, low mood)

**This lesson will:**
1. Establish your **baseline** (current habits, screen time, use patterns)
2. Identify your **problem areas** (sleep disruption, attention fragmentation, comparison, compulsive use)
3. Assess **values alignment** (is your technology use supporting or undermining what matters to you?)
4. Clarify your **readiness and motivation** for change

**Next lesson (Lesson 20):** You'll use this assessment data to build your personalized 7-step Digital Wellness Action Plan.

<Callout type="info" title="This Is Data Collection — Not Self-Critique">
**Important mindset:**
This assessment is about gathering honest data, not judging yourself.

**Not helpful:**
"I'm so bad at this. I spend way too much time on my phone. I have no self-control."

**Helpful:**
"I currently spend 4 hours/day on Instagram and TikTok. I notice I feel worse after scrolling (comparison, time loss). I want to reduce to 1 hour/day and replace with reading and exercise."

**The goal:** Understand your current state clearly so you can design effective interventions — not shame yourself into change (shame doesn't work; data-driven strategy does).
</Callout>

## Domain 1: Baseline Screen Time and App Use

**First, establish objective data.**

<StepByStep title="Collecting Your Screen Time Baseline">
<Step title="Step 1: Access Your Screen Time Data">
**iPhone users:**
Settings → Screen Time → See All Activity (view daily/weekly averages, app-specific use)

**Android users:**
Settings → Digital Wellbeing & Parental Controls → Dashboard (view daily/weekly use, app breakdown)

**Alternative:**
Use third-party apps (RescueTime, Moment, Screen Time) for more detailed tracking

**What to record:**
- Total daily screen time (average over past week)
- Top 3-5 apps by time spent
- Pickups per day (how often you unlock your phone)
- Notifications per day
</Step>

<Step title="Step 2: Compare Self-Perception vs. Reality">
**Before looking at data, estimate:**
- "I think I spend about ___ hours/day on my phone"
- "I think I check my phone about ___ times per day"
- "I think my top app is ___"

**Then look at actual data. Most people are surprised.**

**Common findings:**
- Underestimate total time by 50-100%
- Don't realize how much time goes to specific apps (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube)
- Underestimate pickups (many people check 80-150+ times/day)
</Step>

<Step title="Step 3: Identify Your Top Problem Apps">
**Not all screen time is equal.**

**Questions to ask:**
1. **Which app takes the MOST time?** (raw hours)
2. **Which app leaves me feeling WORST?** (comparison, anxiety, time loss, drained)
3. **Which app interferes with priorities?** (sleep, work, relationships, hobbies)
4. **Which app is hardest to stop using?** (compulsive, loss of control)

**Example:**
- "Instagram: 2 hrs/day, makes me feel inadequate (comparison), disrupts sleep (use before bed), hard to stop scrolling"
- "TikTok: 1.5 hrs/day, lose track of time, displaces reading and exercise"
- "Email: 1 hr/day but constant checking creates stress and interrupts focus"

**Record your top 1-3 problem apps for targeted reduction in Lesson 20.**
</Step>
</StepByStep>

## Domain 2: Sleep Disruption Assessment

**Blue light, nighttime use, and sleep quality.**

<Checkin type="multiChoice" question="Assess your digital sleep hygiene (select all that apply):" options="I use screens (phone, tablet, TV, laptop) within 1 hour of bedtime most nights|I check my phone if I wake up during the night|My phone is within arm's reach when I sleep (on nightstand, under pillow, in bed)|I scroll social media or watch videos in bed before trying to sleep|I have trouble falling asleep or staying asleep (independently of screens)|I wake up and immediately check my phone before getting out of bed|None of the above — my sleep hygiene is good" />

**Interpretation:**

- **0-1 items:** Your digital sleep hygiene is strong. Sleep likely isn't your primary concern.
- **2-3 items:** Moderate sleep disruption from screens. Target nighttime boundaries in your action plan.
- **4+ items:** Severe sleep disruption pattern. This should be a HIGH PRIORITY intervention (strong evidence for harm).

**Evidence reminder (Lesson 13):**
Blue light suppresses melatonin. Nighttime device use delays circadian rhythm. Cognitive and emotional arousal from content (news, social media, work email) disrupts sleep onset. This is one of the STRONGEST evidence areas in digital wellness.

**Red flag:** If you have chronic insomnia (difficulty sleeping 3+ nights/week for 3+ months) that's NOT explained by screens alone, seek professional evaluation (sleep specialist, primary care).

## Domain 3: Attention and Focus Assessment

**Notification interruptions, multitasking, and cognitive overload.**

<Checkin type="multiChoice" question="Assess your attention and focus patterns (select all that apply):" options="I check my phone every 5-15 minutes throughout the day (compulsive checking)|I have trouble focusing on one task without checking notifications or switching apps|I multitask frequently (phone while watching TV, email during meetings, texting while talking)|I feel mentally foggy or cognitively overloaded by midday|I struggle to read long-form content (books, articles) without getting distracted|I reach for my phone out of boredom or restlessness (not for specific purpose)|I have 50+ app notifications per day (badges, pop-ups, sounds)|None of the above — my focus and attention are strong" />

**Interpretation:**

- **0-1 items:** Strong attentional control. Focus likely isn't your primary concern.
- **2-3 items:** Moderate attention fragmentation. Notification reduction and deep work blocks recommended.
- **4+ items:** Severe attention disruption. This should be a HIGH PRIORITY intervention.

**Evidence reminder (Lesson 5):**
Attention span has dropped from 2.5 minutes (2004) to 47 seconds (2024). After interruptions, regaining focus takes 23+ minutes. Multitasking is a myth; it's rapid task-switching that reduces productivity and increases cognitive load.

## Domain 4: Social Comparison and Body Image Assessment

**Upward comparison, FOMO, and self-esteem impacts.**

<Checkin type="multiChoice" question="Assess social comparison and body image patterns (select all that apply):" options="I compare my life/appearance/achievements to others on social media and feel worse|I experience FOMO (fear of missing out) when seeing others' social activities online|I feel inadequate, envious, or 'less than' after scrolling Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook|I follow influencers or accounts that make me feel bad about my body, life, or success|I spend time on 'inspiration' content (fitness, beauty, lifestyle) that actually triggers comparison|I curate my own posts heavily to present an idealized version of my life|I check likes/comments compulsively for validation|None of the above — comparison isn't an issue for me" />

**Interpretation:**

- **0-1 items:** Social comparison isn't a primary issue for you.
- **2-3 items:** Moderate comparison pattern. Feed curation (unfollow triggering accounts) recommended.
- **4+ items:** Severe comparison and self-esteem impact. HIGH PRIORITY for targeted reduction or platform breaks.

**Evidence reminder (Lesson 9):**
Upward social comparison on image-based platforms (Instagram, TikTok) is a primary mechanism linking social media to body dissatisfaction, low self-esteem, and depression — particularly in young females. Content curation and media literacy are protective.

**Red flag:** If you're experiencing severe body image disturbance, disordered eating, or suicidal thoughts related to comparison, seek professional help immediately (eating disorder specialist, therapist, crisis support).

## Domain 5: Problematic Use Indicators (PSU Assessment)

**Compulsive use, loss of control, negative consequences.**

This is adapted from Problematic Smartphone Use (PSU) research criteria. PSU affects ~25% of young people.

<Checkin type="multiChoice" question="Assess problematic use patterns (select all that apply):" options="I use my phone more than I intend to (time gets away from me)|I have trouble controlling or reducing my phone use even when I want to|I feel anxious, irritable, or restless when I can't access my phone|I neglect responsibilities (work, school, relationships, self-care) due to phone use|I use my phone to escape negative emotions (boredom, loneliness, anxiety, sadness)|I've tried to cut back on phone use but can't maintain the change|My phone use causes conflict with family, friends, or partners|People in my life have expressed concern about my phone use|None of the above — I don't have problematic use patterns" />

**Interpretation:**

- **0-1 items:** No significant problematic use. Your use is within normal range.
- **2-3 items:** Mild-to-moderate problematic use. Structured reduction recommended.
- **4-5 items:** Moderate problematic use. Targeted interventions + accountability needed.
- **6+ items:** Severe problematic use (PSU). Consider professional support (therapist, digital wellness coach, behavioral health specialist).

**Evidence reminder (Lesson 3):**
PSU is characterized by compulsive use, loss of control, negative consequences, and functional impairment. It's associated with depression, anxiety, stress, and sleep disruption. It exists on a continuum (not binary), and severe cases may benefit from professional intervention.

**Red flag:** If phone use is causing SEVERE functional impairment (can't work, missing important obligations, relationship breakdowns), seek professional evaluation.

## Domain 6: Passive vs. Active Use Assessment

**Intentional engagement vs. mindless scrolling.**

<Checkin type="reflect" question="Estimate what percentage of your social media time is PASSIVE (scrolling feeds, watching stories, consuming without interacting) vs. ACTIVE (messaging friends, commenting meaningfully, posting your own content, video calls). Most people are 70-90% passive. What's your estimate?" />

**Why this matters (Lesson 4):**
Intentional, goal-directed technology use predicts better wellbeing outcomes than passive, habitual consumption — independent of total screen time. Quality > quantity.

**Self-assessment:**
- **High passive use (70%+ passive):** You're consuming more than connecting. Shift toward active engagement or reduce overall use.
- **Balanced (40-60% passive):** Reasonable mix of consumption and interaction.
- **High active use (70%+ active):** You're using platforms for genuine connection. Continue if it feels good; reduce if it's still draining.

## Domain 7: Work-Life Boundaries Assessment

**After-hours use, email stress, right to disconnect.**

<Checkin type="multiChoice" question="Assess your work-life digital boundaries (select all that apply):" options="I check work email or messages in the evenings (after 6 PM)|I check work email or messages on weekends|I check work email or messages during vacation|I feel pressure to respond to work communications immediately|I have work apps on my personal phone with notifications enabled|I wake up and check work email before getting out of bed|I feel guilty or anxious when I don't check work communications|My organization expects or rewards 24/7 availability|None of the above — I have strong work-life boundaries" />

**Interpretation:**

- **0-1 items:** Strong work-life boundaries. Well done.
- **2-3 items:** Moderate boundary issues. After-hours disconnection strategies recommended.
- **4-5 items:** Weak boundaries. High risk for burnout. Urgent boundary-setting + organizational advocacy needed.
- **6+ items:** Severe boundary violation. Organizational culture issue requiring escalation (or exit strategy).

**Evidence reminder (Lesson 18):**
Constant connectivity prevents psychological detachment and recovery from work. This predicts burnout, stress, and work-life conflict. Right to disconnect policies protect workers, but individual boundary-setting is also necessary.

## Domain 8: Values Alignment Assessment

**Is your technology use supporting or undermining what matters to you?**

<StepByStep title="Values Alignment Exercise">
<Step title="Step 1: Identify Your Core Values">
**What matters most to you in life?**

Common values (select 3-5 that resonate most):
- Connection and relationships (family, friends, community)
- Health and wellbeing (physical, mental, sleep, energy)
- Growth and learning (personal development, education, skills)
- Creativity and self-expression (art, writing, making, building)
- Achievement and contribution (work, career, impact, service)
- Presence and mindfulness (being fully engaged, not distracted)
- Rest and recovery (downtime, relaxation, peace)
- Adventure and exploration (new experiences, travel, spontaneity)

**Write down your top 3-5 values.**
</Step>

<Step title="Step 2: Assess Technology Alignment">
**For each value, ask:**
"Is my current technology use SUPPORTING or UNDERMINING this value?"

**Example 1: "Connection and relationships"**
- **Supporting:** I use video calls to stay close with distant family. I use group chat to coordinate plans with friends.
- **Undermining:** I scroll Instagram during family dinner. I'm on my phone when my partner is talking to me.

**Example 2: "Health and wellbeing"**
- **Supporting:** I use a meditation app daily. I track sleep and exercise.
- **Undermining:** I scroll TikTok until 1 AM and wake up exhausted. Social media comparison makes me feel inadequate.

**Example 3: "Creativity and self-expression"**
- **Supporting:** I use Pinterest for creative inspiration. I share my art on Instagram.
- **Undermining:** I passively consume others' creativity instead of making my own. I've stopped drawing because I'm always on my phone.

**Honest assessment creates clarity on WHERE to intervene.**
</Step>

<Step title="Step 3: Calculate Alignment Score">
For each of your 3-5 core values, rate technology alignment:

- **+2:** Strongly supports this value
- **+1:** Somewhat supports this value
- **0:** Neutral (neither supports nor undermines)
- **−1:** Somewhat undermines this value
- **−2:** Strongly undermines this value

**Total score interpretation:**
- **+5 to +10:** Strong alignment. Technology is serving your values well.
- **0 to +4:** Mixed alignment. Some areas support values; others undermine them.
- **−1 to −5:** Misalignment. Technology is undermining what matters to you.
- **−6 to −10:** Severe misalignment. Major intervention needed.
</Step>
</StepByStep>

<Checkin type="reflect" question="What are your top 3 core values? For each, describe one way your technology use SUPPORTS that value and one way it UNDERMINES it. What's your overall alignment score?" />

## Domain 9: Readiness for Change Assessment

**Are you ready to make changes? What's your motivation?**

<SlideNavigation persistKey="readiness-stages">
<Slide title="Stage 1: Pre-contemplation (Not Ready)">
**Characteristics:**
- "I don't think my technology use is a problem."
- "Everyone uses their phone this much."
- "I'm fine — no need to change."

**If this is you:**
You're not ready for behavior change yet. That's okay. Continue through this course for information, but don't force change. Awareness may shift over time.

**Next step:** Observe your patterns without judgment. Notice how you feel after different types of use.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Stage 2: Contemplation (Thinking About Change)">
**Characteristics:**
- "I think my phone use might be a problem."
- "I want to change, but I'm not sure how or if I can."
- "I see pros and cons to reducing screen time."

**If this is you:**
You're aware there's an issue, but ambivalent about change. This is normal.

**Next step:** Complete this assessment to clarify WHAT specifically is problematic and WHY you want to change. Lesson 20 will help you build a realistic plan.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Stage 3: Preparation (Getting Ready)">
**Characteristics:**
- "I'm ready to make changes in the next month."
- "I've thought about what I want to change and why."
- "I'm gathering information and planning."

**If this is you:**
You're ready to build a plan. This assessment provides baseline data. Lesson 20 will guide you through the 7-step action plan.

**Next step:** Identify your top 2-3 priority areas (sleep, attention, comparison, compulsive use, work boundaries) for your action plan.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Stage 4: Action (Making Changes)">
**Characteristics:**
- "I'm actively changing my digital habits right now."
- "I've reduced screen time, set boundaries, or changed app use in the past 6 months."

**If this is you:**
You're already in action. Use this assessment to refine what's working and what needs adjustment.

**Next step:** Lesson 20 will help you formalize your strategy, add accountability, and plan for obstacles.
</Slide>

<Slide title="Stage 5: Maintenance (Sustaining Change)">
**Characteristics:**
- "I've successfully changed my digital habits and maintained it for 6+ months."
- "I have boundaries that work for me."

**If this is you:**
Well done. Use this assessment to identify any areas that still need work or to prevent relapse (habits creeping back).

**Next step:** Lesson 20 will help you sustain gains and address any remaining issues.
</Slide>
</SlideNavigation>

## Key Takeaways

- **Self-assessment establishes baseline data** — most people underestimate screen time by 50%+; objective data reveals actual patterns
- **Multiple domains require assessment** — sleep, attention, comparison, problematic use, passive vs. active, work boundaries, values alignment
- **Problematic Smartphone Use (PSU) exists on a continuum** — 25% of young people show PSU patterns; severe cases benefit from professional support
- **Values alignment reveals WHERE to intervene** — technology that undermines your core values (connection, health, creativity) should be primary target for change
- **Readiness for change matters** — forcing behavior change when you're in pre-contemplation stage doesn't work; meet yourself where you are
- **This assessment feeds directly into Lesson 20** — your baseline data will guide your personalized 7-step Digital Wellness Action Plan
