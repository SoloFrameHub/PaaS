The sleep diary is the foundation of effective insomnia treatment. Before we can improve your sleep, we need to understand exactly what's happening. This lesson teaches you how to track sleep accurately and calculate your sleep efficiency—numbers that will guide your entire treatment.

## Why Track Sleep?

Most people with insomnia **overestimate** how long it takes to fall asleep and **underestimate** how much they actually sleep. When you're lying awake frustrated, time stretches. A 20-minute awakening can feel like an hour.

## The Sleep Diary

Complete your sleep diary **every morning, within 30 minutes of waking**. Don't obsess over exact times—estimates are fine. Watching the clock makes insomnia worse.

### Daily Sleep Diary Questions

**Evening (answer the next morning):**
1. **What time did you get into bed?** (Clock time)
2. **What time did you try to go to sleep?** (May differ from bedtime)
3. **How long did it take to fall asleep?** (Estimate in minutes—don't watch the clock)

**Night:**
4. **How many times did you wake up during the night?** (Number)
5. **How long were you awake total during the night?** (Total minutes across all awakenings)

**Morning:**
6. **What time was your final awakening?** (Clock time)
7. **What time did you get out of bed for the day?** (Clock time)
8. **Rate your sleep quality:** (1-5 scale: 1=very poor, 5=very good)

**Additional tracking (optional but helpful):**
9. **Did you use any sleep aids?** (Medication, alcohol, supplements)
10. **Did you nap yesterday?** (Time and duration)
11. **Rate your daytime functioning:** (1-5 scale)

### Sample Sleep Diary Entry

| Question | Example Answer |
|----------|----------------|
| Time got into bed | 10:30 PM |
| Time tried to sleep | 11:00 PM |
| Time to fall asleep | 45 minutes |
| Number of awakenings | 3 |
| Total time awake during night | 60 minutes |
| Final awakening time | 5:30 AM |
| Time out of bed | 6:30 AM |
| Sleep quality rating | 2/5 |
| Sleep aids used | None |

## Calculating Sleep Efficiency

Sleep efficiency is the most important number in insomnia treatment. It tells us what percentage of your time in bed you're actually sleeping.

### The Formula

```
Sleep Efficiency (%) = (Total Sleep Time / Time in Bed) x 100
```

### Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Calculate Time in Bed (TIB)**
Time from getting INTO bed to getting OUT of bed.

Example:
- Got into bed: 10:30 PM
- Got out of bed: 6:30 AM
- **Time in Bed = 8 hours (480 minutes)**

**Step 2: Calculate Total Sleep Time (TST)**
Time in Bed MINUS all time awake:
- Time to fall asleep (sleep onset latency)
- Time awake during night (WASO - Wake After Sleep Onset)
- Time in bed after final awakening

Example:
- Time in bed: 480 minutes
- Took 45 minutes to fall asleep
- Awake 60 minutes during night
- In bed 60 minutes after final waking (5:30 AM to 6:30 AM, but only 30 of that was after deciding to wake up—let's say 30 minutes lying there)
- **Total Sleep Time = 480 - 45 - 60 - 30 = 345 minutes (5.75 hours)**

**Step 3: Calculate Sleep Efficiency**
```
Sleep Efficiency = (345 / 480) x 100 = 72%
```

### Interpreting Your Sleep Efficiency

| Sleep Efficiency | Rating | Action |
|-----------------|--------|--------|
| >=90% | Excellent | Maintain current habits |
| 85-89% | Good | Minor adjustments may help |
| 75-84% | Fair | Sleep restriction likely helpful |
| &lt;75% | Needs improvement | Sleep restriction recommended |

**The goal**: Get sleep efficiency to **85% or higher**.

## Common Sleep Diary Mistakes

<Callout type="tip" title="Avoid These Common Pitfalls">
The sleep diary is only useful if you can stick with it without adding stress. These are the mistakes that trip people up most often — and they're all easy to fix.
</Callout>

### Mistake 1: Clock-Watching
**Problem**: Constantly checking the time increases anxiety and makes sleep worse.

**Solution**: Put your clock out of sight. Estimate times in the morning—it doesn't need to be exact.

### Mistake 2: Tracking in the Middle of the Night
**Problem**: Writing in your diary during awakenings wakes you up further.

**Solution**: Only complete the diary in the morning. Rough estimates are fine.

### Mistake 3: Trying to Be Too Precise
**Problem**: "Was it 23 minutes or 27 minutes to fall asleep?" This precision anxiety makes insomnia worse.

**Solution**: Round to the nearest 5-10 minutes. "About 25 minutes" is good enough.

### Mistake 4: Skipping Days
**Problem**: Incomplete data makes it hard to see patterns.

**Solution**: Keep the diary by your coffee maker or wherever you go first thing. Make it automatic.

## Finding Your Patterns

After one week of tracking, look for patterns:

### Timing Patterns
- Do you have more trouble on certain nights? (Sunday anxiety before work week?)
- Is falling asleep the problem, or staying asleep?
- What time do you naturally feel sleepy?

### Behavioral Patterns
- Does alcohol help you fall asleep but fragment your sleep later?
- Are naps making nighttime sleep worse?
- Do late meals correlate with worse sleep?

### Environmental Patterns
- Is your bedroom too warm?
- Are you woken by light or noise at specific times?
- Do you sleep better or worse on weekends?

### Emotional Patterns
- Does anxiety worsen on certain days?
- Do you sleep worse after stressful events?
- Is worry about sleep itself becoming the problem?

## Using Technology for Sleep Tracking

### Wearables and Apps
Sleep trackers (Fitbit, Apple Watch, Oura, etc.) can be helpful but have limitations:
- **Pros**: Automatic tracking, trend visualization, don't have to remember
- **Cons**: Often inaccurate for wake time, can increase sleep anxiety

**Recommendation**: Use technology as a supplement, not a replacement for the sleep diary. The diary captures subjective experience that trackers miss.

### Sleep Tracking Apps
Apps like Sleep Cycle analyze movement but can't truly distinguish sleep stages without EEG. Take their "deep sleep" percentages with a grain of salt.

### The Anxiety Trap
For some people, tracking devices **increase** sleep anxiety:
- "My tracker says I only got 45 minutes of deep sleep!"
- "My sleep score dropped—no wonder I feel terrible!"

If tracking is making you more anxious, simplify to just the basic diary.

## Your Two-Week Baseline

Before making any changes to your sleep habits, collect **two weeks** of sleep diary data. This baseline:
- Shows your true average sleep time
- Identifies your sleep efficiency
- Reveals patterns you might not notice night-to-night
- Guides your sleep restriction window (coming in Lesson 4)

## Weekly Metrics to Calculate

At the end of each week, calculate:

1. **Average Sleep Efficiency** (target: >=85%)
2. **Average Sleep Onset Latency** - time to fall asleep (target: &lt;30 minutes)
3. **Average WASO** - wake after sleep onset (target: &lt;30 minutes)
4. **Average Total Sleep Time**
5. **Number of "bad nights"** (track the trend—should decrease)

## Key Takeaways

1. **The sleep diary is essential** - It's the foundation of effective treatment
2. **Don't watch the clock** - Estimates in the morning are good enough
3. **Sleep efficiency is your key metric** - Below 85% = room for improvement
4. **Collect a baseline first** - Two weeks of data before making changes
5. **Look for patterns** - They'll guide which techniques to prioritize
