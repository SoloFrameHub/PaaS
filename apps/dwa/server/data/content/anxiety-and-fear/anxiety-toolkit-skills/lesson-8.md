## Redefining "Success"

It is crucial to redefine what success looks like in this process. Success is **NOT** feeling zero anxiety. If you wait until you are not scared to do something, you might wait forever.

### Success Is TRYING

Success is picking a rung on your ladder and attempting it, regardless of the outcome. The attempt itself is what builds the muscle. Every single time you choose to approach rather than avoid, you are making a deposit in your courage account.

### Success Is LEARNING

Success is staying in the situation long enough for your brain to receive new data: "This was uncomfortable, but I survived. Nothing catastrophic happened." Every exposure is a learning experience. Even if your anxiety stayed high the whole time, your brain still collected evidence that you can tolerate discomfort.

### Success Is SHOWING UP

Success is opening this lesson, reading these words, and continuing on this journey. Many people never take the first step. You have taken many.

## Key Takeaways from the Exposure Section

### 1. Avoidance Shrinks Your World; Approaching Expands It

You have learned that while avoidance provides temporary relief, it strengthens anxiety in the long run. Gently approaching your fears is the only way to take back territory from anxiety and expand your life. Every step up the ladder is a step toward freedom.

### 2. Bravery Is a Muscle, Not a Feeling

You do not wait until you feel brave to act. You act, and that is what builds the feeling of bravery and confidence over time. You are building this muscle with every step you take on your ladder. Like any muscle, it gets stronger with use.

### 3. You Are in Control of Your Actions

This is perhaps the most empowering lesson. Even when you cannot control the feeling of anxiety, you have proven that you can still control your actions. You can choose to stay, to breathe, and to use your tools, even when it is hard. That is power.
