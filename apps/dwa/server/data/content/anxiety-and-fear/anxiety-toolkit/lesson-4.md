You now have three powerful skills in your toolkit: the TIPP Skill, the 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding technique, and Riding the Wave. The final step is building the confidence to know which one to use and when. This lesson will help you practice matching the right skill to the right situation.

## When to Use Each Skill

Before we begin the challenge, here is a quick reference guide for choosing the right tool:

<InsightGrid>
<InsightItem icon="🚨" title="TIPP Skill — Crisis Mode">
Use when you are in a physiological emergency: panic attack, overwhelming rage, or feeling completely out of control. TIPP rapidly changes your body chemistry to bring you out of the "red zone."
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🧠" title="5-4-3-2-1 Grounding — Thought Spirals">
Use when your mind is the problem: racing thoughts, anxious rumination, replaying past events. Grounding redirects attention from internal thoughts to external sensory experiences.
</InsightItem>
<InsightItem icon="🌊" title="Riding the Wave — Strong Emotions">
Use when you feel a strong emotion that is not a crisis: sadness, frustration, moderate anxiety. This skill observes the emotion with curiosity and allows it to pass naturally.
</InsightItem>
</InsightGrid>

<Callout type="tip" title="Quick Decision Rule">
**Body screaming?** → TIPP. **Mind racing?** → Grounding. **Heart aching?** → Ride the Wave. When in doubt, start with whichever comes to mind first — any skill is better than no skill.
</Callout>

## Building Your Decision Framework

<StepByStep>
<Step title="Check Your Body First">
Is your heart racing? Are you shaking? Are you in a physiological crisis? If yes, start with **TIPP** to change your body chemistry immediately.
</Step>
<Step title="Check Your Mind Next">
Is the problem racing thoughts or rumination? Is your mind stuck in loops about the past or future? If yes, use **5-4-3-2-1 Grounding** to anchor in the present.
</Step>
<Step title="Check Your Emotions">
Is the emotion strong but manageable? Is it a wave of sadness, frustration, or moderate anxiety? If yes, **Ride the Wave** — name it, observe it, breathe through it.
</Step>
<Step title="Layer as Needed">
Skills can be combined in sequence: TIPP first to exit the red zone, then Grounding to anchor yourself, then Ride the Wave as the emotion naturally fades.
</Step>
</StepByStep>

Remember: there is no wrong answer. Any skill is better than no skill. And you can always combine them -- use TIPP to get out of the red zone, then Grounding to anchor yourself, then Ride the Wave as the emotion naturally fades.

## Key Takeaways

- [ ] TIPP is best for physiological crises like panic attacks that require rapid body chemistry changes
- [ ] 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding is best for thought spirals, rumination, and racing minds
- [ ] Riding the Wave is best for strong but manageable emotions like sadness or frustration
- [ ] Skills can be combined in sequence: TIPP first, then Grounding, then Riding the Wave
- [ ] Choosing the right tool becomes automatic with practice -- any skill used is better than none
